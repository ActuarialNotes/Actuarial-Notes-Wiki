Given the following information for a company:

| | |
|--|--|
| 5.00 | 12-Ultimate Gross Paid Claims Development Factor |
| 3.30 | 24-Ultimate Gross Paid Claims Development Factor |
| 12,000,000 | Accident Year 2018 Paid Gross Claims at 12 Months |

**Incremental Gross Industry Payment Pattern for 12 to 24 Months, by Quarter:**

| Age | 12–15 | 15–18 | 18–21 | 21–24 |
|-----|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| Percent Paid | 50% | 35% | 10% | 5% |

- The company maintains a quota share reinsurance agreement where they cede 30% of the business.
- Ultimate losses are estimated using the paid development technique.
- Accident Year 2018 Actual Net Paid Claims for the 15-to-18 month period: **$1,450,000**

## Part a (1.25 points)

Calculate the accident year 2018 expected net paid claims for the period 15 to 18 months, based on the following:

i. Claims emerge uniformly between evaluation points.

ii. The industry payment pattern.

### Answer

i. Uniform emergence: **$1,081,818**

ii. Industry pattern: **$1,514,545**

### Explanation

**Step 1: Estimate gross unpaid claims as of 12 months**

$$\text{Gross Ultimate} = 12{,}000{,}000 \times 5.00 = 60{,}000{,}000$$

$$\text{Gross Unpaid at 12 months} = \text{Gross Ultimate} - \text{Gross Paid at 12 months}$$

$$= 60{,}000{,}000 - 12{,}000{,}000 = 48{,}000{,}000$$

**Step 2: Estimate gross expected payments from 12 to 24 months**

$$\% \text{ Paid at 12 months} = \frac{1}{5.00} = 20\%$$

$$\% \text{ Paid at 24 months} = \frac{1}{3.30} = 30.30\%$$

$$\text{Expected Gross Payments from 12 to 24 months} = \text{Gross Unpaid at 12 months} \times \frac{\% \text{ paid}_{24} - \% \text{ paid}_{12}}{1 - \% \text{ paid}_{12}}$$

$$= 48{,}000{,}000 \times \frac{30.30\% - 20\%}{1 - 20\%} = 48{,}000{,}000 \times \frac{10.30\%}{80\%}$$

$$= 48{,}000{,}000 \times 0.12879 = 6{,}181{,}818$$

Alternatively:

$$\text{Gross Expected Paid}_{12-24} = 12{,}000{,}000 \times \left(\frac{5.00}{3.30} - 1\right) = 12{,}000{,}000 \times 0.5152 = 6{,}181{,}818$$

**Step 3: Apply net retention (70% = 1 − 30% cede)**

$$\text{Net Expected Payments}_{12-24} = 6{,}181{,}818 \times 0.70 = 4{,}327{,}273$$

**Part i — Uniform emergence (claims emerge evenly over each 3-month quarter):**

The 15-to-18 month period is one of four equal quarters within the 12-to-24 month period, so it receives $\frac{1}{4}$ of the expected payments:

$$\text{Net Expected Paid}_{15-18} = 4{,}327{,}273 \times 25\% = 1{,}081{,}818$$

**Part ii — Industry payment pattern:**

The industry pattern allocates 35% of the 12-to-24 month incremental payments to the 15-to-18 month quarter:

$$\text{Net Expected Paid}_{15-18} = 4{,}327{,}273 \times 35\% = 1{,}514{,}545$$

## Part b (0.5 points)

The actual net paid claims for accident year 2018 in the 15-to-18 month period were $1,450,000. Recommend whether to change the net estimated unpaid based on the actual results for accident year 2018 in the 15-to-18 month period.

### Explanation

**Recommendation: Do not change the net estimated unpaid.**

The actual net paid claims of $1,450,000 fall between the two expected estimates:
- Uniform assumption: $1,081,818
- Industry pattern: $1,514,545

The industry pattern ($1,514,545) is very close to the actual ($1,450,000), with a difference of only about $64,545 (4%). This level of variation is within normal random fluctuation, especially at early maturities where development factors are large and small changes in timing can produce relatively large dollar differences.

The industry pattern is generally a better assumption than uniform emergence because it reflects how claims actually develop in practice (more payments typically occur early in the 12-to-24 month period, tapering off). The close agreement between actual and expected under the industry pattern suggests the original unpaid estimate is reasonable.

Additionally, the company's own data at this early maturity may not be credible enough to override an industry-based estimate. Changing the projection based on a single period's deviation — particularly when that deviation is small — would introduce unnecessary volatility.

**Conclusion:** Maintain the current net estimated unpaid claims without revision.

### Examiner Report

Candidates were expected to calculate the projected net payments for the 15-to-18 month period under the two scenarios provided (uniform and industry emergence patterns within the 12-to-24 month interim period). Candidates were expected to recommend and justify whether to change the unpaid projection.

**Part a** — Common mistakes included:
- Not applying the 70% quota share (net retention) factor.
- Calculating the emergence for the wrong period (e.g., 12-to-15 months instead of 15-to-18 months).
- Only providing a response under one assumption (uniform or industry) but not both.

**Part b** — Common mistakes included:
- Providing only a recommendation without justification.
- Citing changes in case adequacy (the question uses paid data, so case adequacy is not relevant here).
