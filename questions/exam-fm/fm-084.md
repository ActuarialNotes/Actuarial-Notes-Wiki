An insurance company purchases a perpetuity-due providing a geometric series of quarterly payments for a price of 100,000 based on an annual effective interest rate of $i$. The first and second quarterly payments are 2000 and 2010, respectively.

Calculate $i$.

- A) 10.0%
- B) 10.2%
- C) 10.4%
- D) 10.6%
- E) 10.8%

## Explanation

The first payment is 2,000 and the second is 2,010, so payments grow by a factor of $2010/2000 = 1.005$ each quarter. Since the perpetuity-due has payments at the beginning of each quarter, the equation of value at the quarterly interest rate $j$ is:

$$
100,000 = 2,000 + 2,000(1.005)v + 2,000(1.005)^2 v^2 + \cdots = \frac{2,000}{1-1.005v}
$$

Solving: $1 - 1.005v = 2,000/100,000 = 0.02$, so $v = 0.98/1.005$.

The annual effective rate is:
$$i = v^{-4} - 1 = \left(\frac{1.005}{0.98}\right)^4 - 1 = 0.10601 = 10.6\%$$
