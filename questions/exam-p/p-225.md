$L\sim\text{Exp}(\lambda)$. $P(\text{fully covered by B})=1.9\times P(\text{fully covered by A})$, where A covers up to 1 and B covers up to 2. Calculate $\text{Var}(L)$.

- A) 0.1
- B) 0.4
- C) 2.4
- D) 9.5
- E) 90.1

## Explanation

$(1-e^{-2\lambda})=1.9(1-e^{-\lambda})\Rightarrow e^{-\lambda}=0.9\Rightarrow\lambda=\ln(10/9)$.

$\text{Var}(L)=1/\lambda^2\approx1/(0.1054)^2\approx\mathbf{90.1}$
