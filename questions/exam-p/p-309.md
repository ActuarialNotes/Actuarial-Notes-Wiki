An insurer divides a city into three zones with the following data:

| Zone | $P(\text{fire loss})$ | $\%$ of policies |
|---|---|---|
| A | 0.015 | 40% |
| B | 0.011 | 35% |
| C | 0.008 | 25% |

Given that a fire loss occurs in a home covered by the insurer, calculate the probability that the home is in Zone A.

- A) 0.349
- B) 0.400
- C) 0.441
- D) 0.465
- E) 0.506

## Explanation

$$
\begin{align*}
P(\text{fire}) &= 0.015(0.40)+0.011(0.35)+0.008(0.25) \\
               &= 0.006+0.00385+0.002 \\
               &= 0.01185
\end{align*}
$$

$$
\begin{align*}
P(A \mid \text{fire}) &= \frac{0.006}{0.01185} \\
                      &\approx \mathbf{0.506}
\end{align*}
$$
