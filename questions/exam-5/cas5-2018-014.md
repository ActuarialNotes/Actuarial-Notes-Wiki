For each of the following stakeholders, describe the importance of having accurate unpaid claim estimates:

i. Internal Management
ii. Investors
iii. Regulators

## Part a (0.5 points)

Describe the importance of accurate unpaid claim estimates for Internal Management.

### Explanation

Accurate reserve estimates are used by internal management to make sound business decisions in pricing, underwriting, and corporate strategy. If reserves are understated, management may not recognize a profitability problem until it is too late to take corrective action — for example, they may delay needed rate increases. Conversely, overstated reserves could trigger unnecessary actions such as raising rates or tightening underwriting guidelines when none are warranted. Accurate reserves are also needed to make appropriate reinsurance purchasing decisions.

### Examiner Report

Candidates were expected to know how under-reserving or over-reserving could impact internal management.

Common mistakes include:
- Providing only one brief point when the question asked candidates to "describe," which requires at least two briefly described items for full credit

## Part b (0.5 points)

Describe the importance of accurate unpaid claim estimates for Investors.

### Explanation

Inaccurate reserve estimates distort the financial statements that investors rely on to assess the value of an insurance company. Reserves that are too low will make the company appear more profitable and solvent than it actually is, causing investors to overpay; reserves that are too high will make the company appear weaker than it is, potentially depressing stock value. Accurate reserves are also needed to provide an accurate estimate of dividends the company can pay to shareholders.

### Examiner Report

Candidates were expected to know how under-reserving or over-reserving could impact investors.

Common mistakes include:
- Providing only one brief point when the question asked candidates to "describe"

## Part c (0.5 points)

Describe the importance of accurate unpaid claim estimates for Regulators.

### Explanation

Regulators rely on accurate reserve estimates to assess an insurer's solvency and financial health. If reserves are understated, regulators may not identify a financially troubled insurer until it is too late to intervene and prevent insolvency, leaving policyholders unprotected. Accurate reserves also help regulators assess the appropriateness of premium rate levels and evaluate whether the insurer can meet its obligations to policyholders.

### Examiner Report

Candidates were expected to know how under-reserving or over-reserving could impact regulators.

Common mistakes include:
- Providing only one brief point when the question asked candidates to "describe"
- Specifying that regulators would use the information to determine the appropriate reserve level (rather than describing the broader consequences of inaccurate reserves)
