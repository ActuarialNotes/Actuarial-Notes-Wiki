A loan is amortized with level monthly payments at an annual effective interest rate of 10%. The amount of principal repaid in the 6th month is 500.

Calculate the principal repaid in the 30th month.

- A) 500
- B) 555
- C) 605
- D) 705
- E) 805

## Explanation

Principal repaid at payment $k$ is $Pv^{n-k+1}$ where $v$ is the monthly discount factor. Each month the principal repaid grows by a factor of $(1+j)$ where $j$ is the monthly rate.

The monthly rate: $j = 1.10^{1/12} - 1$.

The ratio of principal at payment 30 to payment 6:
$$(1+j)^{30-6} = (1.10^{1/12})^{24} = 1.10^2 = 1.21$$

Principal at month 30: $500 \times 1.21 = 605$.
