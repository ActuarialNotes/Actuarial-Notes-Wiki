# Canadian Institute of Actuaries Established (1965)

## Historical Significance
By an Act of the Parliament of Canada, the Canadian Institute of Actuaries (CIA) was incorporated as the sole professional organization for practicing actuaries within Canada. It decoupled Canadian standards of practice from British and American frameworks.

## Regulatory and Governance Impact
- **Strict Practice Frameworks:** Mandates that any actuarial valuation or certificate required by Canadian federal or provincial law must be signed by a Fellow of the Canadian Institute of Actuaries (FCIA).
- **Standards of Practice:** Establishes independent oversight for calculating policy liabilities, standardizing definitions of sound financial condition testing for domestic insurance carriers.
