Losses due to accidents at an amusement park are exponentially distributed. An insurance company offers the park owner two policies to insure against losses.

Policy A has a deductible of 1.44. For a random loss, the probability is 0.640 that under this policy, the insurer will pay some money to the park owner. Policy B has a deductible of $d$. For a random loss, the probability is 0.512 that under this policy, the insurer will pay some money to the park owner.

Calculate $d$.

- A) 0.960
- B) 1.152
- C) 1.728
- D) 1.800
- E) 2.160

## Explanation

For an exponential loss $X$ with parameter $\lambda$, $P(\text{insurer pays}) = P(X > d) = e^{-\lambda d}$.

**From Policy A:** $e^{-1.44\lambda} = 0.640 = 0.8^2$.

**From Policy B:** $e^{-\lambda d} = 0.512 = 0.8^3$.

Since $e^{-1.44\lambda} = 0.8^2$ and $e^{-\lambda d} = 0.8^3$:
$$-1.44\lambda = 2\ln(0.8), \quad -\lambda d = 3\ln(0.8)$$
Dividing:
$$\frac{d}{1.44} = \frac{3}{2} \implies d = 1.44 \times 1.5 = \mathbf{2.160}$$
