Sam buys an eight-year, 5000 par bond with an annual coupon rate of 5%, paid annually. The bond sells for 5000. Let $d_1$ be the Macaulay duration just before the first coupon is paid. Let $d_2$ be the Macaulay duration just after the first coupon is paid.

Calculate $\dfrac{d_1}{d_2}$.

- A) 0.91
- B) 0.93
- C) 0.95
- D) 0.97
- E) 1.00

## Explanation

Since the bond sells at par, the Macaulay duration equals the annuity-due present value factor.

Let $d_0$ be the duration at time 0 (an 8-year par bond):
$$d_0 = \ddot{a}_{\overline{8}|0.05} = 6.7864$$

**Just before** the first coupon (1 year later, but the coupon hasn't been paid): the cash flows are the same as at time 0 but all occur one year sooner, so:
$$d_1 = d_0 - 1 = 5.7864$$

**Just after** the first coupon (now a 7-year par bond):
$$d_2 = \ddot{a}_{\overline{7}|0.05} = 6.0757$$

$$\frac{d_1}{d_2} = \frac{5.7864}{6.0757} = 0.9524$$

*Alternatively*: the numerators of $d_1$ and $d_2$ are identical (they differ only by whether the time-1 coupon is included, which contributes 0 to the numerator since $t=0$). The denominator of $d_1$ includes the extra coupon of 250, giving price 5250 vs. 5000, so the ratio is $5000/5250 = 0.9524$.
