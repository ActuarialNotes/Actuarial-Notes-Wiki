An actuary's child will be going to college 15 years from now. To save for the estimated cost of 2000, the actuary makes deposits into a fund at the end of each year for the next 15 years. The first deposit is $X$. Each subsequent deposit is 3% larger than the previous deposit.

The fund earns an annual effective rate of 8%.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 45
- B) 57
- C) 62
- D) 67
- E) 88

## Explanation

The accumulated value at time 15 of a geometrically increasing annuity-immediate (growth 3%, interest 8%):

$$2000 = X\cdot\frac{(1.08)^{15} - (1.03)^{15}}{0.08-0.03} \cdot (1.08)^0 \cdot \frac{1}{...}$$

Actually: $2000 = X\cdot\frac{(1.08)^{14} + 1.03(1.08)^{13} + \cdots + (1.03)^{14}}$

AV $= X \cdot \dfrac{(1.08)^{15} - (1.03)^{15}}{1.08 - 1.03} = X \cdot \dfrac{3.17217 - 1.55797}{0.05} = X \cdot \dfrac{1.61420}{0.05} = 32.284X$

$$X = 2000/32.284 = 61.95 \approx 62$$
