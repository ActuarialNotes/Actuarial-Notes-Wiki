A company is required to pay $50{,}000(1.075)^y$ in $y$ years. The company invests 30,000 in a 28-year zero-coupon bond and 20,000 in a 35-year zero-coupon bond to Redington immunize its position against small changes in interest rates, based on an annual effective interest rate of 7.5%.

Calculate $y$.

- A) 30.80
- B) 31.32
- C) 31.50
- D) 31.68
- E) 32.20

## Explanation

For Redington immunization, the Macaulay duration of the assets must equal the Macaulay duration of the liability $= y$.

Duration of assets (two zero-coupon bonds with maturities 28 and 35):
$$y = D_{assets} = \frac{30{,}000(28) + 20{,}000(35)}{30{,}000 + 20{,}000} = \frac{840{,}000 + 700{,}000}{50{,}000} = \frac{1{,}540{,}000}{50{,}000} = 30.8$$

Also verify: PV of assets at 7.5% $= 50{,}000$ ✓ (given), and convexity of assets $> $ convexity of liability (asset cash flows bracket the liability) ✓.

So $y = 30.8$.
