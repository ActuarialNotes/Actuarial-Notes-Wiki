Suppose that a random draw is made from a normal distribution $N(\mu, \sigma^2)$, where $\sigma$ is known to be 2.

The normal distribution density is given by:

$$f(x; \mu, \sigma) = \frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}}\, e^{-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}}, \qquad -\infty < x < \infty$$

Calculate the Fisher information of $\mu$.

- A) Less than 0.2
- B) At least 0.2, but less than 0.4
- C) At least 0.4, but less than 0.6
- D) At least 0.6, but less than 0.8
- E) At least 0.8

## Explanation

The log-density is

$$\ln f = -\ln(\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}) - \frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}.$$

Differentiate with respect to $\mu$:

$$\frac{\partial \ln f}{\partial \mu} = \frac{x-\mu}{\sigma^2}, \qquad \frac{\partial^2 \ln f}{\partial \mu^2} = -\frac{1}{\sigma^2}.$$

The Fisher information for a single observation is

$$I(\mu) = -E\!\left[\frac{\partial^2 \ln f}{\partial \mu^2}\right] = \frac{1}{\sigma^2} = \frac{1}{2^2} = 0.25.$$

This falls in $[0.2, 0.4)$, so the answer is **B**.
