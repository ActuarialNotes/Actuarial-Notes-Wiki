An insurance company models the number of claims per year as Poisson with rate $\lambda$. It is given that $P(\text{no claims in year 1}) = e^{-1.2}$.

Calculate the probability that the waiting time until the first claim exceeds one year.

- A) 0.01
- B) 0.10
- C) 0.23
- D) 0.30
- E) 0.36

## Explanation

The waiting time until the first claim follows an exponential distribution with rate $\lambda$, and:

$$P(\text{no claims in year 1}) = P(T_1 > 1) = e^{-\lambda}$$

Given $e^{-\lambda} = e^{-1.2}$: $\lambda = 1.2$.

$$
\begin{align*}
P(\text{wait} > 1) &= P(T_1 > 1) \\
                   &= e^{-1.2} \\
                   &\approx \mathbf{0.301 \approx 0.30}
\end{align*}
$$
