A 15-year loan with an annual effective interest rate of 6% has payments of 400 at the end of each year. At the time of the fifth payment, the borrower pays an extra 1000 and then repays the balance over five years with a revised annual payment of $X$.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 264.13
- B) 435.38
- C) 461.51
- D) 556.46
- E) 698.90

## Explanation

Outstanding balance after 4 payments (prospective): $400a_{\overline{11}|0.06} = 400(7.8869) = 3154.76$.

After 5th payment of 400: $3154.76(1.06) - 400 = 3344.05 - 400 = 2944.05$.

After extra 1000: $2944.05 - 1000 = 1944.05$.

$$X = 1944.05/a_{\overline{5}|0.06} = 1944.05/4.21236 = 461.51$$
