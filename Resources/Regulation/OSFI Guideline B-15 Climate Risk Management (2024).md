# OSFI Guideline B-15 (Final March 2023)

## Overview
OSFI's first dedicated climate guideline sets expectations for governance, risk management, scenario analysis, capital planning, and climate-related financial disclosure for federally regulated institutions — phased in starting fiscal 2024 for the largest banks and internationally active insurance groups, fiscal 2025 for other in-scope insurers.

## Key Details
*   **Two Chapters:** (1) Governance and risk management of physical and transition risk; (2) Mandatory climate disclosures aligned with TCFD architecture and converging with ISSB standards.
*   **Standardized Climate Scenario Exercise (SCSE):** Industry-wide scenario analysis exercise requiring insurers to quantify portfolio exposure under prescribed climate pathways.
*   **Climate Risk Returns:** New regulatory data filings; OSFI published lessons-learned from the first filing round (2025) by major banks and internationally active insurers.

## Multi-Agent Execution Mandates
*   **Risk & Actuarial:** Physical-risk modeling (cat model extensions to forward climate states) and transition-risk assessment embedded in ORSA.
*   **Finance & IR:** Audited-adjacent climate disclosures with GHG emissions reporting (including phased Scope 3) on regulatory timelines.
*   **Underwriting:** Pressure to articulate underwriting-portfolio climate strategy, not just investment-side exposure.
