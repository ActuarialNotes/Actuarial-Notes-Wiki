Suppose that $X_1, \ldots, X_{10}$ is a random sample from a normal distribution $N(0, \sigma^2)$ such that:

$$\sum_{i=1}^{10} X_i = 10 \quad \text{and} \quad \sum_{i=1}^{10} X_i^2 = 500$$

Calculate the value of minimum variance unbiased estimator of $\sigma^2$.

- A) Less than 30
- B) At least 30, but less than 50
- C) At least 50, but less than 70
- D) At least 70, but less than 90
- E) At least 90

## Explanation

Because the mean is **known** to be $0$, the minimum variance unbiased estimator (MVUE) of $\sigma^2$ uses the known mean rather than the sample mean:

$$\hat\sigma^2 = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}(X_i - 0)^2 = \frac{\sum X_i^2}{n} = \frac{500}{10} = 50.$$

This estimator is unbiased ($E[X_i^2] = \sigma^2$) and, being a function of the complete sufficient statistic $\sum X_i^2$, it is the MVUE. The value $50$ falls in $[50, 70)$, so the answer is **C**.
