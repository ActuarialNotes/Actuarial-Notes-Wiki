A compound distribution has $N \sim \text{Poisson}(2)$ claims. Each individual claim amount $X_i \sim \text{Poisson}(3)$, independent of everything else. Let $S = \sum_{i=1}^{N} X_i$.

Calculate the standard deviation of $S$.

- A) $\sqrt{24}$
- B) $\sqrt{36}$
- C) $\sqrt{48}$
- D) $\sqrt{60}$
- E) $\sqrt{100}$

## Explanation

For a compound distribution with $N \sim \text{Poisson}(\lambda)$:

$$\text{Var}(S) = \lambda\,E[X^2]$$

With $\lambda=2$ and $X \sim \text{Poisson}(3)$: $E[X^2] = \text{Var}(X)+E[X]^2 = 3+9 = 12$.

$$\text{Var}(S) = 2 \times 12 = 24$$

$$\text{SD}(S) = \sqrt{24} = 2\sqrt{6} \approx 4.90$$
