A debt is amortized with 120 level payments at the end of each month based on an annual effective interest rate of 8%. The amount of principal in the sixth payment is 600.

Calculate the amount of principal in the 24th payment.

- A) 532
- B) 673
- C) 700
- D) 704
- E) 784

## Explanation

Monthly rate $j = 1.08^{1/12}-1 = 0.006434$. Principal in payment $k$ grows by $(1+j)^{k-1}$ relative to the first.

Actually, principal in payment $k$ = $P v^{n-k+1}$ where $n=120$.

$$P_6 = P \cdot (1+j)^{6-1} \cdot v^{120} \cdot (1+j)^{0}... $$

More directly: $P_k = P_6 \cdot (1+j)^{k-6}$.

$$P_{24} = 600 \cdot (1+j)^{24-6} = 600(1.006434)^{18}$$

But monthly rate from annual 8%: $j = 1.08^{1/12}-1 = 0.006434$.

Actually, the principal repaid at payment $k$ is $P v^{n-k+1}$, growing by $(1+j)$ per period:
$$P_{24} = P_6 \cdot (1+j)^{18} = 600(1.006434)^{18} = 600(1.12247) = 673.48 \approx 673$$
