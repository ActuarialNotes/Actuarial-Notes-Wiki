You are given the following information about flights on ABC Airlines:

- Flights are delayed at a Poisson rate of four per month.
- The number of passengers on each flight is independently distributed with mean of 100 and standard deviation of 50.
- Each passenger on a delayed flight is compensated with a payment of 200.

Calculate the standard deviation of the total annual compensation for delayed flights.

- A) Less than 100,000
- B) At least 100,000, but less than 125,000
- C) At least 125,000, but less than 150,000
- D) At least 150,000, but less than 175,000
- E) At least 175,000

## Explanation

The total annual compensation is a **compound Poisson** sum $S = \sum_{i=1}^{M} W_i$, where $M$ is the annual number of delayed flights and $W_i$ is the compensation paid on the $i$-th delayed flight.

**Frequency.** Delays occur at $4$ per month, so the annual rate is

$$\lambda = 4 \times 12 = 48.$$

**Severity per flight.** Let $N$ be the number of passengers (mean $100$, standard deviation $50$). Each passenger receives $200$, so $W = 200N$ with

$$E[W] = 200(100) = 20{,}000, \qquad \operatorname{Var}(W) = 200^2(50^2) = 100{,}000{,}000.$$

$$E[W^2] = \operatorname{Var}(W) + E[W]^2 = 10^8 + (2\times10^4)^2 = 10^8 + 4\times10^8 = 5\times10^8.$$

**Compound Poisson variance.** For a compound Poisson sum, $\operatorname{Var}(S) = \lambda\, E[W^2]$:

$$\operatorname{Var}(S) = 48 \times 5\times10^8 = 2.4\times10^{10}.$$

$$\operatorname{SD}(S) = \sqrt{2.4\times10^{10}} \approx 154{,}919.$$

This falls in $[150{,}000, 175{,}000)$, so the answer is **D**.
