Two immediate annuities have the following characteristics:

- X: Pays $1/m$, $m$ times per year, for 10 years
- Y: Pays $P$ at the end of years 2, 4, 6, 8, and 10

You are given:
  i) The accumulated value at time 1 of $1/m$ paid at times $1/m$, $2/m$, …, 1 is 1.0331.
  ii) $s_{\overline{2}|} = 2.075$.
  iii) The present value of X equals the present value of Y.

Calculate $P$.

- A) 1.94
- B) 2.01
- C) 2.03
- D) 2.07
- E) 2.14

## Explanation

From (i): the accumulated value of $m$ payments of $1/m$ per period at annual rate $i$ over 1 year is $1.0331$. This is $s_{\overline{1}|}^{(m)} = 1.0331/1 = 1.0331$, i.e., an annuity-due accumulated for 1 year.

More precisely, $s_{\overline{1}|}^{(m)}\cdot(1+i)^{-1}$ is the factor for payments within a year. We have $1.0331 a_{\overline{10}|} = $ PV of X.

PV of X $= 1.0331 a_{\overline{10}|}$.

PV of Y $= P(v^2 + v^4 + v^6 + v^8 + v^{10}) = P\cdot\frac{v^2-v^{12}}{1-v^2} = P\cdot\frac{1-v^{10}}{(1+i)^2-1} = P\cdot\frac{a_{\overline{10}|}}{s_{\overline{2}|}}$

Setting PV X = PV Y: $1.0331 a_{\overline{10}|} = Pa_{\overline{10}|}/s_{\overline{2}|}$

$$P = 1.0331 \times s_{\overline{2}|} = 1.0331 \times 2.075 = 2.14$$
