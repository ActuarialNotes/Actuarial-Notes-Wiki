A company's preferred stock will pay level annual dividends forever starting five years from now.

Using an annual effective interest rate of 10%, the modified duration of the stock is $D$.

Calculate $D$.

- A) 13.64
- B) 14.55
- C) 15.00
- D) 16.00
- E) 16.50

## Explanation

Let the annual dividend be 1 (WLOG). PV of the deferred perpetuity (first payment at time 5, so a perpetuity-immediate deferred 4 years):
$$PV = v^4 a_{\overline{\infty}|} = \frac{v^4}{i} = \frac{(1.1)^{-4}}{0.1} = 6.83013$$

The time-weighted PV (numerator for Macaulay duration), with payments at times 5, 6, 7, ...:
$$\begin{align*}
\text{Num} &= v^4\left[4a_{\overline{\infty}|} + (Ia)_{\overline{\infty}|}\right] = v^4\left[\frac{4}{i} + \frac{1+i}{i^2}\right] \\
&= (1.1)^{-4}\left[40 + \frac{1.1}{0.01}\right] = 0.68301(40+110) = 102.452
\end{align*}$$

$$\begin{align*}
\text{Macaulay duration} &= 102.452/6.83013 = 15.00 \\
\text{Modified duration} &= 15.00/1.10 = 13.636 \approx 13.64
\end{align*}$$
