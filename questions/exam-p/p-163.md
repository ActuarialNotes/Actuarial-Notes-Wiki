For a certain health insurance policy, losses are uniformly distributed on the interval $[0, b]$. The policy has a deductible of 180 and the expected value of the unreimbursed portion of a loss is 144.

Calculate $b$.

- A) 236
- B) 288
- C) 388
- D) 450
- E) 468

## Explanation

The unreimbursed portion is $\min(X, 180)$.

$$
\begin{align*}
E[\min(X,180)] &= \int_0^{180} \frac{x}{b}\,dx + 180 \cdot \frac{b - 180}{b} \\
               &= \frac{180^2}{2b} + 180 - \frac{180^2}{b} \\
               &= 180 - \frac{16200}{b}
\end{align*}
$$

Setting equal to 144:
$$180 - \frac{16200}{b} = 144 \implies \frac{16200}{b} = 36 \implies b = \frac{16200}{36} = \mathbf{450}$$
