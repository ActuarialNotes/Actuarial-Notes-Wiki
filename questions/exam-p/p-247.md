Joint probability function $p(x,y)$ for $x=0,1,2$ and $y=0,1,2$:

| | $y=0$ | $y=1$ | $y=2$ |
|---|---|---|---|
| $x=0$ | 1/15 | $a$ | 2/15 |
| $x=1$ | $a$ | $b$ | $a$ |
| $x=2$ | 2/15 | $a$ | 1/15 |

Let $a$ be the value that **minimizes** the variance of $X$. Calculate $\text{Var}(Y)$.

- A) $2/5$
- B) $8/15$
- C) $16/25$
- D) $2/3$
- E) $7/10$

## Explanation

From the sum constraint: $4a+b=3/5$. The marginal $P(X=k)$ gives $E[X]=1$ (constant) and $\text{Var}(X)=2a+2/5$, minimized at $a=0$.

With $a=0$: $b=3/5$. Marginal of $Y$: $P(Y=0)=P(Y=2)=1/5$, $P(Y=1)=3/5$.

$E[Y]=1$, $E[Y^2]=7/5$, $\text{Var}(Y)=7/5-1=\mathbf{2/5}$
