An inspector examines a random sample of three glasses from each incoming box of ten glasses. The inspector accepts the box if at least two of the three examined are found to be in good condition. A box contains exactly two glasses that are not in good condition.

Calculate the probability that the box will be accepted.

- A) 0.10
- B) 0.47
- C) 0.70
- D) 0.90
- E) 0.93

## Explanation

8 good glasses, 2 not good. $\binom{10}{3}=120$.

$P(\text{exactly 2 good}) = \binom{8}{2}\binom{2}{1}/120 = 28\times2/120 = 56/120$

$P(\text{3 good}) = \binom{8}{3}/120 = 56/120$

$$
\begin{align*}
P(\text{accept}) &= \frac{56+56}{120} \\
                 &= \frac{112}{120} \\
                 &= \frac{14}{15} \\
                 &\approx \mathbf{0.933 \approx 0.93}
\end{align*}
$$
