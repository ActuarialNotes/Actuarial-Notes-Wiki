A worker is starting an endowment fund for his charity. The fund will accumulate at a nominal annual interest rate of 12% convertible monthly. Beginning today, the worker will deposit 500 monthly for ten years. No deposits or withdrawals will be made for the subsequent ten years. Exactly twenty years from today, monthly payments of $X$ will be made to his charity and continue forever.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 3270
- B) 3572
- C) 3758
- D) 3796
- E) 3834

## Explanation

Monthly rate $j = 1\%$. The fund grows as a monthly annuity-due for 10 years, then accumulates for another 10 years, and finally funds a monthly perpetuity-due.

Setting the accumulated fund equal to the PV of the perpetuity-due:

$$500\ddot{s}_{\overline{120}|0.01}(1.01)^{120} = \frac{X(1.01)}{0.01}$$

$$
\begin{align*}
500(230.04)(1.01)^{120} &= 101X \\
383{,}404.42 &= 101X \\
X &= 3{,}796.08
\end{align*}
$$
