# Establishment of Equitable Life (1762)

## Historical Significance
The founding of the Society for Equitable Assurances in London marks the birth of modern actuarial science. It was the first life insurance institution to apply scientific, mathematical principles to determine premium rates.

## Core Actuarial Innovation
- **Age-Based Pricing:** Prior to 1762, life insurance used flat premiums regardless of age. Equitable Life introduced premiums that scaled with the applicant's age at entry.
- **Mortality Data Implementation:** Utilized early demographic statistics (derived from the London Bills of Mortality and mathematically structured by James Dodson) to construct life tables for solvency and pricing models.
