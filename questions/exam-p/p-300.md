A group of insurance policies has all been in force for at least three years. The company will pay a dividend on each policy that had no more than one claim incurred in the past three years. The number of claims incurred on a policy in any year follows a Poisson distribution with mean 0.288, and years are independent.

Calculate the probability that a policy chosen at random from the group will receive a dividend.

- A) 0.01
- B) 0.36
- C) 0.42
- D) 0.54
- E) 0.79

## Explanation

Total claims in 3 years: $T \sim \text{Poisson}(3 \times 0.288) = \text{Poisson}(0.864)$.

Dividend if $T \leq 1$:

$$P(T=0) = e^{-0.864} \approx 0.4214$$

$$P(T=1) = 0.864 \cdot e^{-0.864} \approx 0.864(0.4214) \approx 0.3641$$

$$P(T \leq 1) = 0.4214 + 0.3641 = \mathbf{0.7855 \approx 0.79}$$
