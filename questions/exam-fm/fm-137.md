A life insurance company invests two million in a 10-year zero-coupon bond and four million in a 30-year zero-coupon bond. The annual effective yield rate for both bonds is 8%.

When the 10-year bond matures, the company reinvests the proceeds in another 10-year zero-coupon bond. At that time the bond yield rate is 12% annual effective.

After 20 years from the initial investment, the 30-year bond is sold to yield an annual effective rate of 10% to the buyer. The maturity of the second 10-year bond and the sale of the 30-year bond result in a gain of $X$ on the company's initial investment of six million.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 23 million
- B) 29 million
- C) 32 million
- D) 34 million
- E) 42 million

## Explanation

All values in millions.

**10-year bond:** Matures at $2(1.08)^{10} = 4.31785$. Reinvested at 12% for 10 years: $4.31785(1.12)^{10} = 13.4106$.

**30-year bond:** Sold at year 20 to yield 10%. The bond matures in 10 more years for $4(1.08)^{30} = 40.2506$ million. Sale price: $40.2506(1.10)^{-10} = 15.5184$.

Total proceeds at year 20: $13.4106 + 15.5184 = 28.9290$.

Gain: $28.9290 - 6 = 22.929 \approx 23$ million.
