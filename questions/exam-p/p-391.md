Let $N \sim \text{Poisson}(0.6)$.

Calculate the probability that $N=0$ given that $N$ is even.

- A) 0.30
- B) 0.45
- C) 0.60
- D) 0.75
- E) 0.84

## Explanation

For $N \sim \text{Poisson}(\lambda)$:

$$
\begin{align*}
P(N \text{ even}) &= \frac{1+e^{-2\lambda}}{2} \\
                  &= \frac{1+e^{-1.2}}{2} \\
                  &\approx \frac{1+0.3012}{2} \\
                  &= 0.6506
\end{align*}
$$

$$P(N=0) = e^{-0.6} \approx 0.5488$$

$$
\begin{align*}
P(N=0 \mid N \text{ even}) &= \frac{0.5488}{0.6506} \\
                           &\approx \mathbf{0.8435 \approx 0.84}
\end{align*}
$$
