Insurance claims are made according to a Poisson process $\{N(t), t \ge 0\}$ with rate $\lambda = 1$.

Calculate $E[N(1) \cdot N(2)]$.

- A) Less than 1.5
- B) At least 1.5, but less than 2.5
- C) At least 2.5, but less than 3.5
- D) At least 3.5, but less than 4.5
- E) At least 4.5

## Explanation

Write $N(2) = N(1) + [N(2) - N(1)]$, where the increment $N(2) - N(1)$ is independent of $N(1)$ and, for a rate-1 process, both $N(1)$ and $N(2)-N(1)$ are Poisson with mean 1.

$$E[N(1)N(2)] = E\big[N(1)\big(N(1) + (N(2)-N(1))\big)\big] = E[N(1)^2] + E[N(1)]\,E[N(2)-N(1)].$$

For $N(1) \sim \text{Poisson}(1)$: $E[N(1)^2] = \operatorname{Var}(N(1)) + E[N(1)]^2 = 1 + 1 = 2$, and $E[N(1)] = E[N(2)-N(1)] = 1$.

$$E[N(1)N(2)] = 2 + (1)(1) = 3.$$

This falls in $[2.5, 3.5)$, so the answer is **C**.
