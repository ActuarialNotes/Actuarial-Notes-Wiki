You are given:

- A system has 9 independent components, $i = 1, 2, 3, \ldots, 9$
- $p_i = 0.75$, is the probability that the $i^{\text{th}}$ component is functioning
- The system's structure is as pictured below: a left block of three parallel branches — branch (1), branch (2–3–4 in series), and branch (5–6 in series) — placed in series with a right block consisting of component 7 in parallel with the series pair (8–9).

![System structure diagram: nine components arranged as three parallel branches (1; 2-3-4; 5-6) in series with a block of component 7 parallel to series pair 8-9](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ActuarialNotes/Actuarial-Notes-Wiki/main/Media/Attachments/masi-2018f-q6-reliability-diagram.png)

- A new component with probability of functioning $= 0.95$ is available to replace one of the current components.
- The goal is to maximize the improvement of system's reliability.

Determine which one of the following components should be replaced to achieve the goal.

- A) Component 1
- B) Component 2
- C) Component 5
- D) Component 7
- E) Component 8

## Explanation

Replacing a component raises its reliability from $0.75$ to $0.95$. The gain in system reliability is largest for the component with the greatest **reliability (Birnbaum) importance** — roughly, the component whose branch is least "backed up" by redundancy.

Compute the block reliabilities at $p = 0.75$. Left block (three parallel branches with reliabilities $0.75$, $0.75^3 = 0.4219$, $0.75^2 = 0.5625$):

$$R_L = 1 - (0.25)(0.5781)(0.4375) = 0.9368.$$

Right block ($7$ parallel with series $8$–$9$, reliabilities $0.75$ and $0.5625$):

$$R_R = 1 - (0.25)(0.4375) = 0.8906.$$

Evaluating the improvement $\Delta R$ for each candidate (change in its branch reliability times the sensitivity of the system):

$$\text{Comp 1: } 0.045,\quad \text{Comp 2: } 0.011,\quad \text{Comp 5: } 0.019,\quad \text{Comp 7: } 0.082,\quad \text{Comp 8: } 0.035.$$

Component 7 sits alone on a parallel branch of the *less reliable* right block, giving it the highest importance and the largest gain. The answer is **D**.
