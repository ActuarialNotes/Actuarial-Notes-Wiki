A company markets an investment opportunity to four potential customers. The probability of making a sale is 0.5 for each of the first three customers and 0.1 for the fourth customer. Purchases are independent.

Calculate the probability that at most two customers purchase the investment.

- A) 0.38
- B) 0.46
- C) 0.54
- D) 0.84
- E) 0.90

## Explanation

Let $N = X_1+X_2+X_3+X_4$ where $X_{1,2,3} \sim \text{Bern}(0.5)$ and $X_4 \sim \text{Bern}(0.1)$.

$$P(N=0) = (0.5)^3(0.9) = 0.1125$$

$$
\begin{align*}
P(N=1) &= \binom{3}{1}(0.5)^3(0.9) + (0.5)^3(0.1) \\
       &= 0.3375+0.0125 \\
       &= 0.3500
\end{align*}
$$

$$
\begin{align*}
P(N=2) &= \binom{3}{2}(0.5)^3(0.9) + \binom{3}{1}(0.5)^3(0.1) \\
       &= 0.3375+0.0375 \\
       &= 0.3750
\end{align*}
$$

$$
\begin{align*}
P(N \leq 2) &= 0.1125+0.3500+0.3750 \\
            &= \mathbf{0.8375 \approx 0.84}
\end{align*}
$$
