An insurance company has an equal number of claims in each of three territories. In each territory, only three claim amounts are possible: 100, 500, and 1000. Based on the company's data, the probabilities of each claim amount are:

| Claim Amount | 100 | 500 | 1000 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Territory 1 | 0.90 | 0.08 | 0.02 |
| Territory 2 | 0.80 | 0.11 | 0.09 |
| Territory 3 | 0.70 | 0.20 | 0.10 |

Calculate the standard deviation of a randomly selected claim amount.

- A) 254
- B) 291
- C) 332
- D) 368
- E) 396

## Explanation

Equal weights for territories, so average the probabilities:
$$
\begin{align*}
P(100) &= \frac{0.90+0.80+0.70}{3} \\
       &= 0.80, \quad P(500) = \frac{0.39}{3} = 0.13, \quad P(1000) = \frac{0.21}{3} = 0.07
\end{align*}
$$

$$
\begin{align*}
E[X] &= 100(0.80) + 500(0.13) + 1000(0.07) \\
     &= 80 + 65 + 70 \\
     &= 215
\end{align*}
$$
$$
\begin{align*}
E[X^2] &= 10000(0.80) + 250000(0.13) + 1000000(0.07) \\
       &= 8000 + 32500 + 70000 \\
       &= 110500
\end{align*}
$$
$$
\begin{align*}
\text{Var}(X) &= 110500 - 215^2 \\
              &= 110500 - 46225 \\
              &= 64275
\end{align*}
$$
$$\text{SD}(X) = \sqrt{64275} \approx \mathbf{254}$$
