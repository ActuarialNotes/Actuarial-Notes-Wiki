Toby purchased a 20-year par value bond with semiannual coupons of 40 and a redemption value of 1100. The bond can be called at 1200 on any coupon date prior to maturity, starting at the end of year 15.

Calculate the maximum price of the bond to guarantee that Toby will earn an annual nominal interest rate of at least 6% convertible semiannually.

- A) 1251
- B) 1262
- C) 1278
- D) 1286
- E) 1295

## Explanation

Semiannual coupon rate $= 40/1200 = 3.33\% > 3\%$ (desired yield), so early redemption at 1200 is worse for the holder.

**If called at end of year 15 (30 periods):**

$$P = 40a_{\overline{30}|0.03} + 1200v_{0.03}^{30} = 1278.40$$

**If held to maturity (40 periods):**

$$P = 40a_{\overline{40}|0.03} + 1100v_{0.03}^{40} = 1261.80$$

The maximum price guaranteeing at least 6% nominal yield is the **minimum** across all possible redemption dates: $\mathbf{1261.80}$.
