Two independent estimates are made of a building's value. Each estimate is normally distributed with mean $10b$ and variance $b^2$.

Calculate the probability that the first estimate is at least 20% higher than the second.

- A) 0.023
- B) 0.100
- C) 0.115
- D) 0.221
- E) 0.444

## Explanation

Let $D = E_1 - 1.2E_2$.

$$E[D] = 10b - 1.2(10b) = -2b$$
$$\text{Var}(D) = b^2 + (1.2)^2 b^2 = 2.44b^2$$

$$
\begin{align*}
P(D \geq 0) &= P\!\left(Z \geq \frac{0-(-2b)}{b\sqrt{2.44}}\right) \\
            &= P\!\left(Z \geq \frac{2}{\sqrt{2.44}}\right) \\
            &= P(Z \geq 1.281) \\
            &= 1 - \Phi(1.281) \\
            &\approx \mathbf{0.100}
\end{align*}
$$
