A three-year bond with a face value of 1000 pays coupons semiannually. The bond is redeemable at face value. It is bought at issue at a price to produce an annual yield rate of 10% convertible semiannually. If the term of the bond is doubled and the yield rate remains the same, the purchase price would decrease by 49.

Calculate the amount of a coupon.

- A) 37
- B) 46
- C) 54
- D) 63
- E) 74

## Explanation

Let $r$ = semiannual coupon amount. For the 3-year bond ($n=6$ periods):
$$P = ra_{\overline{6}|0.05} + 1000(1.05)^{-6} = 5075.692r + 746.215$$

For the 6-year bond ($n=12$ periods):
$$P - 49 = ra_{\overline{12}|0.05} + 1000(1.05)^{-12} = 8863.252r + 556.837$$

Subtracting: $49 = (5075.692r + 746.215) - (8863.252r + 556.837) = -3787.56r + 189.378$

$$r = (189.378 - 49)/3787.56 = 140.378/3787.56 = 0.037 \implies \text{coupon} = 1000(0.037) = 37$$
