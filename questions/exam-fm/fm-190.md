A loan of 10,000 is being repaid by payments of 1,000 at the end of each quarter for as long as necessary, plus a drop payment.

The annual nominal rate of interest on the loan is 16% convertible quarterly.

Calculate the amount of interest in the tenth payment.

- A) 112
- B) 146
- C) 179
- D) 233
- E) 281

## Explanation

Quarterly rate $j = 4\%$. Level payment $R = 1000$.

Principal repaid in payment 1: $1000 - 10{,}000(0.04) = 600$.

Principal repaid in payment 10 grows by $(1.04)^9$: $600(1.04)^9 = 854$.

Interest in payment 10: $1000 - 854 = 146$.
