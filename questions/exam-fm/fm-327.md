An $n$-year annuity, with a payment of 1 at the end of each year, has a present value of 5.5554 at an annual nominal interest rate, convertible $m$ times per year.

Also:
  i) $j$ is the effective rate of interest per interest conversion period, and
  ii) $(1+j)^m = 1.0614$.

Calculate $n$.

- A) 7
- B) 8
- C) 9
- D) 10
- E) There is insufficient information to calculate $n$.

## Explanation

$(1+j)^m = 1.0614$ means the annual effective rate $i = 6.14\%$.

$$
\begin{align*}
5.5554 &= a_{\overline{n}|0.0614} \\
       &= \frac{1-(1.0614)^{-n}}{0.0614}
\end{align*}
$$

$$
\begin{align*}
1-(1.0614)^{-n} &= 0.0614 \times 5.5554 \\
                &= 0.34110 \\
(1.0614)^{-n} &= 0.65890 \\
n &= -\frac{\ln(0.65890)}{\ln(1.0614)} \\
  &= 7.00
\end{align*}
$$
