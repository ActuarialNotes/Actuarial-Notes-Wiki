A firm has a liability cash flow of 100 at the end of year two and a second liability cash flow of 200 at the end of year three.

The firm also has asset cash flows of $X$ at the end of years one and five.

Using an annual effective interest rate of 10%, calculate the absolute value of the difference between the Macaulay durations of the asset and liability cash flows.

- A) 0.018
- B) 0.020
- C) 0.022
- D) 0.024
- E) 0.026

## Explanation

For the assets to have equal PV, $X$ solves: $Xv + Xv^5 = 100v^2 + 200v^3$.

$$
\begin{align*}
X(0.90909 + 0.62092) &= 100(0.82645) + 200(0.75131) \\
1.53001X &= 232.907 \\
X &= 152.225
\end{align*}
$$

Macaulay duration of liabilities:
$$\bar{d}_L = \frac{2(100v^2)+3(200v^3)}{100v^2+200v^3} = \frac{2(82.645)+3(150.263)}{232.907} = \frac{616.078}{232.907} = 2.6452$$

Macaulay duration of assets:
$$\bar{d}_A = \frac{1(Xv)+5(Xv^5)}{Xv+Xv^5} = \frac{X(0.90909)+5X(0.62092)}{1.53001X} = \frac{4.01369X}{1.53001X} = 2.6233$$

$$|2.6452 - 2.6233| = 0.0219 \approx 0.022$$
