The amount that must be invested at $i\%$ ($0\% < i\% < 10\%$) to accumulate to $Y$ at the end of three years at an annual rate of:

  i) simple interest of $i\%$ is $Q$
  ii) compound interest of $i\%$ is $R$
  iii) simple discount of $i\%$ is $S$
  iv) compound discount of $i\%$ is $T$

Determine which of the following is true.

- A) $Q < R < S < T$
- B) $R < Q < S < T$
- C) $S < T < R < Q$
- D) $T < S < Q < R$
- E) $T < S < R < Q$

## Explanation

For $0 < i < 10\%$ and $n=3$, the PV factors (amount to invest today for FV = $Y$) are:

- Simple interest: $Q = Y/(1+3i)$
- Compound interest: $R = Y/(1+i)^3$
- Simple discount: $S = Y(1-3i)$
- Compound discount: $T = Y(1-i)^3$

Comparing the factors using $i = 7\%$, $Y = 121$ as a numerical check:

| Method | Formula | Value |
|---|---|---|
| Simple discount | $121(1-0.21)$ | $S = 95.59$ |
| Compound discount | $121(0.93)^3$ | $T = 97.33$ |
| Compound interest | $121/(1.07)^3$ | $R = 98.77$ |
| Simple interest | $121/(1.21)$ | $Q = 100.00$ |

The ordering $S < T < R < Q$ holds because:
- Simple discount overstates the discount (removes more than needed), giving the smallest PV.
- Compound discount is less extreme, so $T > S$.
- Compound interest accumulates faster than compound discount discounts, so $R > T$.
- Simple interest under-accumulates relative to compound, so its denominator is smallest and $Q$ is largest.

Therefore $S < T < R < Q$, which is answer **(C)**.
