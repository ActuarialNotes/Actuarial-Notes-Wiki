# Quebec Law 25 — Main Provisions (September 22, 2023)

## Overview
The core obligations of Quebec's modernized private-sector privacy law took effect, imposing the most stringent privacy regime in North America on insurers handling Quebec residents' personal information — with phased provisions in 2022 (privacy officer, breach reporting), 2023 (the bulk), and 2024 (data portability).

## Key Details
*   **Consent & Transparency:** Granular, purpose-specific consent; privacy policies in clear language; privacy-by-default settings.
*   **Privacy Impact Assessments:** Mandatory PIAs for projects involving personal information and for transfers outside Quebec.
*   **Automated Decision-Making:** Individuals must be informed when decisions (e.g., underwriting or claims triage) are based exclusively on automated processing, with a right to have it reviewed — directly constraining insurance AI deployment.
*   **Penalties:** Administrative monetary penalties up to $10M or 2% of worldwide turnover; penal fines up to $25M or 4%.

## Multi-Agent Execution Mandates
*   **Compliance & Data:** Data inventories, retention schedules, and cross-border transfer assessments for all Quebec books of business.
*   **IT & Analytics:** Consent management platforms and explainability/human-review workflows wrapped around pricing, underwriting, and fraud models.
*   **Claims & Distribution:** Breach-notification playbooks and broker data-sharing agreements re-executed under the new standard.
