Trish had a loan with a balance of 4000 at the beginning of month 1. Starting with month 1, and every month thereafter, she made a payment of $X$ in the middle of the month. At the beginning of month 4, and every 6 months thereafter, she borrowed an additional 800.

Trish's loan balance became 4000 again at the end of month 36.

The annual nominal interest rate for the loan is 26.4%, convertible quarterly.

Determine which of the following is an equation of value that can be used to solve for $X$.

- A) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{6}\frac{800}{\left(1+\frac{0.2640}{4}\right)^{2n-1}}=\sum_{n=1}^{36}\frac{X}{\left(1+\frac{0.2640}{4}\right)^{\frac{n-0.5}{3}}}$
- B) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{6}\frac{800}{\left(1+\frac{0.2640}{12}\right)^{6n-3}}=\sum_{n=1}^{36}\frac{X}{\left(1+\frac{0.2640}{12}\right)^{n-0.5}}$
- C) $\displaystyle 4000+\sum_{n=1}^{6}\frac{800}{\left(1+\frac{0.2640}{4}\right)^{\frac{6n-2}{3}}}=\frac{4000}{\left(1+\frac{0.2640}{4}\right)^{12}}+\sum_{n=1}^{36}\frac{X}{\left(1+\frac{0.2640}{4}\right)^{\frac{n}{3}}}$
- D) $\displaystyle 4000+\sum_{n=1}^{6}\frac{800}{\left(1+\frac{0.2640}{12}\right)^{6n-3}}=\frac{4000}{\left(1+\frac{0.2640}{12}\right)^{36}}+\sum_{n=1}^{36}\frac{X}{\left(1+\frac{0.2640}{12}\right)^{n-0.5}}$
- E) $\displaystyle 4000+\sum_{n=1}^{6}\frac{800}{\left(1+\frac{0.2640}{4}\right)^{2n-1}}=\frac{4000}{\left(1+\frac{0.2640}{4}\right)^{12}}+\sum_{n=1}^{36}\frac{X}{\left(1+\frac{0.2640}{4}\right)^{\frac{n-0.5}{3}}}$

## Explanation

The quarterly interest rate is $0.264/4$ and the time unit is quarters (1 month = 1/3 quarter).

The 800 borrowings occur at the beginning of months 4, 10, 16, 22, 28, 34 — which in quarters are times 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11 (i.e., times $2n-1$ for $n = 1,\ldots,6$).

The $X$ payments occur at the middle of months 1–36 — in quarters at times $\frac{n-0.5}{3}$ for $n = 1,\ldots,36$.

The 4000 balance at month 36 = time 12 (quarters).

Setting the PV of cash inflows = PV of cash outflows (discounting to time 0):

$$4000 + \sum_{n=1}^{6}\frac{800}{\left(1+\frac{0.264}{4}\right)^{2n-1}} = \frac{4000}{\left(1+\frac{0.264}{4}\right)^{12}} + \sum_{n=1}^{36}\frac{X}{\left(1+\frac{0.264}{4}\right)^{(n-0.5)/3}}$$

This matches answer **(E)**.
