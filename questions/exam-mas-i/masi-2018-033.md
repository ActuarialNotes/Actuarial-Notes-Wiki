Consider a multiple regression model with an intercept, 3 independent variables and 13 observations. The value of $R^2 = 0.838547$.

Calculate the value of the F-statistic used to test the hypothesis $H_0: \beta_1 = \beta_2 = \beta_3 = 0$.

- A) Less than 5
- B) At least 5, but less than 10
- C) At least 10, but less than 15
- D) At least 15, but less than 20
- E) At least 20

## Explanation

The overall $F$-statistic can be written in terms of $R^2$, the number of predictors $p = 3$, and the sample size $n = 13$:

$$F = \frac{R^2 / p}{(1 - R^2)/(n - p - 1)}.$$

Substitute $R^2 = 0.838547$, $p = 3$, $n - p - 1 = 13 - 3 - 1 = 9$:

$$F = \frac{0.838547 / 3}{(1 - 0.838547)/9} = \frac{0.279516}{0.017939} \approx 15.58.$$

This falls in $[15, 20)$, so the answer is **D**.
