A three-year bond with face amount $X$ and a coupon of 4 paid at the end of every six months is priced at 90.17. A three-year bond with face value of $1.6X$ and a coupon of 4 paid at the end of every six months is priced at 132.47. Both have the same yield rate.

Calculate the annual nominal yield rate, convertible semiannually.

- A) 6%
- B) 8%
- C) 9%
- D) 11%
- E) 12%

## Explanation

Bond 1: $90.17 = 4a_{\overline{6}|j} + Xv^6$

Bond 2: $132.47 = 4a_{\overline{6}|j} + 1.6Xv^6$

Multiplying Bond 1 by 1.6: $144.272 = 6.4a_{\overline{6}|j} + 1.6Xv^6$

Subtracting Bond 2: $11.802 = 2.4a_{\overline{6}|j}$, so $a_{\overline{6}|j} = 4.9175$.

Using BAII Plus: $PV = 4.9175$, $N=6$, $PMT=1$, $FV=0 \implies I/Y = 6\%$ per half-year.

Annual nominal rate convertible semiannually $= 2 \times 6\% = 12\%$.
