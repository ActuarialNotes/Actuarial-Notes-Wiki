Losses under an insurance policy are uniformly distributed on the interval $[0, 100]$. A deductible is set so that the expected claim payment of losses net of the deductible is 32.

Calculate the deductible.

- A) 9
- B) 18
- C) 20
- D) 36
- E) 52

## Explanation

For $X \sim \text{Uniform}[0,100]$, the expected claim payment (above deductible $d$) is:

$$E[(X-d)_+] = \frac{(100-d)^2}{2 \times 100} = \frac{(100-d)^2}{200} = 32$$

$$(100-d)^2 = 6400 \implies 100-d = 80 \implies d = \mathbf{20}$$
