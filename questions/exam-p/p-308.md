A life insurance policy pays 1000 upon death if the policyholder survives at least one year but less than five years after purchasing the policy. Let $X$ denote years survived, with cumulative probabilities $P[X<1]=0.05$, $P[X<2]=0.12$, $P[X<3]=0.21$, $P[X<4]=0.33$, $P[X<5]=0.48$.

Calculate the standard deviation of the payment made under this policy.

- A) 218
- B) 430
- C) 480
- D) 495
- E) 500

## Explanation

$P(\text{payment}=1000) = P(1 \leq X < 5) = 0.48-0.05 = 0.43$.

$P(\text{payment}=0) = 0.57$.

$$E[\text{payment}] = 1000(0.43) = 430$$

$$E[\text{payment}^2] = 1000^2(0.43) = 430{,}000$$

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{Var} &= 430{,}000-430^2 \\
           &= 430{,}000-184{,}900 \\
           &= 245{,}100
\end{align*}
$$

$$\text{SD} = \sqrt{245{,}100} \approx \mathbf{495}$$
