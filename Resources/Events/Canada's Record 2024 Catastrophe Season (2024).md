# Canada's Record Catastrophe Summer (July–August 2024)

## Overview
Four severe events struck within roughly five weeks: the July 16 Toronto-area flash flooding, the Jasper wildfire (late July), the August 5 Calgary hailstorm (among the costliest single events in Canadian history at roughly $3 billion insured), and remnants of Hurricane Debby flooding Quebec. The cluster drove 2024 to approximately $8.5 billion in insured catastrophe losses — by far the costliest year on record in Canada — with claim volumes that overwhelmed industry adjusting capacity.

## Key Details
*   **Urban Flood Concentration:** Toronto and Quebec losses dominated by sewer backup and overland water in dense urban cores, stressing municipal infrastructure narratives.
*   **Hail Severity:** Calgary's exposure growth and hail frequency renewed debate on resilient building materials and impact-resistant roofing incentives.
*   **Wildland-Urban Interface:** Jasper demonstrated WUI exposure inside protected park townsites with constrained mitigation options.
*   **Claims Surge:** Industry bodies cited unprecedented simultaneous claim volumes, with adjuster shortages extending cycle times nationally.

## Multi-Agent Execution Mandates
*   **Actuarial & Reinsurance:** Canadian cat load assumptions and reinsurance program structures (retentions, aggregate covers) reset at subsequent renewals; secondary perils now treated as primary loss drivers.
*   **Underwriting:** Accelerated roof-age rating, hail deductibles, and water-damage mitigation requirements (backwater valves, sump credits).
*   **Government Relations:** Intensified industry advocacy for the national flood insurance program and climate-adaptation infrastructure investment.
