Given the following information as of December 31, 2017:

| Calendar Year | Paid Claims ($) | Paid ULAE ($) |
|---------------|-----------------|---------------|
| 2014 | 21,300 | 1,030 |
| 2015 | 20,900 | 1,040 |
| 2016 | 20,800 | 1,040 |
| 2017 | 21,200 | 1,090 |

| Accident Year | Paid Claims ($) | Reported Claim Counts | Closed Claim Counts | Ultimate Claim Counts | Ultimate Claims ($) |
|---------------|-----------------|-----------------------|---------------------|-----------------------|---------------------|
| 2014 | 20,800 | 335 | 335 | 335 | 20,800 |
| 2015 | 18,000 | 300 | 270 | 330 | 21,400 |
| 2016 | 12,000 | 275 | 190 | 330 | 21,500 |
| 2017 | 5,000 | 200 | 80 | 335 | 21,800 |

- 60%: Percent of unallocated work that occurs when a claim is opened
- 40%: Percent of unallocated work that occurs when a claim is closed
- Claims are fully settled and paid by 48 months.

## Part a (0.75 points)

Estimate the provision for claims incurred but not yet reported (IBNYR) for all accident years as of December 31, 2017.

### Answer

| Accident Year | IBNYR ($) |
|---------------|-----------|
| 2014 | 0 |
| 2015 | 1,700–1,945 |
| 2016 | 3,473–3,583 |
| 2017 | 8,437–8,894 |

### Explanation

IBNYR claims are the portion of IBNR attributable to claims not yet reported (i.e., unreported counts). The key is to separate IBNR into IBNER (case development on known claims) and IBNYR (new unreported claims).

**Method 1: Implied ultimate severity on unreported counts**

$$\text{Implied Ultimate Severity} = \frac{\text{Ultimate Claims}}{\text{Ultimate Claim Counts}}$$

$$\text{IBNYR} = (\text{Ultimate Counts} - \text{Reported Counts}) \times \text{Implied Ultimate Severity}$$

| AY | Unreported Counts | Implied Severity | IBNYR ($) |
|----|------------------|-----------------|-----------|
| 2014 | $335 - 335 = 0$ | $20{,}800/335 = 62.09$ | 0 |
| 2015 | $330 - 300 = 30$ | $21{,}400/330 = 64.85$ | $30 \times 64.85 = 1{,}945$ |
| 2016 | $330 - 275 = 55$ | $21{,}500/330 = 65.15$ | $55 \times 65.15 = 3{,}583$ |
| 2017 | $335 - 200 = 135$ | $21{,}800/335 = 65.07$ | $135 \times 65.07 = 8{,}785$ |

**Alternatively, using ultimate claims directly:**

$$\text{IBNYR} = \text{Ultimate Claims} \times \left(1 - \frac{\text{Reported Counts}}{\text{Ultimate Counts}}\right)$$

- AY 2015: $21{,}400 \times (1 - 300/330) = 1{,}945$
- AY 2016: $21{,}500 \times (1 - 275/330) = 3{,}583$
- AY 2017: $21{,}800 \times (1 - 200/335) = 8{,}785$

## Part b (1 point)

Estimate unpaid ULAE as of December 31, 2017.

### Answer

Approximately $1,017–$1,024

### Explanation

**Step 1: Determine the ULAE ratio**

$$\text{ULAE Ratio} = \frac{\text{CY Paid ULAE}}{\text{CY Paid Claims}}$$

| Calendar Year | Paid ULAE / Paid Claims |
|---------------|------------------------|
| 2014 | $1{,}030 / 21{,}300 = 0.0484$ |
| 2015 | $1{,}040 / 20{,}900 = 0.0498$ |
| 2016 | $1{,}040 / 20{,}800 = 0.0500$ |
| 2017 | $1{,}090 / 21{,}200 = 0.0514$ |

Selected ULAE ratio: **0.05** (approximately)

**Step 2: Separate unpaid claims into IBNYR and non-IBNYR (IBNER + case reserves)**

Using the 60%/40% split:
- 60% of unallocated work occurs at opening (captured when claim is reported — not yet for IBNYR claims)
- 40% of unallocated work occurs at closing (not yet incurred for any open/unreported claim)

For IBNER + case reserves (open claims that are already reported): only 40% of remaining work is yet to occur.

For IBNYR (unreported claims): 100% of work remains (both open and close events).

**Step 3: Calculate unpaid claims by component**

| AY | Total Unpaid = Ult − Paid | IBNYR | IBNER + Case = Unpaid − IBNYR |
|----|--------------------------|-------|-------------------------------|
| 2014 | $20{,}800 - 20{,}800 = 0$ | 0 | 0 |
| 2015 | $21{,}400 - 18{,}000 = 3{,}400$ | 1,945 | 1,455 |
| 2016 | $21{,}500 - 12{,}000 = 9{,}500$ | 3,583 | 5,917 |
| 2017 | $21{,}800 - 5{,}000 = 16{,}800$ | 8,785 | 8,015 |
| **Total** | **29,700** | **14,313** | **15,387** |

**Step 4: Apply the Kittel formula**

$$\text{Unpaid ULAE} = w \times [\text{IBNYR} + 40\% \times (\text{IBNER + Case Reserves})]$$

$$= 0.05 \times [14{,}313 + 0.40 \times 15{,}387]$$

$$= 0.05 \times [14{,}313 + 6{,}155]$$

$$= 0.05 \times 20{,}468 \approx 1{,}023$$

(Slight variation in IBNYR estimates yields approximately **$1,017–$1,024**.)

### Examiner Report

Candidates were expected to estimate incurred but not yet reported (IBNYR) claims and use this information to estimate unpaid ULAE.

**Part a** — Common mistakes included:
- Calculating the unpaid claim amount (ultimate claims minus paid claims) instead of the IBNYR provision.
- Calculating IBNYR claim counts instead of claim dollars, or not knowing how to proceed after calculating IBNYR counts.
- Incorrectly calculating IBNYR and/or open claim counts.
- Estimating case outstanding and IBNER provision instead of the IBNYR provision.
- Attempting to estimate IBNYR by taking the sum of accident year ultimate or paid less calendar year paid.

**Part b** — Common mistakes included:
- Using an invalid ULAE estimation method based on the data presented.
- Using the wrong denominator in the ratio of calendar year paid ULAE to calendar year paid claims.
- Using incorrect weights to apply to different claim provisions (e.g., 50%/50% weighting, 60% to case outstanding + IBNER, etc.).
- Taking 100% of the pure IBNR and then 40% of the total unpaid losses (without subtracting pure IBNR), resulting in 140% weight to pure IBNR.
- Incorrectly calculating case outstanding + IBNER.
- Applying the ratio of calendar year paid ULAE to calendar year paid claims to total unpaid claims.
- Calculating unpaid ULAE for only one accident year.
- Incorrectly mixing calendar and accident year data.
