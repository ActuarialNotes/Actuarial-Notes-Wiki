An insurance company purchases a perpetuity-due at an annual effective yield rate of 12.5% for 9450. The perpetuity provides annual payments according to the repeating three-year pattern 100, $X$, 100, 100, $X$, 100, 100, $X$, 100, ... .

Calculate $X$.

- A) 2950
- B) 2963
- C) 3321
- D) 3344
- E) 3359

## Explanation

Three-year rate: $j = (1.125)^3 - 1 = 0.423828$.

Split into three perpetuities-due with payments 3 years apart, starting at $t=0, 1, 2$:

$$9450 = \frac{100(1+j)}{j} + \frac{X(1+j)}{j(1.125)} + \frac{100(1+j)}{j(1.125)^2}$$

$$9450 = \frac{100(1.423828)}{0.423828} + \frac{X(1.423828)}{0.423828(1.125)} + \frac{100(1.423828)}{0.423828(1.125)^2}$$

$$9450 = 335.73 + \frac{1.423828X}{0.476806} + 298.43$$

$$
\begin{align*}
9450 &= 634.16 + 2.98568X \\
X &= \frac{9450 - 634.16}{2.98568} = 2963.19
\end{align*}
$$
