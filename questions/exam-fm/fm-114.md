Jennifer establishes an investment account to pay for college expenses for her daughter. She plans to invest $X$ at the beginning of each month for the next 21 years. Beginning at the end of the 18th year, she will withdraw 20,000 annually. The final withdrawal at the end of the 21st year will exhaust the account. She anticipates earning an annual effective yield of 8% on the investment.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 137.90
- B) 142.80
- C) 146.40
- D) 150.60
- E) 154.30

## Explanation

The monthly interest rate is $j = (1.08)^{1/12} - 1 = 0.6434\%$.

The present value of withdrawals at time 0 (withdrawals at end of years 18, 19, 20, 21):
$$PV_{withdrawals} = 20,000 \cdot a_{\overline{4}|0.08} \cdot (1.08)^{-17} = 20,000 s_{\overline{4}|0.08} (1.08)^{-21}$$

Alternatively, the AV of deposits at end of year 21 equals the AV of withdrawals at end of year 21:
$$20,000 s_{\overline{4}|0.08} = X \ddot{s}_{\overline{252}|0.00643}$$
$$90,122.24 = 630.99X \implies X = 142.83$$
