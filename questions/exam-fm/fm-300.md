A loan of 5,000,000 is to be repaid by installments of $X$ at the end of each quarter over a period of ten years. The annual nominal interest rate for the loan is 8% compounded quarterly.

The actual quarterly payment for the first five years is $X$ rounded up to the next higher 1000. After that, each quarterly payment is $X$ rounded up to the next higher 100,000, until the loan is paid off with a drop payment.

Calculate the total number of payments, including the drop payment, needed to repay the loan.

- A) 36
- B) 37
- C) 38
- D) 39
- E) 40

## Explanation

Quarterly rate $j = 2\%$. $X = 5{,}000{,}000/a_{\overline{40}|0.02} = 182{,}778.74$.

First 5 years (20 payments): each $= 183{,}000$ (rounded up to next 1000).

$OB_{20} = 5{,}000{,}000(1.02)^{20} - 183{,}000 s_{\overline{20}|0.02} = 7{,}429{,}736.55 - 183{,}000(24.2974) = 7{,}429{,}736.55 - 4{,}446{,}426 = 2{,}983{,}310.55$

Subsequent payments: each $= 200{,}000$ (rounded up to next 100,000).

$$\begin{align*}
2{,}983{,}310.55 &= 200{,}000\,a_{\overline{n}|0.02} \\
a_{\overline{n}|} &= 14.9166 \\
n &= 17.89
\end{align*}$$

So 17 full payments of 200,000 plus 1 drop payment $= 18$ more.

Total $= 20 + 18 = 38$.
