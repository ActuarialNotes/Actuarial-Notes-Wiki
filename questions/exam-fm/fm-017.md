Payments are made to an account at a continuous rate of $(8k + tk)$, where $0 \leq t \leq 10$.

Interest is credited at a force of interest $\delta_t = \dfrac{1}{8+t}$.

After time 10, the account is worth 20,000.

Calculate $k$.

- A) 111
- B) 116
- C) 121
- D) 126
- E) 131

## Explanation

The accumulation function is:

$$
\begin{align*}
a(t) &= e^{\int_0^t \frac{1}{8+r}dr} \\
     &= e^{\ln(8+r)\big|_0^t} \\
     &= \frac{8+t}{8}
\end{align*}
$$

Using the equation of value at the end of 10 years:

$$
\begin{align*}
20,000 &= \int_0^{10}(8k+tk)\frac{a(10)}{a(t)}\,dt \\
&= k\int_0^{10}(8+t)\frac{18/8}{(8+t)/8}\,dt \\
&= k\int_0^{10}18\,dt \\
&= 180k
\end{align*}
$$

$$k = \frac{20,000}{180} = 111$$
