An actuary uses statistical software to run a regression of the median price of a house on 12 predictor variables plus an intercept. He obtains the following (partial) model output:

- Residual standard error: 4.74 on 493 degrees of freedom
- Multiple R-squared: 0.7406
- F-statistic: 117.3 on 12 and 493 DF
- p-value: < 2.2e-16

Calculate the adjusted $R^2$ for this model.

- A) Less than 0.70
- B) At least 0.70, but less than 0.72
- C) At least 0.72, but less than 0.74
- D) At least 0.74, but less than 0.76
- E) At least 0.76

## Explanation

The residual degrees of freedom are $n - p - 1 = 493$ with $p = 12$ predictors, so $n = 506$. Adjusted $R^2$ penalizes for the number of predictors:

$$R^2_{\text{adj}} = 1 - (1 - R^2)\frac{n - 1}{n - p - 1} = 1 - (1 - 0.7406)\frac{505}{493}.$$

$$= 1 - 0.2594 \times 1.02434 = 1 - 0.26571 = 0.7343.$$

This falls in $[0.72, 0.74)$, so the answer is **C**.
