Given the following information for a workers compensation line of business:

| Accident Year | Claim Counts (reported as of 12/31/2012) | Severities (reported as of 12/31/2012) | Payroll ($000) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2010 | 1,549 | $22,418 | $63,438 |
| 2011 | 1,455 | $18,730 | $62,893 |
| 2012 | 1,023 | $12,501 | $67,005 |

| As of Month | Claim Count (Reported %) | Severities (Reported %) |
|---|---|---|
| 12 | 85.0% | 43.0% |
| 24 | 95.0% | 67.0% |
| 36 | 98.0% | 83.0% |

- The reported claim counts for accident year 2012 are unusually low due to a temporary slowdown of claims being opened.
- Annual frequency trend = -2%.
- Annual severity trend = +5%.
- Annual payroll trend = +4%.

Use an appropriate frequency-severity technique to estimate the IBNR for accident year 2012 at December 31, 2012 and justify all selections.

### Answer

$$\text{IBNR} \approx \$31{,}849{,}537$$

(approximately $31.85 million; an alternative approach yields approximately $31.54 million)

### Explanation

**Step 1: Exclude AY 2012 from frequency selection**

Because AY 2012 claim counts are subject to a temporary slowdown in claim opening, they do not reflect true ultimate frequency. Therefore, AY 2012 is excluded from frequency selection. (Severity is still reliable since it is an average per claim and is not affected by the count slowdown.)

**Step 2: Develop claim counts to ultimate and apply frequency trend**

AY 2010 is at 36 months as of 12/31/2012 → reported % = 98%  
AY 2011 is at 24 months as of 12/31/2012 → reported % = 95%  
AY 2012 is at 12 months as of 12/31/2012 → reported % = 85% (excluded from frequency selection)

Ultimate claim counts (divide by % reported):

$$\text{Ult Counts}_{2010} = \frac{1{,}549}{0.98} = 1{,}580.6$$

$$\text{Ult Counts}_{2011} = \frac{1{,}455}{0.95} = 1{,}531.6$$

**Trend ultimate counts to AY 2012 level** (frequency trend = -2% per year):

- AY 2010 is 2 years before 2012: trend factor = $(0.98)^2 = 0.9604$

$$\text{Trended Ult Counts}_{2010} = 1{,}580.6 \times 0.9604 = 1{,}518.0$$

- AY 2011 is 1 year before 2012: trend factor = $(0.98)^1 = 0.98$

$$\text{Trended Ult Counts}_{2011} = 1{,}531.6 \times 0.98 = 1{,}500.9$$

**Step 3: Develop payroll exposure and trend**

AY 2010 payroll trended to AY 2012 level (payroll trend = +4%):

$$\text{Trended Payroll}_{2010} = 63{,}438 \times (1.04)^2 = 63{,}438 \times 1.0816 = 68{,}615$$

AY 2011 payroll trended to AY 2012 level:

$$\text{Trended Payroll}_{2011} = 62{,}893 \times (1.04)^1 = 62{,}893 \times 1.04 = 65{,}409$$

**Step 4: Calculate trended pure premium (frequency × severity)**

An equivalent approach uses trended ultimate loss per $100 of payroll (pure premium = frequency × severity).

Develop severities to ultimate and trend to AY 2012 level (severity trend = +5%):

$$\text{Ult Sev}_{2010} = \frac{22{,}418}{0.83} \times (1.05)^2 = 27{,}009.6 \times 1.1025 = 29{,}778$$

$$\text{Ult Sev}_{2011} = \frac{18{,}730}{0.67} \times (1.05)^1 = 27{,}955.2 \times 1.05 = 29{,}353$$

Trended pure premium per $1,000 of payroll = (Trended Ult Counts / Trended Payroll) × Trended Ult Severity:

$$\text{PP}_{2010} = \frac{1{,}518.0}{68{,}615} \times 29{,}778 = 0.022121 \times 29{,}778 = \$658.81 \text{ per } \$1{,}000$$

$$\text{PP}_{2011} = \frac{1{,}500.9}{65{,}409} \times 29{,}353 = 0.022947 \times 29{,}353 = \$673.57 \text{ per } \$1{,}000$$

**Selected pure premium** (simple average of AY 2010 and AY 2011, excluding AY 2012 for the reasons stated above):

$$\text{Selected PP} = \frac{658.81 + 673.57}{2} = \$666.19 \text{ per } \$1{,}000$$

**Step 5: Estimate AY 2012 ultimate losses**

AY 2012 payroll = $67,005 thousand

$$\text{Ult Losses}_{2012} = 666.19 \times 67{,}005 = \$44{,}638{,}061$$

**Step 6: Calculate IBNR**

Reported losses for AY 2012 as of 12/31/2012:

$$\text{Rptd Losses}_{2012} = 1{,}023 \times 12{,}501 = \$12{,}788{,}523$$

$$\text{IBNR} = 44{,}638{,}061 - 12{,}788{,}523 = \$31{,}849{,}538$$

**Justification of selections:**

- **Frequency selection excludes AY 2012** because the claim count slowdown makes AY 2012 frequency unreliable. Using it would severely underestimate ultimate claim counts.
- **Severity includes all years** (AY 2010, 2011, 2012) or only 2010/2011 for stability; the problem states only the count was affected, not the per-claim severity.
- **Simple average** of AY 2010 and AY 2011 pure premiums is used for stability.

### Examiner Report

Candidates generally performed well on the calculation portion of this question.

Some candidates did not calculate frequency (claim counts / payroll) and simply multiplied the average of 2010 and 2011 claim counts by a severity selection to determine 2012 ultimate claims. This does not account for the 2012 exposure levels and was not awarded full credit.

Some candidates calculated the ultimate loss indication correctly and subsequently lost points by failing to calculate the indicated IBNR associated with the ultimate loss. A small portion of candidates calculated the IBNR for all 3 accident years rather than just 2012.

Some candidates did not justify their selections, as specified in the question. Writing "select average of 2010 and 2011" without explanation did not constitute a justification and did not receive credit.

Common mistakes included:
- Not using trend factors
- Not using loss development factors (i.e., not dividing by the % reported to get ultimate counts/severities)
- Applying development factors or trend factors to the incorrect year (e.g., applying the 36-month factor to 2012 rather than to 2010)
- Assuming that the inverse of the given percentage reported factors were age-to-age factors rather than age-to-ultimate factors
