You are given that the force of interest at time $t$ (in years) is $\dfrac{1}{t+8}$.

Calculate the annual effective rate of interest in year 5.

- A) 6.2%
- B) 6.6%
- C) 7.4%
- D) 8.3%
- E) 9.5%

## Explanation

$a(t) = \exp\!\left(\int_0^t \frac{1}{r+8}\,dr\right) = \exp[\ln(t+8)-\ln(8)] = \frac{t+8}{8}$

Annual effective rate in year 5 (from $t=4$ to $t=5$):
$$i_5 = \frac{a(5)}{a(4)} - 1 = \frac{13/8}{12/8} - 1 = \frac{13}{12} - 1 = \frac{1}{12} = 0.08333 = 8.3\%$$
