# Alberta DCPD Implementation (January 1, 2022)

## Overview
Under Bill 41 (the Insurance (Enhancing Driver Affordability and Care) Amendment Act, 2020), Alberta adopted Direct Compensation for Property Damage: not-at-fault drivers now claim vehicle damage from their own insurer rather than pursuing the at-fault party's insurer, aligning Alberta with Ontario, Quebec, and Atlantic Canada.

## Key Details
*   **Fault Allocation:** Recovery governed by a standardized fault determination framework; subrogation between insurers largely eliminated for covered vehicle damage.
*   **Premium Redistribution:** DCPD shifted physical-damage premium from third-party liability to a rated first-party coverage, requiring revenue-neutral repricing across the book.
*   **Companion Reforms:** Bill 41 also enhanced medical/treatment benefits and permitted use of more current interest rate assumptions in injury settlements.

## Multi-Agent Execution Mandates
*   **Actuarial & Product:** Required full re-segmentation of physical damage rating (driving record now rates the DCPD coverage of the not-at-fault claimant's own policy).
*   **Claims:** Adjusters retrained on fault charts and intercompany settlement conventions; cycle times improved by removing third-party recovery friction.
*   **IT:** PAS and claims platforms rebuilt coverage structures, fault-percentage logic, and stat-plan reporting for the new coverage part.
