The lifetime in years of a battery is modeled by a random variable $T$ with survival function
$$S(t) = \frac{(10-t)^2}{100}, \quad 0 \leq t \leq 10$$

Calculate $E[T]$.

- A) $10/3$
- B) $5$
- C) $20/3$
- D) $100/3$
- E) $10$

## Explanation

For a non-negative random variable, $E[T] = \int_0^\infty S(t)\,dt$:

$$E[T] = \int_0^{10} \frac{(10-t)^2}{100}\,dt = \frac{1}{100}\left[-\frac{(10-t)^3}{3}\right]_0^{10}$$

$$= \frac{1}{100}\left(0+\frac{1000}{3}\right) = \frac{1000}{300} = \frac{10}{3}$$
