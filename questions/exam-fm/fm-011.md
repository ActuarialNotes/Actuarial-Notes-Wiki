Mike buys a perpetuity-immediate with varying annual payments. During the first 5 years, the payment is constant and equal to 10. Beginning in year 6, the payments start to increase. For year 6 and all future years, the payment in that year is $K$% larger than the payment in the year immediately preceding that year, where $K < 9.2$.

At an annual effective interest rate of 9.2%, the perpetuity has a present value of 167.50.

Calculate $K$.

- A) 4.0
- B) 4.2
- C) 4.4
- D) 4.6
- E) 4.8

## Explanation

The present value consists of a level annuity for 5 years plus a deferred geometrically increasing perpetuity:

$$167.50 = 10a_{\overline{5}|9.2\%} + 10(1.092)^{-5}\sum_{t=1}^{\infty}\left[\frac{1+k}{1.092}\right]^t$$

$$167.50 = 38.6955 + 6.44001\cdot\frac{(1+k)/1.092}{1-(1+k)/1.092}$$

$$(167.50 - 38.6955)\left[1 - \frac{1+k}{1.092}\right] = 6.44001\cdot\frac{1+k}{1.092}$$

$$128.8045 = 135.24451\cdot\frac{1+k}{1.092}$$

$$1 + k = 1.0400$$

$$k = 0.0400 \implies K = 4.0\%$$
