A store purchased couch #1 for $X$ two months ago and plans to sell it for 1500 six months from today.

The same store purchases couch #2 for $X$ today and plans to sell it for 1500 four months from today.

The annual force of interest is a constant 10%.

The current value of the store's cash flows from the purchase and sale of couch #2 is 260.

Calculate the current value of the store's cash flows from the purchase and sale of couch #1.

- A) 216
- B) 218
- C) 256
- D) 260
- E) 307

## Explanation

Couch #2 NPV (at time 0):

$$\begin{align*}
260 &= 1500e^{-0.1(4/12)} - X \\
X &= 1500e^{-1/30} - 260 = 1500(0.96721) - 260 = 1190.82
\end{align*}$$

Couch #1 NPV (at time 0): the purchase 2 months ago has current value $-Xe^{0.1(2/12)}$; the sale in 6 months has PV $1500e^{-0.1(6/12)}$:

$$\begin{align*}
\text{Net} &= 1500e^{-0.05} - 1190.82\,e^{1/60} \\
&= 1500(0.95123) - 1190.82(1.01681) \\
&= 1426.84 - 1210.83 \\
&= 216.01 \approx 216
\end{align*}$$
