On any given day, a certain machine has either no malfunctions or exactly one malfunction. The probability of malfunction on any given day is 0.40. Machine malfunctions on different days are mutually independent.

Calculate the probability that the machine has its third malfunction on the fifth day, given that the machine has not had three malfunctions in the first three days.

- A) 0.064
- B) 0.138
- C) 0.148
- D) 0.230
- E) 0.246

## Explanation

Let $A$ be the event "third malfunction occurs on the 5th day".
Let $B$ be the event "not 3 malfunctions in the first 3 days".
Notice that if $A$ occurs, then exactly 2 malfunctions must have occurred in the first 4 days. This strictly implies that there were not 3 malfunctions in the first 3 days. Thus, $A \subset B$, and $A \cap B = A$.

We want $P(A \mid B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} = \frac{P(A)}{P(B)}$.

$P(A)$ is the negative binomial probability of getting the 3rd success on the 5th trial. This means exactly 2 successes in the first 4 days, and a success on the 5th day.
$$P(A) = \binom{4}{2}(0.4)^2(0.6)^2 \times (0.4) = 6(0.16)(0.36)(0.4) = 0.13824$$

$P(B)$ is the probability of NOT getting 3 malfunctions in 3 days. The probability of getting 3 malfunctions in 3 days is $(0.4)^3 = 0.064$.
$$P(B) = 1 - 0.064 = 0.936$$

The conditional probability is:
$$P(A \mid B) = \frac{0.13824}{0.936} = 0.14769 \approx 0.148$$
