A perpetuity-immediate with annual payments consists of ten level payments of $k$, followed by a series of increasing payments. Beginning with the eleventh payment, each payment is 200 larger than the preceding payment.

Based on an annual effective interest rate of 5.2%, the present value of the perpetuity is 50,000.

Calculate $k$.

- A) 34
- B) 86
- C) 163
- D) 283
- E) 409

## Explanation

The PV has two parts:

Level annuity PV: $k \cdot a_{\overline{10}|0.052} = k(7.647284)$

After payment 10, the remaining perpetuity (deferred 10 years) has payments $k+200, k+400, \ldots$ — this is a level perpetuity of $(k+200)$ plus an arithmetically increasing perpetuity of 200.

PV at time 10: $\dfrac{k+200}{0.052} + \dfrac{200}{0.052^2}$

PV at time 0: $v^{10}\left[\dfrac{k+200}{0.052} + \dfrac{200}{0.052^2}\right] = 0.60234\left[\dfrac{k+200}{0.052} + 73{,}964.50\right]$

Total:
$$50{,}000 = 7.647284k + 0.60234\left[\frac{k+200}{0.052} + 73{,}964.50\right]$$

$$50{,}000 = 7.647284k + 0.60234\left[19.2308k + 3846.15 + 73{,}964.50\right]$$

$$50{,}000 = 7.647284k + 11.5836k + 3846.15 + 44{,}527 = 19.231k + 48{,}373$$

$$k = 1{,}627/10.001 \approx 162.83 \approx 163$$
