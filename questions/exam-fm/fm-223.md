Determine which of the following expressions represents the modified duration for a zero-coupon bond that is currently priced at an annual effective yield rate $i$ and an $n$-year maturity.

- A) $n$
- B) $n(1+i)$
- C) $n(1+i)^{-1}$
- D) $\dfrac{\sum_{t=1}^n t(1+i)^{-t}}{\sum_{t=1}^n (1+i)^t}$
- E) $\dfrac{\sum_{t=1}^n t(1+i)^{-t}}{\sum_{t=1}^n (1+i)^{-t}}$

## Explanation

A zero-coupon bond with maturity $n$ has all cash flow at time $n$. Its Macaulay duration is therefore $n$ (the single cash flow time).

Modified duration $= D_{MAC}/(1+i) = n/(1+i) = n(1+i)^{-1}$.
