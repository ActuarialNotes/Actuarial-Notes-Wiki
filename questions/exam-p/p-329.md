A geneticist compiled the following information:

| Parent condition | $P(\text{child left-handed})$ | $P(\text{parent condition})$ |
|---|---|---|
| Two left-handed parents | 1/2 | 1/50 |
| Exactly one left-handed parent | 1/6 | 1/5 |
| No left-handed parents | 1/16 | 39/50 |

Calculate the probability that a randomly selected left-handed child has no left-handed parents.

- A) 0.09
- B) 0.42
- C) 0.53
- D) 0.78
- E) 0.91

## Explanation

$$
\begin{align*}
P(\text{LH child}) &= \frac{1}{50}\cdot\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{5}\cdot\frac{1}{6}+\frac{39}{50}\cdot\frac{1}{16} \\
                   &= \frac{1}{100}+\frac{1}{30}+\frac{39}{800}
\end{align*}
$$

Converting to denominator 2400: $= \frac{24+80+117}{2400} = \frac{221}{2400}$

$$
\begin{align*}
P(\text{0 LH parents}\mid\text{LH child}) &= \frac{(39/50)(1/16)}{221/2400} \\
                                          &= \frac{39/800}{221/2400} \\
                                          &= \frac{39 \times 3}{221} \\
                                          &= \frac{117}{221} \\
                                          &\approx \mathbf{0.530 \approx 0.53}
\end{align*}
$$
