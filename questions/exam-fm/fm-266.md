Let $P(0,t)$ be the current price of a zero-coupon bond that will pay 1 at time $t$.

Let $X$ be the value at time $n$ of an investment of 1 made at time $m$, where $m < n$.

Assume all investments earn the same interest rate.

Determine $X$.

- A) $\dfrac{P(0,m)}{P(0,n)}-1$
- B) $\dfrac{P(0,n)}{P(0,m)}+1$
- C) $\dfrac{P(0,m)}{P(0,n)}+1$
- D) $\dfrac{P(0,m)}{P(0,n)}$
- E) $\dfrac{P(0,n)}{P(0,m)}$

## Explanation

$P(0,t) = (1+i)^{-t}$, so an investment of 1 at time $m$ grows to $(1+i)^{n-m}$ by time $n$.

$$
\begin{align*}
X &= (1+i)^{n-m} \\
  &= \frac{(1+i)^n}{(1+i)^m} \\
  &= \frac{(1+i)^{-m}}{(1+i)^{-n}} \\
  &= \frac{P(0,m)}{P(0,n)}
\end{align*}
$$
