An insurance company has a single liability due in three years. The company fully immunizes its position by purchasing one-year and four-year zero-coupon bonds. The face value of the one-year bond is 20,000 and the face value of the four-year bond is 50,000.

Assume that the yield curve is flat.

Calculate the amount of the liability.

- A) 40,000
- B) 55,699
- C) 69,624
- D) 73,333
- E) 97,500

## Explanation

For full immunization, the duration of the assets must equal the liability duration (= 3 years).

Let $P_1$ and $P_4$ be the present values of the 1-year and 4-year bonds. Duration matching:
$$\frac{P_1(1) + P_4(4)}{P_1 + P_4} = 3 \quad\Rightarrow\quad P_4 = 2P_1$$

$P_1 = 20{,}000/(1+i)$ and $P_4 = 50{,}000/(1+i)^4$, so $P_4 = 2P_1$:

$$\begin{align*}
\frac{50{,}000}{(1+i)^4} &= \frac{2 \times 20{,}000}{1+i} \\
\frac{50{,}000}{(1+i)^3} &= 40{,}000 \\
(1+i)^3 &= 1.25 \\
i &= 0.077217
\end{align*}$$

$P_1 = 20{,}000/1.077217 = 18{,}566.4$, $P_4 = 37{,}132.7$, total $= 55{,}699.1$.

Liability $= 55{,}699.07(1.077217)^3 = 55{,}699.07 \times 1.25 = 69{,}623.83 \approx 69{,}624$.
