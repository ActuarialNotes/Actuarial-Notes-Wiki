A company has liabilities of 1000 due at the end of each of the next three years. The company will exactly match the cash flows of assets and liabilities. The following zero-coupon bonds are available:

| Maturity | Yield |
|---|---|
| 1-Year | 8% |
| 2-Year | 9% |
| 3-Year | 10% |

Calculate how much more will be invested in the 1-year bond than in the 3-year bond.

- A) 0
- B) 84
- C) 132
- D) 158
- E) 175

## Explanation

For exact cash-flow matching, invest exactly enough in each zero-coupon bond to fund each liability:

1-year bond (face 1000): invest $1000/1.08 = 925.93$
2-year bond (face 1000): invest $1000/1.09^2 = 841.68$
3-year bond (face 1000): invest $1000/1.10^3 = 751.31$

Amount more in 1-year than 3-year: $925.93 - 751.31 = 174.62 \approx 175$.
