An investor purchases two bonds. The bonds have the same annual effective yield rate $i$, with $i > 0$.

With respect to the annual effective yield rate, their modified durations are $a$ years and $b$ years, with $0 < a < b$.

One of these two bonds has a Macaulay duration of $c$ years, with $a < c < b$.

Determine which of the following is an expression, in years, for the Macaulay duration of the other bond.

- A) $bc/a$
- B) $ac/b$
- C) $ab/c$
- D) $b + c - a$
- E) $a + c - b$

## Explanation

Modified duration $= $ Macaulay duration $/ (1+i)$, so Macaulay duration $= $ modified duration $\times (1+i)$.

Bond 1 has modified duration $a$, so its Macaulay duration is $a(1+i)$.
Bond 2 has modified duration $b$, so its Macaulay duration is $b(1+i)$.

Since $a < c < b$ and Macaulay $=$ modified $\times (1+i) > $ modified (as $i > 0$), the bond with Macaulay duration $c$ must be the bond with modified duration $a$:
$$c = a(1+i) \implies 1+i = c/a$$

The Macaulay duration of the other bond is:
$$b(1+i) = b(c/a) = bc/a$$
