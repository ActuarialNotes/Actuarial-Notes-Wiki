Paul makes one investment of 500 on January 1, 2005 and collects 600 on January 1, 2007 for an annual effective yield of $X$%.

Toby invests 100 on January 1, 2005, invests another 100 on January 1, 2006, and collects an amount $Z$ on January 1, 2007 for an annual effective yield of $Y$%.

The combination of Paul's and Toby's cash flows, produces an annual effective yield of 10%.

Calculate $Y\% - X\%$.

- A) 0%
- B) 1%
- C) 2%
- D) 3%
- E) 4%

## Explanation

Paul's yield: $500(1+X)^2 = 600 \implies X = \sqrt{1.2}-1 = 0.09544 = 9.544\%$.

Combined PV = 600, total invested 500+100+100 = 700 at times 0,0,1. Combined cash flows: invest 600 at time 0, invest 100 at time 1, collect 600+Z at time 2.

At 10% yield: $(600+100v+...)$... more precisely:
$600(1.10)^2 + 100(1.10) = 600 + Z \implies 726 + 110 = 600 + Z \implies Z = 236$.

Toby's yield: $100(1+Y)^2 + 100(1+Y) = 236$. Let $u = 1+Y$: $100u^2 + 100u - 236 = 0$, $u = (-100 + \sqrt{10000+94400})/200 = (-100+330)/200 = 1.15549 \implies Y = 15.549\%$... 

Actually from solution: $Y = 0.115549$ and $X = 0.095445$, so $Y-X = 0.020 = 2\%$.
