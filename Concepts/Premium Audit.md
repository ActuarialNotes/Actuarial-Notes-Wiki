**Premium audit** is the process of verifying and adjusting the final premium on certain commercial policies — primarily workers compensation, general liability, and commercial auto — where the initial premium is based on estimated exposure (payroll, sales, units) that is measured precisely at policy expiration.

- At inception, the insured pays a **deposit premium** based on estimated exposures; at audit, actual exposures are measured and the final premium is recalculated, producing either an **additional premium** (if actual > estimated) or a **return premium** (if actual < estimated)
- Premium audit is important for ratemaking because written premium at inception does not equal the ultimate earned premium until audits are resolved; audit adjustments must be incorporated to ensure on-level premium is accurate
- Lines subject to audit tend to have **exposure trend** components beyond pure price changes — a growing business naturally generates higher payroll and sales, increasing premium even without rate changes

> [!example]- Workers Comp Audit Adjustment {Example}
> A workers comp policy is written with an estimated payroll of $\$2{,}000{,}000$. The rate is $\$3.50$ per $\$100$ of payroll. At audit, actual payroll was $\$2{,}400{,}000$.
>
> > [!answer]-
> > Deposit premium $= (2{,}000{,}000 / 100) \times 3.50 = \$70{,}000$
> >
> > Audited premium $= (2{,}400{,}000 / 100) \times 3.50 = \$84{,}000$
> >
> > Additional premium due $= 84{,}000 - 70{,}000 = \$14{,}000$
