At an annual effective interest rate of 5%, a 10-year annuity-immediate starting with an annual payment of 100 increases each year by 15% of the previous year's payment and has a present value of $X$.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 597
- B) 772
- C) 1040
- D) 1247
- E) 1484

## Explanation

Geometrically increasing annuity-immediate with first payment 100, growth rate 15%, and interest rate 5%:

$$X = 100\cdot\frac{1-(1.15/1.05)^{10}}{0.05-0.15} = 100\cdot\frac{1-(1.09524)^{10}}{-0.10}$$

$$= 100\cdot\frac{1-2.4841}{-0.10} = 100\cdot\frac{-1.4841}{-0.10} = 100 \times 14.841 = 1484.1$$
