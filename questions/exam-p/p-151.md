The following information is given about a group of high-risk borrowers.

1. Of all these borrowers, 30% defaulted on at least one student loan.
2. Of the borrowers who defaulted on at least one car loan, 40% defaulted on at least one student loan.
3. Of the borrowers who did not default on any student loans, 28% defaulted on at least one car loan.

A statistician randomly selects a borrower from this group and observes that the selected borrower defaulted on at least one student loan.

Calculate the probability that the selected borrower defaulted on at least one car loan.

- A) 0.33
- B) 0.40
- C) 0.44
- D) 0.65
- E) 0.72

## Explanation

Let $S$ = event of defaulting on at least one student loan, and $C$ = event of defaulting on at least one car loan.

The given information translates to:
$$P(S) = 0.30, \quad P(S \mid C) = 0.40, \quad P(C \mid S^c) = 0.28$$

**Step 1: Find $P(C \cap S^c)$.**

$$
\begin{align*}
P(C \cap S^c) &= P(C \mid S^c) \cdot P(S^c) \\
              &= 0.28 \times 0.70 \\
              &= 0.196
\end{align*}
$$

**Step 2: Find $P(C)$ using the Law of Total Probability.**

$$P(C) = P(C \cap S) + P(C \cap S^c)$$

Since $P(S \mid C) = 0.40$, we have $P(S \cap C) = 0.40 \cdot P(C)$. Substituting:

$$P(C) = 0.40 \cdot P(C) + 0.196$$
$$0.60 \cdot P(C) = 0.196$$
$$P(C) = \frac{0.196}{0.60} = \frac{49}{150}$$

**Step 3: Find $P(C \mid S)$ using Bayes' Theorem.**

$$P(S \cap C) = 0.40 \times \frac{49}{150} = \frac{49}{375}$$

$$
\begin{align*}
P(C \mid S) &= \frac{P(S \cap C)}{P(S)} \\
            &= \frac{49/375}{0.30} \\
            &= \frac{49/375}{9/30} \\
            &= \frac{49}{375} \times \frac{30}{9} \\
            &= \frac{49 \times 30}{375 \times 9} \\
            &= \frac{1470}{3375} \\
            &\approx 0.436
\end{align*}
$$

Rounding to two decimal places, $P(C \mid S) \approx \mathbf{0.44}$.
