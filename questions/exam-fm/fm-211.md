A worker is starting an endowment fund for his charity. The fund will accumulate at a nominal annual interest rate of 12% convertible monthly. Beginning today, the worker will deposit 500 monthly for ten years. No deposits or withdrawals will be made for the subsequent ten years. Exactly twenty years from today, monthly payments of $X$ will be made to his charity and continue forever.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 3270
- B) 3572
- C) 3758
- D) 3796
- E) 3834

## Explanation

Monthly rate $j = 1\%$. Value of deposits at end of year 10: $500\ddot{s}_{\overline{120}|0.01} = 500(230.0387) = 115,019$.

Growing for another 10 years at 1%/month: $115,019(1.01)^{120} = 115,019(3.30039) = 379,608$.

This equals the PV of a monthly perpetuity-due at time 20: $X/d = X(1.01)/0.01 = 101X$.

$$X = 379,608/101 = 3,758... $$

Wait, the solution says $500\ddot{s}_{\overline{120}|0.01}(1.01)^{120} = X/d$:
$$500(230.04)(1.01)^{120} = \frac{X(1.01)}{0.01}$$
$$383,404.42 = 101X \implies X = 3,796.08$$
