A bond with a face value of 1000 and a redemption value of 1080 has an annual coupon rate of 8% payable semiannually. The bond is bought to yield an annual nominal rate of 10% convertible semiannually.

At this yield rate, the present value of the redemption value is 601 on the purchase date.

Calculate the purchase price of the bond.

- A) 911
- B) 923
- C) 956
- D) 974
- E) 984

## Explanation

The semiannual yield is 5%, so $601 = 1080v^n$ where $v = 1/1.05$, giving $v^n = 601/1080$.

Then $a_{\overline{n}|0.05} = \dfrac{1-v^n}{0.05} = \dfrac{1-601/1080}{0.05} = \dfrac{479/1080}{0.05} = 8.87037$.

Purchase price: $40a_{\overline{n}|0.05} + 1080v^n = 40(8.87037) + 601 = 354.81 + 601 = 955.81 \approx 956$.
