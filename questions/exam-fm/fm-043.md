Mary purchased a 10-year par value bond with an annual nominal coupon rate of 4% payable semiannually at a price of 1021.50. The bond can be called at 100 over the par value of 1100 on any coupon date starting at the end of year 5 and ending six months prior to maturity.

Calculate the minimum yield that Mary could receive, expressed as an annual nominal rate of interest convertible semiannually.

- A) 4.7%
- B) 4.9%
- C) 5.1%
- D) 5.3%
- E) 5.5%

## Explanation

Since the price exceeds the amount paid for an early call (1021.50 < 1200), the minimum yield is from a call at the latest possible early-call date (end of year 9.5, period 19).

**Early call (19 periods):** $1021.50 = 22a_{\overline{19}|j} + 1200v_j^{19} \implies j = 2.86\%$

**Redemption at maturity (20 periods):** $1021.50 = 22a_{\overline{20}|j} + 1100v_j^{20} \implies j = 2.46\%$

The minimum yield corresponds to the **smaller** $j$ value: $j = 2.46\%$ per period.

Annual nominal rate $= 2 \times 2.46\% = 4.92\%$.
