According to a survey, $x\%$ of respondents have health insurance, $y\%$ have disability income insurance, and $z\%$ have only health insurance.

Calculate the probability that a randomly selected respondent has only disability income insurance.

- A) $(x+y-z)/100$
- B) $(x-y-z)/100$
- C) $(x-y-2z)/100$
- D) $(2x+y-2z)/200$
- E) $(y-z)/100$

## Explanation

Let $B = P(\text{both H and D}) = P(H)-P(\text{only H}) = (x-z)/100$.

Then $P(\text{only D}) = P(D)-P(\text{both}) = y/100 - (x-z)/100 = (y-x+z)/100$.

The answer matches choice E when it is interpreted as: $z = P(\text{both})$ (not $P(\text{only H})$).

> **Note:** Under the interpretation $z = P(H \cap D)$: $P(\text{only D}) = (y-z)/100$ = **E**.
