The probability that a homeowners policyholder reports a property claim in a year increases by 25% per year. Conversely, the probability that a homeowners policyholder reports a liability claim in a year decreases by 25% per year. The probability that a homeowners policyholder reports both a property claim and a liability claim in Year 1 is 0.01. Property and liability claims are independent.

Calculate the probability that a homeowners policyholder reports both a property claim and a liability claim in Year 9.

- A) 0.005
- B) 0.006
- C) 0.010
- D) 0.014
- E) 0.015

## Explanation

Let $P_1$ and $L_1$ be the Year 1 probabilities. $P_1 L_1 = 0.01$.

In Year 9: $P_9 = P_1 \cdot 1.25^8$ and $L_9 = L_1 \cdot 0.75^8$.

$$
\begin{align*}
P_9 L_9 &= P_1 L_1 \cdot (1.25 \times 0.75)^8 \\
        &= 0.01 \cdot (0.9375)^8
\end{align*}
$$

$(0.9375)^2 = 0.8789$, $(0.8789)^2 = 0.7725$, $(0.7725)^2 \approx 0.5968$

$$P_9 L_9 \approx 0.01 \times 0.597 \approx \mathbf{0.006}$$
