Given the following as of December 31, 2018:

| Accident Year | Per Occurrence Retention | Stop-Loss Limit |
|---------------|--------------------------|-----------------|
| 2015          | \$1,000,000              | \*\*            |
| 2016          | \$1,000,000              | \*\*            |
| 2017          | \$1,500,000              | \$5,000,000     |
| 2018          | \$2,000,000              | \$7,000,000     |

\*\*2015 and 2016 have a combined stop-loss limit of \$10,000,000 that applies to all claims occurring in both years.

**Summary of Claims Under Per Occurrence Retention:**

| Accident Year | Reported Claims | Percent Reported |
|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| 2015          | 2,000,000       | 70%             |
| 2016          | 1,500,000       | 35%             |
| 2017          | 800,000         | 20%             |
| 2018          | 450,000         | 10%             |

**Large Claims (not included in the claims table above):**

| Claim | Accident Year | Reported Claim |
|-------|---------------|----------------|
| A     | 2015          | 1,200,000      |
| B     | 2015          | 1,500,000      |
| C     | 2016          | 3,000,000      |
| D     | 2017          | 1,750,000      |

- There will be no further development on large claims.

## Part a (1.5 points)

Calculate the estimated retained IBNR for all accident years using the reported development technique.

### Answer
10250000

### Explanation

**Step 1: Develop Claims Under Per Occurrence Retention to Ultimate**

Using the reported development technique: Ultimate = Reported / % Reported

| AY   | Ultimate (excl. large claims)           |
|------|-----------------------------------------|
| 2015 | $2{,}000{,}000 / 0.70 = 2{,}857{,}143$ |
| 2016 | $1{,}500{,}000 / 0.35 = 4{,}285{,}714$ |
| 2017 | $800{,}000 / 0.20 = 4{,}000{,}000$     |
| 2018 | $450{,}000 / 0.10 = 4{,}500{,}000$     |

**Step 2: Determine Large Claims Retained Under Per Occurrence Limits**

Large claims will not develop further, so the ultimate large claim retained is capped at the per occurrence retention for each accident year:

| Claim | AY   | Gross Claim | Per Occ Retention | Retained Amount |
|-------|------|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| A     | 2015 | 1,200,000   | 1,000,000         | 1,000,000       |
| B     | 2015 | 1,500,000   | 1,000,000         | 1,000,000       |
| C     | 2016 | 3,000,000   | 1,000,000         | 1,000,000       |
| D     | 2017 | 1,750,000   | 1,500,000         | 1,500,000       |

Total retained large claims by accident year:
- AY 2015: $1{,}000{,}000 + 1{,}000{,}000 = 2{,}000{,}000$
- AY 2016: $1{,}000{,}000$
- AY 2017: $1{,}500{,}000$
- AY 2018: $0$

**Step 3: Compute Total Ultimate Claims Gross of Stop-Loss**

$$\text{Total Ultimate (gross of SL)} = \text{Ultimate small/mid claims} + \text{Retained large claims}$$

| AY   | Ultimate excl. large | Retained large | Total gross of SL |
|------|---------------------|----------------|-------------------|
| 2015 | 2,857,143           | 2,000,000      | 4,857,143         |
| 2016 | 4,285,714           | 1,000,000      | 5,285,714         |
| 2017 | 4,000,000           | 1,500,000      | 5,500,000         |
| 2018 | 4,500,000           | 0              | 4,500,000         |

**Step 4: Apply Stop-Loss Limits**

- **AY 2015 and 2016 combined**: gross = $4{,}857{,}143 + 5{,}285{,}714 = 10{,}142{,}857 > 10{,}000{,}000$ → net = $10{,}000{,}000$
- **AY 2017**: gross = $5{,}500{,}000 > 5{,}000{,}000$ → net = $5{,}000{,}000$
- **AY 2018**: gross = $4{,}500{,}000 < 7{,}000{,}000$ → net = $4{,}500{,}000$

$$\text{Total Net Ultimate} = 10{,}000{,}000 + 5{,}000{,}000 + 4{,}500{,}000 = 19{,}500{,}000$$

**Step 5: Calculate IBNR**

$$\text{IBNR} = \text{Total Net Ultimate} - \text{Total Reported}$$

Total currently reported (retained):
$$2{,}000{,}000 + 1{,}500{,}000 + 800{,}000 + 450{,}000 + 2{,}000{,}000 + 1{,}000{,}000 + 1{,}500{,}000 = 9{,}250{,}000$$

(The large claims are already fully reported at their retained amounts since no further development is expected.)

$$\text{IBNR} = 19{,}500{,}000 - 9{,}250{,}000 = 10{,}250{,}000$$

### Examiner Report
Candidates were expected to calculate IBNR considering both per occurrence and stop-loss limits. They were also expected to apply the appropriate per occurrence limits to the large claims, develop the claims under the per occurrence retention to ultimate, apply the stop-loss limits appropriately, and finally, calculate IBNR.

Common mistakes included:
- Not limiting the large claims to the per occurrence limits.
- Not assigning the large claims to the appropriate accident years.
- Applying development factors to the large claims, even though the question states the large claims will have no further development.
- Subtracting only the reported claims under the per occurrence retention from ultimate when calculating IBNR (i.e., forgetting to also subtract the currently reported large claims).
