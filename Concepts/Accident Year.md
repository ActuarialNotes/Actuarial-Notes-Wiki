**Accident Year** (AY) is a data organization method that groups losses by the calendar year in which the loss event occurred, regardless of when the policy was written or when the claim was reported.

- AY is the industry standard for ratemaking because it matches losses to the exposure period — earned premium during AY $n$ funds losses occurring during AY $n$
- AY develops faster than [[Policy Year]] (no policy-term lag) and slower than report year, making it practical for most ratemaking analyses
- A loss is assigned to the accident year of its occurrence date, not its report date or payment date
- IBNR reserves are required because some losses occurring in a given AY have not yet been reported at early evaluations

> [!example]- Accident Year Assignment {Example}
> A policy is effective $10/1/2023$. The insured has a loss on $11/15/2024$, which is reported on $12/1/2024$ and paid on $3/1/2025$.
>
> > [!answer]-
> > The loss date is $11/15/2024$, so this claim belongs to **Accident Year 2024** — regardless of the $2023$ policy effective date or the $2025$ payment date.
