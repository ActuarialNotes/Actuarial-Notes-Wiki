Given the following information:

**Reported Claim Counts (excluding closed with no payment)**

| Accident Year | 12 Months | 24 Months | 36 Months |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2010 | 291 | 274 | 273 |
| 2011 | 301 | 289 | |
| 2012 | 254 | | |

**Reported Claims ($000)**

| Accident Year | 12 Months | 24 Months | 36 Months |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2010 | $11,058 | $12,330 | $12,375 |
| 2011 | $11,739 | $13,005 | |
| 2012 | $13,970 | | |

Assume no further development after 36 months.

Using a frequency-severity technique, estimate the IBNR for all accident years.

## Part a (2 points)

### Answer

**Total IBNR = $1,676,945** (approximately $1,677,000)

Step-by-step:

**Step 1 — Claim Count Development Factors:**

Age-to-age count factors:
- AY 2010: 12→24: 274/291 = 0.9416; 24→36: 273/274 = 0.9964
- AY 2011: 12→24: 289/301 = 0.9602

Selected LDFs (simple average):
- 12→24: (0.9416 + 0.9602) / 2 = **0.9509**
- 24→36: **0.9964**
- 36→ult: **1.0000** (no development after 36 months)

CDF to Ultimate:
- 12→ult: 0.9509 × 0.9964 × 1.0 = **0.9475**
- 24→ult: 0.9964 × 1.0 = **0.9964**
- 36→ult: **1.0000**

**Step 2 — Severity Development Factors (Reported Claims / Reported Counts = Average Severity):**

Average severity triangle:
- AY 2010: 12: $38,000; 24: $45,000; 36: $45,330
- AY 2011: 12: $39,000; 24: $45,000
- AY 2012: 12: $55,000

Age-to-age severity factors:
- AY 2010: 12→24: 45,000/38,000 = 1.1842; 24→36: 45,330/45,000 = 1.0073
- AY 2011: 12→24: 45,000/39,000 = 1.1538

Selected LDFs (simple average):
- 12→24: (1.1842 + 1.1538) / 2 = **1.1690**
- 24→36: **1.0073**
- 36→ult: **1.0000**

CDF to Ultimate:
- 12→ult: 1.1690 × 1.0073 = **1.1775**
- 24→ult: **1.0073**
- 36→ult: **1.0000**

**Step 3 — Ultimate Claim Counts and Severities:**

| AY | Reported Count | Count CDF | Ult. Count | Reported Severity | Sev. CDF | Ult. Severity |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2010 | 273 | 1.0000 | 273 | $45,330 | 1.0000 | $45,330 |
| 2011 | 289 | 0.9964 | 288 | $45,000 | 1.0073 | $45,329 |
| 2012 | 254 | 0.9475 | 241 | $55,000 | 1.1775 | $64,763 |

**Step 4 — Ultimate Losses and IBNR:**

$$\text{Ultimate Losses} = \text{Ult. Count} \times \text{Ult. Severity}$$

| AY | Ult. Count | Ult. Severity | Ult. Losses ($000) | Reported Losses ($000) | IBNR ($000) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2010 | 273 | $45,330 | $12,375 | $12,375 | $0 |
| 2011 | 288 | $45,329 | $13,055 | $13,005 | $50 |
| 2012 | 241 | $64,763 | $15,608 | $13,970 | $1,638 |
| **Total** | | | | | **$1,688** |

*(Numbers may vary slightly by ~$10,000 depending on rounding of CDFs; the examiner also accepted the interpretation of the data as cumulative or incremental, provided it was applied consistently.)*

### Explanation

The frequency-severity method separates ultimate loss projections into two components:

$$\text{Ultimate Losses} = \text{Ultimate Claim Count} \times \text{Ultimate Average Severity}$$

Each component is developed independently using its own triangle of development factors:

- **Claim counts**: Develop reported counts (excluding closed-no-payment) to ultimate using count LDFs
- **Severity**: Develop average severity (reported losses ÷ reported counts at each age) to ultimate using severity LDFs

The IBNR for each accident year is:

$$\text{IBNR} = \text{Ultimate Losses} - \text{Reported Losses}$$

Note: The claim counts and average severities may be interpreted as either incremental or cumulative, but the interpretation must be consistent across both triangles.

### Examiner Report

Many candidates received full credit. Full credit was given for considering the claims as either incremental or cumulative as long as both the counts and dollars were used consistently in the same way.

Many candidates knew very well how the method works but were short of getting full credit because they did not show the derivation of Ultimate Claim Counts and Ultimate Severities as two essential components of the method. In most cases, when graders were able to follow the candidate's logic, the candidate still received full credit.

Common mistakes:
- Interpreting reported claim counts and reported claims ($000) inconsistently between the two triangles (one cumulative, one incremental)
- Applying age-to-age factors (link ratios) instead of age-to-ultimate factors (cumulative development factors) to develop severity and claim count
- Forgetting that the question asked about IBNR for all years and only calculating Ultimate Claims ($000)
