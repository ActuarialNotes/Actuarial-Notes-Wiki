To accumulate 8000 at the end of $3n$ years, deposits of 98 are made at the end of each of the first $n$ years and 196 at the end of each of the next $2n$ years.

The annual effective rate of interest is $i$. You are given $(1 + i)^n = 2.0$.

Calculate $i$.

- A) 11.25%
- B) 11.75%
- C) 12.25%
- D) 12.75%
- E) 13.25%

## Explanation

The 196 annual deposits in years $n+1$ through $3n$ can be split as $98 + 98$, giving two streams of 98 each. The equation of value at the end of $3n$ years is:

$$98s_{\overline{3n}|} + 98s_{\overline{2n}|} = 8000$$

$$\frac{(1+i)^{3n}-1}{i} + \frac{(1+i)^{2n}-1}{i} = 81.63$$

Substituting $(1+i)^n = 2 \implies (1+i)^{3n} = 8$ and $(1+i)^{2n} = 4$:

$$\frac{8-1}{i} + \frac{4-1}{i} = 81.63$$

$$\frac{10}{i} = 81.63$$

$$i = 12.25\%$$
