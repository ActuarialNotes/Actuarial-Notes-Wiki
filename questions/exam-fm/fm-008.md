A perpetuity-immediate pays 100 per year. Immediately after the fifth payment, the perpetuity is exchanged for a 25-year annuity-immediate that will pay $X$ at the end of the first year. Each subsequent annual payment will be 8% greater than the preceding payment.

The annual effective rate of interest is 8%.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 54
- B) 64
- C) 74
- D) 84
- E) 94

## Explanation

The value of the perpetuity immediately after the 5th payment is:

$$\frac{100}{0.08} = 1250$$

Setting up the equation for the 25-year increasing annuity (with growth rate equal to the interest rate):

$$1250 = X\left(v + 1.08v^2 + \cdots + 1.08^{24}v^{25}\right)$$

Since $1.08v = 1$, each term equals $v$:

$$= X(v + v + \cdots + v) = X \cdot \frac{25}{1.08}$$

$$X = 50(1.08) = 54$$
