Two borrowers obtain loans at the same time. Each loan is for the same amount and is repaid with level end-of-month payments.

The first borrower is charged a monthly effective interest rate of $i$ and makes payments of $P$ for $k$ months to pay off the loan, where $k$ is a positive integer.

The second borrower is charged a monthly effective interest rate of $j$ and makes payments of 120 for $5k$ months to pay off the loan.

You are given that $0 < j < 0.04$ and that $i = (1+j)^5 - 1$.

Determine which statement about $P$ is true.

- A) $400 < P \le 450$
- B) $450 < P \le 500$
- C) $500 < P \le 550$
- D) $550 < P \le 600$
- E) $600 < P \le 650$

## Explanation

Since $i = (1+j)^5 - 1$, one period at rate $i$ = five periods at rate $j$. Both loans are for the same amount $L$:

$$L = Pa_{\overline{k}|i} = 120a_{\overline{5k}|j}$$

$$P = 120\cdot\frac{a_{\overline{5k}|j}}{a_{\overline{k}|i}} = 120\cdot\frac{a_{\overline{5k}|j}}{a_{\overline{k}|i}}$$

Since $a_{\overline{5k}|j}/a_{\overline{k}|i}$: each period of $a_{\overline{k}|i}$ corresponds to 5 periods of $a_{\overline{5k}|j}$, so:

$$P = \frac{120a_{\overline{5k}|j}}{a_{\overline{k}|i}} = 120\cdot\frac{i}{j} = 120\cdot\frac{(1+j)^5-1}{j}$$

For $0 < j < 0.04$: $5 < \frac{(1+j)^5-1}{j} < 5.41633$, giving $600 < P \le 650$.
