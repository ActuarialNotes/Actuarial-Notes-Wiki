A Poisson process has the rate function $\lambda(t) = 1.5t$.

$T_i$ is the time of the $i^{\text{th}}$ event.

Calculate the probability that $T_2 > 3$.

- A) Less than 1%
- B) At least 1%, but less than 4%
- C) At least 4%, but less than 7%
- D) At least 7%, but less than 10%
- E) At least 10%

## Explanation

This is a **non-homogeneous Poisson process**. The second event occurs after time 3 exactly when fewer than 2 events have occurred by time 3:

$$\Pr[T_2 > 3] = \Pr[N(3) \le 1] = \Pr[N(3)=0] + \Pr[N(3)=1].$$

The mean number of events by time 3 is

$$m(3) = \int_0^3 1.5t\, dt = 1.5 \cdot \frac{9}{2} = 6.75.$$

So $N(3) \sim \text{Poisson}(6.75)$:

$$\Pr[N(3)=0] = e^{-6.75} = 0.00117, \qquad \Pr[N(3)=1] = 6.75\,e^{-6.75} = 0.00791.$$

$$\Pr[T_2 > 3] = 0.00117 + 0.00791 = 0.00908 \approx 0.91\%.$$

This is less than 1%, so the answer is **A**.
