Matt purchased a 20-year par value bond with an annual nominal coupon rate of 8% payable semiannually at a price of 1722.25. The bond can be called at par value $X$ on any coupon date starting at the end of year 15 after the coupon is paid. The lowest yield rate that Matt can possibly receive is a nominal annual interest rate of 6% convertible semiannually.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 1400
- B) 1420
- C) 1440
- D) 1460
- E) 1480

## Explanation

Since the coupon rate (4% per period) exceeds the yield rate (3% per period), the bond sells at a premium. For a premium bond, the worst case for the holder is the earliest call date (call terminates the premium stream early). Therefore, use the call at end of year 15 (30 periods) to determine the guarantee:

$$1722.25 = 0.04X\,a_{\overline{30}|0.03} + Xv_{0.03}^{30} = 1.196X$$

$$X = \frac{1722.25}{1.196} = 1440$$
