In a certain game of chance, a square board with area 1 is colored with sectors of either red or blue. A player, who cannot see the board, must specify a point on the board by giving an x-coordinate and a y-coordinate. The player wins the game if the specified point is in a blue sector.

The game can be arranged with any number of red sectors, and the red sectors are designed so that $R_i = \left(\frac{9}{20}\right)^i$, where $R_i$ is the area of the $i^{\text{th}}$ red sector.

Calculate the minimum number of red sectors that makes the chance of a player winning less than 20%.

- A) 3
- B) 4
- C) 5
- D) 6
- E) 7

## Explanation

The area of the board is 1. The probability of winning is the area of the blue sectors, which is 1 minus the total area of the red sectors. Let $N$ be the number of red sectors.

$$P(\text{Win}) = 1 - \sum_{i=1}^N \left(\frac{9}{20}\right)^i$$
Using the sum of a finite geometric series:
$$\sum_{i=1}^N \left(\frac{9}{20}\right)^i = \frac{\frac{9}{20} \left(1 - \left(\frac{9}{20}\right)^N\right)}{1 - \frac{9}{20}} = \frac{\frac{9}{20}}{\frac{11}{20}} \left(1 - \left(\frac{9}{20}\right)^N\right) = \frac{9}{11} \left(1 - \left(\frac{9}{20}\right)^N\right)$$

The probability of winning is:
$$P(\text{Win}) = 1 - \frac{9}{11} + \frac{9}{11}\left(\frac{9}{20}\right)^N = \frac{2}{11} + \frac{9}{11}\left(\frac{9}{20}\right)^N$$

We want this probability to be less than 0.2 (or $1/5$):
$$\frac{2}{11} + \frac{9}{11}(0.45)^N < 0.20$$
$$\frac{9}{11}(0.45)^N < \frac{0.2}{11} \implies 9(0.45)^N < 0.2 \implies (0.45)^N < \frac{0.2}{9} \approx 0.02222$$

Solving for $N$:
$$N \ln(0.45) < \ln(0.02222) \implies N > \frac{\ln(0.02222)}{\ln(0.45)} \approx 4.767$$

Since $N$ must be an integer, the minimum number is $N = 5$.
