An insurer models the number of claims per year from a large portfolio as a Poisson random variable with mean 10.

Let $K$ be the largest integer such that the probability of receiving $K$ or fewer claims is less than 0.95.

Calculate $K$.

- A) 10
- B) 12
- C) 13
- D) 14
- E) 15

## Explanation

For $N \sim \text{Poisson}(10)$, we need the largest $K$ with $P(N \leq K) < 0.95$.

Computing Poisson(10) CDF values:

$$P(N \leq 14) \approx 0.917 < 0.95$$

$$P(N \leq 15) \approx 0.951 \geq 0.95$$

Therefore $K = \mathbf{14}$.
