**In-Force** refers to insurance policies or exposure units that are actively providing coverage at a specific point in time, representing a snapshot of the current book of business rather than a flow over a period.

- In-force exposure is distinct from written exposure (policies issued during a period) and earned exposure (coverage provided during a period); in-force is a point-in-time stock measure
- Used for capacity planning, catastrophe accumulation management, and analyzing the current concentration of risk by geography, line, or class
- An in-force policy count at a given date equals all policies whose effective date precedes that date and whose expiration date follows it
- In-force premium (annualized) is often used to measure book size and market share; it does not correspond directly to any accounting period's written or earned premium

> [!example]- Identifying In-Force Policies {Example}
> On June 30, 2024, an insurer reviews its auto book. Policy A: effective 1/1/2024–12/31/2024. Policy B: effective 3/1/2024–2/28/2025. Policy C: effective 7/1/2024–6/30/2025. Which policies are in-force on June 30, 2024?
>
> > [!answer]-
> > Policy A is in-force (6/30 is between 1/1/2024 and 12/31/2024). Policy B is in-force (6/30 is between 3/1/2024 and 2/28/2025). Policy C is not in-force on 6/30/2024 because its effective date of 7/1/2024 has not yet been reached. Only Policies A and B are counted in the June 30 in-force snapshot.
