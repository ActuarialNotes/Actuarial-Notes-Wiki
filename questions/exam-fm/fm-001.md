Bruce deposits 100 into a bank account. His account is credited interest at an annual nominal rate of interest of 4% convertible semiannually.

At the same time, Peter deposits 100 into a separate account. Peter's account is credited interest at an annual force of interest of $\delta$.

After 7.25 years, the value of each account is the same.

Calculate $\delta$.

- A) 0.0388
- B) 0.0392
- C) 0.0396
- D) 0.0404
- E) 0.0414

## Explanation

Given the same principal invested for the same period yields the same accumulated value, the two measures of interest $i^{(2)} = 0.04$ and $\delta$ must be equivalent. Over a one-year period:

$$\left(1 + \frac{i^{(2)}}{2}\right)^2 = e^{\delta}$$

Thus,

$$e^{\delta} = \left(1 + \frac{i^{(2)}}{2}\right)^2 = 1.02^2 = 1.0404$$

$$\delta = \ln(1.0404) = 0.0396$$
