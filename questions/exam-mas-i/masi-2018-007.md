You are given the following information about an insurer:

- The amount of each loss is an exponential random variable with mean 2000.
- Currently, there is no deductible and the insurance company pays for the full amount of each loss.
- The insurance company wishes to introduce a deductible amount, $d$, to reduce the probability of having to pay anything out on a claim by 75%. The insurance company only pays the amount per loss exceeding the deductible.
- The insurance company assumes the underlying loss distribution is unchanged after the introduction of the deductible.

Calculate the minimum amount of deductible, $d$, that will meet the requirement of having 75% fewer claims excess of deductible.

- A) Less than 2,000
- B) At least 2,000, but less than 2,300
- C) At least 2,300, but less than 2,600
- D) At least 2,600, but less than 2,900
- E) At least 2,900

## Explanation

With no deductible the company pays on every loss, so currently the probability of making a payment is $\Pr[X > 0] = 1$. After introducing deductible $d$, the company pays only when the loss exceeds $d$, which has probability

$$\Pr[X > d] = e^{-d/2000}.$$

Reducing the payout probability by 75% means it must drop to $25\%$ of its original value:

$$e^{-d/2000} = 0.25.$$

Solving:

$$\frac{d}{2000} = -\ln(0.25) = \ln 4 = 1.3863 \implies d = 2000 \times 1.3863 \approx 2{,}773.$$

This falls in $[2{,}600, 2{,}900)$, so the answer is **D**.
