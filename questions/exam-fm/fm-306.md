You are given the following information regarding Company J.

  i) It has a single liability of 1.75 million to be paid 12 years from now.
  ii) Its asset portfolio consists of a zero-coupon bond maturing in 5 years for 242,180 and a zero-coupon bond maturing in 14 years for $X$.
  iii) At an annual effective interest rate of 7%, Company J's position is fully immunized.

Calculate the present value of the assets less the present value of the liabilities if the annual effective interest rate immediately changes to 4%.

- A) 5,910
- B) 8,871
- C) 11,029
- D) 14,746
- E) 17,462

## Explanation

At 7%, finding bond face values:

$$\begin{align*}
PV_L &= 1{,}750{,}000(1.07)^{-12} = 777{,}021 \\
PV_{\text{5-yr bond}} &= 242{,}180/1.07^5 = 172{,}671 \\
PV_{\text{14-yr bond}} &= 777{,}021 - 172{,}671 = 604{,}350 \\
\text{Face of 14-yr bond} &= 604{,}350(1.07)^{14} = 1{,}558{,}337
\end{align*}$$

At 4%, the surplus is:

$$PV_A - PV_L = \left(\frac{242{,}180}{1.04^5} + \frac{1{,}558{,}337}{1.04^{14}}\right) - \frac{1{,}750{,}000}{1.04^{12}} = 5{,}910$$
