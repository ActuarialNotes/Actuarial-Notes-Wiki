You are given the following information from a model constructed to predict the probability that a Homeowners policy will be retained into the next policy term:

| | |
|---|---|
| Response variable | retention |
| Response distribution | binomial |
| Link | probit |
| Pseudo $R^2$ | 0.6521 |

| Parameter | df | $\hat\beta$ |
|-----------|----|----|
| Intercept | 1 | 0.4270 |
| Tenure: < 5 years | 0 | 0.0000 |
| Tenure: $\ge$ 5 years | 1 | 0.1320 |
| Prior Rate Change: < 0% | 1 | 0.0160 |
| Prior Rate Change: [0%, 10%] | 0 | 0.0000 |
| Prior Rate Change: > 10% | 1 | -0.0920 |
| Amount of Insurance (000's) | 1 | 0.0015 |

Let $\hat\pi$ be the modeled probability that a policy with 4 years of tenure who experienced a +12% prior rate change and has 225,000 in amount of insurance will be retained into the next policy term.

Calculate $\hat\pi$.

- A) Less than 0.60
- B) At least 0.60, but less than 0.70
- C) At least 0.70, but less than 0.80
- D) At least 0.80, but less than 0.90
- E) At least 0.90

## Explanation

With a probit link, $g(\pi) = \Phi^{-1}(\pi) = \eta$, where $\eta$ is the linear predictor. Select the relevant coefficients:

- Tenure 4 years $\Rightarrow$ "< 5 years" $\Rightarrow 0.0000$
- Prior rate change +12% $\Rightarrow$ "> 10%" $\Rightarrow -0.0920$
- Amount of insurance 225,000 $\Rightarrow 225 \times 0.0015 = 0.3375$

$$\eta = 0.4270 + 0.0000 - 0.0920 + 0.3375 = 0.6725.$$

Invert the probit link with the standard normal CDF:

$$\hat\pi = \Phi(0.6725) \approx 0.749.$$

This falls in $[0.70, 0.80)$, so the answer is **C**.
