You are given the following table of test scores for four pairs of brothers and sisters:

| Pair | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 |
|------|---|---|---|---|
| Brother | 89 | 94 | 78 | 80 |
| Sister | 86 | 95 | 71 | 77 |

- All test scores are normally distributed with a common variance
- Test scores between a brother and his sister are not independent
- You want to test the hypothesis:
  - $H_0$: Mean test scores of brother and sister are equal
  - $H_1$: Mean test scores of brother and sister are not equal

Calculate the p-value of this test.

- A) Less than 0.01
- B) At least 0.01, but less than 0.02
- C) At least 0.02, but less than 0.05
- D) At least 0.05, but less than 0.10
- E) At least 0.10

## Explanation

Because the scores within a pair are dependent, use a **paired $t$-test** on the differences $d = \text{Brother} - \text{Sister}$:

$$d = \{3, -1, 7, 3\}, \quad \bar{d} = 3, \quad s_d^2 = \frac{0^2 + (-4)^2 + 4^2 + 0^2}{3} = 10.667, \quad s_d = 3.266.$$

The test statistic (with $n - 1 = 3$ degrees of freedom) is

$$t = \frac{\bar{d}}{s_d/\sqrt{n}} = \frac{3}{3.266/2} = 1.837.$$

For $t_3$, the value $1.837$ lies between $t_{0.10,3} = 1.638$ and $t_{0.05,3} = 2.353$, so the one-tailed probability is between $0.05$ and $0.10$; the two-tailed p-value is roughly $0.16$.

The p-value is at least $0.10$, so the answer is **E**.
