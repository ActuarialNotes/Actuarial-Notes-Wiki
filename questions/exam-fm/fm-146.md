A 20-year bond priced to have an annual effective yield of 10% has a Macaulay duration of 11. Immediately after the bond is priced, the market yield rate increases by 0.25%. The bond's approximate percentage price change, using a first-order Macaulay approximation, is $X$.

Calculate $X$.

- A) −2.22%
- B) −2.47%
- C) −2.50%
- D) −2.62%
- E) −2.75%

## Explanation

The first-order Macaulay approximation for a yield change $\Delta i$:

$$\frac{\Delta P}{P} \approx -\frac{D_{MAC}}{1+i} \cdot \Delta i = -\text{ModD} \cdot \Delta i$$

Wait — the Macaulay approximation uses:
$$P(i + \Delta i) \approx P(i)\left(\frac{1+i}{1+i+\Delta i}\right)^{D_{MAC}}$$

For small $\Delta i$:
$$\frac{\Delta P}{P} \approx -D_{MAC}\cdot\frac{\Delta i}{1+i} = -11\cdot\frac{0.0025}{1.10} = -0.025 \implies P(0.1025) \approx P(0.10)\left(\frac{1.10}{1.1025}\right)^{11} = 0.97534P$$

Percentage change: $100(0.97534 - 1) = -2.47\%$
