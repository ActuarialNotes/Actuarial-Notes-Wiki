An actuary models the weight of a randomly selected adult using a normal distribution with mean $\mu$ and standard deviation $\sigma$. It is given that $P[X < \mu - \sigma] = 0.16$ and that $P[X > \mu + \sigma] = 0.16$.

Calculate $P[\mu - \sigma < X < \mu + \sigma]$.

- A) 0.32
- B) 0.50
- C) 0.60
- D) 0.68
- E) 0.84

## Explanation

By the symmetry of the normal distribution, the probability within one standard deviation of the mean is:

$$P[\mu-\sigma < X < \mu+\sigma] = 1 - P[X < \mu-\sigma] - P[X > \mu+\sigma]$$

$$= 1-0.16-0.16 = \mathbf{0.68}$$

This is consistent with the empirical rule that approximately 68% of normally distributed data lies within one standard deviation of the mean.
