Matthew deposits 100 into Fund X, which earns a nominal annual rate of discount of 12% convertible quarterly.

At the same time, Sarah deposits 200 into Fund Y, which earns a constant annual force of interest of 0.08.

After $n$ years, the balance of Fund X equals the balance of Fund Y.

Calculate $n$.

- A) 14.5
- B) 16.6
- C) 17.6
- D) 18.1
- E) 18.6

## Explanation

Fund X: $100\left(1-\dfrac{0.12}{4}\right)^{-4n} = 100(0.97)^{-4n}$

Fund Y: $200e^{0.08n}$

Setting equal and solving for $n$:

$$\begin{align*}
100(0.97)^{-4n} &= 200e^{0.08n} \\
(-4n)\ln(0.97) &= \ln 2 + 0.08n \\
4n(0.030459) &= 0.6931 + 0.08n \\
0.12184n - 0.08n &= 0.6931 \\
0.04184n &= 0.6931 \\
n &= 0.6931/0.04184 = 16.57
\end{align*}$$
