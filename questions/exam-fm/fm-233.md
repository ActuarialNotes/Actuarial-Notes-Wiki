An investor's new savings account earns an annual effective interest rate of 3% for each of the first ten years and an annual effective interest rate of 2% for each year thereafter.

The investor deposits an amount $X$ at the beginning of each year, starting with year 1, so that the account balance just after the deposit in the beginning of year 26 is 100,000.

Determine which of the following is an equation of value that can be used to solve for $X$.

- A) $\dfrac{100{,}000}{(1.03)^{10}(1.02)^{15}}=X\displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{11}\frac{1}{(1.03)^{k-1}}+X\sum_{k=12}^{26}\frac{1}{(1.02)^{k-1}}$
- B) $\dfrac{100{,}000}{(1.03)^{10}(1.02)^{16}}=X\displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{10}\frac{1}{(1.03)^k}+X\sum_{k=1}^{26}\frac{1}{(1.02)^k}$
- C) $\dfrac{100{,}000}{(1.03)^{10}(1.02)^{16}}=X\displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{11}\frac{1}{(1.03)^{k-1}}+X\sum_{k=12}^{26}\frac{1}{(1.03)^{10}(1.02)^{k-11}}$
- D) $\dfrac{100{,}000}{(1.03)^{10}(1.02)^{15}}=X\displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{11}\frac{1}{(1.03)^{k-1}}+X\sum_{k=12}^{26}\frac{1}{(1.03)^{10}(1.02)^{k-11}}$
- E) $\dfrac{100{,}000}{(1.03)^{10}(1.02)^{16}}=X\displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{10}\frac{1}{(1.03)^k}+X\sum_{k=11}^{26}\frac{1}{(1.03)^{10}(1.02)^{k-10}}$

## Explanation

Deposits are at times $t = 0, 1, \ldots, 25$ (beginning of years 1–26). The target balance is at $t = 25$ (just after the 26th deposit).

The 3% rate applies for years 1–10 ($t = 0$ to $t = 9$), so 10 periods. The 2% rate applies from year 11 onward ($t = 10+$).

To discount to time 0 (or equivalently compute PV), a deposit at time $k-1$ (for $k=1,\ldots,11$) is discounted at 3% only: factor $1/(1.03)^{k-1}$. A deposit at time $k-1$ for $k=12,\ldots,26$ passes through 10 years of 3% and then additional years of 2%: factor $1/[(1.03)^{10}(1.02)^{k-11}]$.

The target $100{,}000$ at time 25 is discounted to time 0 as $100{,}000/[(1.03)^{10}(1.02)^{15}]$.

This matches answer **(D)**.
