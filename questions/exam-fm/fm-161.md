A small business takes out a 10-year loan with level end-of-quarter payments. The payments are based on an annual nominal interest rate of 12% convertible quarterly.

The amount of principal repaid in the 15th payment is 10,030.27.

Calculate the amount of interest paid in the 25th payment.

- A) 7,521
- B) 8,151
- C) 9,467
- D) 10,030
- E) 11,601

## Explanation

Quarterly rate $j = 3\%$. Principal repaid at payment $k$ is $Rv^{n-k+1}$ where $R$ is the quarterly payment and $n = 40$ total periods.

At payment 15 ($k=15$): $10,030.27 = Rv^{40-15+1} = Rv^{26} = R(1.03)^{-26}$.

$R = 10,030.27(1.03)^{26} = 10,030.27 \times 2.15659 = 21,631.19$.

Interest at payment 25 = $R(1 - v^{40-25+1}) = 21,631.19(1-(1.03)^{-16}) = 21,631.19(0.37675) = 8,151.35$.
