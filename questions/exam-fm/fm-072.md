An investor purchases a 10-year callable bond with face amount of 1000 for price $P$. The bond has an annual nominal coupon rate of 10% paid semi-annually.

The bond may be called at par by the issuer on every other coupon payment date, beginning with the second coupon payment date.

The investor earns at least an annual nominal yield of 12% compounded semi-annually regardless of when the bond is redeemed.

Calculate the largest possible value of $P$.

- A) 885
- B) 892
- C) 926
- D) 965
- E) 982

## Explanation

Coupon = 50 per period, yield = 6% per period, redemption = 1000. Coupon rate (5%) < yield rate (6%), so bond is at a discount. For discount bonds, the worst case is the latest redemption date (maturity).

Possible call dates are periods 2, 4, 6, …, 20. The maximum price guaranteeing at least 6% yield is the minimum price over all call dates. For a discount bond, price decreases as the term increases, so the minimum is at maturity (20 periods):

$$P = 50a_{\overline{20}|0.06} + 1000(1.06)^{-20} = 50(11.4699) + 1000(0.31180) = 573.50 + 311.80 = 885.30$$
