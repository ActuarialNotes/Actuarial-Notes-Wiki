You are given the following information:

- The amount of time one spends in an IRS office is exponentially distributed with a mean of 30 minutes.
- $P_1$ is the probability that John will spend more than an hour of total time in the IRS office, given that he has already been in the office for 20 minutes.
- $P_2$ is the probability that Lucy will spend more than an hour of total time in the IRS office, given that she has just arrived.

Calculate the difference $(P_1 - P_2)$.

- A) Less than 0.00
- B) At least 0.00, but less than 0.05
- C) At least 0.05, but less than 0.10
- D) At least 0.10, but less than 0.15
- E) At least 0.15

## Explanation

Let $T$ be the time spent in the office, $T \sim \text{Exponential}$ with mean $30$, so the survival function is

$$\Pr[T > t] = e^{-t/30}.$$

**Computing $P_1$.** The exponential distribution is *memoryless*: given that John has already been in the office for $20$ minutes, the additional time he will spend has the same exponential distribution. Spending more than an hour total means spending more than $60 - 20 = 40$ additional minutes:

$$P_1 = \Pr[T > 60 \mid T > 20] = \Pr[T > 40] = e^{-40/30} = e^{-4/3} \approx 0.2636.$$

**Computing $P_2$.** Lucy has just arrived, so we need the unconditional probability of exceeding $60$ minutes:

$$P_2 = \Pr[T > 60] = e^{-60/30} = e^{-2} \approx 0.1353.$$

**Difference.**

$$P_1 - P_2 \approx 0.2636 - 0.1353 = 0.1283.$$

This falls in the interval $[0.10, 0.15)$, so the answer is **D**.
