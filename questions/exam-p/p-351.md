A benefit payment to an insured is modeled by the random variable $B$. Given a loss $X$ that is exponentially distributed with mean 5, the benefit is:
$$B = \begin{cases} 0 & X < 2 \\ X - 2 & 2 \leq X < 5 \\ 3 & X \geq 5 \end{cases}$$
Calculate $E[B]$.

- A) 1.22
- B) 1.50
- C) 1.85
- D) 2.00
- E) 2.22

## Explanation

$B = (X-2)_+ - (X-5)_+$. For $X \sim \text{Exp}(\text{mean }5)$, the memoryless property gives $E[(X-d)_+] = 5e^{-d/5}$.

$$E[B] = E[(X-2)_+] - E[(X-5)_+]$$

$$= 5e^{-2/5} - 5e^{-5/5} = 5e^{-0.4} - 5e^{-1}$$

$$\approx 5(0.6703) - 5(0.3679) = 3.351 - 1.839 \approx \mathbf{1.50}$$
