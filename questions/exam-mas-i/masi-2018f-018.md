An exponential distribution is fitted to the loss size data below using the method of moments.

| Interval | Number of Observations |
|----------|------------------------|
| (0, 100] | 5,000 |
| (100, 500] | 3,000 |
| (500, 1000] | 1,500 |
| (1000, 5000] | 500 |

Losses are assumed to follow a uniform distribution within each interval.

Calculate the fitted probability of a loss exceeding 500.

- A) Less than 0.15
- B) At least 0.15, but less than 0.25
- C) At least 0.25, but less than 0.35
- D) At least 0.35, but less than 0.45
- E) At least 0.45

## Explanation

The method of moments sets the fitted mean equal to the sample mean. With losses uniform within each interval, use the interval **midpoints** (50, 300, 750, 3000):

$$\bar{x} = \frac{5000(50) + 3000(300) + 1500(750) + 500(3000)}{10{,}000} = \frac{3{,}775{,}000}{10{,}000} = 377.5.$$

So $\hat\theta = 377.5$. For an exponential distribution,

$$\Pr[X > 500] = e^{-500/377.5} = e^{-1.3245} = 0.266.$$

This falls in $[0.25, 0.35)$, so the answer is **C**.
