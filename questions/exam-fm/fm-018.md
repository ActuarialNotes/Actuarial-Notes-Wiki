You have decided to invest in Bond X, an $n$-year bond with semi-annual coupons and the following characteristics:

(i) Par value is 1000.

(ii) The ratio of the semi-annual coupon rate, $r$, to the desired semi-annual yield rate, $i$, is 1.03125.

(iii) The present value of the redemption value is 381.50.

Given $(1 + i)^{-n} = 0.5889$, calculate the price of Bond X.

- A) 1019
- B) 1029
- C) 1050
- D) 1055
- E) 1072

## Explanation

Let $C$ be the redemption value and $v = 1/(1+i)$. The price is:

$$X = 1000r\,a_{\overline{2n}|} + Cv^{2n}$$

$$= 1000r\cdot\frac{1-v^{2n}}{i} + 381.50$$

$$= 1000 \cdot \frac{r}{i} \cdot (1 - v^{2n}) + 381.50$$

$$= 1000(1.03125)(1 - 0.5889^2) + 381.50$$

$$= 1055.11$$
