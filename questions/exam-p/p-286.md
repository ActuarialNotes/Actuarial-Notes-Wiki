Losses follow an exponential distribution with mean 1. Two independent losses are observed.

Calculate the probability that either of the losses is more than twice the other.

- A) $1/6$\n- B) $1/4$\n- C) $1/3$\n- D) $1/2$\n- E) $2/3$

## Explanation

By symmetry, $P(X_1 > 2X_2 \text{ or } X_2 > 2X_1) = 2P(X_1 > 2X_2)$.

$$P(X_1 > 2X_2) = \int_0^\infty P(X_1 > 2x)\,e^{-x}\,dx$$

$$= \int_0^\infty e^{-2x} \cdot e^{-x}\,dx = \int_0^\infty e^{-3x}\,dx = \frac{1}{3}$$

$$P(\text{one} > \text{twice other}) = 2 \times \frac{1}{3} = \mathbf{\frac{2}{3}}$$
