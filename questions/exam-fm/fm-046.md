The annual force of interest credited to a savings account is defined by

$$\delta_t = \frac{t^2/100}{3 + t^3/150}$$

with $t$ in years. Austin deposits 500 into this account at time 0.

Calculate the time in years it will take for the fund to be worth 2000.

- A) 6.7
- B) 8.8
- C) 14.2
- D) 16.5
- E) 18.9

## Explanation

Find $t$ such that $500\,a(t) = 2000$, i.e., $a(t) = 4$.

$$a(t) = \exp\!\left(\int_0^t \frac{r^2/100}{3+r^3/150}\,dr\right)$$

Note that $\frac{r^2/100}{3+r^3/150} = \frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{r^2/50}{3+r^3/150}$, and the numerator is the derivative of the denominator divided by 50. Integrating:

$$a(t) = \exp\!\left[\frac{1}{2}\ln\!\left(3 + \frac{t^3}{150}\right) - \frac{1}{2}\ln 3\right] = \left(1 + \frac{t^3}{450}\right)^{1/2}$$

Setting $a(t) = 4$:

$$16 = 1 + \frac{t^3}{450} \implies t^3 = 6750 \implies t = 18.90$$
