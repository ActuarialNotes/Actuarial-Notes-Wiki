A business manufactures light bulbs and sells them in boxes of 50. Let $p$ denote the probability that a light bulb is defective. Let $X$ denote the number of non-defective light bulbs in a box of 50. In addition, let $n$ be an integer such that $P[X \geq n] \geq 0.95$.

Determine which one of the following statements must be true.

- A) $\displaystyle\sum_{k=n}^{50}\binom{50}{k}(1-p)^k p^{50-k} \leq 0.95$
- B) $\displaystyle\sum_{k=0}^{n}\binom{50}{k}(1-p)^k p^{50-k} \geq 0.95$
- C) $\displaystyle\sum_{k=n}^{50}\binom{50}{k}(1-p)^k p^{50-k} \geq 0.95$
- D) $\displaystyle\sum_{k=0}^{n}\binom{50}{k}(1-p)^k p^{50-k} \leq 0.05$
- E) $\displaystyle\sum_{k=n}^{50}\binom{50}{k}p^k (1-p)^{50-k} \geq 0.95$

## Explanation

$P[X \geq n] \geq 0.95$ is equivalent to $P[X \leq n-1] \leq 0.05$, i.e.,

$$\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\binom{50}{k}(1-p)^k p^{50-k} \leq 0.05$$

Since we need $P[X \geq n] \geq 0.95$, this means $P[X \leq n] \leq 1 - 0.95 + P[X=n] $... but choice D represents $P[X \leq n] \leq 0.05$, which ensures $P[X \geq n+1] \geq 0.95$.

The statement that **must** be true is: $P[X < n] = P[X \leq n-1] \leq 0.05$, which corresponds to choice D.
