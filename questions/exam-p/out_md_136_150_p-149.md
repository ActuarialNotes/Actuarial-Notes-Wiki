In a shipment of 20 packages, 7 packages are damaged. The packages are randomly inspected, one at a time, without replacement, until the fourth damaged package is discovered.

Calculate the probability that exactly 12 packages are inspected.

- A) 0.079
- B) 0.119
- C) 0.237
- D) 0.243
- E) 0.358

## Explanation

We want the probability that the $12^{\text{th}}$ package inspected is the $4^{\text{th}}$ damaged package.
This happens if and only if exactly 3 damaged packages are found in the first 11 inspections, AND the $12^{\text{th}}$ package inspected is damaged.

1. Probability of exactly 3 damaged packages in the first 11 inspections (Hypergeometric):
$$P(3 \text{ damaged in } 11) = \frac{\binom{7}{3}\binom{13}{8}}{\binom{20}{11}} = \frac{35 \times 1287}{167960} \approx 0.268159$$

2. Given that 3 damaged and 8 undamaged packages were found in the first 11 inspections, there are $20 - 11 = 9$ packages remaining. Of these 9 remaining packages, $7 - 3 = 4$ are damaged.
The probability that the next ($12^{\text{th}}$) package is damaged is $4/9$.

Multiplying these probabilities:
$$P(\text{exactly } 12 \text{ inspections}) = 0.268159 \times \frac{4}{9} \approx 0.11918 \approx 0.119$$
