**Stakeholder reporting** is the communication of reserve estimates and their drivers to different audiences — management, investors, regulators, rating agencies, and auditors — each with distinct information needs, technical depth requirements, and regulatory obligations.

- **Management and board**: focus on reserve adequacy relative to prior period, drivers of change, and range of outcomes; typically presented as dollar amounts with narrative explanation of reserve development
- **Regulators**: require statutory filings (Schedule P in the US) showing reserve triangles, actuarial opinions on adequacy, and compliance with ASOP 43 standards
- **Investors and rating agencies**: focus on reserve volatility, redundancy/deficiency trends, and impact on reported earnings; adverse reserve development must be disclosed in financial statements
- ASOP 43 governs the actuary's communication obligations: scope, methods, assumptions, uncertainty, and any significant limitations must be disclosed

> [!example]- Communicating Adverse Reserve Development {Example}
> Year-end reserves were \$$50{,}000{,}000$. At the next evaluation, \$$5{,}000{,}000$ in adverse development has emerged. How should this be communicated to management vs. regulators?
>
> > [!answer]-
> > **Management**: Present the $5M adverse development by accident year and cause (e.g., higher-than-expected severity in AY 2022 GL claims), compare to prior projections, and recommend whether the current reserve selection should be strengthened.
> >
> > **Regulators**: File an amended actuarial opinion if required, disclose the development in Schedule P, and document the change in the Statement of Actuarial Opinion per ASOP 43 — including the basis for the revised estimate and any remaining uncertainty.
