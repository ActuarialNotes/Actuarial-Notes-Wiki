A perpetuity-due with annual payments consists of ten level payments of $X$ followed by a series of increasing payments. Beginning with the eleventh payment, each payment is 1.5% larger than the preceding payment.

Using an annual effective interest rate of 5%, the present value of the perpetuity is 45,000.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 1,679
- B) 1,696
- C) 1,737
- D) 1,763
- E) 1,781

## Explanation

The present value of the first ten payments is $X\ddot{a}_{\overline{10}|0.05} = 8.10782X$.

The present value of the geometrically increasing perpetuity starting at payment 11 is (using the first payment at time 10 of $X \cdot 1.015^0 = X$, growing at 1.5% per period):
$$v^{10} \cdot \frac{X}{(1-1.015v)} \cdot (1+i) = v^{10} \cdot \frac{X \cdot 1.05}{0.05-0.015} = X(1.05)^{-10}\cdot\frac{1.05}{0.035} = 18.69366X$$

So $45,000 = 8.10782X + 18.69366X = 26.80148X$, giving $X = 1,679$.
