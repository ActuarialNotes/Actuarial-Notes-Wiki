Given the following information:

**Paid Claims Gross of Salvage & Subrogation ($000s)**

| Accident Year | 12 Months | 24 Months | 36 Months | 48 Months |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2009 | $2,000 | $2,400 | $2,500 | $2,500 |
| 2010 | $2,100 | $2,300 | $2,400 | |
| 2011 | $2,100 | $2,400 | | |
| 2012 | $2,500 | | | |

**Paid Salvage & Subrogation ($000s)**

| Accident Year | 12 Months | 24 Months | 36 Months | 48 Months |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2009 | $98 | $166 | $250 | $250 |
| 2010 | $105 | $163 | $240 | |
| 2011 | $107 | $170 | | |
| 2012 | $75 | | | |

- Assume no development after age 48.
- Ultimate claims for accident year 2012 = $2,985 (thousands).

## Part a (0.75 points)

Using a development approach, estimate the ultimate salvage and subrogation for accident year 2012.

### Answer

**$179.8 thousand**

### Explanation

**Step 1: Calculate age-to-age factors for paid S&S**

$$f_{12\text{-}24} = \frac{166 + 163 + 170}{98 + 105 + 107} = \frac{499}{310} = 1.6097$$

$$f_{24\text{-}36} = \frac{250 + 240}{166 + 163} = \frac{490}{329} = 1.4894$$

$$f_{36\text{-}48} = \frac{250}{250} = 1.0000$$

No development after age 48: $f_{48\text{-}ULT} = 1.0000$

**Step 2: Select factors (all-year weighted averages)**

| Age-to-Age | Selected |
|---|---|
| 12-24 | 1.6097 |
| 24-36 | 1.4894 |
| 36-48 | 1.0000 |
| 48-ULT | 1.0000 |

**Step 3: Calculate CDF to ultimate for AY 2012 (currently at 12 months)**

$$\text{CDF}_{12\text{-}ULT} = 1.6097 \times 1.4894 \times 1.0000 \times 1.0000 = 2.3981$$

**Step 4: Project AY 2012 S&S to ultimate**

$$\text{Ult S\&S}_{2012} = 75 \times 2.3981 = \$179.8 \text{ thousand}$$

### Examiner Report

Most candidates received full credit. In limited cases, there were mathematical errors or candidates performed the development but omitted the final calculation of the ultimate paid S&S amount.

## Part b (1.5 points)

Using a ratio approach, estimate the ultimate salvage and subrogation for accident year 2012.

### Answer

**$298.5 thousand**

### Explanation

**Step 1: Compute the ratio of paid S&S to paid claims gross of S&S at each age**

| AY | 12 Months | 24 Months | 36 Months | 48 Months |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2009 | 98/2,000 = 0.049 | 166/2,400 = 0.069 | 250/2,500 = 0.100 | 250/2,500 = 0.100 |
| 2010 | 105/2,100 = 0.050 | 163/2,300 = 0.071 | 240/2,400 = 0.100 | |
| 2011 | 107/2,100 = 0.051 | 170/2,400 = 0.071 | | |
| 2012 | 75/2,500 = 0.030 | | | |

**Step 2: Select age-to-age factors for the ratio triangle**

$$f_{12\text{-}24}^{\text{ratio}} = \frac{0.069 + 0.071 + 0.071}{0.049 + 0.050 + 0.051} = \frac{0.211}{0.150} = 1.4067 \approx 1.407$$

$$f_{24\text{-}36}^{\text{ratio}} = \frac{0.100 + 0.100}{0.069 + 0.071} = \frac{0.200}{0.140} = 1.4286 \approx 1.429$$

$$f_{36\text{-}48}^{\text{ratio}} = \frac{0.100}{0.100} = 1.000$$

No development after age 48: $f_{48\text{-}ULT}^{\text{ratio}} = 1.000$

**Step 3: Select the ultimate ratio**

The ultimate ratio for mature accident years (AY 2009 and AY 2010) is 0.100 at ages 36 and 48. Select **ultimate ratio = 0.100**.

Verify AY 2011 (currently at 24 months): $0.071 \times 1.429 = 0.101 \approx 0.10$ ✓

Verify AY 2012 (currently at 12 months): $0.030 \times 1.407 \times 1.429 = 0.060$

However, the mature AY experience consistently produces an ultimate ratio of 0.10. Because AY 2012 is at the earliest maturity and the ratio approach relies on the historical ultimate ratio, **select the ultimate ratio = 0.100** (consistent with the mature AY pattern).

**Step 4: Apply ultimate ratio to AY 2012 ultimate claims**

$$\text{Ult S\&S}_{2012} = 2{,}985 \times 0.100 = \$298.5 \text{ thousand}$$

### Examiner Report

Most candidates received high partial credit. Very few candidates selected an ultimate ratio for accident year 2012 that appropriately considered the ultimate ratios from prior accident years (i.e., 0.10 from AY 2009 and AY 2010). Many candidates developed the ratio from AY 2012's 12-month ratio and arrived at 0.06, without noting that prior mature year evidence supported 0.10 as the stable ultimate ratio. The ratio approach is most appropriately applied using the stable ultimate ratio observed from mature years rather than projecting from the earliest available data.

## Part c (0.25 points)

Briefly discuss which approach, the development or ratio approach, to select in recommending an ultimate salvage and subrogation estimate for accident year 2012.

### Answer

Select the **ratio approach**.

### Explanation

The ratio approach is preferred because it provides more stability and is less subject to leveraging at early maturities. When accident year 2012 is only at 12 months of development, the paid S&S development factors are large (CDF of ~2.40), making the development approach highly sensitive to small changes in the early data. A single atypical recovery could materially distort the estimate.

The ratio approach anchors the ultimate estimate to the stable historical ultimate S&S-to-claims ratio (0.10) observed across multiple mature accident years, and then applies that ratio to the independently estimated ultimate claims for AY 2012. This produces a more stable and credible estimate at this early stage of development.

### Examiner Report

Many candidates received full credit. Some common mistakes were not selecting a method (stating "it doesn't matter" without justification) or failing to provide a valid reason grounded in the mechanics of the two approaches. A correct answer required identifying the ratio approach and explaining that it is less leveraged (more stable) at early maturities.
