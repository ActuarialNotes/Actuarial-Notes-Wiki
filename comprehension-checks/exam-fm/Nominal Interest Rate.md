Two accounts each quote a 6% nominal annual rate, but one compounds monthly and the other annually. Which earns more over a year?

- A) The monthly-compounding account, because interest is credited more often
- B) The annual-compounding account, because it applies the full 6% at once
- C) They earn the same, since both quote 6%
- D) Neither earns interest until the year ends

<!-- rationale: 1: assumes less frequent compounding earns more · 2: treats the nominal rate as the actual earned rate · 3: misunderstands crediting -->
