An investor deposits 150 today and receives 100 one year from now and 80 two years from now.

Calculate the annual nominal rate of interest convertible quarterly that the investor earns.

- A) 12.8%
- B) 13.0%
- C) 13.2%
- D) 13.4%
- E) 13.6%

## Explanation

$$150 = 100v + 80v^2$$

$$80v^2 + 100v - 150 = 0$$

$$v = \frac{-100 + \sqrt{10000 + 4(80)(150)}}{160} = \frac{-100 + \sqrt{58000}}{160} = \frac{-100 + 240.83}{160} = 0.88019$$

$$i = 1/0.88019 - 1 = 0.13611$$

Converting to nominal quarterly:

$$\begin{align*}
\left(1+\frac{i^{(4)}}{4}\right)^4 &= 1.13611 \\
\frac{i^{(4)}}{4} &= 1.13611^{0.25}-1 = 0.03249 \\
i^{(4)} &= 0.12997 \approx 13.0\%
\end{align*}$$
