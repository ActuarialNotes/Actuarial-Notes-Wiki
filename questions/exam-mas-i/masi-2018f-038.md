You are given a series of plots of a single data set containing two variables. Each plot overlays a solid line (labeled PC 1) and a dashed line (labeled PC 2) on the same scatter of points, which shows a positive linear trend:

- Plot I: PC 1 runs along the positive-sloping cloud (direction of greatest spread); PC 2 is roughly perpendicular to it.
- Plot II: PC 1 is a steep line across the cloud and PC 2 runs along the positive trend (the two directions are swapped).
- Plot III: PC 1 runs along the trend, but PC 2 is drawn vertical (not perpendicular to PC 1).
- Plot IV: PC 1 is horizontal and PC 2 is vertical (neither aligned with the trend).
- Plot V: PC 1 and PC 2 are drawn as curves rather than straight lines.

Determine which of above plots accurately represent the $1^{\text{st}}$ and $2^{\text{nd}}$ principal components (PC1 and PC2, respectively) of this dataset.

- A) I
- B) II
- C) III
- D) IV
- E) V

## Explanation

The principal components must satisfy two properties: **PC1 points in the direction of maximum variance** (the elongation of the scatter), and **PC2 is orthogonal to PC1** (the next direction of variance). They are straight lines (linear combinations of the variables).

- Plot I satisfies both: PC1 lies along the positive-sloping cloud and PC2 is perpendicular to it. ✓
- Plot II has PC1 and PC2 reversed (PC1 is not along the maximum-variance direction).
- Plots III and IV have a PC2 that is not orthogonal to the sloped PC1.
- Plot V draws the components as curves, but principal components are linear.

Only Plot I is correct, so the answer is **A**.
