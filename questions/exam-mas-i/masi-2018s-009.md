You are given the following system: a bridge structure in which the left terminal connects to components 1 (upper) and 2 (lower); component 1 leads to the upper middle node and component 2 to the lower middle node; component 3 is the bridge connecting the upper and lower middle nodes; component 5 connects the upper middle node to the right terminal and component 4 connects the lower middle node to the right terminal.

and the following statements:

- I. $\{1,5\}$ and $\{2,4\}$ are minimal path sets.
- II. $\{1,2\}$ and $\{4,5\}$ are minimal cut sets.
- III. $\{1,3,5\}$ and $\{2,3,4\}$ are both minimal path sets and minimal cut sets.

Determine which of the above statements are correct.

- A) None are correct
- B) I and II only
- C) I and III only
- D) II and III only
- E) The answer is not given by (A), (B), (C), or (D)

## Explanation

This is the classic five-component **bridge network**. Its minimal path sets (smallest sets of working components that connect left to right) and minimal cut sets (smallest sets of failed components that disconnect left from right) are:

$$\text{Minimal path sets: } \{1,5\},\ \{2,4\},\ \{1,3,4\},\ \{2,3,5\}.$$
$$\text{Minimal cut sets: } \{1,2\},\ \{4,5\},\ \{1,3,4\},\ \{2,3,5\}.$$

**Statement I:** $\{1,5\}$ and $\{2,4\}$ are indeed minimal path sets — **correct**.

**Statement II:** $\{1,2\}$ (both left-side components) and $\{4,5\}$ (both right-side components) are indeed minimal cut sets — **correct**.

**Statement III:** $\{1,3,5\}$ is not a path set at all (it does not connect left to right without 2 or 4), and it contains the path $\{1,5\}$, so it cannot be a *minimal* path set — **incorrect**.

Only I and II are correct, so the answer is **B**.
