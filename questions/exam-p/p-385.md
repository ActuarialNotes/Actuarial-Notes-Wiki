An annuity pays 1 at the end of each year as long as the annuitant is alive, for a maximum of 3 years. In each year, conditional on the annuitant being alive at the start of the year, the probability of surviving the year is 0.90. The annual discount factor is $v = 0.95$.

Calculate the expected present value of the annuity payments.

- A) 1.90
- B) 2.10
- C) 2.21
- D) 2.30
- E) 2.50

## Explanation

Let $p_k$ = probability of surviving to year $k$:

$p_1 = 0.90$, $p_2 = 0.81$, $p_3 = 0.729$.

$$E[\text{PV}] = v \cdot p_1 + v^2 \cdot p_2 + v^3 \cdot p_3$$

$$= 0.95(0.90)+0.95^2(0.81)+0.95^3(0.729)$$

$$= 0.855+0.731+0.625 = \mathbf{2.211 \approx 2.21}$$
