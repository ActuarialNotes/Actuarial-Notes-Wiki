A 25-year loan is being repaid with annual payments of 1300 at an annual effective rate of interest of 7%. The borrower pays an additional 2600 at the time of the 5th payment and wants to repay the remaining balance over 15 years.

Calculate the revised annual payment.

- A) 1054.58
- B) 1226.65
- C) 1300.00
- D) 1369.38
- E) 1512.12

## Explanation

Outstanding balance just before the 5th payment: $1300 a_{\overline{20}|0.07} = 1300(10.5940) = 13,772.20$.

After the regular 5th payment and the extra 2600: $13,772.20 - 1300 - 2600 = 9,872.20$.

Wait — outstanding balance just after the 4th payment (before 5th): $1300a_{\overline{21}|0.07} = 13,772.20$. After the 5th payment of 1300: balance $= 13,772.20(1.07) - 1300 = 13,437.25$. After extra 2600: $13,437.25 - 2600 = 10,837.25$. Hmm.

Using the prospective method: balance after 4 payments $= 1300a_{\overline{21}|0.07} = 13,772.20$. After the 5th payment + extra: $13,772.20 - 1300(1) - 2600$... Let me recalculate. 

Outstanding after 4 payments: $1300a_{\overline{21}|0.07}$. Then: extra reduces this by 2600 after the 5th payment. Revised payment over 15 years: $11,172.20/a_{\overline{15}|0.07} = 11,172.20/9.1079 = 1,226.65$.
