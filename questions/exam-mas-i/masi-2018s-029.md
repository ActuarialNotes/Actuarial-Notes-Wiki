You are given the following statements relating to deviance of GLMs:

- I. Deviance can be used to assess the quality of fit for nested models.
- II. A small deviance indicates a poor fit for a model.
- III. A saturated model has a deviance of zero.

Determine which of the above statements are true.

- A) None are true
- B) I and II only
- C) I and III only
- D) II and III only
- E) The answer is not given by (A), (B), (C), or (D)

## Explanation

**Statement I** — *True.* Deviance compares a fitted model to the saturated model; differences in deviance between nested models follow an approximate chi-square distribution and are used to assess relative fit.

**Statement II** — *False.* A *small* deviance indicates a *good* fit (the fitted model is close to the saturated model). Large deviance indicates poor fit.

**Statement III** — *True.* The saturated model fits each observation exactly, so its deviance is zero by definition.

I and III are true, so the answer is **C**.
