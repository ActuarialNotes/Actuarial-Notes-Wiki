An insurer offers a travelers insurance policy. Losses under the policy are uniformly distributed on the interval $[0, 5]$. The insurer reimburses a policyholder for a loss up to a maximum of 4.

Determine the cumulative distribution function, $F$, of the benefit that the insurer pays a policyholder who experiences exactly one loss under the policy.

- A) $F(x) = \begin{cases} 0, & x < 0 \\ 0.20x, & 0 \le x < 4 \\ 1, & x \ge 4 \end{cases}$
- B) $F(x) = \begin{cases} 0, & x < 0 \\ 0.20x, & 0 \le x < 5 \\ 1, & x \ge 5 \end{cases}$
- C) $F(x) = \begin{cases} 0, & x < 0 \\ 0.25x, & 0 \le x < 4 \\ 1, & x \ge 4 \end{cases}$
- D) $F(x) = \begin{cases} 0, & x < 0 \\ 0.25x, & 0 \le x < 5 \\ 1, & x \ge 5 \end{cases}$
- E) $F(x) = \begin{cases} 0, & x < 1 \\ 0.25x, & 1 \le x < 5 \\ 1, & x \ge 5 \end{cases}$

## Explanation

Let $Y$ be the loss and $X$ be the reimbursement. $Y \sim \text{Uniform}(0, 5)$.
The reimbursement is $X = Y$ if $Y < 4$, and $X = 4$ if $Y \ge 4$.

For $x < 0$, $F(x) = P(X \le x) = 0$.

For $0 \le x < 4$, $X \le x$ occurs if and only if $Y \le x$. Since $Y$ is uniform on $[0, 5]$:
$$F(x) = P(Y \le x) = \frac{x - 0}{5 - 0} = 0.2x$$

For $x \ge 4$, since the maximum reimbursement is 4, $X$ will always be less than or equal to $x$:
$$F(x) = 1$$

Thus, the CDF is:
$$F(x) = \begin{cases} 0, & x < 0 \\ 0.20x, & 0 \le x < 4 \\ 1, & x \ge 4 \end{cases}$$
