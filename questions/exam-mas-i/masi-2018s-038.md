You are given data and want to perform a piecewise polynomial regression with knots at $X = \{20, 40, 60, 80\}$ (4 knots, dividing the range into 5 regions).

Determine which of the following models will use the most degrees of freedom.

- A) Cubic Spline
- B) Linear Spline
- C) Natural Cubic Spline
- D) Piecewise Cubic Regression
- E) Piecewise Linear Regression

## Explanation

The degrees of freedom depend on the polynomial degree per region and on how many continuity constraints are imposed at the knots. With 4 knots (5 regions):

| Model | Degrees of freedom |
|---|---|
| Piecewise Cubic Regression (no continuity) | $5 \times 4 = 20$ |
| Piecewise Linear Regression (no continuity) | $5 \times 2 = 10$ |
| Cubic Spline (continuous up to 2nd derivative) | $4 + 4 = 8$ |
| Linear Spline | $4 + 2 = 6$ |
| Natural Cubic Spline (extra boundary constraints) | $\approx 4$ |

**Piecewise Cubic Regression** fits a separate, unconstrained cubic (4 parameters) in each of the 5 regions, using $20$ degrees of freedom — the most of all the choices. The answer is **D**.
