Bill and Joe each put 10 into separate accounts at time $t = 0$, where $t$ is measured in years. Bill's account earns interest at a constant annual effective interest rate of $K/25$, $K > 0$.

Joe's account earns interest at a force of interest $\delta_t = \dfrac{1}{K + 0.25t}$.

At the end of four years, the amount in each account is $X$.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 20.7
- B) 21.7
- C) 22.7
- D) 23.7
- E) 24.7

## Explanation

Equating the two accumulated values after 4 years:

$$10\left(1 + \frac{K}{25}\right)^4 = 10\exp\!\left(\int_0^4 \frac{1}{K+0.25t}\,dt\right)$$

Evaluating the integral:

$$
\begin{align*}
\int_0^4 \frac{dt}{K+0.25t} &= 4\ln(K+0.25t)\big|_0^4 \\
                            &= 4\ln\!\frac{K+1}{K}
\end{align*}
$$

Equating the exponents from both sides:

$$4\ln\!\left(1+\frac{K}{25}\right) = 4\ln\!\frac{K+1}{K}$$

$$1+\frac{K}{25} = \frac{K+1}{K}$$

$$
\begin{align*}
1 + 0.04K &= 1 + \frac{1}{K} \\
0.04K^2 &= 1 \\
K &= 5
\end{align*}
$$

Therefore:
$$
\begin{align*}
X &= 10\left(1+\frac{5}{25}\right)^4 \\
  &= 10(1.2)^4 \\
  &= 20.74
\end{align*}
$$
