An insurer pays an annual dividend equal to $\max(X - 200, 0)$, where $X \sim N(300, 100^2)$ represents the insurer's annual profit.

Calculate the expected annual dividend.

- A) 84
- B) 90
- C) 95
- D) 108
- E) 120

## Explanation

Let $D = (X-200)_+$. For $Y = X - 200 \sim N(100, 100^2)$ with $\mu = 100$, $\sigma = 100$, $z = \mu/\sigma = 1$:

$$E[(X-c)_+] = (\mu-c)\Phi\!\left(\frac{\mu-c}{\sigma}\right)+\sigma\,\phi\!\left(\frac{\mu-c}{\sigma}\right)$$

With $\mu-c = 100$ and $\sigma = 100$:

$$E[D] = 100\,\Phi(1)+100\,\phi(1)$$

$$= 100(0.8413)+100(0.2420) = 84.13+24.20 \approx \mathbf{108}$$
