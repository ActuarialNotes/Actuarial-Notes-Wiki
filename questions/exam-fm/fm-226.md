The price of a 36-year zero-coupon bond is 80% of its face value.

A second bond, with the same price, same face value, and same annual effective yield rate, offers annual coupons with the coupon rate equal to $\dfrac{4}{9}$ of the annual effective yield rate.

Calculate the number of years until maturity for the second bond.

- A) 45
- B) 54
- C) 63
- D) 72
- E) 81

## Explanation

For the zero-coupon bond: $0.8 = v^{36}$, so $i = (1/0.8)^{1/36} - 1 = 0.006218$.

For the second bond, coupon rate $r = (4/9)i$. Dividing both sides by face value $F$ and applying the premium-discount formula with $ia_{\overline{n}|} = 1 - v^n$:

$$
\begin{align*}
0.8 &= v^n + \left(\frac{4}{9}i - i\right)a_{\overline{n}|} \\
0.8 &= v^n - \frac{5i}{9}\cdot a_{\overline{n}|} \\
0.8 &= v^n - \frac{5}{9}(1-v^n) \\
0.8 &= \frac{14}{9}v^n - \frac{5}{9}
\end{align*}
$$

Solving for $v^n$:

$$
\begin{align*}
0.8 + \frac{5}{9} &= \frac{14}{9}v^n \\
v^n &= \frac{9 \times 1.3556}{14} \\
    &= 0.8715
\end{align*}
$$

Using a financial calculator with $PV = 0.8$, $I/Y = 0.6218$, $PMT = 0.002763$, $FV = 1$: $n = 72$.
