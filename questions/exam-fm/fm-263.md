An 18-year bond, with a price 61% higher than its face value, offers annual coupons with the coupon rate equal to 2.25 times the annual effective yield rate.

An $n$-year bond, with the same face value, coupon rate, and yield rate, sells for a price that is 45% higher than its face value.

Calculate $n$.

- A) 10
- B) 12
- C) 14
- D) 17
- E) 20

## Explanation

Let face = 1. Price $= 1 + (r-i)a_{\overline{n}|}$ where $r = 2.25i$.

For the 18-year bond: $1.61 = 1 + (2.25i - i)a_{\overline{18}|} = 1 + 1.25i \cdot a_{\overline{18}|}$.

Since $1.25i \cdot a_{\overline{18}|} = 0.61$ and $i \cdot a_{\overline{18}|} = 1-v^{18}$:
$$1.25(1-v^{18}) = 0.61 \implies v^{18} = 1 - 0.488 = 0.512 \implies v = 0.96342$$

For the $n$-year bond: $1.45 = 1 + 1.25(1-v^n) = 1 + 1.25 - 1.25v^n$

$$1.25v^n = 0.80 \implies v^n = 0.64$$

$$n = \ln(0.64)/\ln(0.96342) = \ln(0.64)/\ln(0.96342) = 12$$
