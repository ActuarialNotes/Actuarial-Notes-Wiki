# Lloyd's State-Backed Cyberattack Exclusion Mandate (2022–2023)

## Overview
Lloyd's Market Bulletin Y5381 required all standalone cyber policies written in the market to exclude losses from war and, critically, from state-backed cyberattacks that significantly impair the essential functions of a state — effective for new and renewing business from March 31, 2023.

## Key Details
*   **Systemic Risk Rationale:** Driven by concern that a state-sponsored systemic cyber event could exceed the market's capacity, in the shadow of the NotPetya coverage disputes (e.g., the Merck and Mondelez litigation under property policies).
*   **Attribution Mechanics:** Policies must set out a basis for attributing an attack to a state — the most contested drafting challenge, given attribution is slow, political, and rarely definitive.
*   **LMA Model Clauses:** The LMA's suite of cyber war exclusion clauses became the de facto market standard, with buyers and brokers negotiating carve-backs.

## Multi-Agent Execution Mandates
*   **Underwriting & Product:** All cyber wordings re-papered; non-Lloyd's carriers globally faced pressure to adopt comparable language for reinsurance compatibility.
*   **Reinsurance:** Treaty wordings aligned to avoid coverage mismatch between original policies and cyber treaties.
*   **Claims & Legal:** Attribution evidence standards and government-statement reliance became core claims-investigation issues for major cyber events.
