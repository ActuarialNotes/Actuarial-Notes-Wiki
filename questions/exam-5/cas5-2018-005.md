## Part a (2 points)

Calculate the projected 2015 accident year loss and ALAE to be used in the rate analysis. Justify any trend selections.

### Answer

Approximately $17,300–$17,735 (depending on trend selections; see explanation for sample calculations)

### Explanation

Because an underwriting change took effect on July 1, 2017, materially altering the composition of the book, a **two-step trend** is required. The pre-change period reflects one trend, and the post-change period reflects another.

**Step 1 — Determine the trend projection endpoint**

Rates go into effect January 1, 2019 and are in effect for 2 years, so the rate level covers January 1, 2019 through January 1, 2021. The average written date is January 1, 2020 and the average earned date (for 6-month policies) is April 1, 2020. The loss trend projects to the average loss date, which is **April 1, 2020**.

The average loss date for accident year 2015 (with 6-month policies written uniformly throughout 2015) is **July 1, 2015**.

**Step 2 — Select trends**

Due to the underwriting change on July 1, 2017:
- **Frequency**: Two-step trend is warranted. The long-term trend (16-point, all-points) of approximately **+6–7%** applies from July 1, 2015 to July 1, 2017 (2 years). The short-term trend (4-point, recent) of approximately **–7%** applies from July 1, 2017 to April 1, 2020 (2.75 years).
- **Severity**: The trend appears stable across all periods (~5%), so a **one-step trend of 5%** is selected over the full period from July 1, 2015 to April 1, 2020 (4.75 years).

**Step 3 — Calculate projected losses**

The 2015 accident year loss and ALAE is $15,000 (thousands).

$$\text{Projected Losses} = 15{,}000 \times (1.06)^{2} \times (0.93)^{2.75} \times (1.05)^{4.75}$$

$$= 15{,}000 \times 1.1236 \times 0.8090 \times 1.2593 \approx \$17{,}405 \text{ (thousands)}$$

Alternatively, using a two-step pure premium trend (selecting 12% pre-change and –3% post-change):

$$\text{Projected Losses} = 15{,}000 \times (1.12)^{2} \times (0.97)^{2.75} \approx \$17{,}304 \text{ (thousands)}$$

The exact answer depends on which trends are selected, but candidates must justify a two-step approach reflecting the July 1, 2017 underwriting change.

### Examiner Report

Candidates were expected to perform a two-step trend, choosing appropriate trends (either frequency and severity or pure premium) and calculating appropriate trend periods, in order to calculate projected losses.

Common mistakes include:

- Performing a one-step trend instead of a two-step trend, which does not reflect the underwriting change.
- Choosing inappropriate trends based on the data provided.
- Failing to provide justification for trend selections.
- Incorrect projection date for trending.
- Calculating the trend periods incorrectly.
