Which of the following is an expression for the present value of a perpetuity with annual payments of 1, 2, 3, …, where the first payment will be made at the end of $n$ years, using an annual effective interest rate of $i$?

- A) $\dfrac{\ddot{a}_{\overline{n}|} - nv^n}{i}$
- B) $\dfrac{n - a_{\overline{n}|}}{i}$
- C) $\dfrac{v^n}{d}$
- D) $\dfrac{v^n}{d^2}$
- E) $\dfrac{v^n}{di}$

## Explanation

The payments are $1, 2, 3, \ldots$ at times $n, n+1, n+2, \ldots$ The present value of a standard increasing perpetuity-immediate (payments starting at time 1) is $(Ia)_{\overline{\infty}|} = 1/(id)$. Deferring $n-1$ additional periods:

$$PV = v^{n-1} \cdot \frac{1}{id}$$

Using the identity $vi = d$ (since $d = i/(1+i) = vi$), multiply numerator and denominator by $v$:

$$
\begin{align*}
PV &= \frac{v^{n-1}}{id} \\
&= \frac{v^{n-1} \cdot v}{id \cdot v} \\
&= \frac{v^n}{d \cdot (vi)} \\
&= \frac{v^n}{d \cdot d} \\
&= \frac{v^n}{d^2}
\end{align*}
$$
