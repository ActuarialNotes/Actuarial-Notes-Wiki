The probability that the economy will improve, remain stable, or decline is 0.30, 0.50, and 0.20 respectively. The percentage price changes for Stock X and Stock Y are:

| State | Stock X | Stock Y |
|---|---|---|
| Improve | +18% | +15% |
| Stable | +8% | +7% |
| Decline | −13% | −6% |

Determine which statement about the percentage price changes is true.

- A) Stock X has a larger variance and a larger mean.
- B) Stock X has a larger variance and the means are equal.
- C) Stock X has a larger variance and a smaller mean.
- D) The variances are equal and Stock X has a larger mean.
- E) Both the variances and the means are equal.

## Explanation

$$
\begin{align*}
E[X] &= 0.30(18)+0.50(8)+0.20(-13) \\
     &= 5.4+4.0-2.6 \\
     &= 6.8
\end{align*}
$$

$$
\begin{align*}
E[Y] &= 0.30(15)+0.50(7)+0.20(-6) \\
     &= 4.5+3.5-1.2 \\
     &= 6.8
\end{align*}
$$

**Means are equal.**

$$
\begin{align*}
E[X^2] &= 0.30(324)+0.50(64)+0.20(169) \\
       &= 97.2+32+33.8 \\
       &= 163
\end{align*}
$$

$$\text{Var}(X) = 163-6.8^2 = 116.76$$

$$E[Y^2] = 0.30(225)+0.50(49)+0.20(36) = 99.2$$

$$\text{Var}(Y) = 99.2-6.8^2 = 52.96$$

**Stock X has larger variance, equal means.** Answer: **B**.
