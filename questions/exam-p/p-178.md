In a large population of patients, 20% have early stage cancer, 10% have advanced stage cancer, and the other 70% do not have cancer. Six patients from this population are randomly selected.

Calculate the expected number of selected patients with advanced stage cancer, given that at least one of the selected patients has early stage cancer.

- A) 0.403
- B) 0.500
- C) 0.547
- D) 0.600
- E) 0.625

## Explanation

Each patient independently falls into: early (0.2), advanced (0.1), or neither (0.7). Let $E_i$, $A_i$ be indicators for patient $i$.

$P(\text{at least one early}) = 1 - 0.8^6 = 1 - 0.2621 = 0.7379$

By symmetry, $E[\text{total advanced} \mid \text{at least one early}] = 6 \cdot P(A_1 = 1 \mid \text{at least one early})$.

Since $A_1 = 1$ and $E_1 = 1$ are mutually exclusive (each patient has one status):
$$P(A_1=1,\;\text{at least one early}) = P(A_1=1) \cdot P(\text{at least one early among } i=2,\ldots,6)$$
$$= 0.1 \times (1 - 0.8^5) = 0.1 \times 0.6723 = 0.06723$$

$$P(A_1=1 \mid \text{at least one early}) = \frac{0.06723}{0.7379} = 0.09113$$

$$E[\text{advanced} \mid \text{at least one early}] = 6 \times 0.09113 = \mathbf{0.547}$$
