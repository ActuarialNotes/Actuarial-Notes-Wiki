An insurance company's annual profit is normally distributed with mean 100 and variance 400.

Let $Z$ be normally distributed with mean 0 and variance 1 and let $F$ be the cumulative distribution function of $Z$.

Determine the probability that the company's profit in a year is at most 60, given that the profit in the year is positive.

- A) $1 - F(2)$
- B) $F(2)/F(5)$
- C) $[1 - F(2)]/F(5)$
- D) $[F(0.25) - F(0.1)]/F(0.25)$
- E) $[F(5) - F(2)]/F(5)$

## Explanation

The profit variable $X$ is normal with $\mu = 100$ and $\sigma = \sqrt{400} = 20$.
We want to find $P(X \le 60 \mid X > 0)$:
$$P(X \le 60 \mid X > 0) = \frac{P(0 < X \le 60)}{P(X > 0)}$$

Convert these bounds to standard normal $Z$ values:
For $X = 0$: $z = \frac{0 - 100}{20} = -5$
For $X = 60$: $z = \frac{60 - 100}{20} = -2$

So the probability is:
$$\frac{P(-5 < Z \le -2)}{P(Z > -5)} = \frac{F(-2) - F(-5)}{1 - F(-5)}$$

For the standard normal distribution, due to symmetry, $F(-x) = 1 - F(x)$.
So $F(-2) = 1 - F(2)$ and $F(-5) = 1 - F(5)$. Substituting these:
$$\frac{(1 - F(2)) - (1 - F(5))}{1 - (1 - F(5))} = \frac{F(5) - F(2)}{F(5)}$$
