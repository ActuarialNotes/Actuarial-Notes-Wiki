An urn contains 4 red balls, 6 blue balls, and 7 green balls. Four balls are drawn at random without replacement.

Calculate the probability that at least two of the drawn balls are red, given that the sample contains at least one ball of each color.

- A) 0.18
- B) 0.21
- C) 0.33
- D) 0.43
- E) 0.60

## Explanation

$\binom{17}{4}=2380$. With $R+B+G=4$ and $R,B,G \geq 1$:

Compositions: $(2,1,1)$, $(1,2,1)$, $(1,1,2)$ — the only one with $R \geq 2$ is $(2,1,1)$.

$$P(R\geq2,\, B\geq1,\, G\geq1) = \frac{\binom{4}{2}\binom{6}{1}\binom{7}{1}}{2380} = \frac{6\cdot6\cdot7}{2380} = \frac{252}{2380}$$

$$P(R\geq1,\, B\geq1,\, G\geq1) = \frac{252+420+504}{2380} = \frac{1176}{2380}$$

$$
\begin{align*}
P(R\geq2 \mid \text{each color}) &= \frac{252}{1176} \\
                                 &= \frac{3}{14} \\
                                 &\approx \mathbf{0.214 \approx 0.21}
\end{align*}
$$
