The working lifetime, in years, of a particular model of bread maker is normally distributed with mean 10 and variance 4.

Calculate the 12th percentile of the working lifetime, in years.

- A) 5.30
- B) 7.65
- C) 8.41
- D) 12.35
- E) 14.70

## Explanation

$X \sim N(10, 4)$, so $\sigma = 2$.

The 12th percentile satisfies $P(X \leq x) = 0.12$, i.e., $P\!\left(Z \leq \frac{x-10}{2}\right) = 0.12$.

From standard normal tables: $\Phi^{-1}(0.12) \approx -1.175$.

$$x = 10 + 2(-1.175) = 10 - 2.35 = \mathbf{7.65}$$
