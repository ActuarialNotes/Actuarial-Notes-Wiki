For a given interest rate $i > 0$, the present value of a 20-year continuous annuity of one dollar per year is equal to 1.5 times the present value of a 10-year continuous annuity of one dollar per year.

Calculate the accumulated value of a 7-year continuous annuity of one dollar per year.

- A) 5.36
- B) 5.55
- C) 8.70
- D) 9.01
- E) 9.33

## Explanation

Let $\delta$ be the force of interest. The condition is:
$$\bar{a}_{\overline{20}|} = 1.5\bar{a}_{\overline{10}|} \implies \frac{1-e^{-20\delta}}{\delta} = 1.5\cdot\frac{1-e^{-10\delta}}{\delta}$$

$$1-e^{-20\delta} = 1.5(1-e^{-10\delta})$$

Let $X = e^{-10\delta}$: $1 - X^2 = 1.5(1-X)$, so $(1-X)(1+X) = 1.5(1-X)$, giving $1+X = 1.5$, thus $X = 0.5$ and $e^{-10\delta} = 0.5$, so $\delta = \ln(0.5)/(-10) = 0.069315$.

The accumulated value of a 7-year continuous annuity:
$$\bar{s}_{\overline{7}|} = \frac{e^{7\delta}-1}{\delta} = \frac{e^{7(0.069315)}-1}{0.069315} = \frac{1.6309-1}{0.069315} = 9.01$$
