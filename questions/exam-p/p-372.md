A homeowner's property is insured against hailstorm damage. Hailstorm damages have the following probability distribution:

| Damage | Probability |
|---|---|
| 20,000 (large) | 0.01 |
| 5,000 (medium) | 0.04 |
| 0 (none) | 0.95 |

Calculate the standard deviation of the hailstorm damage.

- A) 670
- B) 1,200
- C) 2,200
- D) 4,200
- E) 5,000

## Explanation

$E[X] = 20000(0.01)+5000(0.04)+0 = 200+200 = 400$

$E[X^2] = 20000^2(0.01)+5000^2(0.04) = 4{,}000{,}000+1{,}000{,}000 = 5{,}000{,}000$

$\text{Var}(X) = 5{,}000{,}000-400^2 = 5{,}000{,}000-160{,}000 = 4{,}840{,}000$

$$\text{SD}(X) = \sqrt{4{,}840{,}000} \approx \mathbf{2200}$$
