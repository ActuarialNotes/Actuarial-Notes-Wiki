An insurer has a liability that is expected to result in the following cash outflows.

| End of year | Cash Outflow |
|---|---|
| 1 | 10 |
| 2 | 12 |
| 3 | 15 |
| 4 | 20 |
| 5 | 30 |

The insurer uses an 8% annual effective interest rate to discount future cash flows.

Calculate the Macaulay duration of this liability.

- A) 3.1 years
- B) 3.2 years
- C) 3.4 years
- D) 3.5 years
- E) 3.6 years

## Explanation

Computing PV at 8% and period-weighted PV:

| Year | CF | PV at 8% | Period × PV |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 10 | 9.26 | 9.26 |
| 2 | 12 | 10.29 | 20.58 |
| 3 | 15 | 11.91 | 35.73 |
| 4 | 20 | 14.70 | 58.80 |
| 5 | 30 | 20.42 | 102.10 |
| **Total** | | **66.58** | **226.47** |

Macaulay duration $= 226.47/66.58 = 3.40$ years.
