Annuity A pays 1 at the beginning of each year for three years. Annuity B pays 1 at the beginning of each year for four years.

The Macaulay duration of Annuity A at the time of purchase is 0.93. Both annuities offer the same yield rate.

Calculate the Macaulay duration of Annuity B at the time of purchase.

- A) 1.240
- B) 1.369
- C) 1.500
- D) 1.930
- E) 1.965

## Explanation

The Macaulay duration of Annuity A is:
$$0.93 = \frac{0(1)+1(v)+2(v^2)}{1+v+v^2} = \frac{v+2v^2}{1+v+v^2}$$

This gives the quadratic $1.07v^2 + 0.07v - 0.93 = 0$, with unique positive solution $v = 0.9$.

The Macaulay duration of Annuity B:
$$D_B = \frac{0(1)+1(v)+2(v^2)+3(v^3)}{1+v+v^2+v^3} = \frac{0+0.9+2(0.81)+3(0.729)}{1+0.9+0.81+0.729} = \frac{4.947}{3.439} = 1.369$$
