**IBNR** (Incurred But Not Reported) is the reserve for claims that have occurred but have not yet been reported to the insurer, equal to the difference between estimated ultimate losses and currently reported losses.

> $$IBNR = \text{Ultimate Losses} - \text{Reported Losses}$$

- Reported losses include paid losses plus [[Case Reserves|case reserves]] on known claims; IBNR covers the gap to ultimate
- Sometimes used broadly to include **IBNER** (Incurred But Not Enough Reported) — development on known open claims where reserves are inadequate
- IBNR is the primary output of reserving methods such as the [[Chain Ladder Method|chain ladder]], [[Bornhuetter-Ferguson Method|Bornhuetter-Ferguson]], and [[Expected Loss Method]]
- Long-tail lines carry far larger IBNR relative to reported losses than short-tail lines; see [[Long Tail Lines]]

> [!example]- Calculating IBNR for an Accident Year {Example}
> An accident year has estimated ultimate losses of \$$1{,}200{,}000$ and reported losses (paid + case reserves) of \$$800{,}000$. Calculate the required IBNR reserve.
>
> > [!answer]-
> > $$IBNR = \$1{,}200{,}000 - \$800{,}000 = \$400{,}000$$
> > The insurer must hold \$$400{,}000$ in IBNR reserves to cover claims not yet reported.
