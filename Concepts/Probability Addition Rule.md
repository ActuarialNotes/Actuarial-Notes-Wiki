# Probability Addition Rule

## Definition

The ==probability addition rule== states:

$$ P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B) $$

For mutually exclusive events, this simplifies to $P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B)$.

> [!example]- <u>Example</u>
> In a group of 100 policyholders, 40 have auto insurance, 50 have home insurance, and 20 have both. What is the probability a randomly selected policyholder has at least one type?
>
> $$P(A \cup H) = P(A) + P(H) - P(A \cap H) = \frac{40}{100} + \frac{50}{100} - \frac{20}{100} = \frac{70}{100} = 0.70$$
>
> The probability a policyholder has neither type is $1 - 0.70 = 0.30$.
