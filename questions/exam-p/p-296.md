An actuary compiles the following information about a portfolio of life insurance policies:

1. There are 150 more policies on males than females.
2. There are 100 more policies on female nonsmokers than male nonsmokers.
3. There are 350 policies on smokers.

Calculate the number of policies on female smokers within this portfolio.

- A) 50
- B) 100
- C) 200
- D) 250
- E) 300

## Explanation

Let $M$ = males, $F$ = females. $M = F+150$.

Let $M_S, F_S$ = male/female smokers. $M_S + F_S = 350$.

From (1): $(M_{NS}+M_S)-(F_{NS}+F_S) = 150$, so $M_{NS}-F_{NS} = 150-(M_S-F_S)$.

From (2): $F_{NS} - M_{NS} = 100$, i.e., $M_{NS} - F_{NS} = -100$.

So: $150 - (M_S - F_S) = -100$, giving $M_S - F_S = 250$.

Combined with $M_S + F_S = 350$:

$$M_S = 300, \quad F_S = \mathbf{50}$$
