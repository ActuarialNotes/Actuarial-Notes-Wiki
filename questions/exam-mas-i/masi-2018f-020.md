$X_i$ is the severity of claim $i$, which has an exponential distribution with mean $= \theta$.

The payment for the claim under an insurance policy is capped at $u$.

There are $(n + s)$ total claims, with $\{x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n\}$ claims with payment less than $u$, and $s$ claims with payment capped at $u$.

Determine which of the following is the MLE for $\theta$.

- A) $\dfrac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i + \dfrac{u}{s}$
- B) $\dfrac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i + \dfrac{nu}{s}$
- C) $\dfrac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i + \dfrac{u}{n}$
- D) $\dfrac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i + \dfrac{su}{n}$
- E) None of (A), (B), (C), or (D) are correct

## Explanation

The $s$ capped claims are **right-censored** at $u$: each contributes the survival term $e^{-u/\theta}$ to the likelihood, while each uncensored claim contributes the density $\tfrac{1}{\theta}e^{-x_i/\theta}$. The log-likelihood is

$$\ell(\theta) = -n\ln\theta - \frac{1}{\theta}\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i - \frac{s\,u}{\theta}.$$

Setting $\ell'(\theta) = 0$:

$$-\frac{n}{\theta} + \frac{\sum x_i + su}{\theta^2} = 0 \implies \hat\theta = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i + su}{n} = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i + \frac{su}{n}.$$

This matches choice **D**.
