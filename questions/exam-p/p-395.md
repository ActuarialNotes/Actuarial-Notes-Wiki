Aggregate insurance claims $S$ follow a compound Poisson process. The Poisson claim count has mean $\lambda$ and each individual claim size $X$ has an exponential distribution with mean $\beta$. It is given that $E[S] = 5000$ and $\text{Var}(S) = 50{,}000{,}000$.

Calculate $\lambda$.

- A) 0.5
- B) 1
- C) 2
- D) 5
- E) 10

## Explanation

$E[S] = \lambda\beta = 5000$ and $\text{Var}(S) = \lambda E[X^2] = \lambda \cdot 2\beta^2 = 50{,}000{,}000$.

$$
\begin{align*}
\frac{\text{Var}(S)}{E[S]} &= \frac{2\lambda\beta^2}{\lambda\beta} \\
                           &= 2\beta \\
                           &= \frac{50{,}000{,}000}{5000} \\
                           &= 10{,}000
\end{align*}
$$

$$\beta = 5000, \quad \lambda = \frac{5000}{5000} = \mathbf{1}$$
