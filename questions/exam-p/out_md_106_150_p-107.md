Let $X$ represent the number of customers arriving during the morning hours and let $Y$ represent the number of customers arriving during the afternoon hours at a diner. You are given:

i) $X$ and $Y$ are Poisson distributed.
ii) The first moment of $X$ is less than the first moment of $Y$ by 8.
iii) The second moment of $X$ is 60% of the second moment of $Y$.

Calculate the variance of $Y$.

- A) 4
- B) 12
- C) 16
- D) 27
- E) 35

## Explanation

Let $\lambda_X$ and $\lambda_Y$ be the parameters (which equal the mean and variance) for the Poisson distributions of $X$ and $Y$.
The second moment is $E[X^2] = \lambda_X + \lambda_X^2$.

From (ii): $\lambda_X = \lambda_Y - 8$
From (iii): $E[X^2] = 0.6 E[Y^2] \implies \lambda_X + \lambda_X^2 = 0.6(\lambda_Y + \lambda_Y^2)$

Substitute the first equation into the second:
$$(\lambda_Y - 8) + (\lambda_Y - 8)^2 = 0.6\lambda_Y + 0.6\lambda_Y^2$$
$$\lambda_Y - 8 + \lambda_Y^2 - 16\lambda_Y + 64 = 0.6\lambda_Y + 0.6\lambda_Y^2$$
$$0.4\lambda_Y^2 - 15.6\lambda_Y + 56 = 0$$

Solve this quadratic equation for $\lambda_Y$:
$$\lambda_Y = \frac{15.6 \pm \sqrt{(-15.6)^2 - 4(0.4)(56)}}{2(0.4)} = \frac{15.6 \pm \sqrt{153.76}}{0.8} = \frac{15.6 \pm 12.4}{0.8}$$

Possible solutions are $\lambda_Y = \frac{28}{0.8} = 35$ or $\lambda_Y = \frac{3.2}{0.8} = 4$.
If $\lambda_Y = 4$, then $\lambda_X = 4 - 8 = -4$, which is impossible for a Poisson parameter.
Therefore, $\lambda_Y = 35$. The variance of $Y$ is $\lambda_Y = 35$.
