Given the following information as of December 31, 2017:

| Calendar Year | Earned Premium ($000) | On-Level Adjustment |
|---------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| 2014 | 127,500 | 0.710 |
| 2015 | 117,600 | 0.660 |
| 2016 | 64,300 | 0.850 |
| 2017 | 58,900 | 1.000 |

| Accident Year | Ultimate Claim Counts | Ultimate Severity ($) |
|---------------|-----------------------|-----------------------|
| 2014 | 2,200 | 32,600 |
| 2015 | 1,970 | 35,300 |

- -1.3% Annual claim count trend
- 6.0% Annual severity trend
- 15% Estimated savings on claims occurring after January 1, 2017 due to legislative change

## Part a (3 points)

Estimate the ultimate claims for accident years 2016 and 2017 using an appropriate frequency-severity technique.

### Answer

AY 2016 ultimate claims: $49,300 thousand  
AY 2017 ultimate claims: $47,247 thousand

### Explanation

**Step 1: Compute on-level earned premium (OLEP)**

$$\text{OLEP} = \text{Earned Premium} \times \text{On-Level Adjustment}$$

| AY | Earned Premium ($000) | On-Level Adj | On-Level EP ($000) |
|----|----------------------|--------------|--------------------|
| 2014 | 127,500 | 0.710 | 90,525 |
| 2015 | 117,600 | 0.660 | 77,616 |

**Step 2: Compute ultimate frequency for AYs 2014 and 2015, trended to 2017**

The frequency trend is -1.3% per year. Trend periods from AY midpoint to the 2017 midpoint:
- AY 2014: 3 years, trend factor = $(0.987)^3 = 0.9614$
- AY 2015: 2 years, trend factor = $(0.987)^2 = 0.9742$

$$\text{Trended Ultimate Frequency} = \frac{\text{Ultimate Claim Counts} \times \text{Trend Factor}}{\text{On-Level EP}}$$

| AY | Ult Counts | Trend Factor | Trended Counts | OLEP ($000) | Trended Ult Freq |
|----|------------|--------------|----------------|-------------|-----------------|
| 2014 | 2,200 | 0.9614 | 2,115 | 90,525 | 2.34% |
| 2015 | 1,970 | 0.9742 | 1,919 | 77,616 | 2.47% |

**Selected trended frequency (2017 level): 2.40%**

**Step 3: Project ultimate severity for AYs 2014 and 2015, trended to 2017**

The severity trend is 6.0% per year. The 15% tort savings apply to claims occurring on or after January 1, 2017, so only AY 2017 is adjusted. When trending to a common 2017 base, apply the tort factor (multiply by 0.85) since the 2017 environment includes the savings.

- AY 2014: trend period = 3 years, factor = $(1.06)^3 = 1.1910$; tort adj = 0.85
- AY 2015: trend period = 2 years, factor = $(1.06)^2 = 1.1236$; tort adj = 0.85

$$\text{Trended Ult Severity} = \text{Ult Severity} \times \text{Severity Trend Factor} \times \text{Tort Factor}$$

| AY | Ult Severity ($) | Sev Trend | Tort Adj | Trended Ult Severity ($) |
|----|-----------------|-----------|----------|--------------------------|
| 2014 | 32,600 | 1.1910 | 0.85 | 33,003 |
| 2015 | 35,300 | 1.1236 | 0.85 | 33,714 |

**Selected trended severity (2017 level): $33,358**

**Step 4: Project AY 2016 estimates — detrend back from 2017 to 2016**

AY 2016 is one year before 2017, so:
- Detrend frequency: reverse 1 year of frequency trend and reverse tort factor (claims pre-2017 do not get the 15% savings)

$$\text{Freq}_{2016} = \frac{0.0240}{0.987 \times 0.85} = \frac{0.0240}{0.83895} = 2.07\%$$

$$\text{Sev}_{2016} = \frac{33,358}{1.06 \times 0.85} = \frac{33,358}{0.901} = 37,024$$

**Step 5: Calculate ultimate claims**

$$\text{Ultimate Claims} = \text{Earned Premium} \times \text{Selected Frequency} \times \text{Selected Severity}$$

| AY | Earned Premium ($000) | Selected Freq | Ult Claim Counts | Selected Severity ($) | Ultimate Claims ($000) |
|----|----------------------|---------------|------------------|-----------------------|------------------------|
| 2016 | 64,300 | 2.07% | 1,332 | 37,024 | 49,300 |
| 2017 | 58,900 | 2.40% | 1,414 | 33,358 | 47,247 |

For AY 2016: $64,300 \times 0.0207 \times 37,024 \approx 49,300$

For AY 2017: $58,900 \times 0.0240 \times 33,358 \approx 47,247$

### Examiner Report

Candidates were expected to use the frequency/severity technique incorporating trend to calculate ultimate claims. Candidates were expected to trend the data from different accident years consistently, separately for frequency and severity. Then, provided a new exposure base for years 2016 and 2017, candidates were expected to estimate the total ultimate claims for each. Candidates were expected to apply frequency trend, severity trend, adjust for a change in premium per exposure, and apply an adjustment for a change in the tort environment.

Common mistakes include:
- Failing to convert claim counts to frequency
- Incorrectly applying the on-level factors or applying them inconsistently
- Not applying the tort factor, applying it to both years, or applying it to 2016 only
- Multiplying the calculated frequency and severity together, but not multiplying by premium (exposure)
- Attempting to calculate a loss ratio
- Averaging the 2014 and 2015 data without individually trending them

## Part b (0.5 points)

Briefly describe two key assumptions of frequency-severity techniques.

### Explanation

Any two of the following assumptions:

1. **Claim counts develop similarly in future** — the historical development pattern of claim counts is expected to persist.

2. **Claim counts are consistent over time** — the definition and counting methodology for claims is stable across accident years.

3. **Mix by claim type is consistent / homogeneous** — the distribution of claim types within the portfolio does not shift materially over time.

4. **Consistent definition of exposures** — the exposure base and how it is measured does not change over the experience period.

5. **Stable settlement pattern** — the rate at which claims are settled remains consistent so that frequency and severity can be meaningfully compared across accident years.

6. **Stable case reserve adequacy** — the level of case reserves relative to ultimate losses remains consistent across accident years and evaluation dates.

### Examiner Report

Candidates were expected to describe two key assumptions of the frequency/severity technique.

Common mistakes include:
- Providing a generic answer regarding the availability or accuracy of data
- Providing a specific required definition of claim count. The assumption of the frequency/severity technique is that the definition of a claim count is consistent, but there are alternate valid ways to define claim counts.
