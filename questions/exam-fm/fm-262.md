The first payment of a five-year annuity is due in five years in the amount of 1000. The subsequent four annual payments increase by 500 each year. The annual effective interest rate is $i$.

Determine which of the following formulas gives the present value of the annuity.

- A) $v^6\left[500a_{\overline{5}|i} + 500(Ia)_{\overline{5}|i}\right]$
- B) $v^6\left[500\ddot{a}_{\overline{5}|i} + 500(I\ddot{a})_{\overline{5}|i}\right]$
- C) $v^5\left[500a_{\overline{5}|i} + 500(I\ddot{a})_{\overline{5}|i}\right]$
- D) $v^5\left[500\ddot{a}_{\overline{5}|i} + 500(I\ddot{a})_{\overline{5}|i}\right]$
- E) $v^5\left[1000\ddot{a}_{\overline{5}|i} + 500(I\ddot{a})_{\overline{5}|i}\right]$

## Explanation

The payments are 1000, 1500, 2000, 2500, 3000 at times 5, 6, 7, 8, 9.

Think of them as 500 times a 5-payment increasing annuity-due (payments 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 at times 5–9) plus 500 times a 5-payment level annuity-due (1 at times 5–9), all discounted from time 5 to time 0.

PV at time 5: $500\ddot{a}_{\overline{5}|} + 500(I\ddot{a})_{\overline{5}|}$

PV at time 0: $v^5\left[500\ddot{a}_{\overline{5}|} + 500(I\ddot{a})_{\overline{5}|}\right]$
