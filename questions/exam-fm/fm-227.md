An investor purchases a 1200 face amount zero-coupon bond for a price of 1000. With respect to the bond's annual effective yield rate, the Macaulay duration is four years and the modified duration is $d$ years.

Calculate $d$.

- A) 3.33
- B) 3.82
- C) 3.86
- D) 4.00
- E) 4.19

## Explanation

For a zero-coupon bond, the Macaulay duration equals the term to maturity, which is 4 years (confirmed by the problem).

The yield rate: $(1+i)^4 = 1200/1000 = 1.2 \implies i = 1.2^{1/4} - 1 = 0.046635$.

Modified duration $= D_{MAC}/(1+i) = 4/1.046635 = 3.8219 \approx 3.82$.
