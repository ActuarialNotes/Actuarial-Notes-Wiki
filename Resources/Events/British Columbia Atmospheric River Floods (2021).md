# BC Atmospheric River Floods (November 2021)

## Overview
A series of atmospheric rivers caused catastrophic flooding and landslides across southern British Columbia, inundating the Sumas Prairie, severing every highway and rail link between Vancouver and the interior, and producing roughly $675 million in insured losses — then the costliest severe-weather event in BC history — against far larger economic losses.

## Key Details
*   **Protection Gap Exposed:** Much of the residential loss was overland flood in high-risk zones where coverage was unavailable or not purchased, fueling national debate that led to the federal commitment to a public-private flood insurance program for high-risk properties.
*   **Supply-Chain BI:** Port of Vancouver rail/road severance created contingent business interruption losses far beyond the physical damage footprint.
*   **Compound Catastrophe Year:** Followed the same year's Lytton heat-dome wildfire, demonstrating sequential, correlated climate perils within one region and calendar year.

## Multi-Agent Execution Mandates
*   **Actuarial & Risk:** Accelerated adoption of probabilistic Canadian flood models and accumulation tracking by watershed rather than postal code alone.
*   **Underwriting:** Tightened overland water eligibility rules and deductible structures in mapped high-hazard zones.
*   **Government Relations:** Industry (via IBC) engaged in design of the national high-risk flood pool announced in Budget 2023.
