A six-sided die is rolled 180 times and the following results were recorded:

| Roll Result | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 |
|-------------|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Frequency | 30 | 30 | 30 | 30 | X | Y |

- You use a Chi-squared test to evaluate the following hypothesis:
  - $H_0$: The die is fair (each roll result is equally likely)
  - $H_1$: The die is not fair
- The test significance level $\alpha = 0.05$
- $X < Y$

Calculate the largest value of X that would lead to a rejection of the null hypothesis.

- A) Less than 17
- B) 17
- C) 18
- D) 19
- E) At least 20

## Explanation

Under $H_0$ each face is expected $180/6 = 30$ times. Since $X + Y = 180 - 120 = 60$, we have $Y = 60 - X$. The chi-square statistic is

$$\chi^2 = \frac{(X-30)^2}{30} + \frac{(Y-30)^2}{30} = \frac{(X-30)^2 + (30-X)^2}{30} = \frac{(X-30)^2}{15}.$$

With $6 - 1 = 5$ degrees of freedom, reject when $\chi^2 > \chi^2_{0.05,5} = 11.070$:

$$\frac{(X-30)^2}{15} > 11.070 \implies (X-30)^2 > 166.05 \implies |X - 30| > 12.886.$$

Since $X < Y$ forces $X < 30$, we need $X < 30 - 12.886 = 17.114$. Checking: $X = 17$ gives $\chi^2 = 169/15 = 11.27 > 11.07$ (reject), while $X = 18$ gives $9.6$ (do not reject). The largest such $X$ is $17$, so the answer is **B**.
