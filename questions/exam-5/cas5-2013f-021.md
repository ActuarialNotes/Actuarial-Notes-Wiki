**Cumulative Paid Claims ($000)**

| Accident Year | 12 Months | 24 Months | 36 Months | 48 Months |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2009 | $2,000 | $2,800 | $4,340 | $5,425 |
| 2010 | $2,100 | $3,360 | $4,872 | |
| 2011 | $2,000 | $3,750 | | |
| 2012 | $1,600 | | | |

| Accident Year | Ultimate Claim Counts |

## Part a (2.75 points)

Estimate the ultimate claims for accident year 2012 as of December 31, 2012 using the Berquist-Sherman paid adjustment.

### Answer

$8,982,400 (approximately $8,982k)

### Explanation

The Berquist-Sherman paid adjustment re-states the historical paid claims data to reflect the current disposal rate pattern, removing the distortion caused by changes in claim closure speed.

**Step 1 — Calculate cumulative disposal rates** (cumulative closed counts ÷ ultimate claim counts):

| AY | 12 Months | 24 Months | 36 Months | 48 Months |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2009 | 250/500 = 0.500 | 400/500 = 0.800 | 450/500 = 0.900 | 500/500 = 1.000 |
| 2010 | 225/450 = 0.500 | 360/450 = 0.800 | 405/450 = 0.900 | |
| 2011 | 250/625 = 0.400 | 500/625 = 0.800 | | |
| 2012 | 175/700 = 0.250 | | | |

**Step 2 — Identify the current disposal rate pattern.**

Looking at the latest calendar year diagonal (2012 CY = AY2012@12, AY2011@24, AY2010@36, AY2009@48), the most recent disposal rates are:

- Age 12: 0.250 (from AY 2012)
- Age 24: 0.800 (from AY 2011 — unchanged, no adjustment needed)
- Age 36: 0.900 (from AY 2010 — unchanged, no adjustment needed)
- Age 48: 1.000 (from AY 2009)

There is a slowdown at 12 months: AY 2009–2010 had disposal rate 0.500 at age 12, but AY 2011 shows 0.400 and AY 2012 shows 0.250. The latest CY disposal rate at age 12 is **0.250**.

Since ages 24–48 show no change in disposal rate, only the 12-month column requires adjustment.

**Step 3 — Adjust cumulative paid claims at age 12 using linear interpolation.**

The adjustment maps what the paid claims would have been at a disposal rate of 0.250 given the historical payment pattern (interpolating between age 0 with $0 paid and actual age 12 data):

$$\text{Adjusted Paid}_{12} = \frac{\text{Actual Paid}_{12} - 0}{\text{Disposal Rate}_{12} - 0} \times 0.250$$

| AY | Actual Paid @12 ($000) | Actual Disposal Rate @12 | Adjusted Paid @12 ($000) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2009 | 2,000 | 0.500 | (2,000 / 0.500) × 0.250 = **1,000** |
| 2010 | 2,100 | 0.500 | (2,100 / 0.500) × 0.250 = **1,050** |
| 2011 | 2,000 | 0.400 | (2,000 / 0.400) × 0.250 = **1,250** |
| 2012 | 1,600 | 0.250 | unchanged = **1,600** |

**Step 4 — Construct the adjusted paid claims triangle.**

Ages 24+ are unchanged:

| AY | 12 (adj) | 24 | 36 | 48 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2009 | 1,000 | 2,800 | 4,340 | 5,425 |
| 2010 | 1,050 | 3,360 | 4,872 | |
| 2011 | 1,250 | 3,750 | | |
| 2012 | 1,600 | | | |

**Step 5 — Calculate age-to-age factors from the adjusted triangle.**

$$f_{12\text{-}24} = \frac{2{,}800 + 3{,}360 + 3{,}750}{1{,}000 + 1{,}050 + 1{,}250} = \frac{9{,}910}{3{,}300} = 3.003$$

$$f_{24\text{-}36} = \frac{4{,}340 + 4{,}872}{2{,}800 + 3{,}360} = \frac{9{,}212}{6{,}160} = 1.495$$

$$f_{36\text{-}48} = \frac{5{,}425}{4{,}340} = 1.250$$

$$f_{48\text{-ult}} = 1.000$$

**Step 6 — Calculate cumulative development factors (CDF) to ultimate.**

$$\text{CDF}_{36} = 1.250 \times 1.000 = 1.250$$

$$\text{CDF}_{24} = 1.495 \times 1.250 = 1.869$$

$$\text{CDF}_{12} = 3.003 \times 1.869 = 5.614$$

**Step 7 — Estimate ultimate claims for AY 2012.**

AY 2012 is at 12 months with adjusted paid claims of $1,600k:

$$\text{Ultimate}_{2012} = 1{,}600 \times 5.614 = \$8{,}982k$$

### Examiner Report

Most candidates correctly calculated the disposal rate and implemented the paid loss development factors (link ratios, selecting LDFs, calculating CDFs, and applying to the appropriate paid dollars). Candidates had trouble with interpolating at age 12. Many candidates realized that ages 24–48 did not need to be adjusted. The most common errors were:

- Incorrectly interpolating at age 12 — the linear interpolation from $0 at disposal rate 0.0 to the actual paid amount at the actual disposal rate should use the target (latest CY) disposal rate of 0.250.
- Calculating incremental paids and adding periods together to reach the ultimate rather than correctly applying the CDF to the adjusted 12-month paid amount.
