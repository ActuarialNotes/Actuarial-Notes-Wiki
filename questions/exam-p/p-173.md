Let $N$ denote the number of accidents occurring during one month on the northbound side of a highway and $S$ denote the number occurring on the southbound side. Suppose that $N$ and $S$ are jointly distributed as follows:

| $N \backslash S$ | 0 | 1 | 2 | $\geq$3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | 0.04 | 0.06 | 0.10 | 0.04 |
| 1 | 0.10 | 0.18 | 0.08 | 0.03 |
| 2 | 0.12 | 0.06 | 0.05 | 0.02 |
| $\geq$3 | 0.05 | 0.04 | 0.02 | 0.01 |

Calculate $\text{Var}(N \mid N + S = 2)$.

- A) 0.48
- B) 0.55
- C) 0.67
- D) 0.91
- E) 1.25

## Explanation

Events with $N + S = 2$: $(N=0, S=2)$, $(N=1, S=1)$, $(N=2, S=0)$.

$P(N+S=2) = 0.10 + 0.18 + 0.12 = 0.40$

Conditional distribution of $N$:
$$P(N=0 \mid N+S=2) = \frac{0.10}{0.40} = 0.25, \quad P(N=1 \mid \cdot) = 0.45, \quad P(N=2 \mid \cdot) = 0.30$$

$$E[N \mid N+S=2] = 0(0.25) + 1(0.45) + 2(0.30) = 1.05$$
$$E[N^2 \mid N+S=2] = 0(0.25) + 1(0.45) + 4(0.30) = 1.65$$
$$\text{Var}(N \mid N+S=2) = 1.65 - 1.05^2 = 1.65 - 1.1025 = \mathbf{0.5475 \approx 0.55}$$
