A loan of 20,000 is to be repaid with ten increasing installments payable at the end of each year. Each installment will be 10% greater than the preceding installment. The annual effective interest rate on the loan is 9%.

Calculate the amount of principal in the second installment.

- A) 292
- B) 527
- C) 975
- D) 1435
- E) 1774

## Explanation

First payment $R_1$:

$$\begin{align*}
20{,}000 &= R_1 \cdot \frac{1-(1.10/1.09)^{10}}{-0.01} = R_1 \times 9.562 \\
R_1 &= 2091.61
\end{align*}$$

Second payment: $R_2 = 2091.61(1.10) = 2300.71$.

Outstanding balance before 2nd payment:

$$20{,}000(1.09) - R_1 = 21{,}800 - 2091.61 = 19{,}708.39$$

Interest in 2nd payment: $19{,}708.39 \times 0.09 = 1773.76$.

Principal in 2nd payment:

$$R_2 - \text{interest} = 2300.71 - 1773.76 = 526.95 \approx 527$$
