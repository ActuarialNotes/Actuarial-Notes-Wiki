A perpetuity-immediate is purchased at a price of 5000 based on an annual effective rate of $i$, where $i > 0$. The perpetuity pays 150 in the first year and increases by 10 each year thereafter.

Calculate $i$.

- A) 5.7%
- B) 6.2%
- C) 6.7%
- D) 7.2%
- E) 7.7%

## Explanation

The perpetuity is an arithmetically increasing perpetuity:

$$5000 = \frac{150}{i} + \frac{10}{i^2}$$

$$5000i^2 - 150i - 10 = 0$$

$$i = \frac{150 \pm \sqrt{(-150)^2 - 4(5000)(-10)}}{2(5000)} = \frac{150 \pm \sqrt{22500+200000}}{10,000}$$

$$= \frac{150 \pm \sqrt{222500}}{10,000} = \frac{150 + 471.70}{10,000} = 0.06217 \approx 6.2\%$$
