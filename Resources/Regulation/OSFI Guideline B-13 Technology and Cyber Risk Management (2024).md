# OSFI Guideline B-13 Effective (January 1, 2024)

## Overview
Guideline B-13 established OSFI's expectations for technology and cyber risk management across all federally regulated financial institutions, organized into three domains: governance and risk management; technology operations and resilience; and cyber security.

## Key Details
*   **Defined Accountabilities:** Senior-management ownership of technology and cyber risk, with board-level oversight and a documented risk appetite for technology risk.
*   **Resilience Expectations:** Asset inventories, patch and vulnerability management, disaster recovery objectives, and tested incident response capabilities.
*   **Companion Requirements:** Operates alongside OSFI's Technology and Cyber Security Incident Reporting Advisory (24-hour reporting of material incidents) and B-10 third-party risk guideline (renewed 2023) covering cloud and outsourcing.

## Multi-Agent Execution Mandates
*   **IT & Operations:** Control frameworks (often mapped to NIST CSF) evidenced against B-13 domains; cloud arrangements brought under B-10/B-13 joint compliance.
*   **Risk & Compliance:** Technology risk integrated into ORSA and operational risk capital narratives; self-assessments against B-13 became standing supervisory artifacts.
*   **Executive:** Cyber posture reporting elevated to board cadence with defined escalation thresholds.
