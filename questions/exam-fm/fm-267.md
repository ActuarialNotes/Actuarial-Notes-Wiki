A borrower planned to repay a loan of $L$ with level payments at the end of each month for 30 years. The loan had an annual nominal interest rate of 6%, convertible monthly. Starting with the 181st payment, the borrower increased the monthly payment to 2000, which enabled the borrower to pay off the loan five years earlier than planned.

Calculate $L$.

- A) 253,554
- B) 277,772
- C) 310,414
- D) 330,347
- E) 333,583

## Explanation

Monthly rate $j = 0.5\%$. The outstanding balance after 180 payments (= end of year 15):

$OB_{180} = 2000a_{\overline{120}|0.005} = 2000(90.0735) = 180{,}147$.

The original payment $P$ satisfies $OB_{180} = Pa_{\overline{180}|0.005}$:
$$P = 180{,}147/a_{\overline{180}|0.005} = 180{,}147/119.6162 = 1{,}506.02$$

Hmm — but the solution shows $OB_{180} = 2000a_{\overline{120}|0.005}$ and original payment via $L = Pa_{\overline{360}|0.005}$.

Actually: $OB_{180} = Pa_{\overline{180}|0.005}$, and from the increased payment: $OB_{180} = 2000a_{\overline{120}|0.005}$.

$P \cdot a_{\overline{180}|} = 2000 \cdot a_{\overline{120}|} \implies P = 1520.18$

$L = 1520.18 \cdot a_{\overline{360}|0.005} = 1520.18(166.7916) = 253{,}554$
