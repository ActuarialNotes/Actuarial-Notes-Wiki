Bond A is a 15-year 1000 face amount bond with an annual coupon rate of 9% paid semiannually. Bond A will be redeemed at 1200 and is bought to yield 8.4% convertible semiannually.

Bond B is an $n$-year 1000 face amount bond with an annual coupon rate of 8% paid quarterly. Bond B will be redeemed at 1376.69 and is bought to yield 8.4% convertible quarterly.

The two bonds have the same purchase price.

Calculate $n$.

- A) 12
- B) 14
- C) 15
- D) 16
- E) 18

## Explanation

Bond A: $P_A = 45a_{\overline{30}|0.042} + 1200(1.042)^{-30} = 45(17.2920) + 1200(0.29427) = 778.14 + 353.12 = 1131.26$.

Wait — using $j = 0.042$ per half-year:
$P_A = 45a_{\overline{30}|0.042} + 1200(1.042)^{-30}$. Using BAII Plus: $P_A \approx 1108.85$.

Bond B: quarterly yield $j = 0.021$, quarterly coupon $= 20$, redemption $= 1376.69$.

$$1108.85 = 20a_{\overline{4n}|0.021} + 1376.69(1.021)^{-4n}$$

Using a financial calculator with PV=1108.85, PMT=20, FV=1376.69, I/Y=2.1: $N=48$, so $4n=48$, $n=12$.
