A disease affects 30% of the population. A diagnostic test returns positive for 80% of those who have the disease and positive for 20% of those who do not. Suppose also that in the tested population, 50% of tests come back positive overall. If a randomly selected person tests positive, what is the probability they actually have the disease?

- A) 0.24
- B) 0.48
- C) 0.63
- D) 0.80

## Explanation

By Bayes' theorem, $P(A \mid B) = \dfrac{P(B \mid A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)}$.

Let $A$ = has disease and $B$ = tests positive. We are given $P(A) = 0.3$, $P(B \mid A) = 0.8$, and $P(B) = 0.5$ (stated directly in the problem). Therefore:

$$P(A \mid B) = \frac{0.8 \times 0.3}{0.5} = \frac{0.24}{0.5} = 0.48$$
