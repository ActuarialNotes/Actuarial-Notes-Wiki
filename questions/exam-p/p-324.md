An actuary determines that the daily auto accident count within a city can be modeled by a Poisson random variable with mean 4. Accident counts on different days are mutually independent.

Calculate the approximate probability that at least 6,496 accidents occur during a period of 1,600 days.

- A) 0.01
- B) 0.12
- C) 0.19
- D) 0.27
- E) 0.49

## Explanation

Total $N \sim \text{Poisson}(6400) \approx N(6400, 6400)$ by CLT. $\text{SD}=80$.

$$P(N \geq 6496) = P\!\left(Z \geq \frac{6496-6400}{80}\right) = P(Z \geq 1.2)$$

$$= 1-\Phi(1.2) \approx 1-0.8849 = \mathbf{0.115 \approx 0.12}$$
