Given the following information:

| Territory | Premium | Current Relativity | Indicated Relativity |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | \$195,000 | 0.85 | 0.75 |
| 2 | \$475,000 | 1.00 | 1.00 |
| 3 | \$330,000 | 1.30 | 1.20 |
| **Total** | **\$1,000,000** | | |

Management is requiring that **both** of the following objectives are achieved with the upcoming rate change:

- Target an overall rate level increase of 20%.
- Revise territorial relativities to the indicated relativity, while capping any territory rate impact at 25% overall.

Calculate the territorial relativities that will be implemented with the rate change.

### Answer

**Final implemented relativities:**
- Territory 1: **0.7631**
- Territory 2: **1.0000**
- Territory 3: **1.2210**

### Explanation

**Step 1: Compute the revenue-neutral relativity change factor for each territory.**

The indicated change in relativity for each territory relative to the current relativity:

$$\text{Rel. Change}_i = \frac{\text{Indicated}_i}{\text{Current}_i}$$

| Territory | Indicated / Current |
|---|---|
| 1 | 0.75 / 0.85 = 0.88235 |
| 2 | 1.00 / 1.00 = 1.00000 |
| 3 | 1.20 / 1.30 = 0.92308 |

**Step 2: Compute the premium-weighted average relativity change (off-balance factor).**

$$\text{Wtd Avg} = \frac{195{,}000 \times 0.88235 + 475{,}000 \times 1.00000 + 330{,}000 \times 0.92308}{1{,}000{,}000}$$

$$= \frac{172{,}059 + 475{,}000 + 304{,}615}{1{,}000{,}000} = \frac{951{,}674}{1{,}000{,}000} = 0.951674$$

**Step 3: Compute the revenue-neutral relativity factor for each territory.**

Divide by the weighted average to produce a revenue-neutral change:

$$\text{Rev-Neutral}_i = \frac{\text{Rel. Change}_i}{0.951674}$$

| Territory | Revenue-Neutral Factor |
|---|---|
| 1 | 0.88235 / 0.951674 = 0.92716 |
| 2 | 1.00000 / 0.951674 = 1.05078 |
| 3 | 0.92308 / 0.951674 = 0.96995 |

**Step 4: Apply the 20% overall target increase.**

Total change for each territory = Revenue-Neutral factor × 1.20:

| Territory | Total Uncapped Change |
|---|---|
| 1 | 0.92716 × 1.20 = 1.11259 |
| 2 | 1.05078 × 1.20 = 1.26094 |
| 3 | 0.96995 × 1.20 = 1.16394 |

**Step 5: Apply the 25% cap on individual territory rate changes.**

Territory 1: 1.11259 — within cap (< 1.25). No cap needed.
Territory 2: 1.26094 — exceeds cap. Cap at 1.25.
Territory 3: 1.16394 — within cap (< 1.25). No cap needed.

**Step 6: Calculate the premium shortfall due to the cap on Territory 2.**

Capped premium change for Territory 2 = 1.25.
Uncapped premium change for Territory 2 = 1.26094.
Excess ratio = 1.26094 - 1.25 = 0.01094.

Premium shortfall = $475,000 × 0.01094 = $5,194.6.

**Step 7: Adjust for the base rate cap on Territory 2.**

The base rate (Territory 2, where current relativity = 1.00) is also being capped. The base rate change must be reduced:

$$\text{Adj. to base due to cap} = \frac{1.25}{1.26094} = 0.99133$$

**Step 8: Recover the shortfall by adjusting uncapped territories (1 and 3).**

Premium from uncapped territories at their uncapped total change levels:

$$\text{PP from T1 and T3} = 195{,}000 \times 1.11259 + 330{,}000 \times 1.16394 = 216{,}955 + 384{,}100 = 601{,}055$$

Additional adjustment needed to recover shortfall from capped territory, while also compensating for the lower base rate:

$$\text{Adj. for shortfall} = \frac{5{,}194.6}{601{,}055} + 1 = 1.008643$$

$$\text{Total adj. for T1 and T3} = \frac{1}{0.99133} \times 1.008643 = 1.00874 \times 1.008643 \approx 1.01747$$

**Step 9: Compute the final implemented relativities.**

Starting from the indicated relativities, multiply the non-capped territories by the combined adjustment factor:

| Territory | Calculation | Final Relativity |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0.75 × 1.01747 = | **0.7631** |
| 2 | 1.00 (capped base) | **1.0000** |
| 3 | 1.20 × 1.01747 = | **1.2210** |

### Examiner Report

In general, most candidates were able to correctly calculate the weighted impact of the proposed relativity changes and recognize the need for an off-balance factor to neutralize the overall premium back to the starting premium. Most candidates were also able to then apply the targeted rate change of 20% to derive a total uncapped change for each territory.

Some candidates only showed that Territory 2 would exceed the maximum rate cap of 25% without explicitly demonstrating that Territories 1 and 3 would not. When attempting to calculate the premium shortfall due to the cap on Territory 2, some candidates failed to identify the correct premium base to which the excess ratio should be applied. Another common error involved candidates capping the rate change at the overall targeted change of 20% rather than 25%. Most candidates struggled with the final step — either not correctly identifying the denominator of premiums to which the excess premium should be applied, or forgetting to make an adjustment to compensate for the base rate cap.
