# coefficient of variation

## Definition

The ==coefficient of variation== is a dimensionless measure of relative variability:

$$ CV = \frac{\sigma}{\mu} $$

> [!example]- <u>Example</u>
> Continuing from the previous examples where $\mu = 2.7$ and $\sigma = 0.9$:
>
> $CV = \frac{0.9}{2.7} = \frac{1}{3} \approx 0.333$
>
> Now compare two distributions:
> - Distribution A: $\mu_A = 100$, $\sigma_A = 15$ $\Rightarrow$ $CV_A = 15/100 = 0.15$
> - Distribution B: $\mu_B = 10$, $\sigma_B = 3$ $\Rightarrow$ $CV_B = 3/10 = 0.30$
>
> Although $\sigma_A > \sigma_B$, Distribution B has **greater relative variability** ($CV_B > CV_A$).
