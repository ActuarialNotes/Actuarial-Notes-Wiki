A car is new at the beginning of a calendar year. The time, in years, before the car experiences its first failure is exponentially distributed with mean 2.

Calculate the probability that the car experiences its first failure in the last quarter of some calendar year.

- A) 0.081
- B) 0.088
- C) 0.102
- D) 0.205
- E) 0.250

## Explanation

Let $X$ be the time in years before the car experiences its first failure. $X \sim \text{Exp}(\lambda = 0.5)$.
The last quarter of calendar year $k$ corresponds to the interval $(k + 0.75, k + 1]$ for $k = 0, 1, 2, \dots$.

We want to find the total probability:
$$P = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} P(k + 0.75 < X \le k + 1)$$

Using the CDF of the exponential distribution $F(x) = 1 - e^{-0.5x}$:
$$P(k + 0.75 < X \le k + 1) = F(k + 1) - F(k + 0.75) = (1 - e^{-0.5(k+1)}) - (1 - e^{-0.5(k+0.75)})$$
$$= e^{-0.5(k+0.75)} - e^{-0.5(k+1)} = e^{-0.5k} (e^{-0.375} - e^{-0.5})$$

Summing this geometric series over $k$ from 0 to $\infty$:
$$P = (e^{-0.375} - e^{-0.5}) \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (e^{-0.5})^k$$

The sum of the geometric series $\sum r^k$ is $\frac{1}{1-r}$:
$$P = (e^{-0.375} - e^{-0.5}) \left( \frac{1}{1 - e^{-0.5}} \right)$$
$$= \frac{0.687289 - 0.606531}{1 - 0.606531} = \frac{0.080758}{0.393469} \approx 0.205$$
