In a group of 25 factory workers, 20 are low-risk and five are high-risk. Two of the 25 factory workers are randomly selected without replacement.

Calculate the probability that exactly one of the two selected factory workers is low-risk.

- A) 0.160
- B) 0.167
- C) 0.320
- D) 0.333
- E) 0.633

## Explanation

This is a hypergeometric probability problem where we select 2 workers without replacement from a population of 25 (20 low-risk and 5 high-risk).

The probability of selecting exactly 1 low-risk (and therefore exactly 1 high-risk) is:
$$P = \frac{\binom{20}{1} \binom{5}{1}}{\binom{25}{2}} = \frac{20 \times 5}{\frac{25 \times 24}{2}} = \frac{100}{300} = \frac{1}{3} \approx 0.333$$

Alternatively, consider the order of selection. The first could be low-risk and the second high-risk, or vice versa:
$$P(\text{Low, High}) = \frac{20}{25} \times \frac{5}{24} = \frac{100}{600}$$
$$P(\text{High, Low}) = \frac{5}{25} \times \frac{20}{24} = \frac{100}{600}$$
Total probability = $\frac{100}{600} + \frac{100}{600} = \frac{200}{600} = \frac{1}{3} \approx 0.333$.
