The number of auto claims $X$ and home claims $Y$ filed by a randomly chosen policyholder have the following distribution: $P[X=0]=0.75$, $P[X=1]=0.25$; $P[Y=0]=0.80$, $P[Y=1]=0.20$; $P[X=0,Y=0]=0.60$.

Calculate $\text{Corr}(X, Y)$.

- A) -0.32
- B) -0.10
- C) 0.00
- D) 0.10
- E) 0.32

## Explanation

$P(X=1,Y=1)$: from the joint table, $P(X=0,Y=0)=0.60$, $P(X=0,Y=1)=0.15$, $P(X=1,Y=0)=0.20$, $P(X=1,Y=1)=0.05$.

Note: $0.75 \times 0.80 = 0.60 = P(X=0,Y=0)$, so $X$ and $Y$ are **independent**.

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{Cov}(X,Y) &= E[XY]-E[X]E[Y] \\
                &= P(X=1,Y=1) - (0.25)(0.20) \\
                &= 0.05-0.05 \\
                &= 0
\end{align*}
$$

$$\text{Corr}(X,Y) = \mathbf{0}$$
