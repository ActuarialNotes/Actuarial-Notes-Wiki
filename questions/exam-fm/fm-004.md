A perpetuity costs 77.1 and makes end-of-year payments. The perpetuity pays 1 at the end of year 2, 2 at the end of year 3, …, $n$ at the end of year $(n+1)$. After year $(n+1)$, the payments remain constant at $n$. The annual effective interest rate is 10.5%.

Calculate $n$.

- A) 17
- B) 18
- C) 19
- D) 20
- E) 21

## Explanation

The present value of the perpetuity is:

$$77.1 = v(Ia)_{\overline{n}|} + \frac{nv^{n+1}}{i}$$

$$= v\left[\frac{\ddot{a}_{\overline{n}|} - nv^n}{i}\right] + \frac{nv^{n+1}}{i}$$

$$= \frac{a_{\overline{n}|}}{i} - \frac{nv^{n+1}}{i} + \frac{nv^{n+1}}{i}$$

$$= \frac{a_{\overline{n}|}}{i} = \frac{1-v^n}{i^2} = \frac{1-v^n}{0.011025}$$

Therefore:

$$0.85003 = 1 - v^n$$

$$1.105^{-n} = 0.14997$$

$$n = -\frac{\ln(0.14997)}{\ln(1.105)} = 19$$
