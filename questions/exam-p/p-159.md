The number of traffic accidents per week at intersection $Q$ has a Poisson distribution with mean 3. The number of traffic accidents per week at intersection $R$ has a Poisson distribution with mean 1.5.

Let $A$ be the probability that the number of accidents at intersection $Q$ exceeds its mean. Let $B$ be the corresponding probability for intersection $R$.

Calculate $B - A$.

- A) 0.00
- B) 0.09
- C) 0.13
- D) 0.19
- E) 0.31

## Explanation

**For $Q \sim \text{Poisson}(3)$:** mean = 3, so $A = P(Q > 3)$.
$$A = 1 - P(Q \leq 3) = 1 - e^{-3}\!\left(1 + 3 + \frac{9}{2} + \frac{27}{6}\right) = 1 - 13e^{-3} \approx 1 - 0.6472 = 0.3528$$

**For $R \sim \text{Poisson}(1.5)$:** mean = 1.5, so $B = P(R > 1.5) = P(R \geq 2)$.
$$B = 1 - P(R = 0) - P(R = 1) = 1 - e^{-1.5} - 1.5e^{-1.5} = 1 - 2.5e^{-1.5} \approx 1 - 0.5578 = 0.4422$$

$$B - A = 0.4422 - 0.3528 = \mathbf{0.0894 \approx 0.09}$$
