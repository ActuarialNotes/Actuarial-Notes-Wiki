The minimum force required to break a particular type of cable is normally distributed with mean 12,432 and standard deviation 25. A random sample of 400 cables of this type is selected.

Calculate the probability that at least 349 of the selected cables will not break under a force of 12,400.

- A) 0.62
- B) 0.67
- C) 0.92
- D) 0.97
- E) 1.00

## Explanation

First, find the probability $p$ that a randomly selected cable will not break under a force of 12,400. Let $Y$ be the breaking force.
$$p = P(Y > 12,400) = P\left(Z > \frac{12,400 - 12,432}{25}\right) = P(Z > -1.28) = P(Z < 1.28)$$
From the standard normal table, $P(Z < 1.28) = 0.8997 \approx 0.9$.

The number of cables, $N$, that will not break has a binomial distribution with $n=400$ and $p=0.9$.
This can be approximated by a normal distribution with:
$$\mu_N = np = 400(0.9) = 360$$
$$\sigma_N^2 = np(1-p) = 400(0.9)(0.1) = 36 \implies \sigma_N = 6$$

With the continuity correction, we want $P(N \ge 349) \approx P(N > 348.5)$:
$$P(N > 348.5) = P\left(Z > \frac{348.5 - 360}{6}\right) = P(Z > -1.9167) = P(Z < 1.9167)$$
From the standard normal table, $P(Z < 1.92) \approx 0.9713$. The closest answer is 0.97.
