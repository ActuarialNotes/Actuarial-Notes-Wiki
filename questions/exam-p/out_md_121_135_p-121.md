An urn contains four fair dice. Two have faces numbered 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6; one has faces numbered 2, 2, 4, 4, 6, and 6; and one has all six faces numbered 6. One of the dice is randomly selected from the urn and rolled. The same die is rolled a second time.

Calculate the probability that a 6 is rolled both times.

- A) 0.174
- B) 0.250
- C) 0.292
- D) 0.380
- E) 0.417

## Explanation

Let event A be the selection of the die with faces (1,2,3,4,5,6), event B be the selection of the die with faces (2,2,4,4,6,6) and event C be the selection of the die with all 6's.

The probability of selecting each die is:
* $P(A) = 2/4 = 1/2$
* $P(B) = 1/4$
* $P(C) = 1/4$

The probability of rolling two 6's given the die is:
* $P(6,6 \mid A) = (1/6)^2 = 1/36$
* $P(6,6 \mid B) = (2/6)^2 = 1/9$
* $P(6,6 \mid C) = (6/6)^2 = 1$

The desired probability is, using the law of total probability:
$$P(6,6) = P(6,6 \mid A)P(A) + P(6,6 \mid B)P(B) + P(6,6 \mid C)P(C)$$
$$= (1/36)(1/2) + (1/9)(1/4) + 1(1/4)$$
$$= 1/72 + 1/36 + 1/4 = 1/72 + 2/72 + 18/72 = 21/72 \approx 0.2917$$
