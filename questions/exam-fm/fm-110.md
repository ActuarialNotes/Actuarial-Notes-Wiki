An insurance company wants to match liabilities of 25,000 payable in one year and 20,000 payable in two years with specific assets. The following assets are currently available:

  i) One-year bond with an annual coupon of 6.75% at par
  ii) Two-year bond with annual coupons of 4.50% at par
  iii) Two-year zero-coupon bond yielding 5.00% annual effective

Calculate the smallest amount the company needs to disburse today to purchase assets that will exactly match these liabilities.

- A) 41,220
- B) 41,390
- C) 41,560
- D) 41,660
- E) 41,750

## Explanation

The strategy is to use the two highest-yielding assets: the one-year bond (yielding 6.75%) and the two-year zero-coupon bond (yielding 5.00%, which is higher than the 4.50% coupon bond).

The one-year bond exactly matches the 25,000 payment at year 1 at a cost of $25{,}000/1.0675$.

The two-year zero-coupon bond matches the 20,000 payment at year 2 at a cost of $20{,}000/1.05^2$.

Total cost:
$$\frac{25{,}000}{1.0675} + \frac{20{,}000}{1.05^2} = 23{,}419.20 + 18{,}140.59 = 41{,}560$$
