A borrower takes out a 50-year loan, to be repaid with payments at the end of each year. The loan payment is 2500 for each of the first 26 years. Thereafter, the payments decrease by 100 per year. Interest on the loan is charged at an annual effective rate of $i$ ($0\% < i < 10\%$). The principal repaid in year 26 is $X$.

Determine the amount of interest paid in the first year.

- A) $Xv^{25}$
- B) $2500v^{25} - Xv^{25}$
- C) $2500 - X$
- D) $2500 - Xv^{25}$
- E) $25Xi$

## Explanation

The outstanding balance at time 25 is the present value of all future payments from time 26 onwards:

$$OB_{25} = 100(Da)_{\overline{25}|} = 100\cdot\frac{25 - a_{\overline{25}|}}{i}$$

The principal repaid in year 26 is:
$$X = 2500 - i \cdot OB_{25} = 2500 - i(100)\frac{25-a_{\overline{25}|}}{i} = 100a_{\overline{25}|}$$

The loan amount (at time 0) is $PV = 2500a_{\overline{25}|} + v^{25}(100)(Da)_{\overline{25}|}$.

Interest paid in year 1 $= i \times PV$. Expanding:

$$i\left[2500a_{\overline{25}|} + v^{25}\cdot 100\cdot\frac{25-a_{\overline{25}|}}{i}\right] = 2500(1-v^{25}) + v^{25}(2500 - X) = 2500 - Xv^{25}$$
