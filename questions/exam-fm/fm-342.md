An investor opens a savings account with no initial deposit that pays a constant 4.5% annual force of interest. The investor deposits 700 at the end of every six-month period for 20 years.

The account balance immediately after the last deposit is $X$.

Determine which of the following is an equation of value that can be used to solve for $X$.

- A) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{39}700e^{0.0225n} = Xe^{-0.9}$
- B) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{39}700e^{-0.0225n} = Xe^{0.9}$
- C) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{39}700e^{-0.0225n} = Xe^{-0.9}$
- D) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{39}700e^{0.0225n} = Xe^{0.9}$
- E) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{40}700e^{-0.0225n} = Xe^{-0.9}$

## Explanation

Semi-annual force of interest $= 0.045/2 = 0.0225$. Deposits are at times $0.5, 1, 1.5, \ldots, 20$ years (i.e., 40 deposits). The balance $X$ is at time 20.

Discounting all cash flows to time 0: PV of $X$ at time 20 is $Xe^{-0.045(20)} = Xe^{-0.9}$.

The $n$th deposit (at time $n/2$ years for $n=1,2,\ldots,40$) has PV $= 700e^{-0.0225n}$.

$$\sum_{n=1}^{40}700e^{-0.0225n} = Xe^{-0.9}$$

This is **answer (E)**.
