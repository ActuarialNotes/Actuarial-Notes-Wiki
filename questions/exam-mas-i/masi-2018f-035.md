You are fitting a linear regression model of the form $y = X\beta + e;\ e_i \sim N(0, \sigma^2)$ and are given the response vector

$$y = [19,\ 32,\ 19,\ 17,\ 13,\ 15]^T$$

and the hat matrix $H = X(X^T X)^{-1} X^T$, whose $5^{\text{th}}$ row is

$$[-0.146,\ -0.038,\ 0.184,\ 0.411,\ 0.443,\ 0.146].$$

Calculate the residual for the $5^{\text{th}}$ observation.

- A) Less than -1
- B) At least -1, but less than 0
- C) At least 0, but less than 1
- D) At least 1, but less than 2
- E) At least 2

## Explanation

The fitted values are $\hat{y} = Hy$, so $\hat{y}_5$ is the 5th row of $H$ dotted with $y$:

$$\hat{y}_5 = (-0.146)(19) + (-0.038)(32) + (0.184)(19) + (0.411)(17) + (0.443)(13) + (0.146)(15)$$
$$= -2.774 - 1.216 + 3.496 + 6.987 + 5.759 + 2.190 = 14.442.$$

The residual is

$$e_5 = y_5 - \hat{y}_5 = 13 - 14.442 = -1.442.$$

This is less than $-1$, so the answer is **A**.
