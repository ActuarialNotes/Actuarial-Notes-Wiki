A couple decides to save money for their child's first year college tuition.

The parents will deposit 1700 $n$ months from today and another 3400 $2n$ months from today.

All deposits earn interest at a nominal annual rate of 7.2%, compounded monthly.

Calculate the maximum integral value of $n$ such that the parents will have accumulated at least 6500 five years from today.

- A) 11
- B) 12
- C) 18
- D) 24
- E) 25

## Explanation

Monthly interest rate $= 0.072/12 = 0.006$. The target of 6500 in 60 months has present value:

$$6500(1.006)^{-60} = 4539.77$$

The equation of value today:

$$4539.77 = 1700(1.006)^{-n} + 3400(1.006)^{-2n}$$

Let $x = 1.006^{-n}$. Then:

$$3400x^2 + 1700x - 4539.77 = 0$$

$$x = \frac{-1700 + \sqrt{1700^2 + 4(3400)(4539.77)}}{2(3400)} = 0.93225$$

$$1.006^{-n} = 0.93225 \implies n = 11.73$$

Maximum integral value $= \mathbf{11}$.
