A study of automobile accidents produced the following data:

| Model year | Proportion of all vehicles | Probability of involvement in an accident |
|---|---|---|
| 2014 | 0.16 | 0.05 |
| 2013 | 0.18 | 0.02 |
| 2012 | 0.20 | 0.03 |
| Other | 0.46 | 0.04 |

An automobile from one of the model years 2014, 2013, and 2012 was involved in an accident.

Calculate the probability that the model year of this automobile is 2014.

- A) 0.22
- B) 0.30
- C) 0.33
- D) 0.45
- E) 0.50

## Explanation

Let $B$, $C$, $D$ = events of 2014, 2013, 2012 model years, $A$ = event of an accident occurring.

We condition only on years 2014, 2013, 2012 (i.e. given $A$ occurred for one of these):

$$P[B|A] = \frac{P[A|B]P[B]}{P[A|B]P[B] + P[A|C]P[C] + P[A|D]P[D]}$$

$$= \frac{(0.05)(0.16)}{(0.05)(0.16) + (0.02)(0.18) + (0.03)(0.20)}$$

$$= \frac{0.0080}{0.0080 + 0.0036 + 0.0060} = \frac{0.0080}{0.0176} \approx 0.45$$
