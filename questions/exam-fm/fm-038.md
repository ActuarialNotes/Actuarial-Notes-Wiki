Joe must pay liabilities of 2000 due one year from now and another 2000 due two years from now. There are two available investments:

**Mortgage I:** Provides cash flows only at time one. The yield rate is 6% per year.

**Mortgage II:** Provides level cash flows at time one and time two. The yield rate is 7% per year.

Calculate the amount Joe must invest in Mortgage I and Mortgage II combined to exactly match the liabilities.

- A) 2600
- B) 2682
- C) 2751
- D) 2825
- E) 3000

## Explanation

**Mortgage II** is the only investment providing a cash flow at time 2. It pays $Y/a_{\overline{2}|0.07} = 0.553092Y$ at each of times 1 and 2. To provide exactly 2000 at time 2:

$$0.553092Y = 2000 \implies Y = 1808.02$$

This also provides $0.553092 \times 1808.02 = 1000$ at time 1.

**Mortgage I** must provide $2000 - 1000 = 1000$ at time 1:

$$X = \frac{1000}{1.06} = 943.40$$

Total investment $= 943.40 + 1808.02 = 2751.42$
