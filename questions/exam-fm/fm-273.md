An investor purchases a 20-year, 1000 face amount bond with semiannual coupons at a price equal to the redemption value of 900. The bond yields an annual nominal rate of 10% convertible semiannually.

After ten years, the investor sells the bond, yielding to the buyer an annual nominal rate of 8% convertible semiannually. The investor uses the proceeds to purchase a 10-year, 1000 face amount bond with redemption value 1100 and semiannual coupons. The yield rate on the new bond is an annual nominal rate of 8% convertible semiannually.

Calculate the semiannual coupon payment for the new bond.

- A) 34
- B) 38
- C) 41
- D) 45
- E) 50

## Explanation

Bond 1: $900 = 1000ra_{\overline{40}|0.05} + 900(1.05)^{-40}$. Solving: $r = 0.045$ (4.5% semiannual), coupon $= 1000(0.045) = 45$.

After 10 years (20 periods), sold at 8% nominal ($j=4\%$):
$$P_{sale} = 45a_{\overline{20}|0.04} + 900(1.04)^{-20} = 45(13.5903) + 900(0.45639) = 611.56 + 410.75 = 1022.31$$

New bond: $1022.31 = Fra_{\overline{20}|0.04} + 1100(1.04)^{-20}$

$= Fra_{\overline{20}|} + 1100(0.45639) = Fr(13.5903) + 501.95$

$Fr = (1022.31 - 501.95)/13.5903 = 520.36/13.5903 = 38.29 \approx 38$
