You are given the following information about an $n$-year bond, where $n > 10$:

  i) The bond pays 8% semiannual coupons and has face amount 1000.
  ii) The bond is redeemable at par.
  iii) The bond is callable at par 5 years after issue or 10 years after issue.
  iv) $P$ is the price to guarantee a yield of 6.8% convertible semiannually and $Q$ is the price to guarantee a yield of 8.8% convertible semiannually.
  v) $|P - Q| = 123.36$.

Calculate $n$.

- A) 11
- B) 15
- C) 19
- D) 22
- E) 26

## Explanation

At 6.8% (< 8% coupon), the bond is a premium bond and the worst call is earliest (year 5): $P = 40a_{\overline{10}|0.034} + 1000(1.034)^{-10} = 1050.15$.

At 8.8% (> 8% coupon), the bond is at discount and the latest call is worst. For $n > 10$: $Q = 40a_{\overline{2n}|0.044} + 1000(1.044)^{-2n}$.

Since $P > Q$ (premium vs discount): $P - Q = 123.36$, so $Q = 926.79$.

$$926.79 = \frac{40}{0.044} + (1.044)^{-2n}\left(1000 - \frac{40}{0.044}\right) = 909.09 + 90.90(1.044)^{-2n}$$

$(1.044)^{-2n} = 17.70/90.90 \implies 2n = -\ln(17.70/90.90)/\ln(1.044) = 38 \implies n = 19$
