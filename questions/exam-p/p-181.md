Losses covered by an insurance policy are modeled by a uniform distribution on the interval $[0, 1000]$. An insurance company reimburses losses in excess of a deductible of 250.

Calculate the difference between the median and the 20th percentile of the insurance company reimbursement, over all losses.

- A) 225
- B) 250
- C) 300
- D) 375
- E) 500

## Explanation

Reimbursement $Y = (X - 250)_+$ where $X \sim \text{Uniform}[0, 1000]$.

**CDF of $Y$:**
$$F_Y(0) = P(Y = 0) = P(X \leq 250) = 0.25$$
$$F_Y(y) = 0.25 + \frac{y}{1000}, \quad 0 < y \leq 750$$

**20th percentile:** Since $F_Y(0) = 0.25 > 0.20$, the 20th percentile is $y_{0.20} = 0$.

**Median:** $F_Y(m) = 0.5 \Rightarrow 0.25 + m/1000 = 0.5 \Rightarrow m = 250$.

**Difference = 250 - 0 = $\mathbf{250}$.**
