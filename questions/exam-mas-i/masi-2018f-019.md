An actuary observes the following 20 losses for a select insurance policy:

$$\{20, 25, 36, 38, 42, 52, 55, 57, 65, 66, 69, 71, 72, 73, 74, 74, 74, 80, 81, 82\}$$

She believes the distribution which fits the data the best is loglogistic with the following probability density:

$$f(x; \gamma, \theta) = \frac{\gamma x^{\gamma-1}}{\theta^\gamma \left[1 + \left(\frac{x}{\theta}\right)^\gamma\right]^2}$$

She uses the $20^{\text{th}}$ and $80^{\text{th}}$ percentiles to estimate the two parameters of this distribution.

Calculate the estimated value of $\theta$.

- A) Less than 20
- B) At least 20, but less than 30
- C) At least 30, but less than 40
- D) At least 40, but less than 50
- E) At least 50

## Explanation

The loglogistic CDF is $F(x) = \dfrac{(x/\theta)^\gamma}{1 + (x/\theta)^\gamma}$, so setting $F(\pi_p) = p$ gives

$$\left(\frac{\pi_p}{\theta}\right)^\gamma = \frac{p}{1-p}.$$

**Smoothed empirical percentiles** (position $(n+1)p$ with $n = 20$): the $20$th percentile is at position $4.2$, giving $\pi_{0.20} = 38 + 0.2(42-38) = 38.8$; the $80$th percentile is at position $16.8$, giving $\pi_{0.80} = 74$.

Set up the two percentile equations:

$$\left(\frac{38.8}{\theta}\right)^\gamma = \frac{0.20}{0.80} = 0.25, \qquad \left(\frac{74}{\theta}\right)^\gamma = \frac{0.80}{0.20} = 4.$$

Dividing eliminates $\theta$: $\left(\frac{74}{38.8}\right)^\gamma = 16 \implies \gamma = \frac{\ln 16}{\ln(1.9072)} = 4.294$.

Then from $\left(\frac{74}{\theta}\right)^\gamma = 4$: $\ \theta = 74 \cdot 4^{-1/4.294} = 74 / 1.381 = 53.6.$

This is at least $50$, so the answer is **E**.
