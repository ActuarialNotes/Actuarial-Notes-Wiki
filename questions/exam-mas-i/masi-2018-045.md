You are given the following statements about time series:

- I. Stochastic trends are characterized by explainable changes in direction.
- II. Deterministic trends are better suited to extrapolation than stochastic trends.
- III. Deterministic trends are typically attributed to high serial correlation with random error.

Determine which of the above statements are true.

- A) I only
- B) II only
- C) III only
- D) I, II and III
- E) The answer is not given by (A), (B), (C), or (D)

## Explanation

**Statement I** — *False.* Stochastic trends exhibit *unexplainable*, random changes in direction; it is *deterministic* trends that have explainable, systematic direction.

**Statement II** — *True.* Deterministic trends follow a fixed functional form, making them more reliable for extrapolation than stochastic trends, whose future path is driven by random shocks.

**Statement III** — *False.* High serial correlation with random error characterizes *stochastic* trends, not deterministic ones.

Only Statement II is true, so the answer is **B**.
