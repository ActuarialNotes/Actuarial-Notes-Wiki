An insurer offers policies for which insured loss amounts $X$ have density function
$$f(x) = \frac{x}{50}, \quad 0 < x < 10$$

Policy 1 has no deductible and a limit of 4. Policy 2 has a deductible of 4 and no limit.

Calculate the absolute value of the difference between the insurer's expected claim payments under Policies 1 and 2.

- A) 0.32
- B) 0.64
- C) 0.79
- D) 0.91
- E) 1.12

## Explanation

$E[X] = \int_0^{10} x^2/50\,dx = [x^3/150]_0^{10} = 1000/150 = 20/3$.

**Policy 1** ($\min(X,4)$):
$$
\begin{align*}
E_1 &= \int_0^4 \frac{x^2}{50}dx + 4 \cdot P(X>4) \\
    &= \frac{64}{150} + 4\!\left(1-\frac{16}{100}\right) \\
    &= \frac{64}{150}+3.36 \\
    &\approx 3.787
\end{align*}
$$

**Policy 2** ($(X-4)_+$): Since $\min(X,4)+(X-4)_+ = X$,

$$
\begin{align*}
E_2 &= E[X] - E_1 \\
    &= \frac{20}{3} - 3.787 \\
    &\approx 2.880
\end{align*}
$$

$$|E_1-E_2| = 3.787-2.880 \approx \mathbf{0.91}$$
