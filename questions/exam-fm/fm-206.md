Bond X and Bond Y are $n$-year bonds with face amount of 10,000 and semiannual coupons, each yielding an annual nominal interest rate of 7% convertible semiannually. Bond X has an annual coupon rate of 6% and redemption value $c$. Bond Y has an annual coupon rate of 5% and redemption value $c + 50$. The price of Bond X exceeds the price of Bond Y by 969.52.

Calculate $n$.

- A) 14
- B) 17
- C) 23
- D) 34
- E) 46

## Explanation

Let $X$ = price of Bond X. Then:
$$X = 10{,}000(0.03)a_{\overline{2n}|0.035} + c(1.035)^{-2n}$$
$$X - 969.52 = 10{,}000(0.025)a_{\overline{2n}|0.035} + (c+50)(1.035)^{-2n}$$

Subtracting:
$$969.52 = 50a_{\overline{2n}|0.035} - 50(1.035)^{-2n}$$
$$969.52 = \frac{50}{0.035}[1-(1.035)^{-2n}] - 50(1.035)^{-2n}$$

Let $w = (1.035)^{-2n}$:
$$969.52 = \frac{50}{0.035}(1-w) - 50w = 1428.57(1-w) - 50w$$
$$969.52 = 1428.57 - 1478.57w$$
$$w = (1428.57-969.52)/1478.57 = 0.310469$$
$$2n = -\ln(0.310469)/\ln(1.035) = 34 \implies n = 17$$
