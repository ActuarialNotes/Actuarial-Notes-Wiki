A policyholder purchases automobile insurance for two years. Define the following events:

$F$ = the policyholder has exactly one accident in year one.

$G$ = the policyholder has one or more accidents in year two.

Determine the number of events from the list below that are the **same** as $F \cap G$.

1. The policyholder has exactly one accident in year one and has more than one accident in year two.
2. The policyholder has at least two accidents during the two-year period.
3. The policyholder has exactly one accident in year one and has at least one accident in year two.
4. The policyholder has exactly one accident in year one and has a total of two or more accidents in the two-year period.
5. The policyholder has exactly one accident in year one and has more accidents in year two than in year one.

- A) None
- B) Exactly one
- C) Exactly two
- D) Exactly three
- E) All

## Explanation

$F \cap G$ = {exactly one accident in year one} $\cap$ {one or more accidents in year two} = exactly one in year 1 AND $\geq 1$ in year 2.

**Event (i):** Exactly one in year 1 AND $>1$ in year 2. This is a **strict subset** of $F \cap G$ (requires $\geq 2$ in year 2). Not equal.

**Event (ii):** At least two accidents total. Could be 2 in year 1 alone, or other combinations. Does not require exactly one in year 1. Not equal.

**Event (iii):** Exactly one in year 1 AND $\geq 1$ in year 2. This is precisely $F \cap G$. **Equal.**

**Event (iv):** Exactly one in year 1 AND total $\geq 2$. Since year 1 has exactly one, total $\geq 2$ requires $\geq 1$ in year 2. This is $F \cap G$. **Equal.**

**Event (v):** Exactly one in year 1 AND more in year 2 than year 1, i.e., $\geq 2$ in year 2. This is a strict subset of $F \cap G$. Not equal.

Events (iii) and (iv) match $F \cap G$: **exactly two**.
