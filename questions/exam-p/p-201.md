A group of 100 patients is tested, one patient at a time, for three risk factors until either all patients are tested or a patient tests positive for more than one risk factor. Each risk factor is positive with probability $p$, independently.

Determine an expression for the probability that exactly $n$ patients are tested, where $n$ is a positive integer less than 100.

- A) $\left[1 - 3p(1-p)^2 - 3p^2(1-p)\right]^{n-1}\left[3p^2(1-p)\right]$
- B) $\left[1 - 3p^2(1-p) - p^3\right]^{n-1}\left[3p^2(1-p)\right]$
- C) $\left[(1-p)^2(1+2p)\right]^{n-1}\left[p^2(3-2p)\right]$
- D) $n\left[(1-p)^2(1+2p)\right]^{n-1}\left[p^2(3-2p)\right]$
- E) $\left[(1-p)^3\right]^{n-1}\left[1-(1-p)^3\right]\left[1-(1-p)^2\right]$

## Explanation

Let $q = P(\leq 1 \text{ factor positive}) = (1-p)^3 + 3p(1-p)^2 = (1-p)^2(1+2p)$ and $s = 1-q = p^2(3-2p)$.

Testing stops at patient $n$ means first $n-1$ had $\leq1$ positive factor, and patient $n$ had $>1$:
$$
\begin{align*}
P(n) &= q^{n-1} \cdot s \\
     &= \left[(1-p)^2(1+2p)\right]^{n-1}\cdot p^2(3-2p)
\end{align*}
$$
