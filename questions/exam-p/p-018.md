An auto insurance company insures drivers of all ages. An actuary compiled the following statistics on the company's insured drivers:

| Age of Driver | Probability of Accident | Portion of Company's Insured Drivers |
|---|---|---|
| 16–20 | 0.06 | 0.08 |
| 21–30 | 0.03 | 0.15 |
| 31–65 | 0.02 | 0.49 |
| 66–99 | 0.04 | 0.28 |

A randomly selected driver that the company insures has an accident.

Calculate the probability that the driver was age 16–20.

- A) 0.13
- B) 0.16
- C) 0.19
- D) 0.23
- E) 0.40

## Explanation

Apply Bayes' Formula. Let $A$ = event of an accident; $B_1, B_2, B_3, B_4$ = events the driver is in age ranges 16–20, 21–30, 31–65, 66–99 respectively.

$$P(B_1 | A) = \frac{P(A|B_1)P(B_1)}{\sum_{i=1}^{4} P(A|B_i)P(B_i)}$$

$$= \frac{(0.06)(0.08)}{(0.06)(0.08) + (0.03)(0.15) + (0.02)(0.49) + (0.04)(0.28)}$$

$$= \frac{0.0048}{0.0048 + 0.0045 + 0.0098 + 0.0112} = \frac{0.0048}{0.0303} \approx 0.1584 \approx 0.16$$
