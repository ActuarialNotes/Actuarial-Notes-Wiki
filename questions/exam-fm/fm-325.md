An investor buys a one-year bond with a face amount of 100 and an annual coupon rate of 6% paid semiannually. The bond is purchased at a discount of 1.50 to yield an annual nominal rate of $j$ convertible semiannually.

Calculate $j$.

- A) 0.022
- B) 0.038
- C) 0.068
- D) 0.076
- E) 0.136

## Explanation

Price $= 100 - 1.50 = 98.5$. Coupons of 3 at $t=0.5$ and $t=1$, redemption 100 at $t=1$.

$$98.5 = 3a_{\overline{2}|j/2} + 100(1+j/2)^{-2}$$

Using financial calculator: $PV=-98.5$, $PMT=3$, $FV=100$, $N=2$, $CPT\ I/Y = 3.7929\% = j/2$, so $j = 0.07586 \approx 0.076$.
