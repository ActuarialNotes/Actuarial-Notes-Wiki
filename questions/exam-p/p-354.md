A discrete random variable $X$ has the following distribution:
$$P[X=1/6] = 1/3, \quad P[X=1/4] = 1/4, \quad P[X=1/3] = 5/12$$

An insurer pays a benefit equal to $2X - 1/2$ if $X \geq 1/4$, and 0 otherwise.

Calculate the expected benefit paid by the insurer.

- A) $0$
- B) $1/72$
- C) $5/72$
- D) $1/12$
- E) $5/6$

## Explanation

At $X=1/6$: $1/6 < 1/4$, so benefit $= 0$.

At $X=1/4$: $2(1/4)-1/2 = 1/2-1/2 = 0$.

At $X=1/3$: $2(1/3)-1/2 = 2/3-1/2 = 1/6$.

$$
\begin{align*}
E[\text{benefit}] &= 0\!\cdot\!\tfrac{1}{3}+0\!\cdot\!\tfrac{1}{4}+\tfrac{1}{6}\!\cdot\!\tfrac{5}{12} \\
                  &= \frac{5}{72}
\end{align*}
$$
