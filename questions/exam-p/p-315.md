The random variable $X_1 = e^{Y_1}$ characterizes an insurer's annual property losses, where $Y_1 \sim N(16, 1.50^2)$. The random variable $X_2 = e^{Y_2}$ characterizes annual liability losses, where $Y_2 \sim N(15, 2^2)$. Property and liability losses are independent.

Calculate the probability that, in a given year, the minimum of the insurer's property and liability losses exceeds $e^{16}$.

- A) 0.126
- B) 0.154
- C) 0.250
- D) 0.309
- E) 0.346

## Explanation

$P(X_1 > e^{16}) = P(Y_1 > 16) = P\!\left(Z > \frac{16-16}{1.5}\right) = P(Z>0) = 0.500$

$P(X_2 > e^{16}) = P(Y_2 > 16) = P\!\left(Z > \frac{16-15}{2}\right) = P(Z>0.5) = 1-\Phi(0.5) \approx 0.3085$

$$P(\min > e^{16}) = 0.500 \times 0.3085 \approx \mathbf{0.154}$$
