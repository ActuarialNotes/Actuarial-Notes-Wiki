# Ontario Auto Insurance Reform: SABS Optionality (July 1, 2026)

## Regulation Overview
Effective July 1, 2026, the province of Ontario has transitioned from a standard mandatory benefits package to a highly flexible, unbundled "à la carte" system. 

## Core Legislative Adjustments
*   **Mandatory Core:** Standard medical, rehabilitation, and attendant care benefits remain legally mandatory in every policy to protect core healthcare access.
*   **Newly Optional Benefits:** All other statutory accident benefits—including **Income Replacement ($400/wk standard)**, **Caregiver**, **Non-Earner ($185/wk)**, **Housekeeping**, **Death**, and **Funeral** coverages—become optional. Policyholders can elect to decline these in writing to reduce premium costs.
*   **The OPCF 47R Endorsement:** Introduced as the official legal rider to execute mid-term adjustments and track opt-out consents.
*   **First Payor Shifts:** Auto insurers now serve as the first payor for medical and rehabilitation claims (excluding prescription drug costs), superseding private or employer-provided extended health care plans.
*   **Targeted Coverage Limits:** Optional coverages apply strictly to the named insured, spouse, dependents, and listed drivers. Non-listed passengers, pedestrians, or e-bike riders struck by a vehicle receive only standard mandatory medical/attendant-care and must pursue civil litigation for income losses.

## Multi-Agent Execution Mandates
*   **Actuarial & Product:** Must isolate pure premium pricing for every individual benefit line so options can be clearly itemized on consumer digital quoting systems.
*   **Underwriting & IT (PAS):** Core policy engines (e.g., Guidewire, Duck Creek) must support the unbundled rating structures, prompt for mandatory written opt-out confirmations, and dynamically generate the OPCF 47R form.
*   **Distribution & Customer Experience (CX):** Directs the immediate use of plain-language comparison scripts. Intermediaries must actively build early awareness, advising clients to audit their existing workplace disability plans before making opt-out choices.
*   **Claims:** Adjusts claim-handling guidelines to immediately check driver logs and policy exclusions post-accident. Triage must verify if the injured claimant was a listed driver or third-party pedestrian before processing optional benefit allocations.
