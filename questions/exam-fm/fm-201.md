You are given the following information about a perpetuity with annual payments:

  i) The first 15 payments are each 2500, with the first payment to be made three years from now.
  ii) Beginning with the 16th payment, each payment is $k$% larger than the previous payment.
  iii) Using an annual effective interest rate of 3.5%, the present value of the perpetuity is 115,000.

Calculate $k$.

- A) 1.66
- B) 1.74
- C) 1.78
- D) 1.83
- E) 1.89

## Explanation

The PV has two parts. The first 15 level payments of 2500 (deferred 2 years as a regular annuity):
$$PV_1 = 2500(1.035)^{-2}a_{\overline{15}|0.035} = 2500(1.035)^{-2}(11.5174) = 26,968$$

The growing perpetuity starts at payment 17 (time $17+2=19$... actually payment 16 is at time $2+16=18$, with first payment $2500(1+k/100)$). The PV of the growing part, valued at time 17:
$$PV_{grow} = \frac{2500(1+k/100)}{0.035-k/100}$$

Discounting to time 0: $PV_{grow}(1.035)^{-17}$. Total:
$$115,000 = 2500(1.035)^{-2}a_{\overline{15}|0.035} + \frac{2500(1+k/100)}{0.035-k/100}(1.035)^{-17}$$

$$115,000 = 26,968 + \frac{2500(1+k/100)}{0.035-k/100}(0.55720)$$

$$\frac{1+k/100}{0.035-k/100} = \frac{88,032}{1392.99} = 63.19$$

$$1+k/100 = 63.19(0.035-k/100) \implies 1+k/100 = 2.2116 - 63.19(k/100)$$

$$64.19(k/100) = 1.2116 \implies k = 1.887 \approx 1.89$$
