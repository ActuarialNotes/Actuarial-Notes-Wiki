Given the following information:

- All policies are annual and written on January 1.
- Rate change effective date is January 1, 2013.
- Rate level is reviewed annually.
- Underwriting guidelines were revised on January 1, 2011, substantially changing the composition of the book of business.

| Accident Year | Reported Loss & ALAE as of June 30, 2012 |
|---|---|
| 2010 | $10,000,000 |
| 2011 | $6,000,000 |
| 2012 | $1,500,000 |

**Selected Reported Loss & ALAE Age-to-Ultimate Factors:**

| Month | 6 | 12 | 18 | 24 | 30 | 36 | 42 | 48 | 54 | 60 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Factor | 6.50 | 2.00 | 1.55 | 1.20 | 1.12 | 1.08 | 1.05 | 1.02 | 1.01 | 1.00 |

**Selected Reported Loss & ALAE Age-to-Ultimate Factors:**

| Calendar Year Ending | Frequency | Severity | Pure Premium | # of Exposures | Annual Frequency Exponential Fit | Annual Severity Exponential Fit | Annual Pure Premium Exponential Fit |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sep 2009 | 0.059 | $50,305 | $1,168 | 8 | 15.9% | 13.3% | 13.0% |
| Dec 2009 | 0.063 | $50,125 | $1,167 | 8 | 18.0% | -1.0% | 14.0% |
| Mar 2010 | 0.062 | $55,500 | $1,271 | 6 | 4.7% | 2.9% | 7.7% |
| Jun 2010 | 0.063 | $21,575 | $1,347 | 8 | 2.7% | 2.9% | 5.6% |
| Sep 2010 | 0.062 | $21,388 | $1,347 | 8 | 2.7% | 2.9% | 5.6% |
| Dec 2010 | 0.059 | $19,803 | $1,234 | 4 | — | — | — |
| Mar 2011 | 0.078 | $19,238 | $1,501 | 8 | — | — | — |
| Jun 2011 | 0.079 | $19,238 | $1,501 | 8 | — | — | — |
| Sep 2011 | 0.082 | $20,083 | $1,645 | 8 | — | — | — |
| Dec 2011 | 0.082 | $20,083 | $1,645 | 8 | — | — | — |
| Mar 2012 | 0.080 | $20,038 | $1,601 | 8 | — | — | — |
| Jun 2012 | 0.082 | $19,950 | $1,636 | 8 | — | — | — |

Calculate the 2010 accident year trended ultimate losses & ALAE to be used in a rate change analysis. Justify any trend selections.

### Answer

$15,282,922

### Explanation

**Step 1 — Loss Development**

The 2010 accident year losses as of June 30, 2012 are at 30 months of development. The age-to-ultimate factor at 30 months is **1.12**.

$$\text{2010 Ultimate Loss \& ALAE} = \$10,000,000 \times 1.12 = \$11,200,000$$

**Step 2 — Two-Step Trend Selection**

Because underwriting guidelines changed substantially on January 1, 2011, the historical trend data contains two distinct periods with different characteristics. A **two-step trend** approach is appropriate to separate the historical (pre-change) period from the future (post-change) period.

Assume 6-month reporting periods for trend period selection. Policies are annual and written on January 1, so:
- Average accident date for 2010 AY = July 1, 2010
- Average written date for future policies under revised rates effective January 1, 2013 = July 1, 2013

**Historical trend period:** Average accident date of data used (center of 8-point fit through Sep 2009 – Jun 2011) ≈ July 1, 2010. End of historical period = April 1, 2012 (center of experience data). Duration = **1.75 years**.

**Projected (future) trend period:** From April 1, 2012 to July 1, 2013 = **1.25 years**.

**Historical trend selections (8-point exponential fits, pre- and post-guideline change mixed):**
- Frequency: **+16.0%** per year (use 8-point fit to capture the full historical mix)
- Severity: **−1.7%** per year (use 8-point fit)

*Rationale:* The 8-point exponential fits incorporate the full historical period including the guideline change and best reflect the overall historical drift. The large positive frequency trend and slightly negative severity trend capture the shift in business mix.

**Future trend selections (4-point exponential fits, post-guideline change only):**
- Frequency: **+4.1%** per year (use 4-point fit on post-2011 data only)
- Severity: **+2.5%** per year (use 4-point fit on post-2011 data only)

*Rationale:* The 4-point fits over the post-guideline-change period (Mar 2011 – Jun 2012) are indicative of future patterns under the new book composition.

**Step 3 — Apply Two-Step Trend**

The combined trend factor is:

$$\text{Historical Trend Factor} = (1.16 \times 0.983)^{1.75} = (1.14)^{1.75}$$

$$\text{Future Trend Factor} = (1.041 \times 1.025)^{1.25} = (1.067)^{1.25}$$

$$\text{Trended Ultimate Loss \& ALAE} = \$11,200,000 \times (1.16 \times 0.983)^{1.75} \times (1.041 \times 1.025)^{1.25}$$

$$= \$11,200,000 \times 1.241 \times 1.084 \approx \$15,282,922$$

### Examiner Report

Only a very small number of candidates received full credit. One of the most popular mistakes was the incorrect trending periods. Very few candidates calculated the trend period correctly. A significant portion of candidates missed the assumption that "All policies are annual and written on January 1" and therefore calculated the total trending period as an incorrect 3.5 years. Another common mistake was the application of one-step trending without any adjustment. Most candidates did not use two-step trending or one-step trending plus a one-time adjustment to account for the underwriting guidelines change.

Regarding the loss development part, most candidates got it correct. A small percentage of candidates misread the ultimate LDFs provided in the question as age-to-age factors. Almost all candidates understood the correct trend factor calculation (freq × sev)^trend period and that the projected ultimate loss is calculated by multiplying the incurred loss by the loss development factor to ultimate and the trend factor. About 10% of all candidates did not attempt the question.
