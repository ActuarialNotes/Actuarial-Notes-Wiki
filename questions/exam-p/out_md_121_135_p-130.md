This year, a medical insurance policyholder has probability 0.70 of having no emergency room visits, 0.85 of having no hospital stays, and 0.61 of having neither emergency room visits nor hospital stays.

Calculate the probability that the policyholder has at least one emergency room visit and at least one hospital stay this year.

- A) 0.045
- B) 0.060
- C) 0.390
- D) 0.667
- E) 0.840

## Explanation

Let $E$ be the event of at least one emergency room visit, and $H$ be the event of at least one hospital stay.
We are given the probabilities of the complements:
* $P(E^c) = 0.70 \implies P(E) = 1 - 0.70 = 0.30$
* $P(H^c) = 0.85 \implies P(H) = 1 - 0.85 = 0.15$
* $P(E^c \cap H^c) = 0.61 \implies P(E \cup H) = 1 - 0.61 = 0.39$

We want to find the probability of both events occurring, $P(E \cap H)$.
Using the inclusion-exclusion principle:
$$P(E \cup H) = P(E) + P(H) - P(E \cap H)$$
$$0.39 = 0.30 + 0.15 - P(E \cap H)$$
$$0.39 = 0.45 - P(E \cap H)$$
$$P(E \cap H) = 0.45 - 0.39 = 0.060$$
