You are given the following term structure of interest rates:

| Length of investment (years) | Spot rate |
|---|---|
| 1 | 7.50% |
| 2 | 8.00% |
| 3 | 8.50% |
| 4 | 9.00% |
| 5 | 9.50% |
| 6 | 10.00% |

Calculate the one-year forward rate, deferred four years, implied by this term structure.

- A) 9.5%
- B) 10.0%
- C) 11.5%
- D) 12.0%
- E) 12.5%

## Explanation

The one-year forward rate deferred four years (${}_1f_4$) satisfies:

$$(1+s_5)^5 = (1+s_4)^4(1+{}_1f_4)$$

$$\frac{1.095^5}{1.090^4} - 1 = \frac{1.57424}{1.41158} - 1 = 0.1153 = 11.5\%$$
