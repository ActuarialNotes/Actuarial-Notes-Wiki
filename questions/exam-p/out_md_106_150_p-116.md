A student takes a multiple-choice test with 40 questions. The probability that the student answers a given question correctly is 0.5, independent of all other questions.

The probability that the student answers more than $N$ questions correctly is greater than 0.10. The probability that the student answers more than $N+1$ questions correctly is less than 0.10.

Calculate $N$ using a normal approximation with the continuity correction.

- A) 23
- B) 25
- C) 32
- D) 33
- E) 35

## Explanation

Let $C$ be the number of correct answers. $C \sim \text{Binomial}(n=40, p=0.5)$.
Mean $\mu = np = 40(0.5) = 20$.
Variance $\sigma^2 = np(1-p) = 40(0.5)(0.5) = 10 \implies \sigma = \sqrt{10}$.

We want to approximate $P(C > N)$ using the normal distribution with continuity correction:
$$P(C > N) = P(C \ge N+1) \approx P\left(Z > \frac{(N+1) - 0.5 - 20}{\sqrt{10}}\right) = P\left(Z > \frac{N + 0.5 - 20}{\sqrt{10}}\right)$$

We need this probability to be $\approx 0.10$. From normal tables, $P(Z > 1.282) = 0.10$.
$$\frac{N - 19.5}{\sqrt{10}} = 1.282$$
$$N = 19.5 + 1.282\sqrt{10} = 19.5 + 1.282(3.162) \approx 19.5 + 4.05 = 23.55$$

Because $N$ must be an integer and the probabilities cross the 0.10 threshold between $N$ and $N+1$, $N$ must be 23. At $N=23$, the probability exceeds 0.10, and at $N=24$, it is less than 0.10.
