A state is starting a lottery game. To enter, a player uses a machine that randomly selects six distinct numbers from the first 30 positive integers. The lottery randomly selects six distinct numbers from the same 30 integers. A winning entry must match the lottery's six numbers exactly. The entry fee is 1, each winning entry receives 500,000, and all other entries receive nothing.

Calculate the probability that the state will lose money, given that 800,000 entries are purchased.

- A) 0.33
- B) 0.39
- C) 0.61
- D) 0.67
- E) 0.74

## Explanation

$P(\text{win}) = 1/\binom{30}{6} = 1/593775$.

With 800,000 entries, the number of winners $W \approx \text{Poisson}(\lambda)$ where $\lambda = 800000/593775 \approx 1.347$.

The state collects 800,000 in revenue. It loses money when total payouts $> 800,000$, i.e., $500,000W > 800,000$, meaning $W \geq 2$.

$$
\begin{align*}
P(W \geq 2) &= 1 - e^{-\lambda}(1 + \lambda) \\
            &= 1 - e^{-1.347}(2.347) \\
            &\approx 1 - (0.2598)(2.347) \\
            &\approx 1 - 0.610 \\
            &= \mathbf{0.390 \approx 0.39}
\end{align*}
$$
