The claim $X$ for a dental insurance policy has distribution: $P[X=0]=0.5$, $P[X=1]=0.2$, $P[X=2]=0.3$. The premium equals 125% of the expected claim amount. Calculate the approximate probability that total claims on 76 independent policies exceed total premiums collected.

- A) 0.02
- B) 0.07
- C) 0.17
- D) 0.25
- E) 0.40

## Explanation

$E[X] = 0.8$, $E[X^2] = 0+0.2+1.2 = 1.4$, $\text{Var}(X) = 1.4-0.64 = 0.76$.

Premium per policy $= 1.25 \times 0.8 = 1.0$. Total premium collected $= 76$.

$S = \sum_{i=1}^{76} X_i$. By CLT: $S \approx N(76(0.8),\; 76(0.76)) = N(60.8,\; 57.76)$.

$$
\begin{align*}
P(S > 76) &= P\!\left(Z > \frac{76-60.8}{\sqrt{57.76}}\right) \\
          &= P(Z > 2.0) \\
          &= 1 - \Phi(2.0) \\
          &\approx 1 - 0.9772 \\
          &= \mathbf{0.0228 \approx 0.02}
\end{align*}
$$
