**Salvage and Subrogation** (S&S) are loss recoveries that reduce an insurer's net ultimate losses: salvage is the value recovered from the sale of damaged property (e.g., totaled vehicles), while subrogation is the recovery of amounts paid from a liable third party (e.g., an at-fault driver).

> $$\text{Net Ultimate}$$
>
> $$= \text{Gross Ultimate} - \text{S\&S Ultimate}$$

- S&S recoveries are developed separately using their own triangles because recovery patterns typically lag behind loss payments — subrogation especially can take years to collect from third parties
- Gross CDFs are applied to gross reported losses; S&S CDFs are applied to reported S&S recoveries; net losses $=$ gross $-$ S&S at the ultimate level
- Salvage is most significant in auto physical damage and property lines; subrogation arises in auto liability, workers compensation (third-party cases), and product liability
- Omitting S&S from the reserve analysis, or developing it using loss factors, overstates net ultimate losses and produces an excess reserve

> [!example]- Net Loss After S&S {Example}
> AY 2023: gross reported losses $= \$1{,}000{,}000$, gross CDF $= 1.200$; reported salvage $= \$80{,}000$ and reported subrogation $= \$40{,}000$, S&S CDF $= 1.250$.
>
> > [!answer]-
> > Gross Ultimate $= 1{,}000{,}000 \times 1.200 = \$1{,}200{,}000$
> >
> > S&S Ultimate $= (80{,}000 + 40{,}000) \times 1.250 = 120{,}000 \times 1.250 = \$150{,}000$
> >
> > Net Ultimate $= 1{,}200{,}000 - 150{,}000 = \$1{,}050{,}000$
> >
> > Net IBNR $= 1{,}050{,}000 - (1{,}000{,}000 - 120{,}000) = 1{,}050{,}000 - 880{,}000 = \$170{,}000$
