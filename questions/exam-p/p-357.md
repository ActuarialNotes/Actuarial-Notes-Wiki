An insurer's losses follow a distribution with density function
$$f(x) = \frac{6}{(1+x)^7}, \quad x > 0$$

The company sells a policy with a deductible of 2 and a maximum covered loss of 6 (maximum benefit of 4).

Calculate the expected benefit paid per loss.

- A) $5.79 \times 10^{-4}$
- B) $8.11 \times 10^{-4}$
- C) $1.07 \times 10^{-3}$
- D) $2.16 \times 10^{-3}$
- E) $4.32 \times 10^{-3}$

## Explanation

Using $E[(X-d)_+] = \int_d^\infty(x-d)\cdot\frac{6}{(x+1)^7}dx$, substitute $u=x+1$:

$$
\begin{align*}
E[(X-d)_+] &= \frac{6}{5(d+1)^5} - \frac{6}{6(d+1)^5} \\
           &= \frac{1}{5(d+1)^5}
\end{align*}
$$

Wait — simplifying: $E[(X-d)_+] = \dfrac{1}{5(d+1)^5}$.

$$
\begin{align*}
E[\text{benefit}] &= E[(X-2)_+]-E[(X-6)_+] \\
                  &= \frac{1}{5(3)^5}-\frac{1}{5(7)^5} \\
                  &= \frac{1}{1215}-\frac{1}{84035} \\
                  &\approx \mathbf{8.11\times10^{-4}}
\end{align*}
$$
