10% type A (Poisson 1), 50% type B (Poisson 2), 40% type C (Poisson 10). $X$ = annual claims. Calculate $\text{Var}(X)$.

- A) 5.10
- B) 16.09
- C) 21.19
- D) 42.10
- E) 47.20

## Explanation

$E[X]=5.1$, $E[\text{Var}(X|T)]=5.1$, $E[\lambda_T^2]=42.1$, $\text{Var}(E[X|T])=42.1-26.01=16.09$.

$\text{Var}(X)=5.1+16.09=\mathbf{21.19}$
