A loan of 50,000 is based on an annual effective interest rate of 6%.

You are given:

  i) The loan is to be repaid with quarterly payments of 2125 and a final drop payment.
  ii) The first payment is due 1.5 years after the loan is taken out.

Calculate the number of payments of 2125 to be made.

- A) 28
- B) 29
- C) 30
- D) 31
- E) 32

## Explanation

Quarterly rate: $i^{(4)}/4 = 1.06^{1/4}-1 = 0.014674$.

The first payment is due 1.5 years = 6 quarters after the loan, so the loan grows for 6 quarters:

$$50{,}000(1.014674)^6 = 54{,}602.93$$

Solving for the number of payments:

$$\begin{align*}
54{,}602.93 &= 2125\,a_{\overline{n}|0.014674} \\
n &= 31.86
\end{align*}$$

So **31 payments** of 2125 (plus a final drop payment).
