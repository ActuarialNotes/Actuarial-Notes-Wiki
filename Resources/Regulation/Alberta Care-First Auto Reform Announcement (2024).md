# Alberta Care-First Auto Reform Announcement (November 2024)

## Overview
Alberta announced a transition to a privately delivered "care-first" auto insurance model targeted for January 2027 — substantially limiting tort litigation for collision injuries in exchange for enhanced, lifetime-where-needed medical and income benefits, while retaining the right to sue at-fault drivers convicted of specified offences.

## Key Details
*   **Benefit-for-Tort Trade:** Enhanced treatment, rehabilitation, and income support replace most bodily-injury lawsuits — Alberta's version of the grand bargain, modeled on study of other Canadian regimes.
*   **Interim Rate Measures:** Pending implementation, the government maintained premium controls for "good drivers" (a capped annual increase regime) following the prior rate pause.
*   **Private Delivery:** Unlike Crown-run no-fault provinces (BC, SK, MB, QC), the care-first system remains delivered by private insurers, preserving the competitive market structure.

## Multi-Agent Execution Mandates
*   **Actuarial & Product:** Complete repricing architecture required — BI severity distributions replaced by structured benefit costing with long-tail care obligations.
*   **Claims:** Buildout of care-coordination and long-duration benefit administration capabilities ahead of 2027.
*   **Legal & Compliance:** Tracking of enabling legislation and regulations defining litigation carve-outs, benefit schedules, and dispute resolution design.
