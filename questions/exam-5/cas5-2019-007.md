Given the following data as of December 31, 2018:

| Accident Year | Reported Loss and ALAE (\$000s) | Calendar Year | Earned Premium (\$000s) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2016 | 2,000 | 2016 | 4,600 |
| 2017 | 1,750 | 2017 | 5,100 |
| 2018 | 800 | 2018 | 5,800 |

| Development Age to Ultimate | Selected Cumulative Development Factors |
|---|---|
| 48 | 1.00 |
| 36 | 1.46 |
| 24 | 2.08 |
| 12 | 4.90 |

| | |
|---|---|
| 65% | Expected loss and ALAE ratio |
| 4% | Annual loss and ALAE trend |
| 6% | Variable expense ratio |
| 5% | Fixed expense ratio as a % of loss and ALAE |
| 8% | ULAE provision as a % of loss and ALAE |
| 5% | Profit and contingencies provision |
| 26% | ALAE provision as a % of loss and ALAE (already in loss data) |
| 8% | Complement of credibility of historical experience |
| 70% | Credibility of historical experience |

Rate change history:
- −2% Rate change effective January 1, 2016
- +3% Rate change effective July 1, 2018

- All policies are annual.
- Exposures are written evenly throughout each calendar year.
- New rates will be in effect for one year starting January 1, 2020.

## Part a (0.5 points)

Calculate the ultimate loss and ALAE for each accident year using the reported Bornhuetter-Ferguson technique.

### Explanation

The B-F formula is:

$$\text{B-F Ultimate} = \text{Reported Loss} + \left(1 - \frac{1}{\text{CDF to Ult}}\right) \times \text{Earned Premium} \times \text{Expected Loss Ratio}$$

For AY 2016 (age 36, CDF = 1.46):

$$2{,}000 + \left(1 - \frac{1}{1.46}\right) \times 4{,}600 \times 0.65 = 2{,}000 + 0.3151 \times 2{,}990 = 2{,}000 + 942 = 2{,}942$$

For AY 2017 (age 24, CDF = 2.08):

$$1{,}750 + \left(1 - \frac{1}{2.08}\right) \times 5{,}100 \times 0.65 = 1{,}750 + 0.5192 \times 3{,}315 = 1{,}750 + 1{,}721 = 3{,}471$$

For AY 2018 (age 12, CDF = 4.90):

$$800 + \left(1 - \frac{1}{4.90}\right) \times 5{,}800 \times 0.65 = 800 + 0.7959 \times 3{,}770 = 800 + 3{,}001 = 3{,}801$$

### Examiner Report

Candidates were expected to calculate ultimate losses using the Bornhuetter-Ferguson technique.

A common mistake was using trended or on-level premium in place of earned premium in the B-F formula.

## Part b (3.5 points)

Calculate the credibility-weighted indicated rate change using the latest three accident years.

### Explanation

**Step 1: On-Level Factors**

Rate change history:
- January 1, 2016: −2% change → rate level index drops to 0.98 (relative to pre-2016 = 1.00)
- July 1, 2018: +3% change → rate level index rises to $0.98 \times 1.03 = 1.0094$

Current rate level = 1.0094.

Average rate level by calendar year (using the parallelogram method, policies written evenly throughout the year, annual policies):

**CY 2016:**
- First half of year: rate index = 1.00 (area = 0.5 × 0.5 = 0.25)
- Second half of year: rate index = 0.98 (area = 0.5 × 0.5 = 0.25, plus 0.5 carried into 2017)

Actually using the parallelogram: CY 2016 average rate level:

$$\bar{RL}_{2016} = \frac{1.00 + 0.98}{2} = 0.99$$

$$\text{OLF}_{2016} = \frac{1.0094}{0.99} = 1.020$$

**CY 2017:**
- Entire year at rate index = 0.98

$$\bar{RL}_{2017} = 0.98 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \text{OLF}_{2017} = \frac{1.0094}{0.98} = 1.030$$

**CY 2018:**
- Jan 1–Jul 1, 2018 (7/8 of the earned premium exposure): rate = 0.98
- Jul 1, 2018 onward (overlapping area): rate = 1.0094

$$\bar{RL}_{2018} = \frac{7}{8}(0.98) + \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{2} \times 2 \times (1.0094 - 0.98) \approx 0.9837$$

$$\text{OLF}_{2018} = \frac{1.0094}{0.9837} = 1.026$$

**Step 2: On-Level Earned Premium**

| Calendar Year | Earned Premium | OLF | On-Level EP |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2016 | 4,600 | 1.020 | 4,692 |
| 2017 | 5,100 | 1.030 | 5,253 |
| 2018 | 5,800 | 1.026 | 5,951 |

**Step 3: Premium Trend**

Annual loss and ALAE trend = 4%. New rates effective January 1, 2020, so average future loss date = January 1, 2021.

Average written date of future policies = July 1, 2020; average loss date = January 1, 2021.

Average written (and loss) dates of historical earned premium:
- CY 2016 EP: average written date ≈ January 1, 2016
- CY 2017 EP: average written date ≈ January 1, 2017
- CY 2018 EP: average written date ≈ January 1, 2018

Premium trend period (to future average written date July 1, 2020):
- CY 2016: 4.5 years
- CY 2017: 3.5 years
- CY 2018: 2.5 years

$$\text{Trended OLEP}_{2016} = 4{,}692 \times 1.03^{4.5} = 5{,}360$$

$$\text{Trended OLEP}_{2017} = 5{,}253 \times 1.03^{3.5} = 5{,}826$$

$$\text{Trended OLEP}_{2018} = 5{,}951 \times 1.03^{2.5} = 6{,}407$$

**Step 4: Loss Trend**

Average accident dates by AY:
- AY 2016: July 1, 2016
- AY 2017: July 1, 2017
- AY 2018: July 1, 2018

Loss trend period to future average loss date January 1, 2021:
- AY 2016: 4.5 years
- AY 2017: 3.5 years
- AY 2018: 2.5 years

Including ULAE provision (8% of loss and ALAE):

$$\text{Trended Ult L\&ALAE}_{2016} = 2{,}942 \times 1.04^{4.5} \times 1.08 = 3{,}791$$

$$\text{Trended Ult L\&ALAE}_{2017} = 3{,}471 \times 1.04^{3.5} \times 1.08 = 4{,}301$$

$$\text{Trended Ult L\&ALAE}_{2018} = 3{,}801 \times 1.04^{2.5} \times 1.08 = 4{,}528$$

**Step 5: Loss Ratios**

$$\text{LR}_{2016} = \frac{3{,}791}{5{,}360} = 70.76\%$$

$$\text{LR}_{2017} = \frac{4{,}301}{5{,}826} = 73.82\%$$

$$\text{LR}_{2018} = \frac{4{,}528}{6{,}407} = 70.66\%$$

$$\bar{LR} = \text{weighted average} \approx 71.74\%$$

**Step 6: Overall Indication (Loss Ratio Method)**

$$\text{Indicated Change} = \frac{\bar{LR} + FE\%}{1 - V - Q} - 1 = \frac{0.7174 + 0.05}{1 - 0.06 - 0.05} - 1 = \frac{0.7674}{0.89} - 1 = 12.6\%$$

(Note: $V = 6\%$ variable expenses, $Q = 5\%$ profit and contingencies, $FE\% = 5\%$ fixed expenses as % of loss and ALAE; ULAE already included in trended losses.)

**Step 7: Credibility-Weighted Indicated Change**

$$\text{Cred Wtd Ind Chg} = 0.70 \times 12.6\% + 0.30 \times 8\% = 8.82\% + 2.4\% = 11.22\%$$

### Examiner Report

Candidates were expected to determine the appropriate trend period for earned premium and ultimate losses, incorporate on-leveling to determine on-level trended earned premium, calculate an overall indication incorporating ULAE and expenses using the loss ratio method, then adjust for credibility and the credibility complement.

Common mistakes included:
- Incorrectly calculating the on-level factors, particularly for CY 2018 (where the rate change occurs mid-year).
- Not using the appropriate trend period for losses and premium.
- Using trended or on-level premium in B-F (instead of actual earned premium) — this error in Part a carried forward.

## Part c (0.75 points)

Briefly describe a disadvantage of using a competitor rate change filing as the complement of credibility and justify a more suitable alternative.

### Explanation

**Disadvantage of competitor rate change as complement:**

A competitor may have a different mix of business, policy limits, deductibles, underwriting guidelines, or capacity constraints. As a result, their rate change indication may not be directly comparable to the company's own experience. The competitor's indicated change could be biased relative to the company's actual required rate level change.

**More suitable alternative:**

Use the company's own regional or countrywide indication for the same line of business. This is more appropriate because the company's own data has a similar mix of business and reflects the same underwriting practices. Another alternative is **trended present rates** — this relies on the company's own experience and is directly applicable to the ratemaking exercise.

A third alternative is the **Harwayne method** (geographic complement), which is independent and unbiased by the competitor's different book characteristics.

### Examiner Report

Candidates were expected to explain a disadvantage of the given credibility complement and justify an alternative complement of credibility.

Common mistakes included:
- Providing an incomplete disadvantage for the given credibility complement (e.g., just saying "biased" without explaining why).
- Not providing justification for why the proposed alternative is more suitable.
