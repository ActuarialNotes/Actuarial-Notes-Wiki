**Underwriting year** (also called policy year or writing year) is a data organization method where all policies incepting in the same calendar year are grouped together, with all associated losses and premiums included regardless of when claims occurred or were paid.

- Similar to [[Policy Year|policy year]] but sometimes applied specifically in reinsurance contexts where the binding/underwriting date determines the grouping
- Underwriting year data takes the longest to develop fully — the actuary must wait for all policies to expire and all resulting claims to be reported and settled before the cohort closes
- Provides the most complete picture of cohort profitability because premiums and losses are perfectly matched to the same set of policies; contrast with [[Accident Year|accident year]], which mixes policies from multiple underwriting years
- Because development is slowest on an underwriting year basis, large [[IBNR]] reserves are held for many years; this is especially true for [[Long Tail Lines|long-tail lines]]

> [!example]- Underwriting Year vs. Accident Year Data {Example}
> A general liability policy is written on July 1, 2022 (underwriting year 2022). A claim occurs on March 15, 2023 and is reported in June 2023. Where does this claim appear?
>
> > [!answer]-
> > - **Underwriting year basis**: the claim appears in UY 2022, because the policy was written in 2022
> > - **Accident year basis**: the claim appears in AY 2023, because the loss occurred in 2023
> >
> > This illustrates why underwriting year triangles take longer to develop — they must wait for all claims under the 2022 policy cohort, even those occurring years after policy inception.
