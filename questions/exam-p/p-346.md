$A$, $B$, and $C$ are events such that $A$ and $C$ are mutually exclusive and $B$ and $C$ are mutually exclusive. The probability that at least one of the three events occurs is 0.90. The probability that exactly two of the three events occur is 0.06. The probability that exactly one of the events $A$ or $B$ occurs is 0.38.

Calculate $P[C]$.

- A) 0.32
- B) 0.46
- C) 0.52
- D) 0.56
- E) 0.58

## Explanation

Since $A \cap C = B \cap C = \emptyset$, the only "exactly two" event possible is $A \cap B$.

$$P(A \cap B) = 0.06$$

From $P(A \cup B \cup C) = 0.90$:

$$P(A)+P(B)+P(C)-P(A\cap B) = 0.90$$

$$P(A)+P(B)+P(C) = 0.96$$

From "exactly one of A or B": $P(A)+P(B)-2P(A\cap B) = 0.38$:

$$P(A)+P(B) = 0.38+0.12 = 0.50$$

$$P(C) = 0.96-0.50 = \mathbf{0.46}$$
