Which of the following is an expression for the present value of a perpetuity with annual payments of 1, 2, 3, …, where the first payment will be made at the end of $n$ years, using an annual effective interest rate of $i$?

- A) $\dfrac{\ddot{a}_{\overline{n}|} - nv^n}{i}$
- B) $\dfrac{n - a_{\overline{n}|}}{i}$
- C) $\dfrac{v^n}{d}$
- D) $\dfrac{v^n}{d^2}$
- E) $\dfrac{v^n}{di}$

## Explanation

The present value is:

$$\begin{align*}
PV &= v^{n-1}/i + v^n/i + v^{n+1}/i + \cdots \\
&= \frac{v^{n-2}}{i}(v + v^2 + v^3 + \cdots) \\
&= \frac{v^{n-2}}{i} \cdot \frac{v}{1-v} \\
&= \frac{v^{n-1}}{i} \cdot \frac{1}{d}
\end{align*}$$

Since $vi = \dfrac{i}{1+i} = d$, we have $v^{n-1}/d = v^n \cdot (1+i)/d$ and:

$$PV = \frac{v^n}{di}= \frac{v^n}{d^2}$$

because $d = iv$ so $di = d \cdot (d/v) = d^2/v$ and $v \cdot di = d^2$.
