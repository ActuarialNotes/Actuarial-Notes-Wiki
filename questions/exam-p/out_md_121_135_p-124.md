In a given region, the number of tornadoes in a one-week period is modeled by a Poisson distribution with mean 2. The numbers of tornadoes in different weeks are mutually independent.

Calculate the probability that fewer than four tornadoes occur in a three-week period.

- A) 0.13
- B) 0.15
- C) 0.29
- D) 0.43
- E) 0.86

## Explanation

The sum of independent Poisson variables is also Poisson, with the means added.
Thus the number of tornadoes in a three-week period is Poisson with a mean of $\lambda = 3 \times 2 = 6$.

We want the probability of fewer than four tornadoes ($X < 4$, so $X = 0, 1, 2, 3$):
$$P(X < 4) = P(X=0) + P(X=1) + P(X=2) + P(X=3)$$
$$= e^{-6}\left(\frac{6^0}{0!} + \frac{6^1}{1!} + \frac{6^2}{2!} + \frac{6^3}{3!}\right)$$
$$= e^{-6} \left( 1 + 6 + \frac{36}{2} + \frac{216}{6} \right) = e^{-6} (1 + 6 + 18 + 36) = 61e^{-6} \approx 0.1512$$
