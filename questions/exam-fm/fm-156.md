Graham is the beneficiary of an annuity due. At an annual effective interest rate of 5%, the present value of payments is 123,000 and the modified duration is $D_{MOD}$.

Tyler uses the first-order Macaulay approximation to estimate the present value of Graham's annuity due at an annual effective interest rate of 5.4%. Tyler estimates the present value to be 121,212.

Calculate $D_{MOD}$, the modified duration of Graham's annuity at 5%.

- A) 3.67
- B) 3.75
- C) 3.85
- D) 3.95
- E) 4.04

## Explanation

The first-order Macaulay approximation:
$$121{,}212 = 123{,}000\left(\frac{1.05}{1.054}\right)^{D_{MAC}}$$

$$\frac{121{,}212}{123{,}000} = \left(\frac{1.05}{1.054}\right)^{D_{MAC}}$$

$$\ln(121{,}212/123{,}000) = D_{MAC} \cdot \ln(1.05/1.054)$$

$$D_{MAC} = \frac{\ln(0.98547)}{\ln(0.99620)} = \frac{-0.01465}{-0.003808} = 3.848$$

$$D_{MOD} = D_{MAC}/(1.05) = 3.848/1.05 = 3.665 \approx 3.67$$
