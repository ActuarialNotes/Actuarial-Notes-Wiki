Let $S$ be the accumulated value of 1000 invested for two years at a nominal annual rate of discount $d$ convertible semiannually, which is equivalent to an annual effective interest rate of $i$.

Let $T$ be the accumulated value of 1000 invested for one year at a nominal annual rate of discount $d$ convertible quarterly.

$$S/T = (39/38)^4$$

Calculate $i$.

- A) 10.0%
- B) 10.3%
- C) 10.8%
- D) 10.9%
- E) 11.1%

## Explanation

Express $S$ and $T$ in terms of $d$:

$$S = 1000(1-d/2)^{-4}, \quad T = 1000(1-d/4)^{-4}$$

$$\frac{S}{T} = \frac{(1-d/2)^{-4}}{(1-d/4)^{-4}} = \left(\frac{1-d/4}{1-d/2}\right)^4 = \left(\frac{39}{38}\right)^4$$

$$\frac{1-d/4}{1-d/2} = \frac{39}{38} \implies \frac{1-d/2}{1-d/4} = \frac{38}{39}$$

$$39 - 39(d/2) = 38 - 38(d/4) \implies d(39/2 - 38/4) = 1 \implies d = \frac{1}{19.5-9.5} = 0.1$$

$$1+i = (1-d/2)^{-2} = (0.95)^{-2} = 1.108 \implies i = 10.8\%$$
