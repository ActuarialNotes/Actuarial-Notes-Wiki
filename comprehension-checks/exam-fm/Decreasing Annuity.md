A decreasing annuity pays 5, 4, 3, 2, 1 at the ends of years 1–5, while an increasing one pays 1, 2, 3, 4, 5. Which has the larger present value?

- A) The decreasing annuity, because its larger payments arrive earlier
- B) The increasing annuity, because its largest payments come last
- C) They are equal, since both total 15
- D) It cannot be determined without the interest rate
