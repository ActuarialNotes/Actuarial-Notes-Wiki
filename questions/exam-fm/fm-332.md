An investor wants to accumulate 150,000 to purchase a business in ten years. The investor deposits 14,000 into an account at the beginning of each of years one through five. The investor deposits an additional amount $X$ at the beginning of years four and five to meet this goal. The account earns interest at an annual effective rate of 8%.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 2,113
- B) 2,592
- C) 5,958
- D) 9,595
- E) 11,574

## Explanation

AV of base deposits (14,000 at beginning of years 1–5):

$$14{,}000\ddot{s}_{\overline{5}|0.08}(1.08)^5 = 14{,}000(5.8666)(1.46933) = 14{,}000(8.6231) = 120{,}723$$

AV of extra $X$ deposits (at beginning of years 4 and 5, growing 7 and 6 years):

$$X\ddot{s}_{\overline{2}|0.08}(1.08)^5 = X(2.08)(1.08)^5(1.08)... $$

Using the solution:

$$
\begin{align*}
(14{,}000\,\ddot{s}_{\overline{5}|} + X\,\ddot{s}_{\overline{2}|})(1.08)^5 &= 150{,}000 \\
(88{,}703 + 2.2464X)(1.46933) &= 150{,}000 \\
88{,}703 + 2.2464X &= 102{,}087 \\
2.2464X &= 13{,}384 \\
X &= 5958
\end{align*}
$$
