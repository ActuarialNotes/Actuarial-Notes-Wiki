The lifespan, in years, of a certain computer is exponentially distributed. The probability that its lifespan exceeds four years is 0.30.

Let $f(x)$ represent the density function of the computer's lifespan, in years, for $x > 0$.

Determine which of the following is an expression for $f(x)$.

- A) $1 - (0.3)^{-x/4}$
- B) $1 - (0.7)^{x/4}$
- C) $1 - (0.3)^{x/4}$
- D) $-\frac{\ln 0.7}{4}(0.7)^{x/4}$
- E) $-\frac{\ln 0.3}{4}(0.3)^{x/4}$

## Explanation

The cumulative distribution function for an exponential distribution is $F(x) = 1 - e^{-\lambda x}$, for $x > 0$.
The probability that the lifespan exceeds 4 years is:
$$P(X > 4) = 1 - F(4) = e^{-4\lambda} = 0.30$$
Thus, $-4\lambda = \ln(0.30) \implies \lambda = -\frac{\ln(0.30)}{4}$.

The probability density function is $f(x) = \lambda e^{-\lambda x}$.
We know $e^{-4\lambda} = 0.30 \implies e^{-\lambda} = (0.30)^{1/4}$.
Thus, $e^{-\lambda x} = ((0.30)^{1/4})^x = (0.30)^{x/4}$.

Substituting $\lambda$ into the density function:
$$f(x) = \left(-\frac{\ln 0.30}{4}\right) (0.30)^{x/4}$$
