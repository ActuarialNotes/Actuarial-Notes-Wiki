Two fair dice are tossed. One die is red and one die is green.

Calculate the probability that the sum of the numbers on the two dice is an odd number given that the number that shows on the red die is larger than the number that shows on the green die.

- A) 1/4
- B) 5/12
- C) 3/7
- D) 1/2
- E) 3/5

## Explanation

Of the 36 possible pairs, 6 have equal numbers. Of the remaining 30 pairs, by symmetry, half must have the red number larger than the green number.
So there are 15 pairs where the red die is strictly larger than the green die.

We need to find how many of these 15 pairs have an odd sum. An odd sum means one number is even and the other is odd.
The 15 pairs where Red > Green are:
* Red=2: Green=1 (sum=3, odd)
* Red=3: Green=1, 2 (sum=4, 5 - one odd)
* Red=4: Green=1, 2, 3 (sum=5, 6, 7 - two odd)
* Red=5: Green=1, 2, 3, 4 (sum=6, 7, 8, 9 - two odd)
* Red=6: Green=1, 2, 3, 4, 5 (sum=7, 8, 9, 10, 11 - three odd)

Counting the odd sums:
* Red=2: 1
* Red=3: 1
* Red=4: 2
* Red=5: 2
* Red=6: 3
Total odd sums = $1 + 1 + 2 + 2 + 3 = 9$.

The conditional probability is $9/15 = 3/5$.
