Which of the following is an expression for the present value of a perpetuity with annual payments of 1, 2, 3, …, where the first payment will be made at the end of $n$ years, using an annual effective interest rate of $i$?

- A) $\dfrac{\ddot{a}_{\overline{n}|} - nv^n}{i}$
- B) $\dfrac{n - a_{\overline{n}|}}{i}$
- C) $\dfrac{v^n}{d}$
- D) $\dfrac{v^n}{d^2}$
- E) $\dfrac{v^n}{di}$

## Explanation

The payments are $1, 2, 3, \ldots$ at times $n, n+1, n+2, \ldots$ The present value of a standard increasing perpetuity-immediate (payments $1, 2, 3, \ldots$ starting at time 1) is $1/id$. Deferring by $n-1$ periods gives:

$$PV = v^{n-1} \cdot \frac{1}{id}$$

Since $d = iv$ (because $d = i/(1+i) = iv$):

$$PV = \frac{v^{n-1}}{id} = \frac{v^{n-1}}{i \cdot iv} = \frac{v^{n-2}}{i^2}$$

Alternatively, using $id = (iv)d = d^2/v$:

$$PV = \frac{v^{n-1}}{id} = \frac{v^n}{v \cdot id} = \frac{v^n}{d^2}$$

since $v \cdot id = vi \cdot d = d \cdot d = d^2$.
