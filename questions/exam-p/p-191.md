Let $A$, $B$, and $C$ be events such that $P[A] = 0.2$, $P[B] = 0.1$, and $P[C] = 0.3$. The events $A$ and $B$ are independent, the events $B$ and $C$ are independent, and the events $A$ and $C$ are mutually exclusive.

Calculate $P[A \cup B \cup C]$.

- A) 0.496
- B) 0.540
- C) 0.544
- D) 0.550
- E) 0.600

## Explanation

From the given conditions:
$$
\begin{align*}
P(A \cap B) &= P(A)P(B) \\
            &= 0.02, \quad P(B \cap C) = P(B)P(C) = 0.03, \quad P(A \cap C) = 0
\end{align*}
$$
$$P(A \cap B \cap C) = 0 \quad (\text{since } A \cap C = \emptyset)$$

By inclusion-exclusion:
$$
\begin{align*}
P(A \cup B \cup C) &= 0.2 + 0.1 + 0.3 - 0.02 - 0.03 - 0 + 0 \\
                   &= \mathbf{0.550}
\end{align*}
$$
