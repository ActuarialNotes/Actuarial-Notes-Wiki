Given the following as of December 31, 2018:

| | |
|---|---|
| 2,500 | Earned premium (\$000s) |
| 60% | Expected claims ratio |
| 1,285 | Reported claims at 12 months (\$000s) |
| 1.385 | Reported age-to-ultimate factor at 12 months |
| 625 | Paid claims at 12 months (\$000s) |

- Tort reform is passed on January 1, 2019, with an expected reduction of 20% on future claim payments.

Determine the ultimate claims using the following techniques incorporating tort reform:

i. Reported development technique.

ii. Reported Bornhuetter-Ferguson technique.

## Part a — Reported Development Technique (0.625 points)

### Answer
1549

### Explanation
The tort reform passed January 1, 2019 reduces **future** claim payments by 20%. Claims already paid (\$625K) are unaffected. The unpaid claims (both case reserves and IBNR) are reduced by 20%.

**Without tort reform:**

$$\text{Ult (no tort)} = \text{Reported} \times LDF = 1{,}285 \times 1.385 = 1{,}779.725$$

**Total unpaid without tort reform:**

$$\text{Unpaid} = \text{Ult (no tort)} - \text{Paid} = 1{,}779.725 - 625 = 1{,}154.725$$

This unpaid amount includes both case reserves (reported but unpaid: $1{,}285 - 625 = 660$) and pure IBNR ($1{,}779.725 - 1{,}285 = 494.725$). Both components represent **future** payments subject to the tort reduction.

**After 20% tort reform reduction:**

$$\text{Adjusted Unpaid} = 1{,}154.725 \times 0.80 = 923.78$$

$$\text{Ult (with tort)} = \text{Paid} + \text{Adjusted Unpaid} = 625 + 923.78 = 1{,}548.78 \approx 1{,}549$$

Alternatively:

$$\text{Ult} = 1{,}285 \times (1.385 - 1.0) \times 0.80 + 0.80 \times (1{,}285 - 625) + 625 = 395.78 + 528 + 625 = 1{,}548.78$$

### Examiner Report
Candidates were expected to adjust the reported development technique for a change in the legal environment.

Common mistakes included:
- Not reducing IBNR for the tort reform
- Reducing the paid losses by the tort reform (there would be no impact to losses already paid before the tort reform)
- Not reducing the case reserves for the tort reform
- Not properly applying the development technique formula

## Part b — Reported Bornhuetter-Ferguson Technique (0.625 points)

### Answer
1487

### Explanation
Under the BF technique, the expected IBNR is:

$$\text{BF IBNR (no tort)} = ECR \times EP \times \left(1 - \frac{1}{LDF}\right) = 60\% \times 2{,}500 \times \left(1 - \frac{1}{1.385}\right) = 1{,}500 \times 0.2779 = 416.85$$

Case reserves (unreduced by tort reform since they are already-reported but unpaid):

$$\text{Case Reserves} = \text{Reported} - \text{Paid} = 1{,}285 - 625 = 660$$

Total future payments before tort reform:

$$\text{Unpaid} = \text{Case Reserves} + \text{BF IBNR} = 660 + 416.85 = 1{,}076.85$$

Apply the 20% tort reform reduction to all future payments:

$$\text{Adjusted Unpaid} = 1{,}076.85 \times 0.80 = 861.48$$

$$\text{Ult (BF with tort)} = \text{Paid} + \text{Adjusted Unpaid} = 625 + 861.48 = 1{,}486.48 \approx 1{,}487$$

### Examiner Report
Candidates were expected to adjust the Bornhuetter-Ferguson technique for a change in the legal environment.

Common mistakes included:
- Not reducing IBNR for the tort reform in the BF technique
- Reducing the paid losses by the tort reform (there would be no impact to losses already paid)
- Not reducing the case reserves for the tort reform
- Not properly applying the Bornhuetter-Ferguson technique formula
