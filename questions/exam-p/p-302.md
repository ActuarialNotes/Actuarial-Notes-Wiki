The number of automobile accidents on any day in a city has a Poisson distribution with mean 4. The number of accidents on a given day is independent of the number on any other day.

Calculate the probability that at most one accident occurs in a three-day period.

- A) $13e^{-12}$
- B) $72e^{-12}$
- C) $85e^{-12}$
- D) $5e^{-4}$
- E) $13e^{-4}$

## Explanation

Total in 3 days: $T \sim \text{Poisson}(12)$.

$$
\begin{align*}
P(T \leq 1) &= P(T=0)+P(T=1) \\
            &= e^{-12}+12e^{-12} \\
            &= \mathbf{13e^{-12}}
\end{align*}
$$
