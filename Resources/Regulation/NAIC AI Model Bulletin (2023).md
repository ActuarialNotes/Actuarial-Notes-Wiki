# NAIC AI Model Bulletin (December 2023)

## Overview
The NAIC adopted a model bulletin setting regulator expectations for insurers' use of AI systems, anchored to existing unfair trade practice, claims, and rating laws. A majority of US states adopted versions of the bulletin during 2024–2025, making it the de facto US baseline for insurance AI governance.

## Key Details
*   **AIS Program Requirement:** Insurers expected to maintain a written AI Systems program covering governance, risk management, and internal controls proportionate to risk of adverse consumer outcomes.
*   **Lifecycle Controls:** Expectations span model development, validation, deployment, monitoring, and documentation — including testing for unfair discrimination.
*   **Third-Party Accountability:** Insurers remain responsible for AI and data acquired from vendors; due-diligence and contractual audit rights expected.
*   **Market Conduct Hook:** AIS documentation is examinable in market conduct reviews, giving the bulletin enforcement teeth without new statute.

## Multi-Agent Execution Mandates
*   **Analytics & Actuarial:** Model inventories, bias testing protocols, and documentation standards formalized for pricing, underwriting, claims, and fraud models.
*   **Compliance:** State-by-state tracking of bulletin adoptions and variations (e.g., Colorado's quantitative testing regime for life insurers under SB 21-169 regulations).
*   **IT & Vendors:** Vendor AI contracts re-papered for transparency, audit, and validation support obligations.
