On his 65th birthday, an investor withdrew an amount $P$ from a fund of 1,000,000 and withdrew the same amount on each successive birthday. On the date of his 82nd birthday, the fund was again equal to 1,000,000 after the withdrawal.

The fund earns an annual effective interest rate of 10%.

Calculate $P$.

- A) 81,655
- B) 88,915
- C) 90,909
- D) 98,879
- E) 109,729

## Explanation

Withdrawals occur at ages 65, 66, ..., 82 — that is 18 withdrawals. The fund starts at 1,000,000 on birthday 65 (before withdrawal) and returns to 1,000,000 on birthday 82 (after withdrawal).

The equation of value (at time 17, i.e., birthday 82):
$$1{,}000{,}000(1.10)^{17} - Ps_{\overline{18}|0.10} = 1{,}000{,}000$$

$$5{,}054{,}470.28 - P(45.5992) = 1{,}000{,}000$$

$$P = 4{,}054{,}470.28 / 45.5992 = 88{,}915.43$$
