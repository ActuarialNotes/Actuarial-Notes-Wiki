**Commercial Lines Rating** encompasses the mechanisms used to individualize insurance rates for commercial risks beyond the class manual rate, incorporating the insured's own experience and individual risk characteristics to achieve equitable pricing.

- The three primary individualization mechanisms are [[Experience Rating]] (statistical modification based on historical loss experience), [[Schedule Rating]] (judgmental credits/debits for risk characteristics), and [[Retrospective Rating]] (premium adjustment after the policy period based on actual losses)
- Commercial risks are large enough that their own experience carries statistical credibility, justifying departure from the manual rate that applies to the average risk in the class
- Manual rates provide the starting point; the final charged rate reflects manual rate modified by experience mod and schedule credits/debits, then adjusted retrospectively if applicable
- The goal is equity: each insured pays a rate that reflects its own expected loss costs rather than being entirely subsidized by or cross-subsidizing others in its class

> [!example]- Combining Manual Rate with Experience Mod {Example}
> A commercial auto risk has a manual premium of $50,000. Its experience modification factor (mod) is 1.15, and schedule rating credits total −5%. What is the final standard premium?
>
> > [!answer]-
> > $$\text{Modified Premium} = \$50{,}000 \times 1.15 = \$57{,}500$$
> > $$\text{Standard Premium} = \$57{,}500 \times (1 - 0.05) = \$57{,}500 \times 0.95 = \$54{,}625$$
> > The insured pays more than manual because its loss experience is worse than average, partially offset by favorable individual risk characteristics.
