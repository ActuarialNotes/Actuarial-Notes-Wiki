# Los Angeles Wildfires (January 2025)

## Overview
The Palisades and Eaton fires destroyed large portions of Pacific Palisades and Altadena, generating insured loss estimates broadly in the tens of billions of dollars (commonly cited in the US$30–50B range) — among the costliest wildfire events in history — and striking a California market already suffering carrier withdrawals and FAIR Plan concentration.

## Key Details
*   **FAIR Plan Assessment:** The residual-market FAIR Plan levied a roughly $1 billion assessment on member insurers, with partial pass-through to policyholders — demonstrating residual-market contagion mechanics.
*   **Moratorium Powers:** California imposed a one-year moratorium on non-renewals and cancellations in affected ZIP codes.
*   **Sustainable Insurance Strategy Context:** The event landed mid-implementation of California's reforms permitting forward-looking catastrophe models and reinsurance cost in rate filings in exchange for high-risk-area writing commitments, intensifying scrutiny of whether reforms could stabilize availability.

## Multi-Agent Execution Mandates
*   **Actuarial & Underwriting:** Wildfire model vintages, vegetation/fuel data recency, and urban-conflagration assumptions (structure-to-structure spread) re-validated; brush-zone and WUI appetite re-set continent-wide.
*   **Reinsurance:** January renewal positions tested immediately; wildfire-exposed program pricing and retentions hardened.
*   **Claims & Government Relations:** Mass smoke-damage claim standards, additional living expense durations, and total-loss settlement rules became regulatory flashpoints with cross-border relevance for Canadian WUI portfolios.
