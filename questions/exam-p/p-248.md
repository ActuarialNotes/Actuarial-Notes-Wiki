$X$ takes $-1, 0, 1$ each with probability $1/3$. Let $Y=X^2$. Which of the following is true?

- A) Cov$(X,Y)>0$; $X$ and $Y$ are dependent.
- B) Cov$(X,Y)>0$; $X$ and $Y$ are independent.
- C) Cov$(X,Y)=0$; $X$ and $Y$ are dependent.
- D) Cov$(X,Y)=0$; $X$ and $Y$ are independent.
- E) Cov$(X,Y)<0$; $X$ and $Y$ are dependent.

## Explanation

$E[XY]=E[X^3]=(-1)^3(1/3)+0+1^3(1/3)=0$, $E[X]=0$.

$\text{Cov}(X,Y)=E[XY]-E[X]E[Y]=0$.

However, $Y=X^2$ is a function of $X$, so $X$ and $Y$ are clearly **dependent** (knowing $X$ determines $Y$).

Answer: **Cov $=0$, dependent.**
