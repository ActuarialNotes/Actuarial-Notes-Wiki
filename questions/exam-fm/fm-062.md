Lucas opens a bank account with 1000 and lets it accumulate at an annual nominal interest rate of 6% convertible semiannually. Danielle also opens a bank account with 1000 at the same time as Lucas, but it grows at an annual nominal interest rate of 3% convertible monthly.

For each account, interest is credited only at the end of each interest conversion period.

Calculate the number of months required for the amount in Lucas's account to be at least double the amount in Danielle's account.

- A) 276
- B) 282
- C) 285
- D) 286
- E) 288

## Explanation

Let $n$ = years. Lucas's account grows at $(1.03)^{2n}$ and Danielle's at $(1.0025)^{12n}$.

We need $(1.03)^{2n} \geq 2(1.0025)^{12n}$:

$$2n\ln(1.03) + \ln(1000) \geq \ln(2000) + 12n\ln(1.0025)$$

$$0.029155n = 0.69315 \implies n = 23.775 \text{ years} = 285.3 \text{ months}$$

Since interest is credited only at conversion dates:
- Lucas's interest is credited every 6 months (next credit at 288 months)
- Danielle's interest is credited every month

The condition is first met when Lucas's account is credited, which is the next multiple of 6 after 285.3: **288 months**.
