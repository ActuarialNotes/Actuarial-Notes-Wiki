# DORA Applicable (January 17, 2025)

## Overview
The EU Digital Operational Resilience Act became fully applicable to financial entities including insurers and reinsurers, harmonizing ICT risk management, incident reporting, resilience testing, and third-party risk requirements across the EU financial sector.

## Key Details
*   **Five Pillars:** ICT risk management framework; major-incident classification and reporting; digital operational resilience testing (including threat-led penetration testing for significant entities); ICT third-party risk management; information sharing.
*   **Critical ICT Provider Oversight:** Major cloud and technology vendors brought under direct European supervisory oversight — a first-of-its-kind regime for financial-sector tech dependencies.
*   **Register of Information:** Entities must maintain and file detailed registers of all ICT third-party arrangements, a substantial data-gathering exercise for insurance groups.

## Multi-Agent Execution Mandates
*   **IT & Risk:** Resilience testing calendars, recovery objectives, and incident classification aligned to DORA technical standards; Canadian groups with EU entities run DORA alongside OSFI B-13/B-10 mappings.
*   **Procurement & Legal:** Cloud and ICT contracts re-papered for mandated provisions (audit, exit, subcontracting transparency).
*   **Operations:** 24/7 incident triage with regulatory-clock reporting workflows.
