The number of severe storms striking city $J$ in a year follows a binomial distribution with $n = 5$ and $p = 0.6$. Given that $m$ severe storms strike city $J$ in a year, the number striking city $K$ in the same year is $m$ with probability $1/2$, $m+1$ with probability $1/3$, and $m+2$ with probability $1/6$.

Calculate the expected number of severe storms that strike city $J$ in a year during which 5 severe storms strike city $K$.

- A) 3.5
- B) 3.7
- C) 3.9
- D) 4.0
- E) 5.7

## Explanation

$P(K=5 \mid J=m)$ is nonzero only for $m = 3, 4, 5$:

| $m$ | $P(K=5\mid J=m)$ | $P(J=m)$ | Joint |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5 | $1/2$ | $0.6^5=0.07776$ | $0.03888$ |
| 4 | $1/3$ | $5(0.6)^4(0.4)=0.2592$ | $0.08640$ |
| 3 | $1/6$ | $10(0.6)^3(0.4)^2=0.3456$ | $0.05760$ |

$P(K=5) = 0.03888 + 0.08640 + 0.05760 = 0.18288$

$$
\begin{align*}
E[J \mid K=5] &= \frac{5(0.03888)+4(0.08640)+3(0.05760)}{0.18288} \\
              &= \frac{0.1944+0.3456+0.1728}{0.18288} \\
              &= \frac{0.7128}{0.18288} \\
              &\approx \mathbf{3.9}
\end{align*}
$$
