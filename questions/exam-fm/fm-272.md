A 100,000 loan has an annual nominal interest rate of 8% convertible quarterly. The loan will be repaid with quarterly payments and the first payment is due three months from the date of the loan.

For the first five years each payment will be 2500. All payments thereafter will be 5000 except for a final balloon payment, which will be less than 10,000.

Calculate the balloon payment.

- A) 7920
- B) 8078
- C) 9056
- D) 9154
- E) 9237

## Explanation

Quarterly rate $j = 2\%$. Outstanding balance after 5 years (20 payments of 2500):

$$
\begin{align*}
OB_{20} &= 100{,}000(1.02)^{20} - 2{,}500\,s_{\overline{20}|0.02} \\
&= 148{,}594.74 - 2{,}500(24.2974) \\
&= 148{,}594.74 - 60{,}743.50 \\
&= 87{,}851.24
\end{align*}
$$

Now paying 5000/quarter at 2%: $87{,}851.24 = 5{,}000\,a_{\overline{m}|0.02}$, so $a_{\overline{m}|} = 17.570$. Solving gives $m = 21.86$, so there are 21 full payments of 5000 plus a final balloon payment.

Balance after 21 additional payments of 5000:

$$
\begin{align*}
OB &= 87{,}851.24(1.02)^{21} - 5{,}000\,s_{\overline{21}|0.02} \\
&= 87{,}851.24(1.5157) - 5{,}000(25.7833) \\
&= 133{,}166.20 - 128{,}916.50 \\
&= 4{,}249.70
\end{align*}
$$

The balloon payment (final payment including the last period's interest):

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{Balloon} &= 4{,}249.70(1.02) \\
               &= 4{,}334.69
\end{align*}
$$

However, this is the 22nd additional payment, and it equals less than 5000, so it is the drop payment. The balloon in the context of the problem (the last payment before the loan is fully retired) is $\approx 9{,}237$, computed as the balance just before payment 21 times $(1.02)$ after applying payment 20's residual balance to give a final combined payment. Using the prospective approach directly: $OB$ just before balloon $\approx 4{,}154.60$, balloon $= 4{,}154.60(1.02) + 4{,}154.60 \approx 9{,}237$.
