Susan won the lottery today which will pay an annual perpetuity of $X$, with the first payment occurring five years from today.

The perpetuity has a present value of 100,000 based on an annual effective interest rate of 1% for the first ten years and 5% for all years thereafter.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 4100
- B) 4224
- C) 4357
- D) 4401
- E) 5696

## Explanation

The PV of the perpetuity must equal 100,000. The first payment is at year 5.

For years 5–10 (6 payments) at 1%, and year 11+ at 5%:

PV at time 10 of the perpetuity continuing forever at 5%: $X/0.05 = 20X$.

PV at time 4 of the 6 payments at years 5–10 and the continuing perpetuity (discounting at 1%):
$$100,000 = \left[Xa_{\overline{6}|0.01} + 20X(1.01)^{-6}\right](1.01)^{-4}$$

$$100,000 = [X(5.79548) + 20X(0.94205)](1.01)^{-4}$$

$$100,000 = [5.79548X + 18.841X](0.96098) = 23.636X$$

$$X = 100,000/23.6751 = 4223.85 \approx 4224$$
