An insured's annual losses follow a Uniform distribution on $[0, b]$. The loss per claim $Y$ is related to $X$ by $Y = X/3 + 4$. If $E[Y] = 20$, find $b$.

- A) 48
- B) 96
- C) 120
- D) 144
- E) 192

## Explanation

$E[X] = b/2$ for $X \sim \text{Uniform}[0,b]$.

$$E[Y] = \frac{E[X]}{3}+4 = \frac{b/2}{3}+4 = \frac{b}{6}+4 = 20$$

$$\frac{b}{6} = 16 \implies b = \mathbf{96}$$
