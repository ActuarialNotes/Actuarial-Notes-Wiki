A perpetuity-due with semi-annual payments consists of two level payments of 300, followed by a series of increasing payments. Beginning with the third payment, each payment is 200 larger than the preceding payment.

Using an annual effective interest rate of $i$, the present value of the perpetuity is 475,000.

Calculate $i$.

- A) 4.05%
- B) 4.09%
- C) 4.13%
- D) 4.17%
- E) 4.21%

## Explanation

Let $j$ be the semi-annual interest rate. The perpetuity-due PV:
$$475,000 = 300 + 300a_{\overline{\infty}|j} + (1+j)^{-1} \cdot 200(Ia)_{\overline{\infty}|j}$$
$$= 300 + \frac{300}{j} + (1+j)^{-1}\cdot\frac{200}{j^2}$$

Multiplying through by $j^2$:
$$474,700j^2 - 300j - 200 = 0$$

$$j = \frac{300 + \sqrt{300^2 + 4(474,700)(200)}}{2(474,700)} = 0.02084$$

Annual effective rate: $i = (1+j)^2 - 1 = (1.02084)^2 - 1 = 0.04212 = 4.21\%$
