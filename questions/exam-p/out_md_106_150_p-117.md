In each of the months June, July, and August, the number of accidents occurring in that month is modeled by a Poisson random variable with mean 1. In each of the other 9 months of the year, the number of accidents occurring is modeled by a Poisson random variable with mean 0.5.

Assume that these 12 random variables are mutually independent.

Calculate the probability that exactly two accidents occur in July through November.

- A) 0.084
- B) 0.185
- C) 0.251
- D) 0.257
- E) 0.271

## Explanation

The sum of independent Poisson random variables is also a Poisson random variable, with a mean equal to the sum of the individual means.

The months July through November include:
* July (mean 1)
* August (mean 1)
* September (mean 0.5)
* October (mean 0.5)
* November (mean 0.5)

The total mean is $\lambda = 1 + 1 + 0.5 + 0.5 + 0.5 = 3.5$.

The probability of exactly two accidents in this period is:
$$P(X = 2) = \frac{e^{-3.5} (3.5)^2}{2!} = \frac{12.25 e^{-3.5}}{2} = 6.125 e^{-3.5} \approx 0.185$$
