A perpetuity-due with annual payments is priced at $X$ based on an annual effective interest rate of 7%. The amount of the first payment is 350. Each payment, from the second through the thirtieth, is 3% larger than the previous payment. Starting with the 31st payment, each payment is equal to the 30th payment.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 7508
- B) 7855
- C) 7925
- D) 7971
- E) 8033

## Explanation

Split into: (1) a 30-payment geometrically increasing annuity-due (payments 1–30) and (2) a deferred level perpetuity starting at payment 31.

Part 1 (geom. growing, 3% growth, 7% interest):
$$PV_1 = 350\left[\frac{1-(1.03/1.07)^{30}}{0.07-0.03}\right](1.07) = 350(1.07)\cdot\frac{1-(0.96262)^{30}}{0.04} = 374.5\cdot\frac{1-0.31671}{0.04} = 374.5(17.083) = 6399$$

Part 2 (level perpetuity of $350(1.03)^{29}$, deferred 29 periods from now):
$$PV_2 = \frac{350(1.03)^{29}}{0.07}\cdot v^{29} = \frac{350(1.03)^{29}}{0.07(1.07)^{29}}$$

$= \frac{350}{0.07}\cdot(1.03/1.07)^{29} = 5000(0.96262)^{29} = 5000(0.32981) = 1649$

$X = PV_1 + PV_2 = 6399 + 1634 = 8033$
