A random sample of 4 independent claims is drawn, where each claim size $X$ has a lognormal distribution with $\ln X \sim N(6, 1^2)$. Let $M$ be the maximum claim size.

Calculate the probability that $M$ exceeds $e^{7}$.

- A) $1-(1-e^{-1/2})^4$\n- B) $(1-\Phi(1))^4$\n- C) $\Phi(1)^4$\n- D) $1-\Phi(1)^4$\n- E) $[\Phi(1)]^4$

## Explanation

$P(X \leq e^7) = P(\ln X \leq 7) = P\!\left(Z \leq \frac{7-6}{1}\right) = \Phi(1)$.

$$P(M \leq e^7) = P(\text{all 4} \leq e^7) = [\Phi(1)]^4$$

$$P(M > e^7) = 1-[\Phi(1)]^4 \approx 1-(0.8413)^4 \approx 1-0.501 = \mathbf{0.499}$$
