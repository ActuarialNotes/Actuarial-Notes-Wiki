A policyholder has probability 0.7 of having no claims, 0.2 of having exactly one claim, and 0.1 of having exactly two claims. Claim amounts are uniformly distributed on the interval $[0, 60]$ and are independent. The insurer covers 100% of each claim.

Calculate the probability that the total benefit paid to the policyholder is 48 or less.

- A) 0.320
- B) 0.400
- C) 0.800
- D) 0.892
- E) 0.924

## Explanation

Consider the three cases based on the number of claims $N$.
If $N = 0$, the total benefit is 0, so the probability of the total being 48 or less is 1.

If $N = 1$, the claim amount $X \sim U(0, 60)$. The probability that $X \le 48$ is:
$$P(X \le 48 \mid N=1) = \frac{48 - 0}{60 - 0} = 0.8$$

If $N = 2$, the total benefit is $X_1 + X_2$, where $X_1, X_2 \sim U(0, 60)$. The joint density is uniform on a $60 \times 60$ square. The event $X_1 + X_2 \le 48$ is a triangle in the corner of this square with base 48 and height 48. 
The area of this triangle is $\frac{1}{2}(48)(48) = 1152$.
The area of the entire square is $60 \times 60 = 3600$.
$$P(X_1 + X_2 \le 48 \mid N=2) = \frac{1152}{3600} = 0.32$$

Using the law of total probability:
$$P(\text{Total} \le 48) = P(\text{Total} \le 48 \mid N=0)P(N=0) + P(\text{Total} \le 48 \mid N=1)P(N=1) + P(\text{Total} \le 48 \mid N=2)P(N=2)$$
$$= 1(0.7) + 0.8(0.2) + 0.32(0.1) = 0.7 + 0.16 + 0.032 = 0.892$$
