Given the following information:

- All policies have six-month terms.
- Policies are written uniformly during each six-month period and cannot be cancelled.
- The rating algorithm is: **base rate × class factor + expense fee**.
- The proposed effective date of the next rate change is July 1, 2013.
- A rate review is performed every six months.

**Rate history (base rate per exposure × class factor + expense fee):**

| Effective Date of Rates | Base Rate Per Exposure | Class Factor A | Class Factor B | Expense Fee |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| January 1, 2011 | $480 | 1.00 | 0.70 | $45 |
| July 1, 2011 | $488 | 1.00 | 0.70 | $45 |
| January 1, 2012 | $504 | 1.00 | 0.70 | $50 |
| July 1, 2012 | $500 | 1.00 | 0.75 | $50 |
| January 1, 2013 | $500 | 1.00 | 0.80 | $55 |

**Written Exposures (000s) by policy effective date period:**

| Policy Effective Dates | Class A | Class B |
|---|---|---|
| January 1, 2011 – June 30, 2011 | 125 | 50 |
| July 1, 2011 – December 31, 2011 | 150 | 100 |
| January 1, 2012 – June 30, 2012 | 175 | 150 |
| July 1, 2012 – December 31, 2012 | 200 | 200 |

Using the **extension of exposures method**, calculate the **calendar year 2012 earned premium at current rate level**.

Current rate level = January 1, 2013 rates.

## Answer

$330,750,000

(Equivalently expressed as $330,750 when exposures are in 000s and the answer is in 000s of dollars.)

## Explanation

**Step 1 — Determine current rate level premiums per exposure.**

Current rates are the January 1, 2013 rates: base rate = $500, class factor A = 1.00, class factor B = 0.80, expense fee = $55.

$$\text{Premium per exposure, Class A} = \$500 \times 1.00 + \$55 = \$555$$

$$\text{Premium per exposure, Class B} = \$500 \times 0.80 + \$55 = \$455$$

**Step 2 — Determine what percentage of each cohort of 6-month policies is earned during CY 2012.**

All policies have 6-month terms. Policies written uniformly during a half-year period have an average written date at the midpoint of that period. A 6-month policy written at the midpoint earns over a 6-month window centered on that midpoint. The key question is: what fraction of each cohort's exposure is earned during calendar year 2012 (January 1 – December 31, 2012)?

| Policy Effective Period | Avg. Written Date | Policy Earns | % Earned in CY 2012 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Jan 1 – Jun 30, 2011 | Apr 1, 2011 | Apr 1 – Sep 30, 2011 | 0% |
| Jul 1 – Dec 31, 2011 | Oct 1, 2011 | Oct 1, 2011 – Mar 31, 2012 | 50% (Jan 1 – Mar 31, 2012) |
| Jan 1 – Jun 30, 2012 | Apr 1, 2012 | Apr 1 – Sep 30, 2012 | 100% |
| Jul 1 – Dec 31, 2012 | Oct 1, 2012 | Oct 1, 2012 – Mar 31, 2013 | 50% (Oct 1 – Dec 31, 2012) |

**Step 3 — Calculate CY 2012 earned exposures at current rate level for each cohort and class.**

| Policy Effective Period | % Earned in 2012 | Earned Exp A (000s) | Earned Exp B (000s) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Jan 1 – Jun 30, 2011 | 0% | 0 | 0 |
| Jul 1 – Dec 31, 2011 | 50% | 75 | 50 |
| Jan 1 – Jun 30, 2012 | 100% | 175 | 150 |
| Jul 1 – Dec 31, 2012 | 50% | 100 | 100 |
| **Total** | | **350** | **300** |

**Step 4 — Calculate CY 2012 earned premium at current rate level.**

$$\text{EP at CRL} = 350{,}000 \times \$555 + 300{,}000 \times \$455$$

$$= \$194{,}250{,}000 + \$136{,}500{,}000 = \mathbf{\$330{,}750{,}000}$$

Note: The expense fee is embedded in the per-exposure premium and earns pro-rata with the policy, so the same earning percentages apply to the full premium (base rate × factor + expense fee).

## Examiner Report

Most candidates correctly used the current rates and calculated the on-level premium accurately. For candidates that incorrectly calculated the on-level premium, there were various errors related to using older rates. About half the candidates calculated the earnings percentage correctly. Some common errors were assuming the latest 2 years of exposures were fully earned, or assuming the expense fee is fully earned immediately. Some candidates forced the calculation into a parallelogram method. The question explicitly said to use the extension of exposures method. This would cause a significant amount of additional work and would result in lost points since it was not the appropriate method.
