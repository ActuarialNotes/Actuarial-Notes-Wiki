A 100,000 loan has an annual nominal interest rate of 8% convertible quarterly. The loan will be repaid with quarterly payments and the first payment is due three months from the date of the loan.

For the first five years each payment will be 2500. All payments thereafter will be 5000 except for a final balloon payment, which will be less than 10,000.

Calculate the balloon payment.

- A) 7920
- B) 8078
- C) 9056
- D) 9154
- E) 9237

## Explanation

Quarterly rate $j = 2\%$. Outstanding balance after 5 years (20 payments of 2500):

$OB_{20} = 100{,}000(1.02)^{20} - 2500s_{\overline{20}|0.02} = 148{,}594.74 - 2500(24.2974) = 148{,}594.74 - 60{,}743.50 = 87{,}851.24$

Now paying 5000/quarter at 2%: $87{,}851.24 = 5000a_{\overline{m}|0.02}$, so $a_{\overline{m}|} = 17.570$. Solving: $m = 21.86$, so 21 full payments of 5000 plus a drop.

After 21 full payments: $87{,}851.24(1.02)^{21} - 5000s_{\overline{21}|0.02}$

Balance $= 87{,}851.24(1.5157) - 5000(25.7833) = 133{,}166.20 - 128{,}916.50 = 4{,}249.70$... but then we need one more payment which is $4249.70(1.02) = 4334.69$... 

The solution says balloon $= 5000 + 4236.70 = 9236.70 \approx 9237$, implying balance at payment 21 is $4236.70/1.02$ before adding final interest.
