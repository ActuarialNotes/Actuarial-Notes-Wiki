A study of life insurance policyholders found that 1/6 of policyholders exercise regularly, with lifetime $\sim \text{Exp}(\text{mean } 3/2)$. The remaining 5/6 do not exercise regularly, with lifetime $\sim \text{Exp}(\text{mean } 1/4)$. All lifetimes are independent.

Calculate the probability that a randomly selected policyholder exercises regularly, given that the policyholder is alive one year after the policy was purchased.

- A) 0.06
- B) 0.17
- C) 0.39
- D) 0.47
- E) 0.85

## Explanation

$P(\text{alive}\mid\text{exercise}) = e^{-1/(3/2)} = e^{-2/3} \approx 0.5134$

$P(\text{alive}\mid\text{no exercise}) = e^{-1/(1/4)} = e^{-4} \approx 0.0183$

$$P(\text{alive}) = \frac{1}{6}(0.5134)+\frac{5}{6}(0.0183) \approx 0.1008$$

$$P(\text{exercise}\mid\text{alive}) = \frac{(1/6)(0.5134)}{0.1008} \approx \mathbf{0.85}$$
