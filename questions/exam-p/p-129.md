The proportion $X$ of yearly dental claims that exceed 200 is a random variable with probability density function
$$f(x) = \begin{cases} 60x^3(1-x)^2, & 0 < x < 1 \\ 0, & \text{otherwise.} \end{cases}$$

Calculate $Var\left(\frac{X}{1-X}\right)$.

- A) 149/900
- B) 10/7
- C) 6
- D) 8
- E) 10

## Explanation

Let $Y = \frac{X}{1-X}$. We need to find $E[Y]$ and $E[Y^2]$.

$$
\begin{align*}
E[Y] &= \int_0^1 \frac{x}{1-x} 60x^3(1-x)^2 dx \\
     &= 60 \int_0^1 x^4(1-x) dx \\
     &= 60 \int_0^1 (x^4 - x^5) dx \\
     &= 60 \left[ \frac{x^5}{5} - \frac{x^6}{6} \right]_0^1 \\
     &= 60 \left( \frac{1}{5} - \frac{1}{6} \right) \\
     &= 60 \left( \frac{1}{30} \right) \\
     &= 2
\end{align*}
$$

$$
\begin{align*}
E[Y^2] &= \int_0^1 \left(\frac{x}{1-x}\right)^2 60x^3(1-x)^2 dx \\
       &= 60 \int_0^1 \frac{x^2}{(1-x)^2} x^3(1-x)^2 dx \\
       &= 60 \int_0^1 x^5 dx \\
       &= 60 \left[ \frac{x^6}{6} \right]_0^1 \\
       &= 60 \left( \frac{1}{6} \right) \\
       &= 10
\end{align*}
$$

Calculate variance:
$$
\begin{align*}
Var(Y) &= E[Y^2] - (E[Y])^2 \\
       &= 10 - 2^2 \\
       &= 10 - 4 \\
       &= 6
\end{align*}
$$
