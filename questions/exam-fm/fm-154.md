A bond has a modified duration of 8 and a price of 112,955 calculated using an annual effective interest rate of 6.4%.

$E_{MAC}$ is the estimated price of this bond at an interest rate of 7.0% using the first-order Macaulay approximation.

$E_{MOD}$ is the estimated price of this bond at an interest rate of 7.0% using the first-order modified approximation.

Calculate $E_{MAC} - E_{MOD}$.

- A) 91
- B) 102
- C) 116
- D) 127
- E) 143

## Explanation

Modified duration $= 8$, so Macaulay duration $= 8(1.064) = 8.512$.

**Macaulay approximation:**
$$E_{MAC} = 112,955\left(\frac{1.064}{1.07}\right)^{8.512} = 112,955(0.994393)^{8.512} \approx 107,676$$

**Modified approximation:**
$$E_{MOD} = 112,955[1-(0.07-0.064)(8)] = 112,955(1-0.048) = 112,955(0.952) = 107,533$$

$$E_{MAC} - E_{MOD} = 107,676 - 107,533 = 143$$
