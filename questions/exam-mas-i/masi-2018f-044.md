You are given the following statements about time series and generalized least squares regression (GLS):

- I. When there is positive serial correlation in a time series, the standard errors of the estimated regression parameters are likely to be over-estimated.
- II. GLS is an improvement over ordinary least squares regression for serially correlated time series because GLS is based on maximizing the likelihood given the white noise in the data.
- III. GLS can be used to provide better estimates of standard errors of the regression parameters to account for autocorrelation in the residual series.

Determine which of the above statements are true.

- A) I only
- B) II only
- C) III only
- D) I, II and III
- E) The answer is not given by (A), (B), (C), or (D)

## Explanation

**Statement I** — *False.* With positive serial correlation, ordinary least squares tends to **under**-estimate the standard errors of the regression parameters (making them appear more precise than they are), not over-estimate them.

**Statement II** — *False.* GLS improves on OLS by weighting/transforming the data to account for the residual covariance structure; it is not correctly characterized as "maximizing the likelihood given the white noise in the data."

**Statement III** — *True.* GLS explicitly incorporates the autocorrelation structure of the residuals, yielding more appropriate standard errors for the regression parameters.

Only III is true, so the answer is **C**.
