**Report Year** (RY) is a data organization method that groups losses by the calendar year in which the claim was first reported to the insurer, regardless of when the loss occurred or the policy was effective.

- RY is the natural basis for **claims-made policies**, where coverage is triggered by the report date rather than the occurrence date; for occurrence policies, [[Accident Year]] is preferred
- Development is faster under report year than accident year since losses are assigned to the year of first notice; however, reporting lags cause some accident year losses to appear in a different report year
- RY data is also used for operational analysis — report lag studies, claims department capacity planning, and early trend detection

> [!example]- Report Year Assignment {Example}
> A general liability policy is effective $6/1/2023$. A loss occurs on $11/15/2023$, but is not reported to the insurer until $2/3/2024$.
>
> > [!answer]-
> > The claim is **Report Year 2024** (year of first report to insurer), **Accident Year 2023** (year of occurrence). Under a claims-made policy, the 2024 report year would determine whether coverage applies.
