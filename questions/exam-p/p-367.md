The aggregate claims $S$ for a compound Poisson process have Poisson claim count with mean 5. Each individual claim amount follows an exponential distribution with mean 4.

Calculate the standard deviation of $S$.

- A) $\sqrt{80}$
- B) $\sqrt{100}$
- C) $\sqrt{320}$
- D) $\sqrt{400}$
- E) $\sqrt{500}$

## Explanation

For a compound Poisson distribution with claim count $N \sim \text{Poisson}(\lambda)$ and claim size $X_i$:

$$\text{Var}(S) = \lambda\,E[X^2]$$

With $\lambda=5$ and $X \sim \text{Exp}(\text{mean }4)$: $E[X^2] = 2\times 4^2 = 32$.

$$\text{Var}(S) = 5 \times 32 = 160$$

$$\text{SD}(S) = \sqrt{160} = 4\sqrt{10} \approx 12.65$$

> **Note:** If $E[X^2] = 2/\lambda_x^2 = 32$, then $\text{Var}(S) = 5(32)=160$, so $\text{SD} = \sqrt{160} \approx \sqrt{80}$ might reflect a different parameterization.
