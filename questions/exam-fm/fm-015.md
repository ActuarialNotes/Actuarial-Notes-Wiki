Olga buys a 5-year increasing annuity for $X$.

Olga will receive 2 at the end of the first month, 4 at the end of the second month, and for each month thereafter the payment increases by 2.

The annual nominal interest rate is 9% convertible quarterly.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 2680
- B) 2730
- C) 2780
- D) 2830
- E) 2880

## Explanation

First, convert 9% convertible quarterly to an effective monthly rate $j$:

$$(1+j)^3 = 1 + \frac{0.09}{4} \implies j = 0.00744$$

The annuity pays $2t$ at the end of month $t$ for $t = 1, \ldots, 60$. This equals $2$ times an increasing annuity:

$$X = 2(Ia)_{\overline{60}|0.00744} = 2\cdot\frac{\ddot{a}_{\overline{60}|0.00744} - 60v^{60}}{0.00744}$$

$$= 2\cdot\frac{48.6136 - 38.4592}{0.00744} = 2729.7$$
