# EU AI Act in Force (August 1, 2024)

## Overview
The world's first comprehensive AI statute entered into force, with phased application through 2026–2027. Annex III designates AI systems used for risk assessment and pricing in life and health insurance as high-risk, imposing the Act's full conformity regime on those systems.

## Key Details
*   **High-Risk Obligations:** Risk management systems, data governance and bias controls, technical documentation, human oversight, accuracy/robustness standards, and registration — applying to insurers as deployers and model vendors as providers.
*   **Phased Timeline:** Prohibited practices from February 2025; general-purpose AI rules from August 2025; most high-risk obligations from August 2026.
*   **Extraterritorial Effect:** Applies where AI output is used in the EU, capturing non-EU insurance groups with European operations and setting a reference point regulators elsewhere (including Canadian discussions post-AIDA's lapse) measure against.

## Multi-Agent Execution Mandates
*   **Analytics & Actuarial:** EU-touching life/health pricing models inventoried, documented, and bias-tested to conformity-assessment standard.
*   **Compliance & Legal:** Provider-vs-deployer role mapping across the model supply chain; vendor contracts amended for documentation and incident duties.
*   **IT:** Logging, traceability, and human-oversight controls engineered into model serving infrastructure.
