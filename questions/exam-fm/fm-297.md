A 27-year bond, with a price equal to 75% of its face value, offers annual coupons with the coupon rate equal to $\dfrac{21}{37}$ of the annual effective yield rate.

An $n$-year zero-coupon bond has the same price, face value, and yield rate.

Calculate $n$.

- A) 5
- B) 7
- C) 9
- D) 11
- E) 13

## Explanation

For the 27-year bond (face $F$, price $0.75F$, coupon rate $r = (21/37)i$):

$$0.75F = F\cdot\frac{21}{37}i \cdot a_{\overline{27}|} + Fv^{27} = F\left[\frac{21}{37}(1-v^{27}) + v^{27}\right] = F\left[\frac{21}{37} + \frac{16}{37}v^{27}\right]$$

$$0.75 = \frac{21}{37} + \frac{16}{37}v^{27} \implies \frac{16}{37}v^{27} = 0.75 - \frac{21}{37} = \frac{27.75-21}{37} = \frac{6.75}{37}$$

$$
\begin{align*}
v^{27} &= \frac{6.75}{16} = 0.421875 \\
i &= (1/0.421875)^{1/27}-1 = 0.032482
\end{align*}
$$

For the zero-coupon bond: $0.75F = Fv^n \implies v^n = 0.75$

$$n = \frac{\ln(0.75)}{\ln(v)} = \frac{-0.2877}{-0.03197} = 9.00$$
