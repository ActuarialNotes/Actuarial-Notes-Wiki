You would like to determine if coffee consumption varies by start time of job shifts. A random sample of 400 adults were surveyed and the results are as follows:

|       | Low | Medium | High | Total |
|-------|-----|--------|------|-------|
| Day   | 100 | 25     | 75   | 200   |
| Night | 25  | 125    | 50   | 200   |
| Total | 125 | 150    | 125  | 400   |

Calculate the value of Chi-square statistic used in this test.

- A) Less than 60
- B) At least 60, but less than 80
- C) At least 80, but less than 100
- D) At least 100, but less than 120
- E) At least 120

## Explanation

This is a chi-square test of independence. The **expected** count for each cell is $\dfrac{(\text{row total})(\text{column total})}{\text{grand total}}$. Both rows total 200 and the grand total is 400, so the expected counts are

$$E_{\text{Low}} = \frac{200 \times 125}{400} = 62.5,\quad E_{\text{Medium}} = \frac{200 \times 150}{400} = 75,\quad E_{\text{High}} = 62.5,$$

identically for Day and Night.

$$\chi^2 = \sum \frac{(O - E)^2}{E}.$$

Low cells: $\dfrac{(100-62.5)^2}{62.5} = 22.5$ and $\dfrac{(25-62.5)^2}{62.5} = 22.5$.

Medium cells: $\dfrac{(25-75)^2}{75} = 33.33$ and $\dfrac{(125-75)^2}{75} = 33.33$.

High cells: $\dfrac{(75-62.5)^2}{62.5} = 2.5$ and $\dfrac{(50-62.5)^2}{62.5} = 2.5$.

$$\chi^2 = 22.5 + 22.5 + 33.33 + 33.33 + 2.5 + 2.5 = 116.67.$$

This falls in $[100, 120)$, so the answer is **D**.
