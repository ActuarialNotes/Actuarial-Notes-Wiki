Let $X$ be an exponentially distributed random variable with mean 2.

Calculate $E[e^{-X}]$.

- A) $1/3$\n- B) $1/2$\n- C) $2/3$\n- D) $e^{-2}$\n- E) $e^{-1/2}$

## Explanation

For $X \sim \text{Exp}(\lambda)$ with $\lambda = 1/2$ (mean = 2), the moment generating function is:

$$E[e^{tX}] = \frac{\lambda}{\lambda - t}, \quad t < \lambda$$

$$E[e^{-X}] = \frac{1/2}{1/2+1} = \frac{1/2}{3/2} = \frac{1}{3}$$
