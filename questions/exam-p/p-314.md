An auto insurance company tracks first-year and multi-year policyholders separately. First-year policyholders account for 15% of the company's business; multi-year policyholders account for the rest. First-year claims follow Poisson(0.50) and multi-year claims follow Poisson(0.20).

Calculate the probability that a policyholder is a first-year policyholder, given that the policyholder reports at least one claim in a year.

- A) 0.246
- B) 0.277
- C) 0.306
- D) 0.476
- E) 0.685

## Explanation

$P(\geq1 \mid \text{first}) = 1-e^{-0.5} \approx 0.3935$

$P(\geq1 \mid \text{multi}) = 1-e^{-0.2} \approx 0.1813$

$$P(\geq1) = 0.15(0.3935)+0.85(0.1813) = 0.05903+0.15411 = 0.21314$$

$$P(\text{first}\mid\geq1) = \frac{0.05903}{0.21314} \approx \mathbf{0.277}$$
