A certain brand of refrigerator has a useful life that is normally distributed with mean 10 years and standard deviation 3 years. The useful lives of these refrigerators are independent.

Calculate the probability that the total useful life of two randomly selected refrigerators will exceed 1.9 times the useful life of a third randomly selected refrigerator.

- A) 0.407
- B) 0.444
- C) 0.556
- D) 0.593
- E) 0.604

## Explanation

Let $X, Y,$ and $Z$ be the useful lives of the three refrigerators. We want $P(X + Y > 1.9Z)$.
This is equivalent to $P(W > 0)$ where $W = X + Y - 1.9Z$.

Since $X, Y, Z$ are independent and normal, $W$ is normally distributed.
$$E[W] = E[X] + E[Y] - 1.9E[Z] = 10 + 10 - 1.9(10) = 20 - 19 = 1$$
$$Var(W) = Var(X) + Var(Y) + (-1.9)^2 Var(Z) = 3^2 + 3^2 + 1.9^2(3^2) = 9 + 9 + 3.61(9) = 18 + 32.49 = 50.49$$
Standard deviation $\sigma_W = \sqrt{50.49} \approx 7.1056$.

The desired probability is:
$$P(W > 0) = P\left(Z > \frac{0 - 1}{7.1056}\right) = P(Z > -0.1407) = P(Z < 0.1407)$$

Using the standard normal table, $P(Z < 0.14) = 0.5557$ and $P(Z < 0.15) = 0.5596$. Interpolating gives approximately 0.556.
