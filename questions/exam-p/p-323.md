A broker markets four separate products. The probabilities of selling these products to a client are 0.45 (auto), 0.55 (homeowners), 0.60 (health), and 0.60 (life), independent.

Calculate the probability that the broker sells more than two products to a client.

- A) 0.24
- B) 0.30
- C) 0.39
- D) 0.61
- E) 0.76

## Explanation

Let A=auto, B=homeowners, C=health, D=life. $P(N=4)=0.45(0.55)(0.60)(0.60)=0.0891$.

$P(N=3) = P(A\cap B\cap C\cap D')+P(A\cap B\cap C'\cap D)+P(A\cap B'\cap C\cap D)+P(A'\cap B\cap C\cap D)$

$= 0.45(0.55)(0.60)(0.40)+0.45(0.55)(0.40)(0.60)+0.45(0.45)(0.60)(0.60)+0.55(0.55)(0.60)(0.60)$

$= 0.0594+0.0594+0.0729+0.1089 = 0.3006$

$$P(N>2) = 0.3006+0.0891 = \mathbf{0.3897 \approx 0.39}$$
