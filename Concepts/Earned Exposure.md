**Earned Exposure** is the portion of total written exposure units that has been consumed during the experience period, providing coverage for that time, and serves as the denominator in pure premium calculations.

> $$\text{Earned Exposure} = \text{Written Exposure} - \Delta\text{Unearned Exposure}$$

- Earned exposure is the exposure analogue of [[Earned Premium]]: just as premium is earned pro-rata over the policy period, exposure units are earned as coverage is provided
- For annual policies written uniformly throughout a calendar year, earned exposure for that year approximates written exposure (each policy contributes on average 6 months)
- The pure premium is computed as $PP = \text{Losses} / \text{Earned Exposure}$; using written or in-force exposure instead would distort the result
- Earned exposure must be on-level (adjusted for classification changes) to be consistent with on-level earned premium in ratemaking analyses

> [!example]- Calendar Year Earned Car-Years {Example}
> An insurer writes 1,200 annual auto policies uniformly throughout calendar year 2024 (100 per month). How many car-years are earned in 2024?
>
> > [!answer]-
> > A policy written on January 1 contributes 12/12 = 1.0 car-years; one written July 1 contributes 6/12 = 0.5 car-years; one written December 1 contributes 1/12 car-years. On average each policy earns 6.5/12 of a year. Total earned car-years $\approx 1{,}200 \times 6.5/12 = 650$ car-years—approximately half of written exposure, consistent with uniform writings.
