Susan receives annual payments from a 20-year annuity-immediate. The payment in year 1 is 100 and in each succeeding year the payment is 90% of the prior year's payment.

Upon receipt of each payment, Susan invests the payment in a savings account earning interest at a 3% annual effective rate.

Calculate the balance in the savings account immediately after Susan invests the last annuity payment.

- A) 696
- B) 717
- C) 739
- D) 1296
- E) 1335

## Explanation

The accumulated value of all payments at end of year 20 (each grows at 3%):

$$FV = 100\left[(1.03)^{19} + 0.9(1.03)^{18} + 0.9^2(1.03)^{17} + \cdots + 0.9^{19}(1.03)^0\right]$$

$$= 100(1.03)^{19}\left[1 + \frac{0.9}{1.03} + \left(\frac{0.9}{1.03}\right)^2 + \cdots + \left(\frac{0.9}{1.03}\right)^{19}\right]$$

$$= 100\cdot\frac{(1.03)^{19}-(0.9)^{20}(1.03)^{-1}}{1-0.9/1.03} = 100\cdot\frac{(1.03)^{20}-(0.9)^{20}}{1.03-0.9} = 100\cdot\frac{1.8061-0.1216}{0.13} = 1295.8 \approx 1296$$
