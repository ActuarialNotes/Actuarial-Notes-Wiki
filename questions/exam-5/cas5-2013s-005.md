A company is reviewing the rate level adequacy. Given the following information for a book of business:

- All policies are annual.
- Current rates have been in effect for three years.
- New rates will be in effect for 18 months beginning on July 1, 2013.
- Annual premium trend = −1%.
- Annual loss trend = +3%.
- Loss adjustment expense provision = 2.5% of loss.
- Historical expense ratios:
  - Fixed = 6%.
  - Variable = 30%.
- Underwriting profit and contingencies provision = 5%.
- Ultimate losses are estimated using the reported development technique.
- On January 1, 2014, the company will reduce agency commissions by 3% of premium.

| Calendar Year Ending | Earned Premium ($000s) |
|---|---|
| December 31, 2011 | $2,163 |
| December 31, 2012 | $2,120 |

Reported Losses ($000s):

| Accident Year | 12 months | 24 months | 36 months | 48 months | 60 months |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2008 | $780 | $928 | $1,030 | $1,083 | $1,094 |
| 2009 | $765 | $921 | $1,004 | $1,053 | |
| 2010 | $760 | $920 | $1,012 | | |
| 2011 | $805 | $966 | | | |
| 2012 | $890 | | | | |

Calculate the indicated rate change.

### Answer

+3.45%

### Explanation

**Step 1 — On-level factor.**

Rates have been unchanged for three years, so the on-level factor for both CY 2011 and CY 2012 earned premium is **1.00**.

**Step 2 — Select loss development factors (LDFs) to ultimate.**

Compute age-to-age factors from the reported loss triangle:

| Period | 2008 | 2009 | 2010 | Selected ATA |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 12–24 | $928/$780 = 1.190 | $921/$765 = 1.204 | $920/$760 = 1.211 | **1.200** |
| 24–36 | $1,030/$928 = 1.110 | $1,004/$921 = 1.090 | $1,012/$920 = 1.100 | **1.100** |
| 36–48 | $1,083/$1,030 = 1.051 | $1,053/$1,004 = 1.049 | | **1.050** |
| 48–60 | $1,094/$1,083 = 1.010 | | | **1.010** |
| 60–Ult | | | | **1.000** |

Cumulative LDFs to ultimate:

| Age | CDF to Ultimate |
|---|---|
| 12 months | $1.200 \times 1.100 \times 1.050 \times 1.010 \times 1.000 = 1.400$ |
| 24 months | $1.100 \times 1.050 \times 1.010 \times 1.000 = 1.167$ |
| 36 months | $1.050 \times 1.010 \times 1.000 = 1.061$ |
| 48 months | $1.010 \times 1.000 = 1.010$ |
| 60 months | $1.000$ |

AY 2011 is at 24 months → CDF = **1.167**; AY 2012 is at 12 months → CDF = **1.400**.

**Step 3 — Determine loss trend periods.**

Losses occur uniformly throughout the year (average accident date = July 1 of each accident year). The future rate period is July 1, 2013 through December 31, 2014 (18 months). For annual policies written uniformly over this period, the average written date is April 1, 2014, so the average date of loss is **October 1, 2014** (April 1, 2014 + 6 months).

| Accident Year | Avg Loss Date | Trend Period |
|---|---|---|
| 2011 | July 1, 2011 | Oct 1, 2014 − Jul 1, 2011 = **3.25 years** |
| 2012 | July 1, 2012 | Oct 1, 2014 − Jul 1, 2012 = **2.25 years** |

Annual loss trend = +3%.

| AY | Loss Trend Factor |
|---|---|
| 2011 | $(1.03)^{3.25} \approx 1.0998$ |
| 2012 | $(1.03)^{2.25} \approx 1.0686$ |

**Step 4 — Compute trended, developed ultimate losses (with LAE loading).**

LAE provision = 2.5% of loss, so the LAE loading factor = 1.025.

$$\text{Projected Ultimate Loss + LAE} = \text{Reported Loss} \times \text{CDF} \times \text{Loss Trend} \times 1.025$$

| AY | Reported Loss | CDF | Trend Factor | LAE Factor | Projected Ult Loss + LAE ($000s) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2011 | $966 | 1.167 | 1.0998 | 1.025 | $966 \times 1.167 \times 1.0998 \times 1.025 \approx \mathbf{1{,}272}$ |
| 2012 | $890 | 1.400 | 1.0686 | 1.025 | $890 \times 1.400 \times 1.0686 \times 1.025 \approx \mathbf{1{,}365}$ |

**Step 5 — Determine premium trend periods.**

For CY earned premium (annual policies, uniform writes), the average written date is January 1 of each year. The average written date of the future rate period is April 1, 2014.

| CY | Avg Written Date | Trend Period |
|---|---|---|
| 2011 | Jan 1, 2011 | Apr 1, 2014 − Jan 1, 2011 = **3.25 years** |
| 2012 | Jan 1, 2012 | Apr 1, 2014 − Jan 1, 2012 = **2.25 years** |

Annual premium trend = −1%.

**Step 6 — Compute projected (trended) earned premium.**

$$\text{Projected EP} = \text{Earned Premium} \times (0.99)^t$$

| CY | Earned Premium ($000s) | Premium Trend Factor | Projected EP ($000s) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2011 | $2,163 | $(0.99)^{3.25} \approx 0.9680$ | $2,163 \times 0.9680 \approx \mathbf{2{,}093}$ |
| 2012 | $2,120 | $(0.99)^{2.25} \approx 0.9779$ | $2,120 \times 0.9779 \approx \mathbf{2{,}073}$ |

**Step 7 — Compute loss ratios and weighted average.**

| AY/CY | Proj Ult Loss + LAE ($000s) | Proj EP ($000s) | Loss Ratio |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2011 | 1,272 | 2,093 | $1{,}272 / 2{,}093 = 0.6076$ |
| 2012 | 1,365 | 2,073 | $1{,}365 / 2{,}073 = 0.6586$ |
| **Average** | | | **0.6331** |

**Step 8 — Determine the weighted permissible loss ratio.**

The 18-month rate period has two segments with different variable expense levels:
- **July 1 – December 31, 2013** (1/3 of the period): agency commissions at 30% variable → $V = 30\%$, $PLR_1 = 1 - 0.30 - 0.05 = 0.65$
- **January 1 – December 31, 2014** (2/3 of the period): commissions reduced by 3% → $V = 27\%$, $PLR_2 = 1 - 0.27 - 0.05 = 0.68$

$$\text{Weighted PLR} = \frac{1}{3}(0.65) + \frac{2}{3}(0.68) = 0.2167 + 0.4533 = \mathbf{0.67}$$

**Step 9 — Calculate the indicated rate change.**

$$\text{Indicated Rate Change} = \frac{\text{Avg Loss Ratio} + \text{Fixed Expense Ratio}}{\text{Weighted PLR}} - 1$$

$$= \frac{0.6331 + 0.06}{0.67} - 1 = \frac{0.6931}{0.67} - 1 = 1.0345 - 1$$

$$\boxed{= +3.45\%}$$

### Examiner Report

In general, this question was completed well, although there were a couple of common errors:

1. Most candidates recognized that an adjustment was needed for the commission change, but the adjustment was not consistently done correctly.

2. The trend period for losses and premium was often determined incorrectly. Although rates were in effect for 18 months, candidates are expected to know how to properly determine the trend period (the rate period is 18 months, not 12 months, so the average written date is not simply July 1, 2013).
