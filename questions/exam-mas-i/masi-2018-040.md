An actuary fits a Poisson distribution to a sample of data, $X$.

$$f(x) = \frac{\theta^x e^{-\theta}}{x!}$$

To assure convergence of the maximum likelihood fitting procedure, the actuary plots three quantities of interest across different values of $\theta$:

- Plot I: Score Function, $U$ — a decreasing curve that crosses zero.
- Plot II: Deviance — a convex curve with a single minimum.
- Plot III: Information, $J$ — a positive curve that decreases monotonically.

Determine which of the three plots the actuary can use to visually approximate the maximum likelihood estimate for $\theta$.

- A) None can be used
- B) I and II only
- C) I and III only
- D) II and III only
- E) The answer is not given by (A), (B), (C), or (D)

## Explanation

The MLE is the value of $\theta$ that maximizes the log-likelihood. Each plot relates to that optimum differently:

- **Plot I (Score function $U$):** the score is the first derivative of the log-likelihood, and the MLE occurs where $U(\theta) = 0$. Since this curve crosses zero, the MLE can be read off — **usable**.
- **Plot II (Deviance):** deviance is minimized at the MLE, so its minimum locates $\hat\theta$ — **usable**.
- **Plot III (Information $J$):** the Fisher information measures the curvature (precision) of the estimate, not the location of the maximum; its monotone shape has no special feature at the MLE — **not usable** to find $\hat\theta$.

Plots I and II can both be used, so the answer is **B**.
