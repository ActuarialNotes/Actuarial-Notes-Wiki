You are given the following information:

- There are two independent lives (35) and (55)
- The mortality of (35) follows the Illustrative Life Table
- The mortality of (55) follows the Illustrative Life Table, except that the annualized mortality after age 80 stays constant at the age 80 rate, where $q_{80} = 0.0803$

Calculate the probability that (35) is the only one of the two that lives to age 90.

- A) Less than 0.075
- B) At least 0.075, but less than 0.085
- C) At least 0.085, but less than 0.095
- D) At least 0.095, but less than 0.105
- E) At least 0.105

## Explanation

The event is: (35) survives to 90 **and** (55) does not. By independence,

$$\Pr = {}_{55}p_{35} \cdot \big(1 - {}_{35}p_{55}\big).$$

**(35) surviving to 90** uses the ordinary Illustrative Life Table:

$${}_{55}p_{35} = \frac{l_{90}}{l_{35}} = \frac{1{,}058{,}491}{9{,}420{,}657} = 0.11236.$$

**(55) surviving to 90** uses the table up to age 80, then a constant $q_{80} = 0.0803$ (so $p = 0.9197$) for the last 10 years:

$${}_{35}p_{55} = \frac{l_{80}}{l_{55}} \cdot (0.9197)^{10} = \frac{3{,}914{,}365}{8{,}640{,}861}\,(0.9197)^{10} = 0.45299 \times 0.43308 = 0.19618.$$

$$\Pr = 0.11236 \times (1 - 0.19618) = 0.11236 \times 0.80382 = 0.0903.$$

This falls in $[0.085, 0.095)$, so the answer is **C**.
