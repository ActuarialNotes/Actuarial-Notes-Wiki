Insurance claims are made according to a Poisson process with rate $\lambda$.

Calculate the probability that exactly 3 claims were made by time $t = 1$, given that exactly 6 claims are made by time $t = 2$.

- A) Less than 0.3
- B) At least 0.3, but less than 0.4
- C) At least 0.4, but less than 0.5
- D) At least 0.5, but less than 0.6
- E) At least 0.6

## Explanation

For a Poisson process, given that $n$ events occur in the interval $[0, t]$, the number of events in a subinterval $[0, s]$ (with $s < t$) is **Binomial** with $n$ trials and success probability $s/t$. Each of the $6$ claims observed by time $t = 2$ independently falls in $[0, 1]$ with probability

$$p = \frac{1}{2}.$$

Therefore, conditional on $N(2) = 6$,

$$N(1) \mid \{N(2) = 6\} \sim \text{Binomial}\left(6, \tfrac{1}{2}\right).$$

The required probability is

$$\Pr[N(1) = 3 \mid N(2) = 6] = \binom{6}{3}\left(\tfrac{1}{2}\right)^3\left(\tfrac{1}{2}\right)^3 = \frac{20}{64} = 0.3125.$$

Note that the rate $\lambda$ cancels out and is not needed. The result falls in $[0.3, 0.4)$, so the answer is **B**.
