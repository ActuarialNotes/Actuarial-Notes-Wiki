You are given the following information:

- A random variable, $X$, is uniformly distributed on the interval $(0, \theta)$.
- $\theta$ is unknown.
- For a random sample of size $n$, an estimate of $\theta$ is $Y_n = \max\{X_1, X_2, \ldots, X_n\}$.

Determine which of the following is a consistent estimator of $\theta$.

- A) $Y_n$
- B) $Y_n / (n - 1)$
- C) $Y_n \cdot (n + 1)$
- D) $Y_n \cdot (n + 1) \cdot (n - 1)$
- E) $Y_n / (n + 1)$

## Explanation

An estimator is **consistent** if it converges in probability to $\theta$ as $n \to \infty$. For the sample maximum of $\text{Uniform}(0,\theta)$,

$$E[Y_n] = \frac{n}{n+1}\theta \longrightarrow \theta, \qquad \operatorname{Var}(Y_n) = \frac{n}{(n+1)^2(n+2)}\theta^2 \longrightarrow 0.$$

Since the bias and variance both vanish, $Y_n \xrightarrow{p} \theta$: the sample maximum itself is consistent.

Checking the alternatives confirms they are not:
- $Y_n/(n-1) \to 0$, $Y_n/(n+1) \to 0$ (both shrink to zero),
- $Y_n\cdot(n+1) \to \infty$ and $Y_n\cdot(n+1)\cdot(n-1) \to \infty$ (both diverge).

Only $Y_n$ converges to $\theta$, so the answer is **A**.
