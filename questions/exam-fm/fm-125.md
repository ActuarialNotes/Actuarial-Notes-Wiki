A borrower took out a loan of 100,000 and promised to repay it with a payment at the end of each year for 30 years.

The amount of each of the first ten payments equals the amount of interest due. The amount of each of the next ten payments equals 150% of the amount of interest due. The amount of each of the last ten payments is $X$.

The lender charges interest at an annual effective rate of 10%.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 3,204
- B) 5,675
- C) 7,073
- D) 9,744
- E) 11,746

## Explanation

During the first ten years, payments equal interest only (no principal reduction), so the balance stays at 100,000.

During years 11–20, payments are $1.5 \times 10\% \times \text{balance} = 0.15 \times \text{balance}$, so the principal paid each year is $0.05 \times \text{balance}$. The balance decays by a factor of 0.95 each year. After 10 more years: $100,000(0.95)^{10} = 59,874$.

Then $X = 59,874 / a_{\overline{10}|0.10} = 59,874 / 6.14457 = 9,744$.
