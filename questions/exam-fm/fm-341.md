An annuity writer sells an annual perpetuity-due, with a first payment of 3000. The payments increase by 7% annually.

The purchase price, $P$, of the perpetuity is based on a 12% annual force of interest.

Calculate $P$.

- A) 41,096
- B) 52,177
- C) 58,829
- D) 60,000
- E) 67,200

## Explanation

Annual effective rate: $i = e^{0.12}-1 = 0.12749685$.

PV of geometrically increasing perpetuity-due (first payment 3000, growth 7%):
$$P = \frac{3000}{i-g}\cdot(1+i) = \frac{3000}{0.12749685-0.07}\cdot(1.12749685) = \frac{3000}{0.05749685}\cdot(1.12749685)$$

$$= 52{,}176\cdot(1.12749685) = 58{,}829.14$$
