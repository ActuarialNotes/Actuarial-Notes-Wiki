A 15-year loan of 60,000 is to be repaid with payments of $X$ at the end of each month based on an annual nominal interest rate of 7.5%, convertible monthly.

When the loan balance is 49,893, the loan is refinanced at an annual nominal interest rate of 6.0%, convertible monthly. Payments remain at $X$ and are paid at the end of each month for as long as necessary, with a smaller final payment.

Calculate the total number of payments, including the smaller final payment.

- A) 162
- B) 164
- C) 166
- D) 168
- E) 170

## Explanation

Monthly rate = 0.625%, so the original payment is:

$$X = \frac{60{,}000}{a_{\overline{180}|0.00625}} = 556.21$$

When the balance is 49,893, finding the number of remaining original payments:

$$\begin{align*}
49{,}893 &= 556.21\,a_{\overline{m}|0.00625} \\
m &= 132 \text{ remaining, so } 180 - 132 = 48 \text{ paid}
\end{align*}$$

After refinancing at 0.5%/month, finding the new number of payments:

$$\begin{align*}
49{,}893 &= 556.21\,a_{\overline{n}|0.005} \\
n &= 119.3
\end{align*}$$

So 120 more payments are needed (119 full + 1 smaller final payment).

Total: $48 + 120 = 168$.
