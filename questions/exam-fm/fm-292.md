An investor purchases a 30-year bond with a face amount of 1000 and annual coupon rate of 8% paid semi-annually. The bond is callable at its face value any time following the coupon payment occurring at the end of the 15th year.

The bond is bought at a premium based on an annual effective yield rate of 7%.

Calculate the purchase price of the bond.

- A) 1067
- B) 1104
- C) 1132
- D) 1141
- E) 1154

## Explanation

Since the bond is at a premium (coupon 8% > yield 7%), the worst case for the investor is the earliest redemption date (end of year 15). The semiannual yield: $(1+j)^2 = 1.07 \implies j = 0.034408$.

$$P = 40a_{\overline{30}|0.034408} + 1000(1.034408)^{-30} = 40(19.3409) + 1000(0.36069)$$

$$= 773.64 + 360.69 = 1103.61 \approx 1104$$

Note: This assumes 30 semiannual periods = 15 years (the first call date). With price > par, worst case is earliest call.
