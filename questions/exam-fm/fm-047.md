A 40-year bond is purchased at a discount. The bond pays annual coupons. The amount for accumulation of discount in the 15th coupon is 194.82. The amount for accumulation of discount in the 20th coupon is 306.69.

Calculate the amount of discount in the purchase price of this bond.

- A) 13,635
- B) 13,834
- C) 16,098
- D) 19,301
- E) 21,135

## Explanation

Let $F$, $C$, $r$, and $i$ have their usual meanings. The discount in coupon $t$ is $(Ci - Fr)v^{n-t+1}$.

$$194.82 = (Ci-Fr)v^{26}$$
$$306.69 = (Ci-Fr)v^{21}$$

Dividing: $0.63523 = v^5 \implies v = 0.91324 \implies i = 0.095$.

Then:

$$(Ci-Fr) = 194.82(1.095)^{26} = 2062.53$$

Total discount $= (Ci-Fr)\,a_{\overline{40}|0.095} = 2062.53\,a_{\overline{40}|0.095} = 21,135$$
