You are given the following information about two bonds, Bond A and Bond B:

(i) Each bond is a 10-year bond with semiannual coupons redeemable at its par value of 10,000, and is bought to yield an annual nominal interest rate of $i$, convertible semiannually.

(ii) Bond A has an annual coupon rate of $(i + 0.04)$, paid semiannually.

(iii) Bond B has an annual coupon rate of $(i - 0.04)$, paid semiannually.

(iv) The price of Bond A is 5,341.12 greater than the price of Bond B.

Calculate $i$.

- A) 0.042
- B) 0.043
- C) 0.081
- D) 0.084
- E) 0.086

## Explanation

Let $j = i/2$ (semiannual yield rate). The semiannual coupon for Bond A is $10,000(j + 0.02)$ and for Bond B is $10,000(j - 0.02)$.

Both bonds have the same yield and redemption, so:

$$P_A - P_B = 10,000[(j+0.02) - (j-0.02)]\,a_{\overline{20}|j} = 10,000(0.04)\,a_{\overline{20}|j} = 400\,a_{\overline{20}|j}$$

Setting equal to 5,341.12:
$$a_{\overline{20}|j} = \frac{5,341.12}{400} = 13.3528$$

Using a financial calculator: $j = 0.042$, so $i = 2(0.042) = 0.084$.
