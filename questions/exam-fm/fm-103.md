Which of the following statements regarding immunization are true?

  I. If long-term interest rates are lower than short-term rates, the need for immunization is reduced.
  II. Either Macaulay or modified duration can be used to develop an immunization strategy.
  III. Both processes of matching the present values of the flows or the flows themselves will produce exact matching.

- A) I only
- B) II only
- C) III only
- D) I, II and III
- E) The correct answer is not given by (A), (B), (C), or (D).

## Explanation

**I. False.** The yield curve structure (whether rates are upward or downward sloping) is not relevant to the need for immunization. Immunization protects against small changes in interest rates regardless of the yield curve shape.

**II. True.** Both Macaulay duration and modified duration can be used to match the duration of assets to liabilities, thereby developing an immunization strategy.

**III. False.** Matching the present values of cash flows (duration matching) does not produce exact matching when interest rates change. Only exact cash flow matching produces exact matching; duration matching only provides protection for small interest rate movements.
