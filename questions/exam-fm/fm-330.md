At time $t = 0$, 3 is deposited into an investment fund earning interest at a force of interest $\delta_t = 1/(t+1)$.

At the same time, $X$ is deposited into a bank account paying interest at an annual effective interest rate of 5%.

In the third year, the interest earned on the first account and the interest credited on the second account are exactly the same.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 5.5
- B) 8.2
- C) 13.0
- D) 25.7
- E) 54.4

## Explanation

Fund 1 accumulation: $a(t) = e^{\int_0^t 1/(r+1)dr} = e^{\ln(t+1)} = t+1$.

Interest earned in year 3 (from $t=2$ to $t=3$):

$$3[a(3)-a(2)] = 3[(3+1)-(2+1)] = 3(4-3) = 3$$

Fund 2 interest in year 3:

$$X(1.05^3 - 1.05^2) = X \cdot 1.05^2(0.05) = 0.055125X$$

Setting equal:

$$\begin{align*}
0.055125X &= 3 \\
X &= 54.42 \approx 54.4
\end{align*}$$
