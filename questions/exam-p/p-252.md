The independent random variables $X$ and $Y$ have the same mean. The coefficients of variation of $X$ and $Y$ are 3 and 4, respectively.

Calculate the coefficient of variation of $\frac{1}{2}(X+Y)$.

- A) $5/4$
- B) $7/4$
- C) $5/2$
- D) $7/2$
- E) $7$

## Explanation

Let mean $= \mu$. Then $\sigma_X = 3\mu$ and $\sigma_Y = 4\mu$.

Let $W = \tfrac{1}{2}(X+Y)$. Then $E[W] = \mu$ and:

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{Var}(W) &= \frac{1}{4}(\sigma_X^2 + \sigma_Y^2) \\
              &= \frac{\mu^2(9+16)}{4} \\
              &= \frac{25\mu^2}{4}
\end{align*}
$$

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{CV}(W) &= \frac{\text{SD}(W)}{E[W]} \\
             &= \frac{5\mu/2}{\mu} \\
             &= \mathbf{\frac{5}{2}}
\end{align*}
$$
