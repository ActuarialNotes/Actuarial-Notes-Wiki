Companies P, Q, and R use routes that take their trucks through a common inspection checkpoint each day. Company P has 4 trucks, Company Q has 3 trucks, and Company R has 2 trucks passing the checkpoint daily.

Calculate the probability that at least one of two randomly chosen trucks is from Company P.

- A) 0.28
- B) 0.31
- C) 0.56
- D) 0.69
- E) 0.72

## Explanation

Total trucks: 9. $\binom{9}{2}=36$.

$P(\text{none from P}) = \binom{5}{2}/\binom{9}{2} = 10/36$

$$P(\text{at least one from P}) = 1 - 10/36 = 26/36 = 13/18 \approx \mathbf{0.72}$$
