An insurance company categorizes its policyholders into three mutually exclusive groups:

| Group | Policyholders | $P$(no claims) |
|---|---|---|
| A | 20,000 | 70% |
| B | 45,000 | 90% |
| C | 35,000 | 50% |

Within each group, claim counts follow independent Poisson distributions. Calculate the expected total number of claims, in thousands, made by all 100,000 policyholders.

- A) 21
- B) 28
- C) 36
- D) 64
- E) 72

## Explanation

For a Poisson($\lambda$) distribution, $P(\text{no claims}) = e^{-\lambda}$, so:

$$\lambda_A = -\ln(0.70) \approx 0.3567$$

$$\lambda_B = -\ln(0.90) \approx 0.1054$$

$$\lambda_C = -\ln(0.50) \approx 0.6931$$

Expected total claims (in thousands):

$$20(0.3567) + 45(0.1054) + 35(0.6931)$$

$$= 7.13 + 4.74 + 24.26 = \mathbf{36.1 \approx 36}$$
