Math SAT scores are normally distributed and when reported are rounded to multiples of ten.

In 1982 Abby's mother scored at the rounded $93^{\text{rd}}$ percentile on the math SAT exam. In 1982 the mean score was 503 and the variance of the scores was 9604.

In 2008 Abby took the math SAT and got the same rounded numerical score as her mother had received 26 years before. In 2008 the mean score was 521 and the variance of the scores was 10,201.

Calculate the percentile corresponding to Abby's rounded score.

- A) $89^{\text{th}}$
- B) $90^{\text{th}}$
- C) $91^{\text{st}}$
- D) $92^{\text{nd}}$
- E) $93^{\text{rd}}$

## Explanation

For the 1982 exam:
Mean $\mu = 503$, Standard Deviation $\sigma = \sqrt{9604} = 98$.
The $93^{\text{rd}}$ percentile of the standard normal distribution is at a z-score of approximately $1.475$.
Score = $\mu + z\sigma = 503 + 1.475(98) = 503 + 144.55 = 647.55$.
When rounded to the nearest multiple of ten, the mother's score was 650.

For the 2008 exam:
Abby's score is also 650.
Mean $\mu = 521$, Standard Deviation $\sigma = \sqrt{10,201} = 101$.
Abby's z-score is:
$$z = \frac{650 - 521}{101} = \frac{129}{101} \approx 1.277$$

From the standard normal table, the probability associated with $z=1.28$ is approximately $0.8997$.
This corresponds to the $90^{\text{th}}$ percentile.
