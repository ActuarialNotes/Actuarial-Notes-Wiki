# FCA COVID-19 BI Test Case — Supreme Court Ruling (2021)

## Overview
The UK Supreme Court ruled substantially in favour of policyholders in the FCA's expedited test case on COVID-19 business interruption coverage, resolving disputed non-damage BI wordings affecting roughly 370,000 policyholders, 700 policy types, and 60 insurers.

## Key Details
*   **Disease & Prevention-of-Access Clauses:** Broad readings adopted; cover triggered by local occurrences of a notifiable disease forming part of a wider outbreak.
*   **Causation Rewritten:** The Court departed from the *Orient-Express* "but for" approach, holding that concurrent uninsured causes (the wider pandemic) do not defeat cover.
*   **Trends Clauses:** Pandemic-driven downturns could not be used to strip indemnity back out of the loss calculation.

## Multi-Agent Execution Mandates
*   **Claims & Legal:** Forced reopening and payment of declined BI claims; established the template for parallel litigation in Canada, Australia, and the US.
*   **Underwriting & Product:** Triggered a global rewrite of infectious disease and non-damage BI wordings, with explicit pandemic exclusions becoming near-universal at subsequent renewals.
*   **Reinsurance:** Sharpened scrutiny of silent pandemic exposure across property treaties.
