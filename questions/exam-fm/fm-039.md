Joe must pay liabilities of 1,000 due one year from now and another 2,000 due three years from now. There are two available investments:

**Bond I:** A one-year zero-coupon bond that matures for 1000. The yield rate is 6% per year.

**Bond II:** A two-year zero-coupon bond with face amount of 1,000. The yield rate is 7% per year.

At the present time the one-year forward rate for an investment made two years from now is 6.5%.

Joe plans to buy amounts of each bond and reinvest the proceeds from Bond II in a one-year zero-coupon bond. Assuming the reinvestment earns the forward rate, calculate the total purchase price of Bond I and Bond II where the amounts are selected to exactly match the liabilities.

- A) 2584
- B) 2697
- C) 2801
- D) 2907
- E) 3000

## Explanation

**Bond I** provides 1000 at time 1. Purchase one unit at cost $1000/1.06 = 943.40$.

**Bond II** matures at time 2, and proceeds are reinvested for one year at the forward rate of 6.5% to match the 2000 liability at time 3. The amount to reinvest at time 2 is $2000/1.065 = 1877.93$. The purchase price of Bond II is:

$$\frac{1877.93}{1.07^2} = 1640.26$$

Total purchase price $= 943.40 + 1640.26 = 2583.66$
