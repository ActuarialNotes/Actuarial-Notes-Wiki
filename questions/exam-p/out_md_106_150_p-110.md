The probability of $x$ losses occurring in year 1 is $(0.5)^{x+1}$ for $x=0,1,2,\dots$

The probability of $y$ losses in year 2 given $x$ losses in year 1 is given by the table:

| Number of losses in year 1 ($x$) | Number of losses in year 2 ($y$) given $x$ losses in year 1 | | | | |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| | **0** | **1** | **2** | **3** | **4+** |
| **0** | 0.60 | 0.25 | 0.05 | 0.05 | 0.05 |
| **1** | 0.45 | 0.30 | 0.10 | 0.10 | 0.05 |
| **2** | 0.25 | 0.20 | 0.30 | 0.20 | 0.05 |
| **3** | 0.15 | 0.20 | 0.20 | 0.30 | 0.15 |
| **4+** | 0.05 | 0.15 | 0.35 | 0.25 | 0.20 |

Calculate the probability of exactly 2 losses in 2 years.

- A) 0.025
- B) 0.031
- C) 0.075
- D) 0.100
- E) 0.131

## Explanation

We want to find $P(X + Y = 2)$, where $X$ is the number of losses in year 1 and $Y$ is the number of losses in year 2.
The possible combinations $(x, y)$ that sum to 2 are $(0, 2)$, $(1, 1)$, and $(2, 0)$.

First, calculate the marginal probabilities for year 1 ($X$):
* $P(X=0) = (0.5)^{0+1} = 0.5$
* $P(X=1) = (0.5)^{1+1} = 0.25$
* $P(X=2) = (0.5)^{2+1} = 0.125$

Use the law of total probability with the conditional probabilities from the table:
* $P(X=0, Y=2) = P(X=0) P(Y=2 \mid X=0) = 0.5 \times 0.05 = 0.025$
* $P(X=1, Y=1) = P(X=1) P(Y=1 \mid X=1) = 0.25 \times 0.30 = 0.075$
* $P(X=2, Y=0) = P(X=2) P(Y=0 \mid X=2) = 0.125 \times 0.25 = 0.03125$

Total probability:
$$P(X + Y = 2) = 0.025 + 0.075 + 0.03125 = 0.13125 \approx 0.131$$
