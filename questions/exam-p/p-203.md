Four distinct integers are chosen randomly from the first twelve positive integers. Let $X$ be the second smallest. Determine $p(x)$ for $x=2,\ldots,10$.

- A) $\frac{(x-1)(11-x)(12-x)}{495}$
- B) $\frac{(x-1)(12-x)(11-x)}{990}$
- C) $\frac{(x-1)(x-2)(12-x)}{990}$
- D) $\frac{(x-1)(x-2)(12-x)}{495}$
- E) $\frac{(10-x)(11-x)(12-x)}{495}$

## Explanation

For $X=x$: choose 1 from $\{1,\ldots,x-1\}$, include $x$, choose 2 from $\{x+1,\ldots,12\}$:
$$p(x)=\frac{\binom{x-1}{1}\binom{12-x}{2}}{\binom{12}{4}}=\frac{(x-1)\cdot\frac{(12-x)(11-x)}{2}}{495}=\frac{(x-1)(12-x)(11-x)}{990}$$
