Bond A and Bond B are both annual coupon, five-year, 10,000 par value bonds bought to yield an annual effective rate of 4%.

  i) Bond A has an annual coupon rate of $r\%$, a redemption value that is 10% below par, and a price of $P$.
  ii) Bond B has an annual coupon rate of $(r+1)\%$, a redemption value that is 10% above par, and a price of $1.2P$.

Calculate $r\%$.

- A) 5.85%
- B) 6.85%
- C) 7.85%
- D) 8.85%
- E) 9.85%

## Explanation

Bond A: $P = 10,000r \cdot a_{\overline{5}|0.04} + 9,000(1.04)^{-5} = 44,518.22r + 7,397.34$

Bond B: $1.2P = 10,000(r+0.01)a_{\overline{5}|0.04} + 11,000(1.04)^{-5} = 44,518.22(r+0.01) + 9,041.76 = 44,518.22r + 9,486.38$

Subtracting: $0.2P = 2089.04 \implies P = 10,445.20$.

Substituting: $10,445.20 = 44,518.22r + 7,397.34 \implies r = 3048/44,518 = 0.0685 = 6.85\%$
