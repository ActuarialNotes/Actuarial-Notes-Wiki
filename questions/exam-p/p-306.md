An insurance company sells policies for which insured loss amounts follow an exponential distribution. 40% of policies are sold to home renters (time until cancellation $\sim \text{Exp}(\text{mean }4)$) and 60% to apartment renters (time until cancellation $\sim \text{Exp}(\text{mean }2)$).

Calculate the probability that the policyholder is a home renter, given that a renter still has a policy one year after purchase.

- A) 0.08
- B) 0.27
- C) 0.46
- D) 0.56
- E) 0.66

## Explanation

$P(\text{still enrolled}\mid \text{home}) = e^{-1/4}$, $P(\text{still}\mid\text{apt}) = e^{-1/2}$.

$$P(\text{still enrolled}) = 0.40e^{-1/4}+0.60e^{-1/2}$$

$$\approx 0.40(0.7788)+0.60(0.6065) = 0.3115+0.3639 = 0.6754$$

$$P(\text{home}\mid\text{still}) = \frac{0.3115}{0.6754} \approx \mathbf{0.46}$$
