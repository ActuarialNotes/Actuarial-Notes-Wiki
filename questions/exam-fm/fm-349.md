The annual force of interest is $\delta_t = \dfrac{2}{10-t}$, for $0 \le t < 10$, in which $t$ is measured in years.

Calculate the equivalent annual nominal discount rate compounded every two years for the period $2.0 \le t \le 2.4$.

- A) 20%
- B) 23%
- C) 27%
- D) 29%
- E) 34%

## Explanation

Accumulation factor from $t=2$ to $t=2.4$:
$$\exp\!\left(\int_2^{2.4}\frac{2}{10-t}\,dt\right) = \exp\!\left[-2\ln(10-t)\Big|_2^{2.4}\right] = \exp[-2\ln(7.6)+2\ln(8)] = \left(\frac{8}{7.6}\right)^2$$

The equivalent biennial (2-year) discount factor $d^{(1/2)}$ for a 0.4-year sub-period:

$\left(1-\dfrac{d^{(1/2)}}{0.5}\right)^{-0.5(0.4)} = \left(\dfrac{8}{7.6}\right)^2$

$\left(1-2d^{(1/2)}\right)^{-0.2} = \left(\dfrac{8}{7.6}\right)^2$

$1-2d^{(1/2)} = \left(\dfrac{7.6}{8}\right)^{10} = (0.95)^{10} = 0.59874$

$d^{(1/2)} = (1-0.59874)/2 = 0.20063 \approx 20\%$
