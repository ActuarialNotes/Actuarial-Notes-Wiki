You are given the following information about a loan of $L$ that is to be repaid with a series of 16 annual payments:

(i) The first payment of 2000 is due one year from now.

(ii) The next seven payments are each 3% larger than the preceding payment.

(iii) From the 9th to the 16th payment, each payment will be 3% less than the preceding payment.

(iv) The loan has an annual effective interest rate of 7%.

Calculate $L$.

- A) 20,689
- B) 20,716
- C) 20,775
- D) 21,147
- E) 22,137

## Explanation

**First 8 payments** (geometrically increasing at 3%):

$$PV_1 = \frac{2000v - 2000(1.03)^8v^9}{1-1.03v} = 13,136.41$$

**Last 8 payments** (starting from $2000(1.03)^7$ then decreasing at 3%):

$$PV_2 = \frac{2000(1.03)^7(0.97)v^9 - 2000(1.03)^7(0.97)^9v^{17}}{1-0.97v} = 7,552.22$$

$$L = PV_1 + PV_2 = 13,136.41 + 7,552.22 = 20,688.63$$
