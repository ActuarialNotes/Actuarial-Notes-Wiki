An insurance company must pay liabilities of 99 at the end of one year, 102 at the end of two years, and 100 at the end of three years. The only investments available are three bonds (par value 100, redeemed at par):

| Bond | Maturity (years) | Yield-to-Maturity | Coupon Rate |
|------|-----------------|-------------------|-------------|
| A    | 1               | 6%                | 7%          |
| B    | 2               | 7%                | 0%          |
| C    | 3               | 9%                | 5%          |

Calculate the number of units of Bond A that must be purchased to match the liabilities exactly.

- A) 0.8807
- B) 0.8901
- C) 0.8975
- D) 0.9524
- E) 0.9724

## Explanation

Work backwards from the latest liability. Let $N_A$, $N_B$, $N_C$ be units purchased.

**Bond C** is the only bond with cash flows at time 3 (pays 105 at time 3, plus 5 at times 1 and 2). To match the time-3 liability of 100:
$$N_C(105) = 100 \implies N_C = 0.9524$$

Bond C also pays $0.9524(5) = 4.762$ at times 1 and 2.

**Bond B** (zero-coupon) matches the remaining time-2 liability. Need $102 - 4.762 = 97.238$ at time 2:
$$N_B(100) = 97.238 \implies N_B = 0.9724$$

**Bond A** matches the remaining time-1 liability. Bond A pays 107 at time 1. Remaining need: $99 - 4.762 - 0 = 94.238$ at time 1 (Bond B pays nothing at time 1):
$$N_A(107) = 99 - 0.9524(5) \implies N_A = \frac{94.238}{107} = 0.8807$$
