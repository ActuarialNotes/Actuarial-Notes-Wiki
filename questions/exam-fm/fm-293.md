The present value of cash flows at an effective interest rate of $i$ is given by the following function:

$$P(i) = 100 + 500(1+i)^{-3} - 1000(1+i)^{-4}, \text{ for } i > 0.$$

Determine which of the following expressions represents the modified duration of these cash flows.

- A) $\dfrac{1500(1+i)^{-4} - 4000(1+i)^{-5}}{100 + 500(1+i)^{-3} - 1000(1+i)^{-4}}$
- B) $\dfrac{1500(1+i)^{-3} - 4000(1+i)^{-4}}{100 + 500(1+i)^{-3} - 1000(1+i)^{-4}}$
- C) $\dfrac{-1500(1+i)^{-4} + 4000(1+i)^{-5}}{100 + 500(1+i)^{-3} - 1000(1+i)^{-4}}$
- D) $\dfrac{-1500(1+i)^{-3} + 4000(1+i)^{-4}}{100 + 500(1+i)^{-3} - 1000(1+i)^{-4}}$
- E) $\dfrac{-6000(1+i)^{-5} + 20000(1+i)^{-6}}{100 + 500(1+i)^{-3} - 1000(1+i)^{-4}}$

## Explanation

Modified duration $= -P'(i)/P(i)$.

$$P'(i) = -3 \cdot 500(1+i)^{-4} + 4 \cdot 1000(1+i)^{-5} = -1500(1+i)^{-4} + 4000(1+i)^{-5}$$

$$-P'(i) = 1500(1+i)^{-4} - 4000(1+i)^{-5}$$

$$D_{MOD} = \frac{1500(1+i)^{-4} - 4000(1+i)^{-5}}{100 + 500(1+i)^{-3} - 1000(1+i)^{-4}}$$
