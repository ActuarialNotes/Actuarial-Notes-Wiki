A loan of amount $a_{\overline{4}|i}$ is repaid with payments of 1 at the end of each year for four years. The sum of the interest paid in the last two years is equal to $(1+i)^2$ times the sum of the principal repaid in the first two years.

Calculate $i$.

- A) 0.543
- B) 0.567
- C) 0.592
- D) 0.618
- E) 0.645

## Explanation

Interest at time $k$: $I_k = 1 - v^{n-k+1}$. Principal: $P_k = v^{n-k+1}$.

$(I_3 + I_4) = (1+i)^2(P_1 + P_2)$:

$(1-v^2)+(1-v) = (1+i)^2(v^4+v^3)$

$(2-v-v^2) = (1+i)^2 v^3(v+1)$

Since $(1+i)^2 v^2 = 1$: $(2-v-v^2) = v^3(v+1)/v^2 \cdot v^2 = (v^4+v^3)$... 

Using $v^2(1-v^2+1-v) = v^4+v^3$ gives $v^2(2-v^2-v) = v^3(v+1)$, so $2-v-v^2 = v(v+1) = v^2+v$, giving $2-v-v^2-v^2-v = 0$: $2v^2+2v-2=0$, $v^2+v-1=0$, $v = (-1+\sqrt{5})/2 = 0.61803$, $i = 0.61803$.
