# Hurricane Fiona (September 2022)

## Overview
Post-tropical storm Fiona struck Atlantic Canada with record-low central pressure for a Canadian landfall, causing roughly $800 million in insured losses — the costliest extreme weather event ever recorded in Atlantic Canada.

## Key Details
*   **Coverage Gap at the Coast:** Homes swept away by storm surge (notably Port aux Basques) were largely uninsured for coastal flood, repeating the BC 2021 protection-gap pattern on the east coast.
*   **Wind vs. Water Allocation:** Claims required careful causation splitting between covered wind damage and excluded surge/flood — a recurring adjusting and litigation flashpoint.
*   **Government Backstop:** Federal and provincial disaster financial assistance covered significant uninsured losses, intensifying policy discussion on the national flood program's scope.

## Multi-Agent Execution Mandates
*   **Claims:** Activated regional catastrophe response and out-of-province adjuster licensing accommodations.
*   **Actuarial & Underwriting:** Hurricane and surge loadings for Atlantic Canada re-examined; Canadian portfolios could no longer treat severe tropical-transition events as a US-only peril.
