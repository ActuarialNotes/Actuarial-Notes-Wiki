# FSRA Auto Reform Implementation Guidance (February 2026)

## Overview
With the July 1, 2026 SABS optionality framework approaching, FSRA published consolidated implementation guidance for insurers — an insurer Q&A (updated February 12, 2026), the suite of FSRA-approved updated forms, an industry communications toolkit, and Oliver Wyman reform costing reports — operationalizing the changes made under O. Reg. 383/24.

## Key Details
*   **Universal Application:** The July 1 changes apply to all customers, not just new business; post-reform rules govern any accident occurring on or after the effective date, even on pre-reform policies.
*   **OPCF 47R Mechanics:** Policy-level endorsement with flexibility for vehicle-specific detail; FSRA does not require retrofitting it onto in-force pre-July policies, creating a transitional window where OPCF 47 and 47R coexist — flagged by adjusters as a priority-dispute risk.
*   **Highest-Available-Coverage Principle:** Where a claimant qualifies under multiple policies with different optional limits, FSRA expects fair treatment ensuring access to the coverage level of the policy they elect to claim under.
*   **Market Readiness Apparatus:** An industry-built two-hour training course (IBAO/IIC/OMIA), broker conduct expectations from RIBO and FSRA emphasizing early customer awareness rather than renewal-only messaging, and documentation standards to manage E&O exposure on opt-out advice.

## Multi-Agent Execution Mandates
*   **Claims:** Transition-window triage rules: confirm responding policy's effective date and 47/47R status before allocating optional benefits; priority-of-payment under s. 268 unchanged but election mechanics differ.
*   **Distribution & CX:** Proactive pre-renewal outreach campaigns with documented needs-based advice on each optional benefit decision.
*   **Actuarial & Product:** Final rate and form filings aligned to FSRA's approved endorsement suite and the Oliver Wyman costing benchmarks.
*   **IT:** Quoting, PAS, and document-generation systems certified against the final forms before the July 1 cutover.
