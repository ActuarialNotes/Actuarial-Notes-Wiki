The time to death (in years) of a 70-year-old person is modeled by a random variable $X$ with probability density function
$$f(x) = \frac{k}{(x+5)^2}, \quad 0 < x < 30$$

Calculate the probability that the man will live five years and then die during the following five years.

- A) 0.004
- B) 0.194
- C) 0.333
- D) 0.583
- E) 0.778

## Explanation

Normalizing constant: $\int_0^{30} k/(x+5)^2 dx = k[1/5-1/35] = k(6/35) = 1$, so $k = 35/6$.

$$
\begin{align*}
P(5 < X < 10) &= \frac{35}{6}\int_5^{10}\frac{dx}{(x+5)^2} \\
              &= \frac{35}{6}\left[\frac{1}{10}-\frac{1}{15}\right] \\
              &= \frac{35}{6} \cdot \frac{1}{30} \\
              &= \frac{35}{180} \\
              &= \frac{7}{36} \\
              &\approx \mathbf{0.194}
\end{align*}
$$
