A ten-year certificate of deposit pays an annual effective interest rate of 8%. If the balance is withdrawn before the end of ten years, the purchaser can choose between two different penalties:

  i) a loss of the last nine months' interest.
  ii) a reduction in the annual effective rate of interest to $j$.

If the purchaser withdraws the funds after three years, the two penalties are equivalent.

Calculate $j$.

- A) 5.74%
- B) 5.94%
- C) 6.14%
- D) 6.34%
- E) 6.54%

## Explanation

Penalty (i) — loss of last 9 months' interest. After 3 years less 9 months = 2.25 years at 8%:
$$AV_i = 1.08^{2.25} = 1.08^{3-0.75} = 1.08^3 / 1.08^{0.75} = 1.189 $$

Penalty (ii) — earn rate $j$ for 3 years: $AV_{ii} = (1+j)^3$.

Setting equal: $(1+j)^3 = 1.08^{2.25} = 1.08^3/1.08^{0.75} = 1.259712/1.059217 = 1.18895$

$j = 1.18895^{1/3} - 1 = 1.05940^{0.333} - 1 \approx 5.94\%$
