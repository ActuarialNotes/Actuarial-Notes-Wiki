An insurance company has two divisions. Total annual claims $X$ in the auto division follow $N(10, 9)$ and total annual claims $Y$ in the property division follow $N(12, 16)$. Assume $X$ and $Y$ are independent. Calculate $P(X + Y \leq 29)$.

- A) 0.61
- B) 0.69
- C) 0.78
- D) 0.84
- E) 0.92

## Explanation

$X+Y \sim N(10+12,\; 9+16) = N(22, 25)$, so $\text{SD}(X+Y) = 5$.

$$
\begin{align*}
P(X+Y \leq 29) &= \Phi\!\left(\frac{29-22}{5}\right) \\
               &= \Phi(1.4) \\
               &\approx \mathbf{0.9192 \approx 0.92}
\end{align*}
$$
