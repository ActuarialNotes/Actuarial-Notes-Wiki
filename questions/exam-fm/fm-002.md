Kathryn deposits 100 into an account at the beginning of each 4-year period for 40 years. The account credits interest at an annual effective interest rate of $i$.

The accumulated amount in the account at the end of 40 years is $X$, which is 5 times the accumulated amount in the account at the end of 20 years.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 4695
- B) 5070
- C) 5445
- D) 5820
- E) 6195

## Explanation

From basic principles, the accumulated values after 20 and 40 years are:

$$100\left[(1+i)^{20} + (1+i)^{16} + \cdots + (1+i)^4\right] = 100\,\frac{(1+i)^4 - (1+i)^{24}}{1 - (1+i)^4}$$

$$100\left[(1+i)^{40} + (1+i)^{36} + \cdots + (1+i)^4\right] = 100\,\frac{(1+i)^4 - (1+i)^{44}}{1 - (1+i)^4}$$

The ratio is 5. Setting $x = (1+i)^4$:

$$5 = \frac{(1+i)^4 - (1+i)^{44}}{(1+i)^4 - (1+i)^{24}} = \frac{x - x^{11}}{x - x^6}$$

$$5x - 5x^6 = x - x^{11}$$

$$5 - 5x^5 = 1 - x^{10}$$

$$x^{10} - 5x^5 + 4 = 0$$

$$(x^5 - 1)(x^5 - 4) = 0$$

Only the second root gives a positive solution:

$$x^5 = 4 \implies x = 1.31951$$

$$X = 100\,\frac{1.31951 - 1.31951^{11}}{1 - 1.31951} = 6195$$

**Alternatively**, using annuity notation:

$$100\,\frac{s_{\overline{40}|}}{a_{\overline{4}|}} = 5(100)\,\frac{s_{\overline{20}|}}{a_{\overline{4}|}}$$

$$\frac{(1+i)^{40} - 1}{i} = 5\,\frac{(1+i)^{20} - 1}{i}$$

$$(1+i)^{40} - 5(1+i)^{20} + 4 = 0$$

$$(1+i)^{20} = 4$$

and the solution proceeds as above.
