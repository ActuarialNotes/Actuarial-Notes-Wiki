A health insurer determines that 35% of policyholders are smokers. Smokers file a claim with probability 0.40, and non-smokers with probability 0.10. Each claim has size 100.

Let $X$ be the annual claim for a randomly selected policyholder. Calculate the standard deviation of $X$.

- A) 35
- B) 40
- C) 43
- D) 47
- E) 53

## Explanation

$E[X] = 100[0.35(0.40)+0.65(0.10)] = 100(0.14+0.065) = 20.5$

$E[X^2] = 100^2[0.35(0.40)+0.65(0.10)] = 10000(0.205) = 2050$

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{Var}(X) &= 2050-20.5^2 \\
              &= 2050-420.25 \\
              &= 1629.75
\end{align*}
$$

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{SD}(X) &= \sqrt{1629.75} \\
             &\approx \mathbf{40.4 \approx 40}
\end{align*}
$$
