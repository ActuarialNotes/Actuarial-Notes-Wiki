A loan of 50,000 is based on an annual effective interest rate of 6%.

You are given:

  i) The loan is to be repaid with quarterly payments of 2125 and a final drop payment.
  ii) The first payment is due 1.5 years after the loan is taken out.

Calculate the number of payments of 2125 to be made.

- A) 28
- B) 29
- C) 30
- D) 31
- E) 32

## Explanation

Quarterly rate: $i^{(4)}/4 = 1.06^{1/4}-1 = 0.014674$.

The first payment is due 1.5 years = 6 quarters after the loan. The loan grows for 6 quarters before the first payment: $50{,}000(1.014674)^6 = 54{,}602.93$.

Wait — actually the loan was just taken out and grows to $50{,}000(1.014674)^5 = 53{,}798$ at 5 quarters (one period before the first payment), so the PV of the annuity at the time of first payment is $54{,}602.93$.

$54{,}602.93 = 2125 a_{\overline{n}|0.014674} \implies n = 31.86$, so **31 payments** of 2125.
