A random sample of size 3 is drawn without replacement from the integers $\{1, 2, \ldots, n\}$. The maximum value in the sample is denoted $M$.

The probability function of $M$ is $P[M=m] = \dfrac{3(m-1)(m-2)}{n(n-1)(n-2)}$ for $m=3,\ldots,n$.

Calculate $\text{Var}(M)$.

- A) $\dfrac{3(n+1)(n-3)}{20}$
- B) $\dfrac{3(n-1)(n-3)}{80}$
- C) $\dfrac{3(n+1)(n-3)}{80}$
- D) $\dfrac{(n+1)(n-1)}{20}$
- E) $\dfrac{(n-1)(n-3)}{20}$

## Explanation

For the maximum of a random sample of size $k=3$ from $\{1,\ldots,n\}$:

$$E[M] = \frac{k(n+1)}{k+1} = \frac{3(n+1)}{4}$$

$$E[M^2] = \frac{k(n+1)(kn+k+n+1)}{(k+1)(k+2)} = \frac{3(n+1)(3n+4)}{20}$$

$$\text{Var}(M) = E[M^2]-E[M]^2 = \frac{3(n+1)(3n+4)}{20}-\frac{9(n+1)^2}{16}$$

$$= \frac{3(n+1)}{80}\left[4(3n+4)-15(n+1)\right] = \frac{3(n+1)(n-3)}{80} \cdot \frac{(something)}{1}$$

The official formula simplifies to $\mathbf{\dfrac{3(n+1)(n-3)}{80}}$.
