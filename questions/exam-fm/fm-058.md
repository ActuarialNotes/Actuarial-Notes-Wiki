Trevor has assets at time 2 of $A$ and at time 9 of $B$. He has a liability of 95,000 at time 5. Trevor has achieved Redington immunization in his portfolio using an annual effective interest rate of 4%.

Calculate $\dfrac{A}{B}$.

- A) 0.7307
- B) 0.9670
- C) 1.0000
- D) 1.0132
- E) 1.3686

## Explanation

Redington immunization requires PV matching and duration matching (first-order).

Let $i = 0.04$, $v = 1/1.04$.

**PV of assets = PV of liability:**
$$Av^2 + Bv^9 = 95{,}000v^5$$
$$0.92456A + 0.70259B = 78{,}083$$

**Duration match (first derivative):**
$$-2Av^3 - 9Bv^{10} = -5(95{,}000)v^6$$
$$-1.7780A - 6.0801B = -375{,}400$$

Solving simultaneously:
$$B = 47{,}630,\quad A = 48{,}259$$

$$\frac{A}{B} = \frac{48{,}259}{47{,}630} = 1.0132$$
