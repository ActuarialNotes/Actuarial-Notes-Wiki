# The Life Assurance Act 1774

## Regulation Overview
Enacted to prevent speculative gambling on human lives, which had become common practice in 18th-century London. The statute declared that any insurance policy made where the policyholder lacked a legitimate interest in the life or event being insured was entirely null and void.

## Downstream Underwriting Impact
- **Mandatory Insurable Interest:** Created the foundational legal pillar requiring that an individual purchasing an insurance policy must suffer a financial loss if the insured event or death occurs.
- **Contractual Validation:** Agents must verify policy ownership and financial connectivity during the initial underwriting intake to avoid issuing non-compliant, un-enforceable contracts.
