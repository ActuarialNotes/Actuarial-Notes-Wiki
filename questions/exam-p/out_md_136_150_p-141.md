Losses covered by a flood insurance policy are uniformly distributed on the interval $[0, 2]$. The insurer pays the amount of the loss in excess of a deductible $d$.

The probability that the insurer pays at least 1.20 on a random loss is 0.30.

Calculate the probability that the insurer pays at least 1.44 on a random loss.

- A) 0.06
- B) 0.16
- C) 0.18
- D) 0.20
- E) 0.28

## Explanation

Let $X$ be the loss, uniformly distributed on $[0, 2]$. The payment is $Y = \max(0, X-d)$.

We are given:
$$P(Y \ge 1.20) = P(X - d \ge 1.20) = P(X \ge 1.20 + d) = 0.30$$
Since $X$ is uniform on $[0, 2]$, $P(X \ge x) = \frac{2 - x}{2}$.
$$0.30 = \frac{2 - (1.20 + d)}{2} = \frac{0.80 - d}{2}$$
$$0.60 = 0.80 - d \implies d = 0.20$$

Now, we calculate the probability that the insurer pays at least 1.44:
$$P(Y \ge 1.44) = P(X - d \ge 1.44) = P(X \ge 1.44 + 0.20) = P(X \ge 1.64)$$
$$P(X \ge 1.64) = \frac{2 - 1.64}{2} = \frac{0.36}{2} = 0.18$$
