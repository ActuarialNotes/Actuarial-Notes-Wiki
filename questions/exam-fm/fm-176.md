A two-year loan of 100 is repaid with a payment of $X$ at the end of the first year and $2X$ at the end of the second year. The annual effective interest rate charged by the lender is 8% in the first year and $i$ in the second year. The annual effective yield rate for the lender is 10%.

Calculate $i$.

- A) 12.8%
- B) 12.9%
- C) 13.0%
- D) 13.1%
- E) 13.2%

## Explanation

Based on the 10% yield rate: $100 = X/1.10 + 2X/1.10^2$, so $X = 39.03$.

After one year, the outstanding loan balance is $100(1.08) - 39.03 = 68.97$.

For the second year balance to be zero: $68.97(1+i) - 2(39.03) = 0$:
$$1+i = 78.06/68.97 = 1.1318 \implies i = 13.18\% \approx 13.2\%$$
