An actuary has submitted the following analysis for a rate level indication:

| Calendar/Accident Year | Calendar Year Earned Premium | AY Reported Losses and Paid ALAE | AY Reported Loss and Paid ALAE Ratio |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2010 | $1,023,549 | $703,902 | 68.8% |
| 2011 | $1,086,756 | $773,430 | 71.2% |
| 2012 | $1,222,930 | $749,249 | 61.3% |

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| Three-Year Average Reported Loss and Paid ALAE Ratio | 67.1% |
| Fixed Expense Provision | 11.0% |
| Variable Expense Provision | 15.0% |
| Underwriting Profit Provision | 8.0% |
| Variable Permissible Loss Ratio | 77.0% |
| Indicated Rate Change | 1.4% |

Recommend five improvements to the analysis and briefly explain the purpose of each.

### Explanation
Any five of the following improvements satisfy the question. Each recommendation must be paired with its purpose:

**1. Adjust earned premium to current rate level.**
*Purpose:* If rates changed during the experience period, the historical loss ratios use premiums at different rate levels, making them incomparable. On-leveling the premium ensures that the loss ratios reflect what the insurer would have collected at today's rates, producing an accurate comparison of loss costs to premium.

**2. Apply a loss trend to the reported losses.**
*Purpose:* Historical losses do not reflect the level of future losses due to changes in claim frequency or severity (e.g., medical inflation, social inflation). Trending projects losses to the expected cost level during the period the new rates will be in effect, so the indicated change accounts for expected future costs.

**3. Develop losses to ultimate (apply loss development factors).**
*Purpose:* Reported losses as of a given evaluation date are incomplete — additional development will occur as IBNR claims emerge. Developing losses to ultimate ensures the loss ratios reflect the full cost of each accident year, preventing an understated rate indication.

**4. Include a provision for unallocated loss adjustment expenses (ULAE).**
*Purpose:* The analysis uses only paid ALAE. ULAE (e.g., general claims department costs) are a real component of the insurer's total claims cost and must be included so that the indicated rate covers all loss-related expenses.

**5. Use a volume-weighted (premium-weighted) average of loss ratios rather than a simple average.**
*Purpose:* A simple average gives equal weight to each year regardless of volume. AY 2012 has substantially more premium than earlier years, making it more representative of the current book of business. Weighting by premium ensures years with more data have greater influence on the selected loss ratio.

**Additional acceptable improvements include:**
- Separate fixed and variable expenses (so fixed expenses are added to the loss ratio rather than embedded in the permissible loss ratio, preventing distortion for risks with above- or below-average premium).
- Include a loss adjustment expense trend (ALAE may change at a different rate than losses).
- Include investment income in the profit provision (the current analysis may not reflect the time value of money).
- Use accident year rather than calendar/accident year data consistently (mixing CY and AY data creates an inconsistency that can distort the ratios).

### Examiner Report
Most candidates identified at least four valid improvements. A common error was confusing concepts — for example, "trend losses to ultimate" conflates loss trend and loss development into a single step and does not receive full credit for either. Restating the improvement as the purpose (e.g., "adjust premium to current rate level so that the premiums are on-level") also did not receive credit; the purpose must explain *why* the adjustment is needed (to enable accurate loss ratio comparison across years). Suggestions for improving the insurer's operations rather than improving the actuarial analysis were not accepted.
