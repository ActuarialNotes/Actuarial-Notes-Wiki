Company Q invests $X$ at the end of each year for 25 years at an annual effective interest rate of 9%. Company R invests 100 at the end of each year for 25 years at an annual effective interest rate of 9%, but, at the end of each year, the interest earned is reinvested at an annual effective interest rate of 8%.

Immediately after the 25th payment, Company R's total investment, including the reinvested interest, has the same value as Company Q's investment.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 91.22
- B) 91.93
- C) 92.67
- D) 93.41
- E) 94.03

## Explanation

Company Q's accumulated value: $Xs_{\overline{25}|0.09} = 84.7009X$.

Company R's accumulated value consists of the principal (100 per year × 25 = 2500) plus reinvested interest. Each year $k$, Company R earns interest of $100 \times 0.09 \times k = 9k$ on its $100k$ balance (prospective view), which is reinvested at 8%. More precisely, R earns $100(0.09)$ on the first deposit, $100(0.09)(2)$ on the running total after year 2, etc. The accumulated value is:

$$100(25) + 9(Is)_{\overline{24}|0.08} = 2500 + 9\cdot\frac{\ddot{s}_{\overline{24}|0.08}-24}{0.08} = 2500 + 9\cdot\frac{72.1059-24}{0.08} = 7,911.91$$

Setting equal: $84.7009X = 7,911.91 \implies X = 93.41$.
