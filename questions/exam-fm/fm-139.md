Claire purchases an eight-year callable bond with a 10% annual coupon rate payable semiannually. The bond has a face value of 3000 and a redemption value of 2800.

The purchase price assumes the bond is called at the end of the fourth year for 2900, and provides an annual effective yield of 10.0%.

Immediately after the first coupon payment is received, the bond is called for 2960. Claire's annual effective yield rate is $i$.

Calculate $i$.

- A) 9.8%
- B) 10.1%
- C) 10.8%
- D) 11.1%
- E) 11.8%

## Explanation

The semiannual yield at purchase: $(1.10)^{1/2} - 1 = 0.0488$.

Purchase price: $150a_{\overline{8}|0.0488} + 2900(1.0488)^{-8} = 150(6.4947) + 1980.87 = 2955.08$.

After the first coupon (received 150), the bond is called for 2960. The equation for the semiannual yield $j$:
$$2955.08 = (150 + 2960)/(1+j)$$
$$1 + j = 3110/2955.08 = 1.05242$$

Annual effective rate: $i = (1.05242)^2 - 1 = 0.1076 = 10.8\%$
