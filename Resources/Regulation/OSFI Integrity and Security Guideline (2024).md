# OSFI Integrity and Security Guideline (January 2024)

## Overview
Following 2023 amendments to the OSFI Act (via the Budget Implementation Act) that expanded OSFI's mandate beyond financial soundness, the Integrity and Security Guideline sets expectations for protecting federally regulated institutions against threats to their integrity and security — including foreign interference.

## Key Details
*   **Integrity Pillar:** Expectations of good character, ethical conduct, and sound governance among institutions' leadership and operations.
*   **Security Pillar:** Protection against physical, technological, and people-based threats — espionage, coercion of personnel, and undue foreign influence explicitly in scope.
*   **Annual Mandate:** OSFI must now examine and report on integrity and security at least annually for each institution, embedding it in core supervision.

## Multi-Agent Execution Mandates
*   **Compliance & HR:** Background screening, insider-risk programs, and conduct frameworks documented to supervisory standard.
*   **Risk & IT:** Foreign-interference threat assessments connected to existing cyber and third-party risk programs.
*   **Executive:** Board attestation and reporting lines established for the expanded non-financial mandate.
