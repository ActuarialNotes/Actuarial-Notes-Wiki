A borrower takes out a loan to be repaid over 20 years. The first payment is 1102 payable at the end of the first month. Each subsequent monthly payment is five more than the previous month's payment.

Calculate the accumulated value of the payments at the end of 15 years using an annual effective interest rate of 6.5%.

- A) 442,031
- B) 443,525
- C) 445,578
- D) 447,287
- E) 448,547

## Explanation

Monthly rate: $j = 1.065^{1/12} - 1 = 0.0052617$. Payments: $1102, 1107, \ldots$ for 240 months (20 years), but accumulate only the first 180 payments at end of 15 years.

The payments form an arithmetically increasing annuity: first payment 1097, increasing by 5 (shifted by 5 per month). Actually: payments at month $k$ are $1097 + 5k$.

$$AV = 1097s_{\overline{180}|j} + 5(Is)_{\overline{180}|j} = 1097(298.733) + 5\cdot\frac{\ddot{s}_{\overline{180}|j} - 180}{j}$$

$$= 327{,}710 + 5\cdot\frac{300.305 - 180}{0.0052617} = 327{,}710 + 5(22{,}864.2) = 327{,}710 + 114{,}321 = 442{,}031$$
