**Ratemaking Data Organization** refers to the choice of time dimension used to group premiums, losses, and exposures for rate analysis. The five primary methods differ in how losses are assigned to a period.

- **[[Accident Year]]** — losses by occurrence date; industry standard for ratemaking; matches losses to the exposure period earning the premium
- **[[Policy Year]]** — all transactions on policies with a given inception year; the most complete profitability view but requires the longest development period
- **[[Calendar Year]]** — transactions in a given calendar year regardless of policy dates; used for financial reporting; closes immediately (no development) but mixes policy cohorts
- **[[Report Year]]** — losses by date first reported; natural for claims-made policies; fastest development but affected by changes in reporting speed
- **Close year** — losses by date of final settlement; used for operational analysis; rarely used in ratemaking

Data may be organized on a **gross** (before reinsurance), **ceded** (reinsurer's share only), or **net** (gross − ceded) basis; net is most common for ratemaking.

> [!example]- Classifying a Loss Under Each Method {Example}
> A policy is effective $7/1/2024$ (expires $6/30/2025$). A loss occurs $11/15/2024$, is reported $12/20/2024$, and the claim closes $3/15/2025$.
>
> > [!answer]-
> > | Method | Year |
> > |---|---|
> > | Policy Year | 2024 (policy inception year) |
> > | Accident Year | 2024 (occurrence date) |
> > | Report Year | 2024 (first report date) |
> > | Calendar Year (loss payment) | 2025 |
> > | Close Year | 2025 (claim closure date) |
