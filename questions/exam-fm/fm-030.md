The current price of an annual coupon bond is 100. The yield to maturity is 8%. Using the first-order Macaulay approximation, the duration of the bond is calculated to be $D$.

Given that the price decreases to $-700$ when the yield increases by 1%, calculate the Macaulay duration $D$.

*(Note: the price sensitivity $dP/di pprox -700$ at the current yield.)*

- A) 7.00
- B) 7.49
- C) 7.56
- D) 7.69
- E) 8.00

## Explanation

The Macaulay duration is the negative derivative of price with respect to yield, scaled by $(1+i)$ and divided by the current price:

$$D = -\frac{dP/di}{P} \cdot (1+i) = -\frac{(-700)}{100}(1.08) = 7.56$$
