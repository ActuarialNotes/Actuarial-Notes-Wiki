Two fair dice, one red and one blue, are rolled.
Let $A$ be the event that the number rolled on the red die is odd.
Let $B$ be the event that the number rolled on the blue die is odd.
Let $C$ be the event that the sum of the numbers rolled on the two dice is odd.

Determine which of the following is true.

- A) A, B, and C are not mutually independent, but each pair is independent.
- B) A, B, and C are mutually independent.
- C) Exactly one pair of the three events is independent.
- D) Exactly two of the three pairs are independent.
- E) No pair of the three events is independent.

## Explanation

Each event has probability 0.5:
* $P(A) = 0.5$ (red is odd)
* $P(B) = 0.5$ (blue is odd)
* $P(C) = 0.5$ (sum is odd, meaning one is odd and one is even)

Check pairwise independence:
* $P(A \cap B) = P(\text{red odd, blue odd}) = 0.25$. Since $P(A)P(B) = 0.5 \times 0.5 = 0.25$, $A$ and $B$ are independent.
* $P(A \cap C) = P(\text{red odd, sum odd}) = P(\text{red odd, blue even}) = 0.25 = P(A)P(C)$. $A$ and $C$ are independent.
* $P(B \cap C) = P(\text{blue odd, sum odd}) = P(\text{blue odd, red even}) = 0.25 = P(B)P(C)$. $B$ and $C$ are independent.

Check mutual independence:
* $P(A \cap B \cap C) = P(\text{red odd, blue odd, sum odd}) = 0$, because if both are odd, the sum is even. This does not equal $P(A)P(B)P(C) = 0.125$.

Therefore, A, B, and C are not mutually independent, but each pair is independent.
