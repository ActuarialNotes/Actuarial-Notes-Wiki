A company's preferred stock will pay level annual dividends forever starting five years from now.

Using an annual effective interest rate of 10%, the modified duration of the stock is $D$.

Calculate $D$.

- A) 13.64
- B) 14.55
- C) 15.00
- D) 16.00
- E) 16.50

## Explanation

Let the annual dividend be 1 (WLOG). PV of the deferred perpetuity: $v^4/i = v^4/0.1$ (first payment at time 5, so deferred 4 years from now from a perpetuity-immediate perspective). Actually, PV $= v^4 a_{\overline{\infty}|} = v^4/i = (1.1)^{-4}/0.1 = 6.83013$.

The time-weighted PV (numerator for Macaulay duration): the payments are at times 5, 6, 7, … This is $v^4[4a_{\overline{\infty}|} + (Ia)_{\overline{\infty}|}] = v^4\left[\frac{4}{i} + \frac{1+i}{i^2}\right] = (1.1)^{-4}\left[40 + \frac{1.1}{0.01}\right] = 0.68301(40+110) = 102.452$.

Macaulay duration $= 102.452/6.83013 = 15.00$.

Modified duration $= 15.00/1.10 = 13.636 \approx 13.64$.
