A loan with an annual nominal interest rate of 9%, convertible monthly, is repaid with 60 monthly payments. The first payment is 1000 and each succeeding payment is 2% less than the previous payment.

Calculate the outstanding loan balance immediately after the 40th payment is made.

- A) 6889
- B) 7289
- C) 7344
- D) 7407
- E) 7862

## Explanation

Monthly rate $j = 0.75\%$, geometric decrease factor $g = 0.98$.

Prospective balance after payment 40: PV of remaining 20 payments (payments 41–60).

Payment 41 $= 1000(0.98)^{40}$. The 20 remaining payments form a geometrically decreasing annuity.

$$B_{40} = 1000(0.98)^{40} \cdot \frac{1-(0.98/1.0075)^{20}}{1-0.98/1.0075}$$

$= 1000(0.98)^{40} \cdot \frac{1-(0.98/1.0075)^{20}}{0.0275/1.0075}$

$(0.98)^{40} = 0.44551$; $(0.98/1.0075)^{20} = (0.97275)^{20} = 0.57677$

$B_{40} = 1000(0.44551)\cdot\frac{1-0.57677}{0.02727} = 445.51 \times 15.479 = 6889$
