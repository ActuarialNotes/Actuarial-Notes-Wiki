An investor deposits 100 into a bank account at time 0. The bank credits interest at an annual nominal interest rate of $i$, compounded semi-annually.

The total amount of interest credited in the twelfth year is twice the amount of interest credited in the fifth year.

Calculate $i$.

- A) 10.15%
- B) 10.24%
- C) 10.32%
- D) 10.41%
- E) 10.48%

## Explanation

Let $j = i/2$ be the semiannual rate. Interest credited in year $k$ (two semiannual periods):
$$\text{Int}_k = 100(1+j)^{2(k-1)}\left[(1+j)^2 - 1\right]$$

Setting year 12 = 2 × year 5:
$$100(1+j)^{22}[(1+j)^2-1] = 2 \times 100(1+j)^{8}[(1+j)^2-1]$$

$$(1+j)^{22} = 2(1+j)^8 \implies (1+j)^{14} = 2 \implies 1+j = 2^{1/14}$$

$$i = 2j = 2(2^{1/14}-1) = 2(1.0508-1) = 0.1015 = 10.15\%$$
