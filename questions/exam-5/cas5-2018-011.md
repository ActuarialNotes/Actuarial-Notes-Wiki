Given the following for a large deductible commercial general liability policy:

| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Per occurrence deductible | $500,000 |
| Loss elimination ratio for a $500,000 deductible | 90.0% |
| ALAE/ground up loss ratio | 10.0% |
| Ground up loss estimate | $2,000,000 |
| Fixed expenses | $100,000 |
| Variable expenses as % of premium | 12.0% |
| Underwriting profit as % of premium | 4.0% |
| Deductible processing cost as % of losses below the deductible | 3.0% |
| Credit risk as % of losses below the deductible | 1.0% |
| Additional risk margin as % of excess losses | 7.0% |

- The insurer will handle all claims, including those that fall below the deductible.
- The insurer will make the payments on all claims and will seek reimbursement for amounts below the deductible from the insured.
- The deductible is for loss only.
- All ALAE is paid by the insurer.

Calculate the premium for the large deductible policy.

### Answer

$697,619

### Explanation

**Step 1: Identify the loss components.**

Ground up loss estimate: $2{,}000{,}000$

$$\text{Losses below deductible} = \text{Ground Up Loss} \times \text{LER} = 2{,}000{,}000 \times 0.90 = 1{,}800{,}000$$

$$\text{Losses above deductible (excess losses)} = \text{Ground Up Loss} \times (1 - \text{LER}) = 2{,}000{,}000 \times 0.10 = 200{,}000$$

**Step 2: Calculate ALAE.**

Since all ALAE is paid by the insurer (applied to the full ground up loss):

$$\text{ALAE} = 2{,}000{,}000 \times 10\% = 200{,}000$$

**Step 3: Calculate the large deductible adjustments.**

$$\text{Deductible processing cost} = 3\% \times 1{,}800{,}000 = 54{,}000$$

$$\text{Credit risk} = 1\% \times 1{,}800{,}000 = 18{,}000$$

$$\text{Additional risk margin} = 7\% \times 200{,}000 = 14{,}000$$

**Step 4: Assemble the numerator (costs the insurer bears).**

The premium must cover: excess losses, ALAE, fixed expenses, deductible processing cost, credit risk, and the additional risk margin.

$$\text{Numerator} = 200{,}000 + 200{,}000 + 100{,}000 + 54{,}000 + 18{,}000 + 14{,}000 = 586{,}000$$

**Step 5: Calculate the premium.**

$$\text{Premium} = \frac{\text{Numerator}}{1 - \text{Variable Expenses} - \text{Profit}} = \frac{586{,}000}{1 - 0.12 - 0.04} = \frac{586{,}000}{0.84} \approx 697{,}619$$

### Examiner Report

Candidates were expected to calculate the premium for a large deductible policy by correctly applying the loss elimination ratio and then including the appropriate costs and expenses.

Common mistakes include:
- Forgetting to include Fixed Expense, ALAE, or Excess Loss in the final calculation
- Calculating the Excess Deductible Loss as (Ground Up Loss × LER) or (Ground Up Loss − $500,000 per occurrence deductible) instead of (Ground Up Loss × (1 − LER))
- Using incorrect loss amount for the Credit Risk and Deductible Processing Fee calculation (e.g., using ground up or excess losses instead of losses below the deductible)
- Including Credit Risk, Deductible Processing Fee, and/or Risk Margin in the denominator (these are costs, not variable loadings)
- Including losses below the deductible in the final premium calculation instead of losses above the deductible
- Only including ALAE related to the Excess Deductible Loss amount, while the problem clearly states that all ALAE is paid by the insurer
