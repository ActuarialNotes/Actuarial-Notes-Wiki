Given the following information:

- All policies have six-month terms.
- New rates will take effect on January 1, 2014.
- Rates will be in effect for one year.
- Selected frequency trend = 0%.
- Selected severity trend = +5%.
- Selected ULAE provision = 10% of loss and ALAE.
- Two accident years with equal weights are used to calculate the pure premium.
- Assume no further development after 36 months.

**Cumulative Paid Loss and ALAE (\$000s):**

| Accident Year | 12 Months | 24 Months | 36 Months |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2009 | \$6,000 | \$17,200 | \$25,800 |
| 2010 | \$4,500 | \$12,200 | |
| 2011 | \$7,900 | | |

**Open Claim Counts:**

| Accident Year | 12 Months | 24 Months | 36 Months |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2009 | 190 | 160 | 75 |
| 2010 | 120 | 110 | |
| 2011 | 165 | | |

**Outstanding Case Loss and ALAE Reserves (\$000s):**

| Accident Year | 12 Months | 24 Months | 36 Months |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2009 | \$16,500 | \$13,000 | \$5,500 |
| 2010 | \$10,000 | \$6,500 | |
| 2011 | \$9,000 | | |

**Earned Exposures (000s):**

| Calendar Year (= Accident Year) | Earned Exposures (000s) |
|---|---|
| 2010 | 200 |
| 2011 | 300 |

Use the Berquist-Sherman case outstanding adjustment technique to calculate the projected ultimate loss and LAE pure premium of the rate level indication.

### Answer

**Projected Ultimate Loss and LAE Pure Premium = $150.26**

### Explanation

**Step 1: Compute average case outstanding (= outstanding reserves / open counts) — actual diagonal.**

$$\text{Avg Case O/S} = \frac{\text{Outstanding Case Reserves}}{\text{Open Claim Counts}}$$

| AY | 12 months | 24 months | 36 months |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2009 | 16,500/190 = 86,842 | 13,000/160 = 81,250 | 5,500/75 = 73,333 |
| 2010 | 10,000/120 = 83,333 | 6,500/110 = 59,091 | |
| 2011 | 9,000/165 = 54,545 | | |

**Step 2: Apply Berquist-Sherman case outstanding adjustment — trend the diagonal backward at the severity trend rate (+5%/year) to re-express each cell at the maturity-level severity of the most recent diagonal.**

The diagonal values are taken as given (most-recent AY at each age). Earlier values in each column are trended back one year per row:

| AY | 12 months | 24 months | 36 months |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2009 | 54,545 / 1.05² = 49,474 | 59,091 / 1.05¹ = 56,277 | 73,333 (diagonal) |
| 2010 | 54,545 / 1.05¹ = 51,948 | 59,091 (diagonal) | |
| 2011 | 54,545 (diagonal) | | |

**Step 3: Compute adjusted reported losses = adjusted case reserves + paid losses.**

Adjusted case reserves = adjusted average case O/S × open claim counts.

| AY | 12 months | 24 months | 36 months |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2009 | 49,474×190 + 6,000 = 15,400 | 56,277×160 + 17,200 = 26,204 | 73,333×75 + 25,800 = 31,300 |
| 2010 | 51,948×120 + 4,500 = 10,734 | 59,091×110 + 12,200 = 18,700 | |
| 2011 | 54,545×165 + 7,900 = 16,900 | | |

(All amounts in \$000s)

**Step 4: Develop age-to-age factors from the adjusted triangle.**

| AY | 12–24 | 24–36 |
|---|---|---|
| 2009 | 26,204 / 15,400 = 1.7016 | 31,300 / 26,204 = 1.1945 |
| 2010 | 18,700 / 10,734 = 1.7421 | |

Selected (simple average): 12–24 = 1.7219; 24–36 = 1.1945; 36–ult = 1.000 (no development after 36 months).

**CDFs to ultimate:** 12-ult = 1.7219 × 1.1945 = 2.0568; 24-ult = 1.1945; 36-ult = 1.000.

**Step 5: Determine loss trend periods.**

Policies have 6-month terms; new rates effective January 1, 2014, in effect for one year. Average accident date of prospective period = July 1, 2014.

- AY 2010 average accident date = July 1, 2010 → trend period = 4.25 years (July 1, 2010 to October 1, 2014)
- AY 2011 average accident date = July 1, 2011 → trend period = 3.25 years (July 1, 2011 to October 1, 2014)

(For 6-month policies effective January 1, 2014, average written date = April 1, 2014; average accident date = October 1, 2014.)

**Step 6: Project ultimate losses with ULAE.**

$$\text{Proj. Ultimate} = \text{Adjusted Reported Losses} \times \text{CDF} \times (1.05)^{\text{trend}} \times 1.10$$

| AY | Adjusted Reported | CDF | Trend | ULAE | Proj. Ultimate (\$000s) | EE (000s) | Pure Premium |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2010 | 18,700 | 1.1945 | $(1.05)^{4.25}$ | 1.10 | 30,232.6 | 200 | 151.16 |
| 2011 | 16,900 | 2.0568 | $(1.05)^{3.25}$ | 1.10 | 44,806.1 | 300 | 149.35 |

**Step 7: Equal-weighted average pure premium.**

$$\text{Proj. Ult. L+LAE PP} = 0.5 \times 151.16 + 0.5 \times 149.35 = \mathbf{150.26}$$

### Examiner Report

Candidates did very well on this question. Most candidates successfully applied the Berquist-Sherman case outstanding adjustment technique and set up the steps for calculating the projected ultimate loss and LAE pure premium. When candidates lost points it was usually for: not trending the losses and LAE to the effective period, using an incorrect trend period, or not applying the ULAE provision.
