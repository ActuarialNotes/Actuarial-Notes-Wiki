A family purchases a perpetuity-immediate that provides annual payments that decrease by 0.4% each year. The price of the perpetuity is 10,000 at an annual force of interest of 0.06.

Calculate the amount of the perpetuity's first payment.

- A) 604
- B) 620
- C) 640
- D) 658
- E) 678

## Explanation

Payments decrease by 0.4% each year, so the geometric ratio is $r = 1 - 0.004 = 0.996$. Let the first payment be $P$. The present value of a geometrically decreasing perpetuity-immediate with force of interest $\delta = 0.06$ is:

$$10,000 = \frac{P}{e^{\delta} - r} = \frac{P}{e^{0.06} - 0.996} = \frac{P}{1.06184 - 0.996} = \frac{P}{0.06184} \cdot e^{-\delta}$$

More precisely: $PV = \frac{Pv}{1 - rv} = \frac{Pe^{-0.06}}{1 - 0.996 e^{-0.06}} = \frac{P \cdot 0.94176}{1 - 0.996(0.94176)} = \frac{0.94176P}{0.058} \approx 15.189P$.

So $P = 10,000/15.189 = 658.37$.
