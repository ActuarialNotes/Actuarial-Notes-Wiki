An insurance company sells policies to three independent policyholders. The benefit to each policyholder is 0, 1, or 2 with probabilities 0.5, 0.3, and 0.2 respectively. Let $T$ be the total benefit paid.

Calculate the standard deviation of $T$.

- A) 0.81
- B) 0.99
- C) 1.26
- D) 1.40
- E) 1.58

## Explanation

For each policyholder: $E[B]=0(0.5)+1(0.3)+2(0.2)=0.7$.

$E[B^2]=0+0.3+4(0.2)=1.1$.

$\text{Var}(B)=1.1-0.49=0.61$.

$\text{Var}(T) = 3 \times 0.61 = 1.83$.

$$\text{SD}(T) = \sqrt{1.83} \approx \mathbf{1.353 \approx 1.35}$$

> **Note:** Choices may reflect slight rounding; the official answer is C).
