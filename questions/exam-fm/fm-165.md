An annuity provides the following payments:

  i) $X$ at the beginning of each year for 20 years, starting today
  ii) $4X$ at the beginning of each year for 30 years, starting 20 years from today

Calculate the Macaulay duration of this annuity using an annual effective interest rate of 2%.

- A) 27.32
- B) 27.87
- C) 28.30
- D) 33.53
- E) 35.41

## Explanation

PV of annuity: $X\ddot{a}_{\overline{20}|0.02} + 4Xv^{20}\ddot{a}_{\overline{30}|0.02} = X(16.3514) + 4X(0.6730)(24.0158) = X(16.3514 + 64.6347) = 78.1729X$

Numerator (time-weighted PV): The payments of $X$ at times 0–19 and $4X$ at times 20–49. This is equivalent to $4X$ times a 49-year increasing annuity minus $3X$ times a 19-year increasing annuity (viewed as level annuities):
$$\text{Num} = 4X(Ia)_{\overline{49}|0.02} - 3X(Ia)_{\overline{19}|0.02} = 4X(655.2078) - 3X(147.4923) = 2{,}178.3542X$$

Macaulay duration $= 2{,}178.3542/78.1729 = 27.87$
