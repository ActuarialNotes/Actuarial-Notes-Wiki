You are given the following:

- A random variable, $X$, is uniformly distributed on the interval $(0, \theta)$
- $\theta$ is unknown
- For a random sample of size $n$ an estimate of $\theta$ is given by:

$$\hat\theta = \frac{2}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} X_i$$

Calculate $\operatorname{Var}(\hat\theta)$.

- A) $\dfrac{4\theta^2}{3n}$
- B) $\dfrac{\theta^2}{3n^2}$
- C) $\dfrac{\theta^2}{12}$
- D) $\dfrac{\theta^2}{6}$
- E) $\dfrac{\theta^2}{3n}$

## Explanation

The estimator is $\hat\theta = 2\bar{X}$, where $\bar{X}$ is the sample mean. For $X \sim \text{Uniform}(0,\theta)$,

$$\operatorname{Var}(X) = \frac{\theta^2}{12}.$$

Then

$$\operatorname{Var}(\hat\theta) = 4\operatorname{Var}(\bar{X}) = 4 \cdot \frac{\operatorname{Var}(X)}{n} = 4 \cdot \frac{\theta^2/12}{n} = \frac{\theta^2}{3n}.$$

The answer is **E**.
