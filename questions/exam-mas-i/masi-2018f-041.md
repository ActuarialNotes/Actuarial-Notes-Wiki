An actuary produces a correlogram for vehicle accident severities over a 10-year period. The ACF starts near 0.95 at lag 1 and declines slowly, but instead of decaying monotonically it dips to about 0.70 near lag 6–7, rises back to a local peak of about 0.75 near lag 12, and then declines again through lag 18 (lag measured in months).

You are also given the following three statements about this time series:

- I. There is a positive trend in accident severity
- II. There is a negative trend in accident severity
- III. The accident severity data shows a seasonal pattern

Determine which of the above statements can be concluded from the above graph.

- A) I only
- B) II only
- C) III only
- D) I, II and III
- E) The answer is not given by (A), (B), (C), or (D)

## Explanation

A **slowly-decaying ACF** signals non-stationarity consistent with a trend, but the correlogram cannot reveal the *direction* of that trend — a rising and a falling series can produce the same autocorrelation pattern. So neither Statement I nor Statement II can be concluded from the ACF alone.

The **local peak in the ACF near lag 12** (with lags in months) indicates a repeating annual cycle — a **seasonal pattern**. Statement III can be concluded.

Only III, so the answer is **C**.
