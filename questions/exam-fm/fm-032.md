A common stock pays dividends at the end of each year into perpetuity. The dividend increases by 2% each year. Using an annual effective interest rate of 5%, calculate the Macaulay duration of the stock.

- A) 27
- B) 35
- C) 44
- D) 52
- E) 58

## Explanation

With dividends $R_t = 1.02^t$ and interest rate $i = 5\%$, let $v = 1/1.05$:

$$\begin{align*}
D &= \frac{\sum_{t=1}^{\infty} tv^t R_t}{\sum_{t=1}^{\infty} v^t R_t} \\
&= \frac{\sum_{t=1}^{\infty} tv^t 1.02^t}{\sum_{t=1}^{\infty} v^t 1.02^t} \\
&= \frac{(Ia)_{\overline{\infty}|j}}{a_{\overline{\infty}|j}} = \frac{1}{d_j}
\end{align*}$$

where $(1+j)^{-1} = 1.02v = 1.02/1.05$, so $j = 0.03/1.02$.

$$\begin{align*}
D &= \frac{1}{d_j} = \frac{1+j}{j} \\
&= \frac{1.05/1.02}{0.03/1.02} = \frac{1.05}{0.03} \\
&= 35
\end{align*}$$
