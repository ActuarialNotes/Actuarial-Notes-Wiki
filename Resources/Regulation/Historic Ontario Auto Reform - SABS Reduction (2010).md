# Ontario Auto Insurance Reform (2010)

## Regulation Overview
A major legislative overhaul of Ontario auto policies designed to combat skyrocketing claims costs and premium inflation. The regulation drastically scaled back mandatory coverage minimums for non-catastrophic injuries.

## Core Structural Changes
- **Medical/Rehabilitation Limits:** Slashed standard non-catastrophic Medical and Rehabilitation benefits from $100,000 down to **$50,000**.
- **Minor Injury Guideline (MIG):** Introduced a strict **$3,500** cap for soft-tissue injuries, forcing claims departments to build clear medical evaluation pathways.
- **Attendant Care Reductions:** Reduced standard attendant care benefits from $72,000 to **$36,000**.

## Agent Analysis Vectors
- **Actuarial:** Caused an immediate reduction in pure premiums for the Bodily Injury (BI) and Accident Benefits (AB) sub-lines, requiring comprehensive retrospective rate filing adjustments.
- **Claims:** Required brand new operational triage protocols to evaluate whether injuries fell within or outside the newly defined Minor Injury Guideline (MIG).
