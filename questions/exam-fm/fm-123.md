A family purchases a perpetuity-immediate that provides annual payments that decrease by 0.4% each year. The price of the perpetuity is 10,000 at an annual force of interest of 0.06.

Calculate the amount of the perpetuity's first payment.

- A) 604
- B) 620
- C) 640
- D) 658
- E) 678

## Explanation

Payments decrease by 0.4% each year, so the geometric ratio is $r = 0.996$. Let the first payment be $P$. For a geometrically decreasing perpetuity-immediate with force of interest $\delta = 0.06$, the present value uses $v = e^{-\delta}$:

$$\begin{align*}
PV &= \frac{Pv}{1 - rv} \\
&= \frac{Pe^{-0.06}}{1 - 0.996\, e^{-0.06}} \\
&= \frac{P \cdot 0.94176}{1 - 0.996(0.94176)} \\
&= \frac{0.94176P}{0.058} \\
&\approx 15.189P
\end{align*}$$

Setting $PV = 10,000$:

$$P = 10{,}000 / 15.189 = 658.37$$
