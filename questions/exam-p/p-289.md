An airport purchases an insurance policy to offset costs associated with excessive snowfall. For every full ten inches of snow in excess of 40 inches, the insurer pays the airport 200 up to a maximum of 500. The snowfall probabilities are the same as in question 118.

Calculate the standard deviation of the amount paid under the policy.

- A) 163.5
- B) 187.6
- C) 208.7
- D) 234.9
- E) 336.6

## Explanation

Benefit levels based on snowfall:

| Snowfall | Benefit | Probability |
|---|---|---|
| $<50$ in | 0 | $0.50+0.22=0.72$ |
| $[50,60)$ | 200 | $0.14$ |
| $[60,70)$ | 400 | $0.06$ |
| $\geq 70$ | 500 | $0.04+0.04=0.08$ |

$$
\begin{align*}
E[B] &= 0(0.72)+200(0.14)+400(0.06)+500(0.08) \\
     &= 28+24+40 \\
     &= 92
\end{align*}
$$

$$
\begin{align*}
E[B^2] &= 200^2(0.14)+400^2(0.06)+500^2(0.08) \\
       &= 5600+9600+20000 \\
       &= 35200
\end{align*}
$$

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{Var}(B) &= 35200 - 92^2 \\
              &= 35200 - 8464 \\
              &= 26736
\end{align*}
$$

$$\text{SD}(B) = \sqrt{26736} \approx \mathbf{163.5}$$
