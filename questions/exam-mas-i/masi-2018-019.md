Let $X_1, \ldots, X_n$ be a random sample from a distribution with density function $f(x) = \dfrac{2\theta^2}{(x+\theta)^3}$, where $n$ is large. You wish to test the hypothesis $H_0: \theta = 1$ against the hypothesis $H_1: \theta \ne 1$.

You perform this test, and the null hypothesis is rejected at a 1% significance using the Chi-squared approximation for the likelihood ratio test. Let $\Lambda$ be the likelihood ratio for this hypothesis test.

Calculate the maximum possible value of $\Lambda$.

- A) Less than 0.01
- B) At least 0.01, but less than 0.02
- C) At least 0.02, but less than 0.03
- D) At least 0.03, but less than 0.04
- E) At least 0.04

## Explanation

The likelihood ratio statistic uses the asymptotic result

$$-2\ln\Lambda \sim \chi^2_{1}$$

(one degree of freedom, since a single parameter $\theta$ is constrained under $H_0$). At the 1% significance level, the critical value is

$$\chi^2_{0.99,\,1} = 6.635.$$

The null is rejected when $-2\ln\Lambda > 6.635$. The boundary of the rejection region gives the **largest** likelihood ratio still leading to rejection:

$$-2\ln\Lambda = 6.635 \implies \ln\Lambda = -3.3175 \implies \Lambda = e^{-3.3175} \approx 0.0362.$$

This falls in $[0.03, 0.04)$, so the answer is **D**.
