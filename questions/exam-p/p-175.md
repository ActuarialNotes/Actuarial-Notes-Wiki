At the start of a week, a coal mine has a high-capacity storage bin that is half full. During the week, 20 loads of coal are added to the storage bin. Each load has a volume normally distributed with mean 1.50 cubic yards and standard deviation 0.25 cubic yards. During the same week, coal is removed and loaded into 4 railroad cars, each with an amount normally distributed with mean 7.25 cubic yards and standard deviation 0.50 cubic yards. All amounts are mutually independent.

Calculate the probability that the storage bin contains more coal at the end of the week than it had at the beginning of the week.

- A) 0.56
- B) 0.63
- C) 0.67
- D) 0.75
- E) 0.98

## Explanation

Let $A$ = total added (20 loads), $R$ = total removed (4 cars).

$$A \sim N(20 \times 1.50,\; 20 \times 0.25^2) = N(30,\; 1.25)$$
$$R \sim N(4 \times 7.25,\; 4 \times 0.50^2) = N(29,\; 1.00)$$

Net change $D = A - R \sim N(30-29,\; 1.25+1.00) = N(1,\; 2.25)$, so $\text{SD}(D) = 1.5$.

$$P(D > 0) = P\!\left(Z > \frac{0-1}{1.5}\right) = P(Z > -0.667) = \Phi(0.667) \approx \mathbf{0.748 \approx 0.75}$$
