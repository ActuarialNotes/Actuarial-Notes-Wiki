Kate buys a five-year 1000 face amount bond today with a 100 discount. The annual nominal coupon rate is 5% convertible semiannually.

One year later, Wallace buys a four-year bond. It has the same face amount and coupon values as Kate's and is priced to yield an annual nominal interest rate of 10% convertible semiannually. The discount on Wallace's bond is $D$.

The book value of Kate's bond at the time Wallace buys his bond is $B$.

Calculate $B - D$.

- A) 724
- B) 738
- C) 756
- D) 838
- E) 917

## Explanation

Kate's bond: price $= 900$, coupons $= 25$ semiannually, face $= 1000$. Yield: $(900) = 25a_{\overline{10}|j} + 1000v^{10}$, solved as $j = 0.0371551$, $i^{(2)}/2 = 3.71551\%$.

Kate's book value after 1 year (2 periods): $B = 25a_{\overline{8}|0.0371551} + 1000v^8 = 25(6.7194) + 1000(0.75058)$

Wait, $B$ is at time 1 (after 2 coupons). Using prospective:
$B = 25a_{\overline{8}|0.0371551} + 1000(1.0371551)^{-8} = 917.19$

Wallace's bond: 4-year, coupons 25 semiannual, face 1000, yield $j=5\%$:
$(1000-D) = 25a_{\overline{8}|0.05} + 1000(1.05)^{-8} = 25(6.4632) + 677.00 = 838.42$
$D = 1000 - 838.42 = 161.58$

$B - D = 917.19 - 161.58 = 755.61 \approx 756$
