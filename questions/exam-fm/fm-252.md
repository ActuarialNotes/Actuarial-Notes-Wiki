Fund X accumulates at a force of interest of $\delta_t = \dfrac{2}{1+2t}$, where $t$ is measured in years, $0 \le t \le 20$. Fund Y accumulates at an annual effective interest rate of $i$. An amount of 1 is invested in each fund at time $t = 0$. After 20 years, Fund X has the same value as Fund Y.

Calculate the value of Fund Y after five years.

- A) 2.46
- B) 2.53
- C) 2.60
- D) 2.67
- E) 2.74

## Explanation

Fund X accumulated value at $t=20$:
$$e^{\int_0^{20}\frac{2}{1+2t}\,dt} = e^{[\ln(1+2t)]_0^{20}} = e^{\ln(41)} = 41$$

So $(1+i)^{20} = 41$, giving $i = 41^{1/20}-1 = 0.20404$.

Fund Y after 5 years: $(1+i)^5 = 41^{5/20} = 41^{0.25} = 2.530$.
