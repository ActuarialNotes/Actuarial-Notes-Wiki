A perpetuity-immediate pays $X$ per year. Brian receives the first $n$ payments, Colleen receives the next $n$ payments, and a charity receives the remaining payments. Brian's share of the present value of the original perpetuity is 40%, and the charity's share is $K$.

Calculate $K$.

- A) 24%
- B) 28%
- C) 32%
- D) 36%
- E) 40%

## Explanation

The present value of the perpetuity $= X/i$.

Let $B$ be the present value of Brian's payments:

$$B = Xa_{\overline{n}|} = 0.4 \cdot \frac{X}{i}$$

$$a_{\overline{n}|} = \frac{0.4}{i} \implies 0.4 = 1 - v^n \implies v^n = 0.6$$

The charity receives payments starting after $2n$ years, so its share is:

$$K = v^{2n} \cdot \frac{X}{i} = (0.6)^2 \cdot \frac{X}{i} = 0.36 \cdot \frac{X}{i}$$

The charity's share is **36%** of the perpetuity's present value.
