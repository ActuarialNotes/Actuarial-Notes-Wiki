A construction firm is facing three liabilities of 1000, due at times 1, 2, and 3 in years. There are three bonds available to match these liabilities, as follows:

- Bond I: A bond due at the end of period 1 with a coupon rate of 1% per year, valued at a annual effective yield rate of 14%.
- Bond II: A bond due at the end of period 2 with a coupon rate of 2% per year, valued at a annual effective yield rate of 15%.
- Bond III: A zero-coupon bond due at time 3 valued at a periodic effective yield rate of 18%.

Calculate the face value of each bond that should be purchased to exactly match the liabilities.

- A) Bond I: 970.68, Bond II: 980.39, Bond III: 1000.00
- B) Bond I: 970.68, Bond II: 1000.00, Bond III: 980.39
- C) Bond I: 980.39, Bond II: 970.68, Bond III: 1000.00
- D) Bond I: 1000.00, Bond II: 980.39, Bond III: 970.68
- E) Bond I: 1000.00, Bond II: 1000.00, Bond III: 1000.00

## Explanation

Work backwards from time 3.

**Bond III** must provide 1000 at time 3. Face value = 1000.

**Bond II** must cover time 2 liability of 1000, minus Bond II's coupon at time 2. Bond II also pays a coupon at time 1. Let $F_{II}$ = face of Bond II. At time 2: $F_{II}(1+0.02) = 1000 + 0.02F_{II}$... the entire cash flow is $F_{II} + 0.02F_{II} = 1.02F_{II}$. But we need $1.02F_{II} = 1000$? No — we need total matching.

Bond II pays $0.02F_{II}$ at time 1 and $1.02F_{II}$ at time 2. Set $1.02F_{II} = 1000$, so $F_{II} = 980.39$.

**Bond I** must cover time 1 liability of 1000, minus Bond II's coupon at time 1 ($0.02 \times 980.39 = 19.61$). Bond I pays $(1+0.01)F_I = 1.01F_I$ at time 1. Set $1.01F_I = 1000 - 19.61 = 980.39 \implies F_I = 970.68$.
