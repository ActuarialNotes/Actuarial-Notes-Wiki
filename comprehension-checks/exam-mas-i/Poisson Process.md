Claims arrive as a Poisson process at 20 per year. Independently, each claim is 'large' with probability 0.10. At what rate do large claims arrive?

- A) 2 per year — the thinned process is Poisson with rate 20 × 0.10
- B) 20 per year — thinning changes the mix of claims, not the arrival rate
- C) 18 per year — large claims are what remain after the other 90%
- D) 0.10 per year — the rate equals the thinning probability
