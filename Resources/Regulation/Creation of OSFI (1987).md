# Creation of OSFI (1987)

## Regulation Overview
The Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions (OSFI) was created by merging the Department of Insurance and the Office of the Inspector General of Banks. This centralized federal oversight across all federally regulated insurance companies and financial institutions in Canada.

## Strategic Impact
- **Financial & Capital Management:** Imposed standardized solvency monitoring, evolving eventually into the Minimum Capital Test (MCT) for P&C insurers and Life Insurance Capital Adequacy Test (LICAT).
- **Risk Management:** Established formal reporting requirements for asset-liability matching (ALM) and required robust corporate stress-testing protocols to protect policyholders from market liquidity collapses.
