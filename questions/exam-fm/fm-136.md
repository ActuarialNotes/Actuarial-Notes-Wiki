Bond X is a 20-year bond with annual coupons and the following characteristics:

  i) Par value is 1000.
  ii) The annual coupon rate is 10%.
  iii) Bond X is callable at par on any of the last five coupon dates.

Calculate the maximum purchase price for Bond X that will guarantee an annual effective yield rate of at least 5%.

- A) 1519
- B) 1542
- C) 1570
- D) 1596
- E) 1623

## Explanation

Since the yield (5%) is less than the coupon rate (10%), the bond sells at a premium. For a premium bond, early call is the worst case for the buyer.

The worst call is at year 16 (earliest of the last five). The price to guarantee a 5% yield if called at year 16:
$$P = 100a_{\overline{16}|0.05} + 1000(1.05)^{-16} = 100(10.0378) + 458.11 = 1{,}461.89$$

But if called at years 17, 18, 19, or 20, the prices are higher. So the maximum purchase price that guarantees at least 5% is the minimum over all possible call dates. The minimum price is achieved at the earliest call date (year 16): $1{,}542$.
