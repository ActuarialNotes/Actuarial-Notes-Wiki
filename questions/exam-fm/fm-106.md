A company has liabilities of 573 due at the end of year 2 and 701 due at the end of year 5.

A portfolio comprises two zero-coupon bonds, Bond A and Bond B.

Determine which portfolio produces a Redington immunization of the liabilities using an annual effective interest rate of 7.0%.

- A) Bond A: 1-year, current price 500; Bond B: 6-years, current price 500
- B) Bond A: 1-year, current price 572; Bond B: 6-years, current price 428
- C) Bond A: 3-years, current price 182; Bond B: 4-years, current price 1092
- D) Bond A: 3-years, current price 637; Bond B: 4-years, current price 637
- E) Bond A: 3.5 years, current price 1000; Bond B: Not used

## Explanation

The liabilities have present value $573/1.07^2 + 701/1.07^5 = 1000$.

The duration of the liabilities: $[2(573)/1.07^2 + 5(701)/1.07^5]/1000 = 3.5$.

For Redington, we need: (1) asset PV = liability PV = 1000, (2) asset duration = 3.5, (3) asset convexity > liability convexity.

Only portfolios A, B, and E have PV = 1000. Portfolio E uses a single bond with duration 3.5, but convexity equal to liability convexity — does not work. Portfolio B has duration $[572(1)+428(6)]/1000 = 3.14 \ne 3.5$.

**Portfolio A**: duration $= [500(1)+500(6)]/1000 = 3.5$ ✓. Convexity $= [500(1)+500(36)]/1000 = 18.5$. Liability convexity $= [4(573)/1.07^2+25(701)/1.07^5]/1000 = 14.5$. Since $18.5 > 14.5$, Redington conditions are satisfied.
