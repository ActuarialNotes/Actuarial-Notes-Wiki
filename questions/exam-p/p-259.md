An insurance company examines its pool of auto insurance customers. All customers insure at least one car; 64% insure more than one car; 20% insure a sports car; of those who insure more than one car, 15% insure a sports car.

Calculate the probability that a randomly selected customer insures exactly one car, and that the car is not a sports car.

- A) 0.16
- B) 0.19
- C) 0.26
- D) 0.29
- E) 0.31

## Explanation

$P(\text{sports, multiple cars}) = 0.64 \times 0.15 = 0.096$

$P(\text{sports, exactly one car}) = 0.20 - 0.096 = 0.104$

$P(\text{exactly one car, no sports}) = P(\text{exactly one car}) - P(\text{sports, exactly one})$

$$= 0.36 - 0.104 = \mathbf{0.256 \approx 0.26}$$
