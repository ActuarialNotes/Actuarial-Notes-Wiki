A health insurance company classifies applicants into categories A, B, or C where $P[A]=5P[C]$ and $P[B]=4P[C]$. The following probabilities of zero claims are given: $P(\text{zero}\mid A)=0.1$, $P(\text{zero}\mid B)=0.2$, $P(\text{zero}\mid C)=0.4$.

Calculate the probability that an insured was classified in category C, given that the insured had zero claims.

- A) 0.040
- B) 0.170
- C) 0.235
- D) 0.294
- E) 0.471

## Explanation

$P[A]+P[B]+P[C]=1 \Rightarrow 10P[C]=1 \Rightarrow P[C]=0.1$, $P[A]=0.5$, $P[B]=0.4$.

$$P(\text{zero}) = 0.5(0.1)+0.4(0.2)+0.1(0.4) = 0.05+0.08+0.04 = 0.17$$

$$P(C\mid\text{zero}) = \frac{0.1 \times 0.4}{0.17} = \frac{0.04}{0.17} \approx \mathbf{0.235}$$
