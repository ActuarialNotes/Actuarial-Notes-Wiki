A health insurer sells policies to residents of territory X and territory Y. Past claims experience indicates: 20% of total policyholders filed no claims; 15% of territory X policyholders filed no claims; 40% of territory Y policyholders filed no claims.

Calculate the probability that a randomly selected policyholder was a resident of territory X, given that the policyholder filed no claims.

- A) 0.09
- B) 0.27
- C) 0.50
- D) 0.60
- E) 0.80

## Explanation

Let $p = P(X)$. By law of total probability:

$$0.15p + 0.40(1-p) = 0.20$$

$$0.40 - 0.25p = 0.20 \implies p = 0.80$$

$$
\begin{align*}
P(X \mid \text{no claims}) &= \frac{0.15 \times 0.80}{0.20} \\
                           &= \frac{0.12}{0.20} \\
                           &= \mathbf{0.60}
\end{align*}
$$
