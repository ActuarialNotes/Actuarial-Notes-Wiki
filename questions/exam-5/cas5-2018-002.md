Given the following premium and loss information:

**Premium Transactions:**

| Policy | Original Effective Date | Original Expiration Date | Transaction Effective Date | Full-Term Premium ($) | Notes |
|--------|------------------------|--------------------------|---------------------------|----------------------|-------|
| A | July 1, 2016 | June 30, 2017 | July 1, 2016 | 800 | Start of New Policy |
| A | July 1, 2016 | June 30, 2017 | April 1, 2017 | 400 | Additional Premium for Endorsement |
| B | April 1, 2017 | March 31, 2018 | April 1, 2017 | 1,000 | Start of New Policy |
| C | October 1, 2017 | September 30, 2018 | October 1, 2017 | 500 | Start of New Policy |
| C | October 1, 2017 | September 30, 2018 | April 1, 2018 | N/A | Policy Cancelled |

**Loss Transactions:**

| Policy | Accident Date | Payment Date | Loss Payment ($) |
|--------|--------------|--------------|-----------------|
| A | October 1, 2016 | October 15, 2016 | 500 |
| A | January 1, 2017 | January 15, 2018 | 500 |
| B | October 1, 2017 | January 15, 2018 | 500 |
| C | October 1, 2017 | January 15, 2018 | 750 |

- Each claim is closed on the payment date.

## Part a (0.75 points)

Calculate the 2017 calendar year earned premium.

### Answer
1,375

### Explanation
For each policy, earn the premium over the portion of the policy period that falls within CY 2017 (January 1 – December 31, 2017).

**Policy A** (July 1, 2016 – June 30, 2017):
- Base premium of $800 (full-term). Policy overlaps CY 2017 from Jan 1 to Jun 30, 2017 = 6 months out of 12.
  $$800 \times \frac{6}{12} = 400$$
- Endorsement effective April 1, 2017 with full-term premium $400. This endorsement attaches to the remaining policy period Apr 1 – Jun 30, 2017 = 3 months. The full-term premium must be prorated for the 3 months remaining in the policy (and all 3 months fall in CY 2017).
  $$400 \times \frac{3}{12} = 100$$
- Policy A CY 2017 earned premium: $400 + 100 = 500$

**Policy B** (April 1, 2017 – March 31, 2018):
- CY 2017 overlap: Apr 1 – Dec 31, 2017 = 9 months.
  $$1{,}000 \times \frac{9}{12} = 750$$

**Policy C** (October 1, 2017 – September 30, 2018):
- CY 2017 overlap: Oct 1 – Dec 31, 2017 = 3 months. The cancellation is effective April 1, 2018, which is after CY 2017, so it does not affect CY 2017 earned premium.
  $$500 \times \frac{3}{12} = 125$$

**Total CY 2017 Earned Premium:**

$$500 + 750 + 125 = 1{,}375$$

### Examiner Report
Candidates were expected to earn the Policy A, B, and C premium transactions for CY 2017 based on their effective and expiration dates, prorating the full-term Policy A endorsement premium for the partial year that the endorsement was in effect, excluding the Policy C cancellation that was effective after CY 2017, and summing the earned premium from all policies for the total premium.

Candidates did not receive credit for stating an assumption that the Policy A endorsement premium was already prorated, as the premium was identified in the question as "Full-Term Premium."

Common mistakes include:
- Not prorating the full-term endorsement premium for Policy A.
- Earning the Policy A premium and endorsement premium over the same time period.

## Part b (1.25 points)

Calculate the 2017 policy year loss ratio evaluated at December 31, 2018.

### Answer
1.00 (100%)

### Explanation
Policy Year 2017 includes all policies with an original effective date in calendar year 2017: Policy B and Policy C. (Policy A has an effective date of July 1, 2016 and belongs to PY 2016.)

**PY 2017 Earned Premium:**

Policy B (Apr 1, 2017 – Mar 31, 2018) is fully earned by Dec 31, 2018:
$$EP_B = 1{,}000$$

Policy C (Oct 1, 2017 – Sep 30, 2018) is cancelled on April 1, 2018, after 6 months of a 12-month term. The earned portion is:
$$EP_C = 500 \times \frac{6}{12} = 250$$

$$\text{PY 2017 Earned Premium} = 1{,}000 + 250 = 1{,}250$$

**PY 2017 Incurred Losses as of December 31, 2018:**

All claims are closed on the payment date, so incurred losses equal paid losses for closed claims.

Policy B: Accident Oct 1, 2017 falls within the policy period Apr 1, 2017 – Mar 31, 2018. Loss = $500.

Policy C: Accident Oct 1, 2017 falls within the policy period Oct 1, 2017 – Sep 30, 2018 (the cancellation on Apr 1, 2018 does not void coverage for accidents prior to cancellation). Loss = $750.

Policy A's losses (Oct 1, 2016 and Jan 1, 2017 accidents) belong to PY 2016 and are excluded.

$$\text{PY 2017 Losses} = 500 + 750 = 1{,}250$$

**PY 2017 Loss Ratio:**

$$\frac{1{,}250}{1{,}250} = 1.00 = 100\%$$

### Examiner Report
Candidates were expected to determine that only Policy B and C pertained to PY 2017, sum the premium and loss transactions for those policies, adjust the Policy C premium for the mid-year cancellation, and calculate the loss ratio.

Common mistakes include:
- Not recognizing the policy cancellation for Policy C.
- Calculation more resembling calendar year than policy year.
