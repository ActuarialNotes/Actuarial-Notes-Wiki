An insurance company has a large number of claims pending. The amount $X$ of an individual pending claim follows a distribution with density function
$$f(x) = \frac{2}{x^3}, \quad x > 1$$

Calculate the probability that the amount of a randomly selected pending claim is less than 4, given that it is at least 3.

- A) 0.04
- B) 0.05
- C) 0.06
- D) 0.11
- E) 0.44

## Explanation

CDF: $F(x) = 1-1/x^2$.

$P(X < 4 \mid X \geq 3) = \dfrac{P(3 \leq X < 4)}{P(X \geq 3)}$

$$
\begin{align*}
P(3 \leq X < 4) &= F(4)-F(3) \\
                &= \frac{15}{16}-\frac{8}{9} \\
                &= \frac{135-128}{144} \\
                &= \frac{7}{144}
\end{align*}
$$

$$P(X \geq 3) = 1-F(3) = \frac{1}{9}$$

$$
\begin{align*}
P(X < 4 \mid X \geq 3) &= \frac{7/144}{1/9} \\
                       &= \frac{7 \times 9}{144} \\
                       &= \frac{63}{144} \\
                       &= \frac{7}{16} \\
                       &\approx \mathbf{0.44}
\end{align*}
$$
