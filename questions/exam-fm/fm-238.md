A perpetuity makes payments every five years with a first payment of 2 to be paid five years from now. Each subsequent payment is 10 more than the previous payment. The annual effective interest rate is 9%.

Calculate the present value of the perpetuity.

- A) 34.47
- B) 35.80
- C) 36.33
- D) 37.12
- E) 38.18

## Explanation

Let $j$ = the 5-year effective rate: $(1+j) = (1.09)^5 = 1.53862$, so $j = 0.538624$.

The perpetuity payments are $2, 12, 22, 32, \ldots$ at times 5, 10, 15, 20, … PV of arithmetic increasing perpetuity-immediate with first payment $P=2$, increase $Q=10$, and 5-year rate $j$:

$$PV = \frac{P}{j} + \frac{Q}{j^2} = \frac{2}{0.538624} + \frac{10}{(0.538624)^2} = 3.713 + 34.468 = 38.18$$
