In a study of driver safety, drivers were categorized according to three risk factors. Exactly 1000 drivers exhibited each individual risk factor. For each of the risk factors, exactly 400 drivers exhibited that risk factor and neither of the other two. Exactly 300 drivers exhibited all three risk factors, and 500 exhibited none.

Calculate the number of drivers in the study.

- A) 2000
- B) 2300
- C) 2450
- D) 2750
- E) 3500

## Explanation

For each factor, "only that factor" $= 400$. Since $|A| = 1000$:

$$\text{(only A\&B)} + \text{(only A\&C)} + 300 = 1000 - 400 = 600$$

By symmetry: only A\&B = only A\&C = only B\&C $= 100$ each.

$$|A \cup B \cup C| = 3(400) + 3(100) + 300 = 1800$$

$$\text{Total} = 1800 + 500 = \mathbf{2300}$$
