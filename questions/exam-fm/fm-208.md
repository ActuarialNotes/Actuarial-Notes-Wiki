A college has a scholarship fund that pays a sum of money twice a year. The scholarship will pay out 500 at the end of six months and another 500 at the end of one year. Every year thereafter, the two semi-annual payments will be increased by 10. For example, in year two, both payments will be 510 and in year three both payments will be 520.

The scholarship fund earns interest at an annual effective interest rate of 7.5%.

Calculate the fund balance needed today to provide this scholarship indefinitely.

- A) 16,000
- B) 16,589
- C) 16,889
- D) 17,134
- E) 17,200

## Explanation

The semiannual rate is $j = 1.075^{0.5} - 1 = 0.036822$.

Split into two perpetuities, one starting at $t = 0.5$ and one at $t = 1$:
- First (at $t=0.5$): payment 500 increasing by 10 each year = $\left(\dfrac{500}{0.075} + \dfrac{10}{0.075^2}\right)(1+j)^{-1}$
- Second (at $t=1$): $\left(\dfrac{500}{0.075} + \dfrac{10}{0.075^2}\right)$

Both use the formula $P/i + Q/i^2$ for an arithmetic increasing perpetuity.

$$PV = \left(\frac{500}{0.075}+\frac{10}{0.075^2}\right)(1+j)^{-1} + \left(\frac{500}{0.075}+\frac{10}{0.075^2}\right)$$

$$= (6666.67+1777.78)(1+j)^{-1} + (6666.67+1777.78) = 8444.44(1.036822+1) = 8444.44 \times 2.03682 = 17,200$$

Wait more precisely: $8444.44(1.036822) + 8444.44 = 8755.39 + 8444.44 = 17,199.83 \approx 17,200$.
