The number of policies that an agent sells has a Poisson distribution with modes at 2 and 3.

$K$ is the smallest number such that the probability of selling more than $K$ policies is less than 25%.

Calculate $K$.

- A) 1
- B) 2
- C) 3
- D) 4
- E) 5

## Explanation

For a Poisson distribution, if the mode occurs at two consecutive integers $k-1$ and $k$, it must be that the mean $\lambda$ is an integer and $\lambda = k$. 
Because the modes are 2 and 3, the parameter is $\lambda = 3$.

We need the smallest $K$ such that $P(X > K) < 0.25$, which is equivalent to $P(X \le K) > 0.75$.
Let's calculate the cumulative probabilities for a Poisson distribution with $\lambda = 3$:
* $P(X = 0) = e^{-3} \approx 0.0498$
* $P(X = 1) = 3e^{-3} \approx 0.1494$
* $P(X = 2) = \frac{9}{2}e^{-3} \approx 0.2240$
* $P(X = 3) = \frac{27}{6}e^{-3} \approx 0.2240$
* $P(X = 4) = \frac{81}{24}e^{-3} \approx 0.1680$

Cumulative probabilities:
* $P(X \le 2) = 0.0498 + 0.1494 + 0.2240 = 0.4232$
* $P(X \le 3) = 0.4232 + 0.2240 = 0.6472$
* $P(X \le 4) = 0.6472 + 0.1680 = 0.8152$

At $K = 4$, the cumulative probability $0.8152$ is the first that exceeds 0.75. Thus, 4 is the smallest number for which the probability of selling more than $K$ policies is less than 0.25.
