Let $X_1,\ldots,X_{100}$ be independent identically distributed random variables such that $P[X_i=0]=P[X_i=2]=0.5$. Let $S=X_1+\cdots+X_{100}$.

Calculate the approximate value of $P[S>115]$.

- A) 0.005
- B) 0.067
- C) 0.144
- D) 0.147
- E) 0.440

## Explanation

$E[X_i] = 0(0.5)+2(0.5) = 1$ and $E[X_i^2] = 4(0.5) = 2$, so $\text{Var}(X_i) = 1$.

By CLT: $S \approx N(100, 100)$, $\text{SD}(S) = 10$.

$$P(S > 115) = P\!\left(Z > \frac{115-100}{10}\right) = P(Z > 1.5)$$

$$= 1 - \Phi(1.5) \approx 1 - 0.9332 = \mathbf{0.0668 \approx 0.067}$$
