Bowl I contains eight red balls and six blue balls. Bowl II is empty. Four balls are selected at random, without replacement, and transferred from Bowl I to Bowl II. One ball is then selected at random from Bowl II.

Calculate the conditional probability that two red balls and two blue balls were transferred from Bowl I to Bowl II, given that the ball selected from Bowl II is blue.

- A) 0.21
- B) 0.24
- C) 0.43
- D) 0.49
- E) 0.57

## Explanation

$\binom{14}{4} = 1001$. Compute joint probabilities for transfer composition and blue ball drawn:

| Transferred | $P(\text{composition})$ | $P(\text{blue} \mid \text{comp})$ | Joint |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0R, 4B | $15/1001$ | $4/4 = 1$ | $15/1001$ |
| 1R, 3B | $160/1001$ | $3/4$ | $120/1001$ |
| **2R, 2B** | **$420/1001$** | **$2/4 = 1/2$** | **$210/1001$** |
| 3R, 1B | $336/1001$ | $1/4$ | $84/1001$ |
| 4R, 0B | $70/1001$ | $0$ | $0$ |

$P(\text{blue drawn}) = (15+120+210+84)/1001 = 429/1001$

$$
\begin{align*}
P(2R,2B \mid \text{blue}) &= \frac{210/1001}{429/1001} \\
                          &= \frac{210}{429} \\
                          &= \frac{70}{143} \\
                          &\approx \mathbf{0.49}
\end{align*}
$$
