An entrepreneur takes out a business loan for 60,000 with a nominal annual interest rate compounded monthly. The loan is scheduled to be paid off with level monthly payments, plus a final drop payment. All payments will be made at the end of the month.

The principal portion of the payment is 1,400 for the first month and 1,414 for the second month.

Calculate the drop payment.

- A) 780
- B) 788
- C) 1183
- D) 1676
- E) 1692

## Explanation

Let $j$ = monthly rate and $X$ = monthly payment. Principal in month 1 is $1400 = X - 60,000j$. Principal in month 2 is $1414 = X - (60,000 - 1400)j$.

From the difference: $14 = 1400j$, so $j = 0.01$ and $X = 2000$.

Number of full payments: $60,000 = 2000a_{\overline{n}|0.01}$ gives $n = 35.8455$, so 35 full payments.

The drop payment $P$ satisfies:

$$
\begin{align*}
60,000 &= 2000a_{\overline{35}|0.01} + Pv^{36} \\
&= 58,817.16 + 0.698925P \\
P &= (60,000 - 58,817.16)/0.698925 \\
&= 1,692
\end{align*}
$$
