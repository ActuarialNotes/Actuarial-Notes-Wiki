An annuity writer sells an annual perpetuity-immediate for 16,000. The first payment is 1,000 and the payments decrease by $r$% each year.

The annual effective yield rate is 5.7%.

Calculate $r$.

- A) 0.52
- B) 0.55
- C) 0.62
- D) 0.73
- E) 0.91

## Explanation

This is a geometrically decreasing perpetuity. The growth factor is $1 - r/100$. With yield $i = 5.7\%$:

$$16,000 = \frac{1,000}{i - (-r/100)} = \frac{1,000}{0.057 - (-r/100)}$$

Wait — payments decrease, so growth factor $= 1 - r/100 < 1$. The PV formula for a geometrically decreasing perpetuity-immediate:

$$16,000 = \frac{1,000}{i+r/100} = \frac{1,000}{0.057+r/100}$$

$$0.057 + r/100 = 1,000/16,000 = 0.0625$$

$$r/100 = 0.0625 - 0.057 = 0.0055 \implies r = 0.55$$
