A four-year 1000 face amount bond, with an annual coupon rate of 5% paid semiannually, has redemption value of $C$. It is bought at a price to yield an annual nominal rate of 6% convertible semiannually. If the term of the bond had been two years, the purchase price would have been 7% less.

Calculate $C$.

- A) 455
- B) 469
- C) 541
- D) 611
- E) 626

## Explanation

Four-year bond: $P = 25a_{\overline{8}|0.03} + Cv^8 = 25(7.01969) + C(0.78941) = 175.49 + 0.78941C$

Two-year bond: $0.93P = 25a_{\overline{4}|0.03} + Cv^4 = 25(3.71710) + C(0.88849) = 92.93 + 0.88849C$

Substituting $P$:
$$0.93(175.49 + 0.78941C) = 92.93 + 0.88849C$$
$$163.21 + 0.73415C = 92.93 + 0.88849C$$
$$70.28 = 0.15434C$$
$$C = 455.37 \approx 455$$
