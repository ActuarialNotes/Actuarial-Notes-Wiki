A borrower takes out a loan of 4000 at an annual effective interest rate of 6%.

Starting at the end of the fifth year, the loan is repaid by annual payments, each of which equals 600 except for a final balloon payment that is less than 1000.

Calculate the final balloon payment.

- A) 616
- B) 639
- C) 642
- D) 688
- E) 696

## Explanation

The outstanding balance at the end of year 4 (just before repayments begin): $4000(1.06)^4 = 5,049.94$.

Find the number of level payments: $5,049.94 = 600 \cdot a_{\overline{n}|0.06}$, so $a_{\overline{n}|} = 8.4165$, giving $n = 12.07$, meaning 11 full payments of 600.

After 11 payments, the outstanding balance is:
$$5,049.94(1.06)^{11} - 600 s_{\overline{11}|0.06} = 5,049.94(1.8983) - 600(14.9716) = 9,587.19 - 8,982.96 = 604.23$$

The balloon payment (one year later): $604.23(1.06) = 640.48 \approx 639$.
