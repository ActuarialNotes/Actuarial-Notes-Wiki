In a group of 15 health insurance policyholders diagnosed with cancer, each policyholder has probability 0.90 of receiving radiation and probability 0.40 of receiving chemotherapy. Radiation and chemotherapy treatments are independent events for each policyholder, and the treatments of different policyholders are mutually independent. The insurance pays 2 for radiation treatment and 3 for chemotherapy treatment.

Calculate the variance of the total amount the insurance company pays for the radiation and chemotherapy treatments for these 15 policyholders.

- A) 13.5
- B) 37.8
- C) 108.0
- D) 202.5
- E) 567.0

## Explanation

For each policyholder $i$, let $R_i \sim \text{Bernoulli}(0.9)$ and $C_i \sim \text{Bernoulli}(0.4)$ be independent. Payment $= 2R_i + 3C_i$.

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{Var}(2R_i + 3C_i) &= 4\,\text{Var}(R_i) + 9\,\text{Var}(C_i) \\
                        &= 4(0.9)(0.1) + 9(0.4)(0.6) \\
                        &= 0.36 + 2.16 \\
                        &= 2.52
\end{align*}
$$

Since the 15 policyholders are independent:
$$\text{Var}(\text{Total}) = 15 \times 2.52 = \mathbf{37.8}$$
