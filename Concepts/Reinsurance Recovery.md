**Reinsurance Recovery** is the amount an insurer expects to collect from its reinsurers for losses and LAE ceded under treaty or facultative reinsurance agreements, such that net losses equal gross losses minus ceded (recovered) losses.

- The relationship is $\text{Net Losses} = \text{Gross Losses} - \text{Ceded Losses}$; each component should be developed separately because gross and ceded losses may have different development patterns, especially under excess-of-loss treaties where ceded amounts are sensitive to large individual claims
- Reinsurance recoveries should be tracked with their own development triangles — ceded loss development can lag gross loss development since reinsurers may dispute, commute, or delay payment on large claims
- Actuaries working on a **net basis** must ensure that reinsurance recoverables are collectible (consider reinsurer credit risk) and that changes in reinsurance structure between years are reflected before applying historical net development factors

> [!example]- Net vs. Gross Loss Calculation {Example}
> An insurer has gross incurred losses of \$$4{,}000{,}000$ for accident year $2023$. Ceded losses under an excess-of-loss treaty (retention \$$500{,}000$ per occurrence) are estimated at \$$900{,}000$.
>
> > [!answer]-
> > Net incurred losses $= \$4{,}000{,}000 - \$900{,}000 = \$3{,}100{,}000$. If the actuary applies development factors to gross and ceded triangles separately and the ceded ultimate is \$$1{,}100{,}000$, the net ultimate loss is \$$4{,}800{,}000 - \$1{,}100{,}000 = \$3{,}700{,}000$, which may differ from applying net factors directly to net losses.
