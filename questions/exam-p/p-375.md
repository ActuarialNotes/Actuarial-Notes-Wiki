$X$ and $Y$ are independent and identically distributed standard normal random variables.

Calculate $P[X^2 < 1 \mid Y = 1]$.

- A) 0.32
- B) 0.45
- C) 0.50
- D) 0.68
- E) 0.95

## Explanation

Since $X$ and $Y$ are independent, $P[X^2 < 1 \mid Y=1] = P[X^2 < 1] = P[-1 < X < 1]$.

$$
\begin{align*}
P[-1 < X < 1] &= 2\Phi(1)-1 \\
              &\approx 2(0.8413)-1 \\
              &= \mathbf{0.6827 \approx 0.68}
\end{align*}
$$
