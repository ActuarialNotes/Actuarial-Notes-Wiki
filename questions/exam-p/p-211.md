A flood insurance company determines that $N$, the number of claims received in a month, satisfies $P[N=n]=\frac{2}{3^{n+1}}$ for $n=0,1,2,\ldots$ Months are independent. Calculate the probability that more than three claims will be received during a consecutive two-month period, given that fewer than two claims were received in the first month.

- A) 0.0062
- B) 0.0123
- C) 0.0139
- D) 0.0165
- E) 0.0185

## Explanation

$P(N<2)=\frac{2}{3}+\frac{2}{9}=\frac{8}{9}$.

$N_1=0$: $P(N_2>3)=\frac{1}{81}$; $N_1=1$: $P(N_2>2)=\frac{1}{27}$.

$$P(\text{sum}>3,N_1<2)=\frac{2}{3}\cdot\frac{1}{81}+\frac{2}{9}\cdot\frac{1}{27}=\frac{4}{243}$$

$$P=\frac{4/243}{8/9}=\frac{1}{54}\approx\mathbf{0.0185}$$
