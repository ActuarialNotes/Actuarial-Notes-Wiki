An actuary is reviewing workers compensation indemnity loss experience for a rate level indication analysis. Given the following information:

- A benefit change having an impact of +5.0% applies to all indemnity losses for accidents occurring after July 1, 2011.
- A benefit change having an impact of +2.0% applies to indemnity losses on policies written after October 1, 2012.
- No other benefit changes are expected within the next few years.
- The annual impact on benefits due to wage inflation has been +2.0% and is expected to continue.
- The proposed effective date for revised loss costs is July 1, 2013.
- Policies are annual.
- Revised loss costs would be in effect for one year.
- Losses occur uniformly throughout the year.

| Accident Year | Estimated Ultimate Losses at Pre-July 1, 2011 Benefit Levels ($000s) |
|---|---|
| 2010 | $1,875 |
| 2011 | $1,875 |
| 2012 | $2,000 |

Calculate the 2010, 2011, and 2012 accident year projected ultimate losses to be used in the rate level indication.

### Answer

| Accident Year | Projected Ultimate Losses ($000s) |
|---|---|
| 2010 | $2,173.7 |
| 2011 | $2,131.2 |
| 2012 | $2,228.5 |

### Explanation

**Step 1 — Determine the future average accident date.**

Revised loss costs are effective July 1, 2013 for one year (through June 30, 2014). For annual policies written uniformly over this period, the average accident date is **July 1, 2014** (average written date = January 1, 2014, plus 6-month policy mid-point).

**Step 2 — Determine benefit change adjustment factors.**

The given losses are expressed at **Pre-July 1, 2011 benefit levels**. Two benefit changes have occurred since then, and both must be reflected in the projected losses used for the future rate period:

- **Benefit change 1 (+5.0%)**: applies to all indemnity losses for accidents occurring after July 1, 2011. Since the future rate period covers accidents in the 2013–2014 policy year (after July 1, 2011), this 5% increase applies. The historical losses are at Pre-July 1, 2011 levels, so all accident years require this adjustment regardless of when their accidents occurred, because future losses will be settled at the higher benefit level.
- **Benefit change 2 (+2.0%)**: applies to losses on policies written after October 1, 2012. All future policies will be written after this date, so this 2% increase is also required for all years.

Combined **benefit level adjustment factor** (applied to all three accident years):

$$\text{Benefit Factor} = 1.05 \times 1.02 = 1.071$$

**Step 3 — Determine wage inflation trend periods.**

Trend from the average accident date of each historical accident year to the average accident date of the future rate period (July 1, 2014), using an annual wage inflation trend of +2.0%.

| Accident Year | Avg Accident Date | Trend Period | Wage Trend Factor |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2010 | July 1, 2010 | 4.0 years | $(1.02)^4 = 1.0824$ |
| 2011 | July 1, 2011 | 3.0 years | $(1.02)^3 = 1.0612$ |
| 2012 | July 1, 2012 | 2.0 years | $(1.02)^2 = 1.0404$ |

**Step 4 — Compute projected ultimate losses.**

$$\text{Projected Ultimate} = \text{Given Ultimate} \times \text{Wage Trend Factor} \times \text{Benefit Factor}$$

**AY 2010:**

$$1{,}875 \times (1.02)^4 \times 1.071 = 1{,}875 \times 1.0824 \times 1.071 = 1{,}875 \times 1.15932 \approx \mathbf{\$2{,}173.7 \text{ (thousands)}}$$

**AY 2011:**

$$1{,}875 \times (1.02)^3 \times 1.071 = 1{,}875 \times 1.0612 \times 1.071 = 1{,}875 \times 1.13662 \approx \mathbf{\$2{,}131.2 \text{ (thousands)}}$$

**AY 2012:**

$$2{,}000 \times (1.02)^2 \times 1.071 = 2{,}000 \times 1.0404 \times 1.071 = 2{,}000 \times 1.11427 \approx \mathbf{\$2{,}228.5 \text{ (thousands)}}$$

**Summary:**

| AY | Given Ult ($000s) | Wage Trend Factor | Benefit Factor | Projected Ult ($000s) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2010 | $1,875 | $(1.02)^4 = 1.0824$ | $1.071$ | $\mathbf{2{,}173.7}$ |
| 2011 | $1,875 | $(1.02)^3 = 1.0612$ | $1.071$ | $\mathbf{2{,}131.2}$ |
| 2012 | $2,000 | $(1.02)^2 = 1.0404$ | $1.071$ | $\mathbf{2{,}228.5}$ |

### Examiner Report

This was a straightforward calculation question. The most challenging aspect was the statement that the given losses were at Pre-July 1, 2011 benefit levels, which means **all three accident years** must be adjusted by both benefit changes for full credit.

The majority of candidates missed this subtlety and instead applied a different benefit change factor to each accident year. Common mistakes among those candidates:
- Treating the +5.0% benefit change as applying to policies written on or after July 1, 2011 (the question stated it applied to losses on or after that date, meaning future losses from all AYs will be at the higher level).
- Treating the +2.0% benefit change as applying to losses on or after October 1, 2012 (the question stated it applied to policies written after that date).

Several candidates correctly calculated the average benefit level for losses in each accident year, but then multiplied the given losses directly by the average benefit level rather than using it to derive an adjustment factor before applying it.
