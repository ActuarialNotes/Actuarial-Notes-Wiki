An investor's retirement account pays an annual nominal interest rate of 4.2%, convertible monthly.

On January 1 of year $y$, the investor's account balance was $X$. The investor then deposited 100 at the end of every quarter. On May 1 of year $(y+10)$, the account balance was $1.9X$.

Determine which of the following is an equation of value that can be used to solve for $X$.

- A) $\dfrac{1.9X}{(1.0105)^{124/3}} + \displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{42}\dfrac{100}{(1.0105)^{k-1}} = X$
- B) $X + \displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{42}\dfrac{100}{(1.0035)^{3(k-1)}} = \dfrac{1.9X}{(1.0035)^{124}}$
- C) $X + \displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{41}\dfrac{100}{(1.0035)^{3k}} = \dfrac{1.9X}{(1.0035)^{124}}$
- D) $X + \displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{41}\dfrac{100}{(1.0105)^{k-1}} = \dfrac{1.9X}{(1.0105)^{124/3}}$
- E) $X + \displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{42}\dfrac{100}{(1.0105)^{k-1}} = \dfrac{1.9X}{(1.0105)^{124/3}}$

## Explanation

Monthly rate $= 0.0035$. Quarterly rate $= 1.0035^3 - 1 = 0.0105$.

The period from Jan 1 year $y$ to May 1 year $y+10$ is $10\text{ yr} + 4\text{ months} = 124$ months. The investor makes quarterly deposits; between Jan 1 year $y$ and Apr 1 year $y+10$ there are 41 complete quarters (the 42nd quarter would end Jul 1 year $y+10$, which is after the evaluation date).

Using Jan 1 year $y$ as the comparison date:

$$X + \sum_{k=1}^{41}\frac{100}{(1.0035)^{3k}} = \frac{1.9X}{(1.0035)^{124}}$$

This is option **(C)**. (Substituting $1.0105 = 1.0035^3$ confirms equivalence with the other form.)
