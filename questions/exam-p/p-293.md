A company provides disability benefits to its employees. There are only two possible benefits: partial disability (costing 40) and total disability (costing 200). For a married couple $(X,Y)$, the joint probability function is:

|  | $y=0$ | $y=40$ | $y=200$ |
|---|---|---|---|
| $x=0$ | 0.9729 | 0.0100 | 0.0020 |
| $x=40$ | 0.0100 | 0.0020 | 0.0005 |
| $x=200$ | 0.0020 | 0.0005 | 0.0001 |

Calculate the standard deviation of the total disability cost $X+Y$.

- A) 12.3
- B) 13.8
- C) 15.7
- D) 16.6
- E) 19.8

## Explanation

$E[X] = 40(0.0125)+200(0.0026) = 0.5+0.52 = 1.02$. By symmetry $E[Y]=1.02$.

$E[X+Y] = 2.04$

For $E[(X+Y)^2]$, summing $(x+y)^2 \cdot p(x,y)$ over all cells:

$$E[(X+Y)^2] = 1600(0.02)+6400(0.002)+57600(0.001)+40000(0.004)+160000(0.0001) = 278.4$$

$$\text{Var}(X+Y) = 278.4 - 2.04^2 = 278.4 - 4.16 = 274.24$$

$$\text{SD}(X+Y) = \sqrt{274.24} \approx \mathbf{16.6}$$
