An insurance company covers 100 independent drivers, each of whom independently files 0, 1, or 2 claims with probabilities 0.65, 0.25, and 0.10 respectively. Let $T$ denote the total number of claims.

Calculate the standard deviation of $T$.

- A) 5.40
- B) 6.25
- C) 6.69
- D) 7.12
- E) 7.81

## Explanation

For each driver: $E[N]=0(0.65)+1(0.25)+2(0.10) = 0.45$.

$E[N^2]=0+0.25+4(0.10)=0.65$.

$\text{Var}(N) = 0.65-0.45^2 = 0.65-0.2025 = 0.4475$.

$T = \sum_{i=1}^{100} N_i$, so by independence:

$$\text{Var}(T) = 100 \times 0.4475 = 44.75$$

$$\text{SD}(T) = \sqrt{44.75} \approx \mathbf{6.69}$$
