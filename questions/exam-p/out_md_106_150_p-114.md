An actuary analyzes a company's annual personal auto claims, $M$, and annual commercial auto claims, $N$. The analysis reveals that $Var(M) = 1600$, $Var(N) = 900$, and the correlation between $M$ and $N$ is 0.64.

Calculate $Var(M+N)$.

- A) 768
- B) 2500
- C) 3268
- D) 4036
- E) 4420

## Explanation

First, calculate the covariance between $M$ and $N$ using the correlation formula:
$$Corr(M, N) = \frac{Cov(M, N)}{\sqrt{Var(M)Var(N)}}$$
$$0.64 = \frac{Cov(M, N)}{\sqrt{1600 \times 900}} = \frac{Cov(M, N)}{40 \times 30} = \frac{Cov(M, N)}{1200}$$
$$Cov(M, N) = 0.64 \times 1200 = 768$$

The variance of the sum is:
$$Var(M+N) = Var(M) + Var(N) + 2Cov(M, N)$$
$$Var(M+N) = 1600 + 900 + 2(768) = 2500 + 1536 = 4036$$
