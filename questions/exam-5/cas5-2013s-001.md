Given the following information for an insurance company that writes 24-month term policies:

| Policy Group | Effective Date | Expiration Date | Number of Vehicles |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | January 1, 2010 | December 31, 2011 | 50 |
| B | July 1, 2010 | June 30, 2012 | 100 |

All policies within each group have the same effective date. An exposure is defined as one vehicle insured for one year.

## Part a (0.5 points)

Calculate the earned car-years for calendar year 2011.

### Answer
150

### Explanation
Both 24-month policies earn exactly one calendar year of exposure during 2011.

- Group A (50 vehicles, Jan 1 2010–Dec 31 2011): earns full year 2011 → $50 \times 2 \times \frac{12}{24} = 50$
- Group B (100 vehicles, Jul 1 2010–Jun 30 2012): earns full year 2011 → $100 \times 2 \times \frac{12}{24} = 100$

$$\text{CY 2011 Earned} = 50 + 100 = 150 \text{ car-years}$$

### Examiner Report
Most candidates answered correctly. A common error was treating the provided vehicle counts as the total car-years over the 24-month policy period (halving the result).

## Part b (0.5 points)

Calculate the earned car-years for policy year 2010 evaluated as of December 31, 2010 and as of December 31, 2011.

### Answer
As of December 31, 2010: 100  
As of December 31, 2011: 250

### Explanation
Policy year 2010 includes all policies written in 2010: Group A (written Jan 1, 2010) and Group B (written Jul 1, 2010). PY 2010 exposure earns as policies are in force.

**As of December 31, 2010:**
- Group A: earns Jan 1–Dec 31 2010 = $50 \times 2 \times \frac{12}{24} = 50$
- Group B: earns Jul 1–Dec 31 2010 = $100 \times 2 \times \frac{6}{24} = 50$

$$\text{PY 2010 Earned (12/31/2010)} = 50 + 50 = 100 \text{ car-years}$$

**As of December 31, 2011:**
- Group A: fully earned Jan 1 2010–Dec 31 2011 = $50 \times 2 \times \frac{24}{24} = 100$
- Group B: earns Jul 1 2010–Dec 31 2011 = $100 \times 2 \times \frac{18}{24} = 150$

$$\text{PY 2010 Earned (12/31/2011)} = 100 + 150 = 250 \text{ car-years}$$

### Examiner Report
Most candidates answered correctly. Common errors included computing only one of the two evaluation dates, or halving the vehicle count as in Part a.

## Part c (0.5 points)

Assume Policy Group B cancels on January 1, 2011. Calculate the 2010 policy year written car-years evaluated as of December 31, 2010 and as of December 31, 2011 for Policy Group B.

### Answer
As of December 31, 2010: 200  
As of December 31, 2011: 50

### Explanation
Group B (100 vehicles, 24-month policy) was written on July 1, 2010 with total written exposure = $100 \times 2 = 200$ car-years.

**As of December 31, 2010:** The cancellation on January 1, 2011 has not yet occurred, so written exposure is the full original amount:

$$\text{PY 2010 Written (12/31/2010)} = 200 \text{ car-years}$$

**As of December 31, 2011:** Upon cancellation on January 1, 2011, the unearned exposure is returned. Group B had earned $100 \times 2 \times \frac{6}{24} = 50$ car-years by January 1, 2011, so the return (cancellation) is $200 - 50 = 150$ car-years:

$$\text{PY 2010 Written (12/31/2011)} = 200 - 150 = 50 \text{ car-years}$$

### Examiner Report
Candidates generally answered correctly. Some incorrectly included Group A or gave a combined value; full credit was awarded when Group B was clearly identified. Some missed that the evaluated written exposure changes upon cancellation by the return of unearned exposure.

## Part d (0.5 points)

Assume Policy Group B cancels on July 1, 2011. Calculate the 2010 and 2011 calendar year written car-years for Policy Group B.

### Answer
CY 2010: 200  
CY 2011: −100

### Explanation
Group B was written on July 1, 2010 (CY 2010), generating $100 \times 2 = 200$ written car-years in CY 2010.

Upon cancellation on July 1, 2011, Group B had earned exactly half its exposure: $100 \times 2 \times \frac{12}{24} = 100$ car-years. The remaining $100$ car-years of unearned exposure are returned, creating a negative written entry in CY 2011:

$$\text{CY 2010 Written} = +200 \text{ car-years}$$
$$\text{CY 2011 Written} = -100 \times 2 \times \frac{12}{24} = -100 \text{ car-years}$$

### Examiner Report
Candidates generally answered correctly. A common error was being unfamiliar with the concept of negative calendar-year written exposures when a multi-year policy is cancelled in a subsequent year — these candidates often answered 0 or 100 for CY 2011.
