You are given the following information about a homogenous Markov chain:

- $$P = \begin{bmatrix} u & v & 0 \\ 1/3 & 1/3 & w \\ x & y & z \end{bmatrix}$$
- The limiting probabilities of all three states are equal.

Calculate $z$.

- A) Less than 0.35
- B) At least 0.35, but less than 0.45
- C) At least 0.45, but less than 0.55
- D) At least 0.55, but less than 0.65
- E) At least 0.65

## Explanation

Equal limiting probabilities means the stationary distribution is $\pi = (1/3, 1/3, 1/3)$. The stationary condition $\pi P = \pi$ with a *uniform* $\pi$ requires every **column** of $P$ to sum to 1 (i.e. $P$ is doubly stochastic):

$$\pi_j = \sum_i \pi_i P_{ij} = \tfrac{1}{3}\sum_i P_{ij} = \tfrac{1}{3} \implies \sum_i P_{ij} = 1.$$

First, each **row** sums to 1. Row 2 gives $\tfrac{1}{3} + \tfrac{1}{3} + w = 1$, so $w = \tfrac{1}{3}$.

Now apply the column-sum condition to column 3:

$$0 + w + z = 1 \implies z = 1 - w = 1 - \tfrac{1}{3} = \tfrac{2}{3} \approx 0.667.$$

This is at least $0.65$, so the answer is **E**.
