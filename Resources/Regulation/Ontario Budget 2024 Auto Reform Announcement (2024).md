# Ontario Budget 2024: Auto Reform Blueprint (March 2024)

## Overview
The 2024 Ontario Budget announced the most significant restructuring of the Statutory Accident Benefits Schedule since 2016: all accident benefits other than medical, rehabilitation, and attendant care would become optional, and auto insurers would become first payor for medical and rehabilitation benefits ahead of extended health plans. Ontario Regulation 383/24 subsequently amended the SABS to give these changes legal form, with implementation set for July 1, 2026.

## Key Details
*   **Optionality Principle:** Income replacement, non-earner, caregiver, housekeeping, death, and funeral benefits designated as optional purchases rather than mandatory inclusions.
*   **First-Payor Reordering:** Auto policy to respond before employer or private extended health coverage for med/rehab (prescription drugs excepted in the final design).
*   **Stated Objective:** Premium affordability through consumer choice, paired with consumer-protection guardrails to be developed with FSRA.

## Multi-Agent Execution Mandates
*   **Actuarial & Product:** Two-year runway to decompose accident benefits pure premiums by individual benefit line and design optional-coverage architecture (later expressed through the OPCF 47R endorsement).
*   **Government Relations & Legal:** Industry consultation on regulation drafting, forms, and transition rules through FSRA technical working groups.
*   **Executive:** Strategic positioning decisions — bundle design, target take-up rates, and channel readiness — initiated well before the 2026 effective date.
