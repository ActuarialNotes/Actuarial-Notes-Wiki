You are given the following information:

- $X$, $Y$, and $Z$ are independent random variables.
- $X$, $Y$, and $Z$ follow exponential distributions with means 2, 4, and 5, respectively.
- A single observation is taken from each of the three random variables.

Calculate the probability that the maximum of the three observed values is less than 6.

- A) Less than 0.20
- B) At least 0.20, but less than 0.30
- C) At least 0.30, but less than 0.40
- D) At least 0.40, but less than 0.50
- E) At least 0.50

## Explanation

The maximum of independent variables is below 6 exactly when **all three** are below 6. By independence,

$$\Pr[\max < 6] = \Pr[X < 6]\,\Pr[Y < 6]\,\Pr[Z < 6].$$

For an exponential with mean $\mu$, $\Pr[W < 6] = 1 - e^{-6/\mu}$:

$$\Pr[X < 6] = 1 - e^{-6/2} = 1 - e^{-3} = 0.95021,$$
$$\Pr[Y < 6] = 1 - e^{-6/4} = 1 - e^{-1.5} = 0.77687,$$
$$\Pr[Z < 6] = 1 - e^{-6/5} = 1 - e^{-1.2} = 0.69881.$$

$$\Pr[\max < 6] = 0.95021 \times 0.77687 \times 0.69881 \approx 0.5159.$$

This is at least $0.50$, so the answer is **E**.
