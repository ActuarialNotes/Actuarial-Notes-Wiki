A company has 100 insureds. 60% have probability 0.20 of filing a claim in a year; 40% have probability 0.10 of filing a claim. Claims are independent. Let $N$ be the total number of claims filed in a year.

Calculate the standard deviation of $N$.

- A) 3.41
- B) 3.56
- C) 3.67
- D) 3.76
- E) 3.87

## Explanation

By the law of total variance: $\text{Var}(N) = E[\text{Var}(N|T)]+\text{Var}(E[N|T])$

where $T$ = type for each insured.

Per insured: $E[\text{Var}(N_i|T_i)] = 0.60(0.2)(0.8)+0.40(0.1)(0.9) = 0.096+0.036 = 0.132$

$\text{Var}(E[N_i|T_i]) = 0.60(0.2)^2+0.40(0.1)^2-(0.16)^2 = 0.024+0.004-0.0256 = 0.0024$

For 100 independent insureds:

$$\text{Var}(N) = 100(0.132)+100(0.0024) = 13.2+0.24 = 13.44$$

$$\text{SD}(N) = \sqrt{13.44} \approx \mathbf{3.67}$$
