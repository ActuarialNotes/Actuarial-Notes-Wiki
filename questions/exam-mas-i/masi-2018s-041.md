You are given the following information from a time series:

| $t$ | $x_t$ |
|---|---|
| 1 | 3.0 |
| 2 | 2.5 |
| 3 | 2.0 |
| 4 | 3.5 |
| 5 | 4.0 |
| 6 | 3.0 |
| $\bar{x}$ | 3.0 |

Calculate the sample lag 3 autocorrelation.

- A) Less than -0.30
- B) At least -0.30, but less than -0.10
- C) At least -0.10, but less than 0.10
- D) At least 0.10, but less than 0.30
- E) At least 0.30

## Explanation

The sample autocorrelation at lag $k$ is

$$r_k = \frac{\sum_{t=1}^{n-k}(x_t - \bar{x})(x_{t+k} - \bar{x})}{\sum_{t=1}^{n}(x_t - \bar{x})^2}.$$

The deviations from $\bar{x} = 3.0$ are $\{0, -0.5, -1.0, 0.5, 1.0, 0\}$.

**Numerator (lag 3):**

$$(0)(0.5) + (-0.5)(1.0) + (-1.0)(0) = -0.5.$$

**Denominator:**

$$0 + 0.25 + 1.0 + 0.25 + 1.0 + 0 = 2.5.$$

$$r_3 = \frac{-0.5}{2.5} = -0.20.$$

This falls in $[-0.30, -0.10)$, so the answer is **B**.
