At the end of each year for 60 years, Marilyn makes a deposit to a bank account that credits interest at an annual effective interest rate of $i$. She deposits 2 at the end of the first year, and each subsequent year her deposit increases by 2. At the end of 60 years, Marilyn uses the accumulated amount to purchase a 5-year annuity-immediate paying annually at the same annual interest rate of $i$, with a first payment of $X$ and each subsequent payment increasing by 5%.

Which of the following expressions represents a correct equation of value?

- A) $\dfrac{2(s_{\overline{60}|}-60)}{i} = \dfrac{X\left(1-(1.05v)^5\right)}{i-0.05}$
- B) $2(Ia)_{\overline{60}|} = Xv^{60}\left(1+1.05v+\ldots+(1.05v)^4\right)$
- C) $2\displaystyle\sum_{t=0}^{59}s_{\overline{60-t}|} = X\left(v+1.05v^2+\ldots+(1.05)^4v^5\right)$
- D) $2(Ia)_{\overline{60}|} = Xv^{60}\left(1+1.05v+\ldots+(1.05)^4v^4\right)$
- E) The correct answer is not given by (A), (B), (C), or (D).

## Explanation

The accumulated value of Marilyn's deposits at end of year 60 is $2(Is)_{\overline{60}|} = 2\sum_{t=0}^{59} s_{\overline{60-t}|}$.

The present value at end of year 60 of the 5-year geometrically increasing annuity:
$$X\left(v + 1.05v^2 + \cdots + (1.05)^4v^5\right)$$

Setting these equal gives statement **III**. Checking statement I: $(s_{\overline{60}|}-60)/i = (Is)_{\overline{60}|}$ ✓ but the RHS uses $a$ not the correct geometric formula. Statement III is the correct one.
