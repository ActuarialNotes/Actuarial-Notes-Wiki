Payments of 1000 at the end of each year are paid on a loan of 12,000. The payments are based on an annual effective interest rate of 10%.

Calculate the outstanding loan balance immediately after the 12th payment.

- A) 909
- B) 10,909
- C) 12,853
- D) 16,277
- E) 20,043

## Explanation

Since the payment of 1000 is less than the interest due in year 1 ($12,000 \times 0.10 = 1,200$), the loan balance grows. Using the retrospective method:

$$
\begin{align*}
OB_{12} &= 12,000(1.10)^{12} - 1000s_{\overline{12}|0.10} \\
        &= 37,661.14 - 1000(21.38428) \\
        &= 16,276.86
\end{align*}
$$
