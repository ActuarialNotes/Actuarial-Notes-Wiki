A 20-year loan of 1000 is repaid with payments at the end of each year.

Each of the first ten payments equals 150% of the amount of interest due. Each of the last ten payments is $X$.

The lender charges interest at an annual effective rate of 10%.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 32
- B) 57
- C) 70
- D) 97
- E) 117

## Explanation

For the first 10 years, each payment equals 150% of interest due. Since the lender charges 10%, and 100% of interest is paid plus an extra 50% of interest = 5% of principal, the principal is reduced by 5% each year.

At the end of 10 years, the outstanding balance is:

$$1000(1 - 0.05)^{10} = 598.74$$

The equation of value for the last 10 years (comparison date: end of year 10):

$$598.74 = Xa_{\overline{10}|10\%} = 6.1446X$$

$$X = 97.44$$
