An insurance company categorizes its policyholders into three mutually exclusive groups: high-risk, medium-risk, and low-risk. An internal study showed that 45% of the policyholders are low-risk and 35% are medium-risk. The probability of death over the next year, given that a policyholder is high-risk, is two times the probability of death of a medium-risk policyholder. The probability of death over the next year, given that a policyholder is medium-risk, is three times the probability of death of a low-risk policyholder. The probability of death of a randomly selected policyholder over the next year is 0.009.

Calculate the probability of death of a policyholder over the next year, given that the policyholder is high-risk.

- A) 0.0025
- B) 0.0200
- C) 0.1215
- D) 0.2000
- E) 0.3750

## Explanation

Let $P(\text{death} | \text{low}) = x$. Then:
$$P(\text{death} | \text{medium}) = 3x, \quad P(\text{death} | \text{high}) = 6x$$

Proportions: low = 45%, medium = 35%, high = 20%.

By the Law of Total Probability:
$$
\begin{align*}
P(\text{death}) &= 0.45x + 0.35(3x) + 0.20(6x) \\
                &= 0.45x + 1.05x + 1.20x \\
                &= 2.70x \\
                &= 0.009
\end{align*}
$$
$$x = \frac{0.009}{2.70} = \frac{1}{300}$$

Therefore:
$$
\begin{align*}
P(\text{death} | \text{high}) &= 6x \\
                              &= \frac{6}{300} \\
                              &= \frac{1}{50} \\
                              &= \mathbf{0.020}
\end{align*}
$$
