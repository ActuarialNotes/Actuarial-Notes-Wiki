SOA Life Insurance Company has a portfolio of two bonds:
- Bond 1 is a bond with a Macaulay duration of 7.28 and a price of 35,000; and
- Bond 2 is a bond with a Macaulay duration of 12.74 and a price of 65,000.

The price and Macaulay duration for both bonds were calculated using an annual effective interest rate of 4.32%.

Bailey estimates the value of this portfolio at an interest rate of $i$ using the first-order Macaulay approximation to be 105,000.

Determine $i$.

- A) 3.49%
- B) 3.62%
- C) 3.85%
- D) 3.92%
- E) 4.03%

## Explanation

The portfolio Macaulay duration: $\frac{35,000(7.28)+65,000(12.74)}{100,000} = 10.829$.

First-order Macaulay approximation:
$$105,000 = 100,000\left(\frac{1.0432}{1+i}\right)^{10.829}$$

$$\left(\frac{1.0432}{1+i}\right)^{10.829} = 1.05$$

$$\frac{1.0432}{1+i} = 1.05^{1/10.829} = 1.004516$$

$$1+i = 1.0432/1.004516 = 1.0385 \implies i = 3.85\%$$
