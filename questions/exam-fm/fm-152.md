A 20-year bond priced to have an annual effective yield of 10% has a Macaulay duration of 11. Immediately after the bond is priced, the market yield rate increases by 0.25%. The bond's approximate percentage price change, using a first-order modified approximation, is $X$.

Calculate $X$.

- A) −2.22%
- B) −2.47%
- C) −2.50%
- D) −2.62%
- E) −2.75%

## Explanation

Modified duration $= D_{MAC}/(1+i) = 11/1.10 = 10$.

The first-order modified duration approximation:
$$\frac{\Delta P}{P} \approx -D_{MOD} \cdot \Delta i = -10 \times 0.0025 = -0.025 = -2.50\%$$
