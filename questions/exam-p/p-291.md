Let $X$ and $Y$ be discrete random variables with joint probability function:

$$p(0,0)=0.250,\; p(0,1)=0.250,\; p(1,0)=0.125,\; p(1,1)=0.375$$

Calculate $\text{Corr}(X, Y)$.

- A) 0.06
- B) 0.23
- C) 0.26
- D) 0.38
- E) 0.63

## Explanation

$P(X=1) = 0.500$, $P(Y=1) = 0.625$.

$$E[X] = 0.5, \quad E[Y] = 0.625, \quad E[XY] = 0.375$$

$$\text{Cov}(X,Y) = 0.375 - (0.5)(0.625) = 0.0625$$

$$\text{SD}(X) = \sqrt{0.5(0.5)} = 0.5$$

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{SD}(Y) &= \sqrt{0.625(0.375)} \\
             &= \sqrt{0.234375} \\
             &\approx 0.4841
\end{align*}
$$

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{Corr}(X,Y) &= \frac{0.0625}{0.5 \times 0.4841} \\
                 &\approx \mathbf{0.258 \approx 0.26}
\end{align*}
$$
