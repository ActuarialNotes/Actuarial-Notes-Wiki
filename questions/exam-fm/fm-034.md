Bill buys a 10-year 1000 par value bond with semi-annual coupons. The coupon rate equals the yield rate, so Bill paid 1000 for the bond. The coupons can be reinvested at a semi-annual rate of $j$.

At the end of 10 years, the total accumulated value (coupons reinvested plus redemption) provides Bill with a yield rate of 7% per annum effective on his 1000 investment.

Calculate the annual effective yield rate, $i$, that Bill earns on his investment.

- A) 9.50%
- B) 9.75%
- C) 10.00%
- D) 10.25%
- E) 10.50%

## Explanation

Since the coupon rate equals the yield rate, Bill paid 1000 and receives 30 every six months. The equation of value at end of 10 years:

$$1000(1.07)^{10} = 30\,s_{\overline{20}|j} + 1000$$

$$s_{\overline{20}|j} = 32.238$$

Solving numerically: $j = 0.0476$. The annual effective yield on reinvestment is:

$$
\begin{align*}
i &= (1.0476)^2 - 1 \\
&= 0.0975 \\
&= 9.75\%
\end{align*}
$$
