Ernie makes deposits of 100 at time 0, and $X$ at time 3. The fund grows at a force of interest

$$\delta_t = \frac{t^2}{100}, \quad t > 0$$

The amount of interest earned from time 3 to time 6 is also $X$.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 385
- B) 485
- C) 585
- D) 685
- E) 785

## Explanation

The accumulation function is:

$$a(t) = \exp\left[\int_0^t \frac{s^2}{100}\,ds\right] = \exp\!\left(\frac{t^3}{300}\right)$$

The accumulated value of the 100 deposit at time 3:

$$100\exp\!\left(\frac{3^3}{300}\right) = 100\exp(0.09) = 109.41743$$

The interest earned from time 3 to 6 equals the accumulated value at time 6 minus that at time 3. Thus:

$$(109.41743 + X)\left[\frac{a(6)}{a(3)} - 1\right] = X$$

$$(109.41743 + X)\left(\frac{2.0544332}{1.0941743} - 1\right) = X$$

$$(109.41743 + X)(0.877613) = X$$

$$96.026159 = 0.122387X$$

$$X = 784.61$$
