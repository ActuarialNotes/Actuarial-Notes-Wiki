Data on a certain pregnancy test show that a pregnant woman will test negative 10% of the time, while a non-pregnant woman will test positive 20% of the time. Thirty percent of the women who take the test are pregnant.

Calculate the probability that a woman is pregnant given that her test outcome is positive.

- A) 0.18
- B) 0.30
- C) 0.66
- D) 0.82
- E) 0.90

## Explanation

$P(\text{pos}\mid\text{preg})=0.90$, $P(\text{pos}\mid\text{not})=0.20$.

$$
\begin{align*}
P(\text{pos}) &= 0.30(0.90)+0.70(0.20) \\
              &= 0.27+0.14 \\
              &= 0.41
\end{align*}
$$

$$
\begin{align*}
P(\text{preg}\mid\text{pos}) &= \frac{0.27}{0.41} \\
                             &\approx \mathbf{0.659 \approx 0.66}
\end{align*}
$$
