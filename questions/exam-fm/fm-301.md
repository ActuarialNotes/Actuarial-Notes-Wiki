A ten-year 100 face amount bond has an annual coupon rate of 8% payable semiannually. The bond is callable at the end of the 5th, 7th, and 9th years at par, immediately after the coupon payment.

Calculate the maximum price of the bond that ensures an annual effective yield of at least 6%.

- A) 108.92
- B) 111.83
- C) 114.41
- D) 115.59
- E) 116.74

## Explanation

The coupon is 4 per period. The semiannual yield equivalent to 6% effective annual is $j = 1.06^{0.5}-1 = 0.029563$.

Since the coupon rate (4%) > yield per period (2.9563%), the bond is at a premium, so the worst case for the investor is the earliest call date (end of year 5, i.e., 10 periods).

$$
\begin{align*}
P &= 4a_{\overline{10}|0.029563} + 100(1.029563)^{-10} \\
  &= 4(8.5666) + 100(0.74726) \\
  &= 34.27 + 74.73 \\
  &= 108.92
\end{align*}
$$

The maximum price to ensure at least 6% yield is **108.92**.
