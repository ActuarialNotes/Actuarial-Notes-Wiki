Four distinct integers are chosen randomly and without replacement from the first twelve positive integers. Let $X$ be the random variable representing the second largest of the four selected integers, and let $p$ be the probability function for $X$.

Determine $p(x)$, for integer values of $x$ where $p(x) > 0$.

- A) $\dfrac{(x-1)(x-2)(12-x)}{990}$\n- B) $\dfrac{(x-1)(x-2)(12-x)}{495}$\n- C) $\dfrac{(x-1)(12-x)(11-x)}{495}$\n- D) $\dfrac{(x-1)(12-x)(11-x)}{990}$\n- E) $\dfrac{(10-x)(12-x)(11-x)}{990}$

## Explanation

For $X = x$ (second largest), the four chosen numbers must include:
- exactly **1** number from $\{x+1, \ldots, 12\}$: $\binom{12-x}{1}$ choices,
- the number $x$ itself,
- exactly **2** numbers from $\{1, \ldots, x-1\}$: $\binom{x-1}{2}$ choices.

$$p(x) = \frac{\binom{x-1}{2}\binom{12-x}{1}}{\binom{12}{4}} = \frac{\frac{(x-1)(x-2)}{2}(12-x)}{495} = \frac{(x-1)(x-2)(12-x)}{990}$$

Valid for $x = 3, 4, \ldots, 11$ (need at least 2 below and at least 1 above).
