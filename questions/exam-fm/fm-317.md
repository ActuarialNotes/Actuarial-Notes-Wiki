A bank issues two 30-year bonds, A and B, each with annual coupons, an annual effective yield rate of 7%, and a face amount of 1000. The total price of these two bonds is 3000.

Bond B's annual coupon rate is equal to Bond A's annual coupon rate plus 0.5%.

Calculate the annual coupon rate of Bond A.

- A) 10.06%
- B) 10.78%
- C) 10.90%
- D) 11.31%
- E) 11.84%

## Explanation

Let $r_A$ = Bond A coupon rate, $r_B = r_A + 0.005$.

$$P_A + P_B = 1000\left[\frac{1}{(1.07)^{30}} + r_A a_{\overline{30}|} + \frac{1}{(1.07)^{30}} + r_B a_{\overline{30}|}\right] = 3000$$

$$3 = \frac{2}{(1.07)^{30}} + (2r_A + 0.005)a_{\overline{30}|0.07}$$

$$3 = 2(0.13137) + (2r_A+0.005)(12.4090) = 0.26273 + 24.818r_A + 0.06205$$

$$r_A = (3-0.26273-0.06205)/24.818 = 2.67522/24.818 = 0.10780 = 10.78\%$$
