Let $X$ be a random variable with density function $f(x)=2x$ for $0<x<1$. A sample of size 3 is randomly selected. Let $Y$ be a random variable representing the median value (second-order statistic) from the sample.

Calculate the variance of $Y$.

- A) 0.019
- B) 0.030
- C) 0.056
- D) 0.500
- E) 0.714

## Explanation

Density of the 2nd order statistic from a sample of 3:

$$
\begin{align*}
f_Y(y) &= \frac{3!}{1!\,1!\,1!}\,[F(y)]^1 f(y)\,[1-F(y)]^1 \\
       &= 6\,y^2\cdot 2y\cdot(1-y^2) \\
       &= 12y^3(1-y^2)
\end{align*}
$$

$$
\begin{align*}
E[Y] &= \int_0^1 12y^4(1-y^2)\,dy \\
     &= 12\!\left(\frac{1}{5}-\frac{1}{7}\right) \\
     &= \frac{24}{35}
\end{align*}
$$

$$
\begin{align*}
E[Y^2] &= \int_0^1 12y^5(1-y^2)\,dy \\
       &= 12\!\left(\frac{1}{6}-\frac{1}{8}\right) \\
       &= \frac{1}{2}
\end{align*}
$$

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{Var}(Y) &= \frac{1}{2}-\left(\frac{24}{35}\right)^2 \\
              &= \frac{1}{2}-\frac{576}{1225} \\
              &= \frac{612.5-576}{1225} \\
              &= \frac{36.5}{1225} \\
              &\approx \mathbf{0.030}
\end{align*}
$$
