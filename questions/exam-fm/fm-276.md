A perpetuity-immediate pays 20 for 10 years, decreases by 1 per year for 19 years, and then pays 1 per year thereafter. At an annual effective interest rate of 6%, the present value is equal to $X$.

Calculate $X$.

- A) 208
- B) 213
- C) 218
- D) 223
- E) 228

## Explanation

Split the perpetuity into three parts:

1. Level payments of 20 for years 1–10: $20a_{\overline{10}|0.06} = 20(7.3601) = 147.20$

2. Decreasing payments of 19, 18, ..., 1 for years 11–29, i.e., $(Da)_{\overline{19}|}$ deferred 10 years: $v^{10}(Da)_{\overline{19}|0.06} = v^{10}\cdot 130.70 = (0.55839)(130.70) = 72.98$

3. Perpetuity of 1 starting at year 29 (deferred 28 years... actually starting at year 30 when the 1 per year begins): $v^{29}/0.06 = (1.06)^{-29}/0.06 = 0.18456/0.06 = 3.08$

Total $= 147.20 + 72.98 + 3.08 = 223.26 \approx 223$
