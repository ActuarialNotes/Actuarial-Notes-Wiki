A company created a portfolio in order to protect its position using Redington immunization.

Under which of the following changes in the yield rate is the immunization strategy guaranteed to be effective?

- A) Only a small decrease in the yield rate
- B) Only a small increase in the yield rate
- C) Only a small change in the yield rate
- D) Any decrease in the yield rate
- E) Any change in the yield rate

## Explanation

Redington immunization is a **first-order** approximation technique. It matches the present value and duration of assets and liabilities, with the asset convexity exceeding the liability convexity.

This construction guarantees protection against **small parallel shifts** in the yield rate, in **either direction** (both increases and decreases). However, for large changes or non-parallel shifts, the immunization may break down.

Therefore, the strategy is guaranteed effective only for **small changes** in the yield rate (answer C).
