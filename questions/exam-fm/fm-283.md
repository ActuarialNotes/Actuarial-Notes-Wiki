Paul makes one investment of 500 on January 1, 2005 and collects 600 on January 1, 2007 for an annual effective yield of $X$%.

Toby invests 100 on January 1, 2005, invests another 100 on January 1, 2006, and collects an amount $Z$ on January 1, 2007 for an annual effective yield of $Y$%.

The combination of Paul's and Toby's cash flows, produces an annual effective yield of 10%.

Calculate $Y\% - X\%$.

- A) 0%
- B) 1%
- C) 2%
- D) 3%
- E) 4%

## Explanation

Paul's yield:

$$
\begin{align*}
500(1+X)^2 &= 600 \\
X &= \sqrt{1.2}-1 = 9.544\%
\end{align*}
$$

Combined cash flows at 10% yield (invest 600 at time 0, invest 100 at time 1, collect $600+Z$ at time 2):

$$
\begin{align*}
600(1.10)^2 + 100(1.10) &= 600 + Z \\
726 + 110 &= 600 + Z \\
Z &= 236
\end{align*}
$$

Toby's yield — let $u = 1+Y$:

$$
\begin{align*}
100u^2 + 100u &= 236 \\
u &= \frac{-100 + \sqrt{10{,}000+94{,}400}}{200} = \frac{-100+330}{200} = 1.15549 \\
Y &= 15.549\%
\end{align*}
$$

Therefore $Y - X = 15.549\% - 9.544\% = 2\%$.
