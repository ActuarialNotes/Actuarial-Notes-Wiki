For a set of data with 40 observations, 2 predictors ($X_1$ and $X_2$), and one response ($Y$), the residual sum of squares has been calculated for several different estimates of a linear model with no intercept. Only integer values from 1 to 5 were considered for estimates of $\beta_1$ and $\beta_2$.

The grid below shows the residual sum of squares for every combination of the parameter estimates, after standardization (rows are $\hat\beta_1$, columns are $\hat\beta_2$):

| $\hat\beta_1 \backslash \hat\beta_2$ | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2,855.0 | 870.3 | 464.4 | 357.2 | 548.6 |
| 2 | 1,059.1 | 488.4 | 216.3 | 242.8 | 567.9 |
| 3 | 657.0 | 220.0 | 81.6 | 241.9 | 700.8 |
| 4 | 368.4 | 65.1 | 60.5 | 354.5 | 947.1 |
| 5 | 193.2 | 23.7 | 152.8 | 580.6 | 1,307.0 |

Let:
- $\hat\beta_1^L$ = Estimate of $\beta_1$ using a lasso with budget parameter $s = 5$
- $\hat\beta_2^L$ = Estimate of $\beta_2$ using a lasso with budget parameter $s = 5$

Calculate the ratio $\hat\beta_1^L / \hat\beta_2^L$.

- A) Less than 0.5
- B) At least 0.5, but less than 1.0
- C) At least 1.0, but less than 1.5
- D) At least 1.5, but less than 2.0
- E) At least 2.0

## Explanation

The lasso minimizes RSS subject to the $\ell_1$ budget $|\beta_1| + |\beta_2| \le s = 5$. Since the candidate estimates are positive integers, we need $\beta_1 + \beta_2 \le 5$. Among all feasible cells, find the smallest RSS:

| $(\beta_1, \beta_2)$ | RSS |
|---|---|
| (1,4) | 357.2 |
| (2,3) | **216.3** |
| (3,2) | 220.0 |
| (4,1) | 368.4 |
| (others with smaller sum) | larger |

The minimum RSS subject to the constraint is $216.3$ at $(\hat\beta_1^L, \hat\beta_2^L) = (2, 3)$. Therefore

$$\frac{\hat\beta_1^L}{\hat\beta_2^L} = \frac{2}{3} \approx 0.667.$$

This falls in $[0.5, 1.0)$, so the answer is **B**.
