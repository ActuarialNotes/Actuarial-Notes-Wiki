# OSFI LICAT Update Letter (July 2025)

## Overview
Via industry letter, OSFI postponed the next planned revision of the Life Insurance Capital Adequacy Test from 2027 to post-2028 — committing to at least one year's notice before restarting — and immediately reduced capital requirements for qualifying domestic infrastructure debt and equity held by federally regulated life insurers.

## Key Details
*   **Stability Signal:** Part of a broader OSFI posture of easing regulatory change-load and supporting economic growth amid macro uncertainty; the in-flight Quantitative Impact Study continues to inform the eventual update.
*   **Infrastructure Incentive:** Criteria-based capital relief for domestic infrastructure investments aligns prudential policy with national investment objectives.
*   **Broad Scope:** Applies to all federally regulated life insurers, including foreign branches, fraternals, and insurance holding companies.

## Multi-Agent Execution Mandates
*   **Investments & Finance:** Asset-allocation and capital-planning models updated for the infrastructure factors; multi-year capital forecasts re-baselined without a 2027 LICAT shock.
*   **Actuarial:** QIS participation remains the channel shaping the eventual framework; internal capital targets reviewed for the extended status quo.
