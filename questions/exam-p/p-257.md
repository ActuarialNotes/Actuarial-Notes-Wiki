Let $X$ be the percentage score on a college-entrance exam for students who did not participate in an exam-preparation seminar. $X \sim \text{Uniform}[a, 100]$. Let $Y$ be the score for students who did participate. $Y \sim \text{Uniform}[1.25a, 100]$. It is given that $E[X^2] = 19600/3$.

Calculate the 80th percentile of $Y$.

- A) 80
- B) 85
- C) 90
- D) 92
- E) 95

## Explanation

For $\text{Uniform}[a,b]$: $E[X^2] = (a^2+ab+b^2)/3$.

$$\frac{a^2+100a+10000}{3} = \frac{19600}{3}$$

$$a^2+100a-9600 = 0 \implies a = \frac{-100+\sqrt{10000+38400}}{2} = \frac{-100+220}{2} = 60$$

So $1.25a = 75$ and $Y \sim \text{Uniform}[75, 100]$.

80th percentile of $Y$:

$$
\begin{align*}
y_{0.80} &= 75 + 0.80(100-75) \\
         &= 75 + 20 \\
         &= \mathbf{95}
\end{align*}
$$
