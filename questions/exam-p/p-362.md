$X$ and $Y$ are independent random variables, each uniformly distributed on $[-10, 30]$.

Calculate $P[X+Y > 0]$.

- A) 0.25
- B) 0.40
- C) 0.56
- D) 0.75
- E) 0.88

## Explanation

The joint distribution is uniform on $[-10,30]^2$ with total area $40^2=1600$.

$P(X+Y \leq 0)$ = area of $\{(x,y): x+y \leq 0\} \cap [-10,30]^2$.

The line $x+y=0$ intersects the square at $(-10,10)$ and $(10,-10)$. The region below this line within the square is a triangle with vertices $(-10,-10)$, $(-10,10)$, $(10,-10)$:

$$\text{Area} = \tfrac{1}{2}(20)(20) = 200$$

$$P(X+Y > 0) = 1 - \frac{200}{1600} = 1-\frac{1}{8} = \frac{7}{8} \approx \mathbf{0.875}$$
