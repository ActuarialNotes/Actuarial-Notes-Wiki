The distribution of the size of claims paid under an insurance policy has probability density function
$$f(x) = a \cdot c^x, \quad 0 < x < 5$$
where $a > 0$ and $c > 0$ (and $f(x) = 0$ otherwise).

For a randomly selected claim, the probability that the size of the claim is less than 3.75 is 0.4871.

Calculate the probability that the size of a randomly selected claim is greater than 4.

- A) 0.404
- B) 0.428
- C) 0.500
- D) 0.572
- E) 0.596

## Explanation

The density has the form $f(x) \propto x^c$, normalized on $[0, 5]$:
$$f(x) = \frac{c+1}{5^{c+1}}\,x^c, \quad F(x) = \left(\frac{x}{5}\right)^{c+1}$$

**Find $c$ from** $F(3.75) = 0.4871$:
$$(0.75)^{c+1} = 0.4871$$
$$(c+1)\ln(0.75) = \ln(0.4871) \implies c+1 = \frac{-0.7186}{-0.2877} = 2.498 \approx 2.5$$

**Compute $P(X > 4)$:**
$$
\begin{align*}
P(X > 4) &= 1 - F(4) \\
         &= 1 - (0.8)^{2.5} \\
         &= 1 - 0.64 \times \sqrt{0.8} \\
         &= 1 - 0.64(0.8944) \\
         &\approx 1 - 0.5724 \\
         &= \mathbf{0.428}
\end{align*}
$$
