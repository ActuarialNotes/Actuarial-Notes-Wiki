You are given the following information:

- There are two individuals, age (45) and age (65).
- Using the Illustrative Life Table, the level premium for a whole life of 1 for age (45) is calculated as $P_{45}$.
- Using the Illustrative Life Table, the level premium for a whole life of 1 for age (65) is calculated as $P_{65}$.

Calculate the difference $(P_{45} - P_{65})$.

- A) Less than -0.04
- B) At least -0.04, but less than -0.02
- C) At least -0.02, but less than 0.00
- D) At least 0.00, but less than 0.02
- E) At least 0.02

## Explanation

The net level annual premium for a unit whole life insurance is

$$P_x = \frac{A_x}{\ddot{a}_x},$$

where $A_x$ is the whole-life APV and $\ddot{a}_x$ is the life annuity-due. From the Illustrative Life Table (at $i = 6\%$):

$$P_{45} = \frac{A_{45}}{\ddot{a}_{45}} = \frac{0.20120}{14.1121} \approx 0.01426,$$

$$P_{65} = \frac{A_{65}}{\ddot{a}_{65}} = \frac{0.43980}{9.8969} \approx 0.04444.$$

The premium rises with issue age, so the difference is negative:

$$P_{45} - P_{65} \approx 0.01426 - 0.04444 = -0.03018.$$

This falls in $[-0.04, -0.02)$, so the answer is **B**.
