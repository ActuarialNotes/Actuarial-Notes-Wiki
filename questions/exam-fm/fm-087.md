A company takes out a loan of 15,000,000 at an annual effective discount rate of 5.5%. You are given:

  i) The loan is to be repaid with $n$ annual payments of 1,200,000 plus a drop payment one year after the $n$th payment.
  ii) The first payment is due three years after the loan is taken out.

Calculate the amount of the drop payment.

- A) 79,100
- B) 176,000
- C) 321,300
- D) 959,500
- E) 1,180,300

## Explanation

The annual effective interest rate is $i = (1-d)^{-1} - 1 = (1-0.055)^{-1} - 1 = 5.82\%$.

The balance at time 2 (just before payments begin) is $15,000,000(1.0582)^2 = 16,796,809$.

Solve for $n$: $1,200,000 \cdot a_{\overline{n}|} = 16,796,809$ gives $n = 29.795$, so $n = 29$ full payments.

The drop payment $X$ satisfies:
$$1,200,000 \cdot a_{\overline{29}|} + X(1.0582)^{-30} = 16,796,809$$
$$X = (16,796,809 - 16,621,012)(5.45799) = 959,490$$
