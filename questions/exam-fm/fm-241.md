An investor pays 4000 today for a three-year investment that returns cash flows of 1400 at the end of each year. The cash flows can be reinvested at the positive annual effective interest rate of $i$. Using an annual effective rate of interest of 4%, the net present value of this investment is 0.

Calculate $i$.

- A) 2.5%
- B) 3.0%
- C) 3.5%
- D) 4.0%
- E) 7.0%

## Explanation

NPV $= 0$ at 4% means the NPV equation holds at $i = 4\%$. The equation of value at the end of year 3 (accumulating the investment and requiring it to equal the accumulated reinvested cash flows) is:

$$4{,}000(1.04)^3 = 1{,}400\left[1 + (1+i) + (1+i)^2\right]$$

$$
\begin{align*}
4{,}499.46 &= 1{,}400\left[1 + (1+i) + (1+i)^2\right] \\
1 + (1+i) + (1+i)^2 &= 3.2139
\end{align*}
$$

Let $x = 1+i$, so $x^2 + x - 2.2139 = 0$:

$$
\begin{align*}
x &= \frac{-1 + \sqrt{1 + 4(2.2139)}}{2} = \frac{-1 + \sqrt{9.8556}}{2} = \frac{-1 + 3.1393}{2} = 1.0697 \\
i &= 6.97\% \approx 7.0\%
\end{align*}
$$
