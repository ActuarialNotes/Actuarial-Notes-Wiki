A 25-year loan is being repaid with annual payments of 1300 at an annual effective rate of interest of 7%. The borrower pays an additional 2600 at the time of the 5th payment and wants to repay the remaining balance over 15 years.

Calculate the revised annual payment.

- A) 1054.58
- B) 1226.65
- C) 1300.00
- D) 1369.38
- E) 1512.12

## Explanation

Using the prospective method, the outstanding balance immediately after the 4th payment (i.e., with 21 payments remaining) is:

$$1300\,a_{\overline{21}|0.07} = 1300(11.7641) = 15,293.33$$

Wait — with a 25-year loan, after 4 payments there are 21 remaining, so the balance is $1300a_{\overline{21}|0.07}$. After the 5th payment of 1300, the balance drops by the principal portion; but more directly:

The outstanding balance after the 5th payment (20 payments remaining, prospective):

$$1300\,a_{\overline{20}|0.07} = 1300(10.5940) = 13,772.20$$

After the extra payment of 2600:

$$13,772.20 - 2600 = 11,172.20$$

The revised annual payment over 15 years:

$$\frac{11,172.20}{a_{\overline{15}|0.07}} = \frac{11,172.20}{9.1079} = 1,226.65$$
