**Close Year** is a data organization method that groups claims by the calendar year in which they were closed or finally settled, regardless of when the loss occurred or was reported.

- Close year is rarely used in ratemaking because it introduces a lag from both the accident date and the report date, making it difficult to match premiums to the corresponding loss period
- It is most useful for **operational analysis** — measuring claims department settlement speed, closure rates, and the efficiency of claims handling workflows
- A single accident year's claims may span many close years, so development patterns under close year are driven more by claims practices than by underlying loss emergence

> [!example]- Close Year Assignment {Example}
> An auto liability claim arises from an accident on $3/10/2022$, is reported on $4/1/2022$, and is litigated until a settlement is reached on $9/14/2024$.
>
> > [!answer]-
> > The claim falls in **Accident Year 2022**, **Report Year 2022**, and **Close Year 2024**. For ratemaking, accident year is used; for measuring how long claims stay open, close year $2024$ is the relevant classification.
