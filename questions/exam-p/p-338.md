The following table shows the joint probability function of a dental patient's annual root canals and fillings:

| Root canals $\backslash$ Fillings | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | 0.40 | 0.26 | 0.05 | 0.04 | 0.01 |
| 1 | 0.04 | 0.03 | 0.03 | 0.03 | 0.02 |
| 2 | 0.01 | 0.01 | 0.02 | 0.03 | 0.02 |

Calculate the expected number of root canals the patient undergoes, given that the patient undergoes at most one filling this year.

- A) 0.11
- B) 0.15
- C) 0.17
- D) 0.33
- E) 0.91

## Explanation

$P(\leq 1 \text{ filling}) = (0.40+0.04+0.01)+(0.26+0.03+0.01) = 0.45+0.30 = 0.75$

Given $\leq 1$ filling:

$$P(0 \text{ RC}) = \frac{0.40+0.26}{0.75} = \frac{0.66}{0.75},\quad P(1\text{ RC}) = \frac{0.07}{0.75},\quad P(2\text{ RC}) = \frac{0.02}{0.75}$$

$$E[\text{RC}\mid\leq1 \text{ filling}] = \frac{0(0.66)+1(0.07)+2(0.02)}{0.75} = \frac{0.11}{0.75} \approx \mathbf{0.147 \approx 0.15}$$
