John made a deposit of 1000 into a fund at the beginning of each year for 20 years.

At the end of 20 years, he began making semiannual withdrawals of 3000 at the beginning of each six months, with a smaller final withdrawal to exhaust the fund. The fund earned an annual effective interest rate of 8.16%.

Calculate the amount of the final withdrawal.

- A) 561
- B) 1226
- C) 1430
- D) 1488
- E) 2240

## Explanation

Accumulated value of the 20 deposits (annuity-due):

$$1000\ddot{s}_{\overline{20}|0.0816} = 50,382.16$$

The semiannual effective rate equivalent to 8.16% annual: $(1.0816)^{1/2} - 1 = 4\%$.

Solve for the number of full withdrawals of 3000 (annuity-due):

$$3000\ddot{a}_{\overline{n}|0.04} \leq 50,382.16 \implies n = 26.47$$

There will be 26 full withdrawals plus one final smaller withdrawal. After 26 payments:

$$50,382.16 = 3000\ddot{a}_{\overline{26}|0.04} + X(1.04)^{-26}$$

$$X = (50,382.16 - 3000\ddot{a}_{\overline{26}|0.04})(1.04)^{26} = 1430$$
