On a block of ten houses, $k$ are not insured. A tornado randomly damages three houses on the block.

The probability that none of the damaged houses are insured is $\dfrac{1}{120}$.

Calculate the probability that at most one of the damaged houses is insured.

- A) $1/5$
- B) $7/40$
- C) $11/60$
- D) $49/60$
- E) $119/120$

## Explanation

$\binom{10}{3} = 120$. The probability that none of the damaged houses are insured is:
$$
\begin{align*}
P(\text{none insured}) &= \frac{\binom{k}{3}}{\binom{10}{3}} \\
                       &= \frac{\binom{k}{3}}{120} \\
                       &= \frac{1}{120}
\end{align*}
$$
So $\binom{k}{3} = 1$, giving $k = 3$ (since $\binom{3}{3} = 1$). Thus 3 houses are uninsured and 7 are insured.

**P(at most one insured) = P(0 insured) + P(1 insured):**
$$
\begin{align*}
P(0) &= \frac{\binom{3}{3}\binom{7}{0}}{\binom{10}{3}} \\
     &= \frac{1}{120}
\end{align*}
$$
$$
\begin{align*}
P(1) &= \frac{\binom{7}{1}\binom{3}{2}}{\binom{10}{3}} \\
     &= \frac{7 \cdot 3}{120} \\
     &= \frac{21}{120}
\end{align*}
$$
$$
\begin{align*}
P(\text{at most 1}) &= \frac{1 + 21}{120} \\
                    &= \frac{22}{120} \\
                    &= \frac{11}{60}
\end{align*}
$$
