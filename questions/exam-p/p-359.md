An insurer pays hospital costs $X \sim \text{Exp}(\text{mean } 3000)$ and doctor costs $Y \sim \text{Exp}(\text{mean } 1500)$, independent. Total claims $W = X + Y$.

Calculate $P[W > 3000]$.

- A) 0.37
- B) 0.45
- C) 0.60
- D) 0.72
- E) 0.86

## Explanation

For $X \sim \text{Exp}(\lambda_1)$ and $Y \sim \text{Exp}(\lambda_2)$ with $\lambda_1 \neq \lambda_2$:

$$P(W > t) = \frac{\lambda_2}{\lambda_2-\lambda_1}e^{-\lambda_1 t} - \frac{\lambda_1}{\lambda_2-\lambda_1}e^{-\lambda_2 t}$$

With $\lambda_1=1/3000$, $\lambda_2=1/1500$, and $\lambda_2-\lambda_1=1/3000$:

$$P(W>3000) = 2e^{-1}-e^{-2}$$

$$\approx 2(0.3679)-0.1353 = 0.7358-0.1353 = \mathbf{0.600}$$
