# FSRA UDAP Rule (Effective April 1, 2022)

## Overview
FSRA's UDAP Rule replaced the prescriptive UDAP regulation under the Insurance Act with a principles-based rule defining outcomes that constitute unfair or deceptive acts or practices — FSRA's first major use of its independent rule-making authority.

## Key Details
*   **Outcome-Based Prohibitions:** Organized around results such as unfair discrimination, misleading conduct, unfair claims practices, and improper incentives, rather than enumerated technical violations.
*   **Claims Conduct:** Codifies that unreasonable delay, deception, or coercion in claims handling is a UDAP.
*   **Incentive Controls:** Tightens rules on inducements and rebating tied to insurance transactions, with auto-specific protections retained.

## Multi-Agent Execution Mandates
*   **Compliance:** Conduct-risk frameworks mapped to outcome principles; evidence of fair-treatment governance now expected in FSRA supervisory reviews.
*   **Claims & CX:** Claims-handling guidelines, denial-letter standards, and complaint-handling metrics aligned to the fairness outcomes.
*   **Distribution:** Incentive and referral arrangements re-papered to survive principles-based scrutiny.
