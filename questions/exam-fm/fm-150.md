A company must pay liabilities of 1000 at the end of year 1 and $X$ at the end of year 2. The only investments available are:

  i) One-year zero-coupon bonds with an annual effective yield of 5%
  ii) Two-year bonds with a par value of 1000 and 10% annual coupons, with an annual effective yield of 6%

The company constructed a portfolio that creates an exact cash flow matching strategy for these liabilities. The total purchase price of this portfolio is 1783.76.

Calculate the amount invested in the one-year zero-coupon bonds.

- A) 784
- B) 831
- C) 871
- D) 915
- E) 935

## Explanation

Let $A$ = redemption value of zero-coupon bonds, $B$ = number of two-year bonds.

The two-year bond pays 100 at year 1 and 1100 at year 2. For exact matching:
- Year 1: $A + 100B = 1000$
- Year 2: $1100B = X$

Total cost: $A/1.05 + B \cdot P_B = 1783.76$ where $P_B = 100/1.06 + 1100/1.06^2 = 94.34 + 978.00 = 1072.34/1.06 = 1073.35$.

Hmm, let me use $P_B = 100(1.06)^{-1} + 1100(1.06)^{-2} = 94.34 + 978.00 = 1072.34$. Total cost: $A/1.05 + 1072.34B = 1783.76$.

From year 1: $A = 1000 - 100B$. Substituting:
$$
\begin{align*}
(1000-100B)/1.05 + 1072.34B &= 1783.76 \\
952.38 - 95.24B + 1072.34B &= 1783.76 \\
977.10B &= 831.38 \\
B &= 0.851
\end{align*}
$$

Amount in zero-coupon bonds:
$$
\begin{align*}
A/1.05 &= (1000 - 85.1)/1.05 \\
       &= 914.9/1.05 \\
       &= 871
\end{align*}
$$
